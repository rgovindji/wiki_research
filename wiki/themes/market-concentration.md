# Market Concentration

## What this is
The S&P 500 is a cap-weighted index, and the top 10 names — almost all Mag 7 plus adjacent — now account for an unprecedented share of total index weight. This makes the index, and the "market" by extension, a leveraged bet on a small number of AI-tilted companies.

## Current state (May 2026)
- **Top 10 stocks: >35% of S&P 500 weight** — exceeds the concentration seen at the 1929 and 2000 peaks
- **Magnificent Seven: ~35% of S&P 500** — same concentration the top 7 had at the dot-com peak
- The seven: [[NVDA]], [[MSFT]], [[GOOGL]], [[AMZN]], [[META]], [[AAPL]], [[TSLA]]

## Why this matters

### Mechanical risk
- A 20% drawdown in Mag 7 alone takes the S&P 500 down ~7% — passive index investors get hit even if the "average stock" is fine.
- The S&P 500 is no longer a diversified index in the textbook sense; it's a tech-heavy basket with a long tail.
- Equal-weight S&P 500 (RSP) is the cleanest hedge / alternative.

### Correlation risk
- Mag 7 ex-TSLA are all **correlated by the same AI capex cycle** (see [[ai-capex-cycle]]). A capex pause hits all of them simultaneously.
- "Diversifying within Mag 7" is largely an illusion at the macro level.

## Bull counter (why concentration may be different this time)
- The 1999 concentration was in **telecoms with limited cash flow and high debt**. Mag 7 are **highly cash-generative, low-debt, high-margin** businesses. The fundamentals are not comparable.
- Network effects and platform economics genuinely produce winner-take-most outcomes; concentration may be a **feature** of modern capital efficiency.
- Earnings concentration roughly tracks weight concentration — the index reflects where profits actually are.

## Bear case (it ends like 2000)
- Even high-quality businesses can de-rate if growth slows below expectations.
- Multiple compression at index level becomes severe if the top 10 re-rate.
- The "493" cannot pick up the slack in a cap-weighted index — by definition, they are <65% of weight.
- Forced selling from passive flows reverses if outflows hit.

## What it means for portfolio construction
- **If you own SPY/VOO**, you already have a 35% bet on Mag 7. Don't double down without intent.
- **Equal-weight S&P (RSP)** is a real diversification choice with structurally lower concentration risk.
- **Active stock picking** within Mag 7 (e.g. preferring [[GOOGL]] over [[TSLA]]) lets you own the AI thesis without owning the whole basket.
- **International/global exposure** ([[TSM]], [[ASML]]) is a partial concentration hedge — same theme, different geography and listings.

## Open questions
- Is concentration a structural feature of the AI / network-effects era, or a transient peak?
- What % of Mag 7 multiple expansion is **passive flow** (mechanical) vs. **earnings driven**?
- If Mag 7 de-rates 25%, does the S&P 500 hit a new low or just consolidate (i.e. does the 493 broaden out)?
