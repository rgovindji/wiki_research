# Space Economy

## What this is

The structural multi-decade investment cycle around commercial space — launch, satellites, space infrastructure, communications, Earth observation, defense space. Adjacent to but distinct from [[ai-capex-cycle]]: the space economy has its own funding sources (DoD + commercial broadband + national security + private capital chasing IPO comps) and its own bottlenecks (launch capacity, regulatory spectrum, rare-earth magnets, orbital slots).

## Why it matters now

The space economy crossed **$470B in 2026** with **78% commercial revenue** (Morgan Stanley + market research). Three forces are compounding:

1. **Launch cost compression of 95% over the past decade** (SpaceX-driven). Falcon 9 reusability brought cost from ~$10,000/kg to ~$2,000/kg. Neutron + Starship target sub-$500/kg by 2028. **Which means:** entire categories of satellite businesses that weren't economical 10 years ago (mega-constellations, direct-to-cell, Earth-observation-as-a-service) are now investable.
2. **DoD Space Force budget at $40.2B FY26 (+40% YoY).** Largest single-year increase ever. **Golden Dome layered missile defense** is the new mega-program. **Commercial Augmentation Space Reserve** plans 20 contracts by EOY 2026 — formalizing commercial as part of DoD's space posture.
3. **SpaceX IPO confidentially filed April 1, 2026 at $1.75-2T**. Whenever it lists, it triggers a re-rating of comparable public space companies upward (RKLB, ASTS) AND a re-anchoring of valuations broadly. Index inclusion in S&P 500 / Nasdaq-100 would be material.

> **What this means:** Space is no longer a "future" theme — it's a $470 billion live economy growing 8%+ a year, with the US government as the anchor customer and SpaceX about to become the largest defense-adjacent IPO in history. The investable question is which sub-categories will see the most asymmetric upside as the cycle scales.

## Key drivers

### Launch cost compression (the foundational driver)

| Vehicle | Cost/kg | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Falcon 9 (SpaceX) | ~$2,000 | Operational; reusable |
| Falcon Heavy | ~$1,500 | Operational; rarely flown |
| Neutron (Rocket Lab) | ~$2,000 (target) | Debut later 2026 |
| Starship (SpaceX) | ~$500 (target) | Test flight phase |
| Electron (RKLB) | ~$25,000 (small lift premium) | Operational |

### Constellation buildout

- **Starlink (SpaceX):** ~9M+ subscribers, $11.4B revenue 2025; ~70% of SpaceX total
- **Kuiper (Amazon):** Planned 3,236 satellites; deploying through 2029
- **AST SpaceMobile ([[ASTS]]):** Direct-to-cell from space; 45 BlueBirds target EOY 2026, 248-satellite FCC authorization
- **Eutelsat OneWeb:** ~648 satellites operational
- **Chinese Guowang + Qianfan:** Megaconstellations planned ~13,000 satellites each (parity / superiority claim with Starlink)

### Defense space buildout

- **Golden Dome layered missile defense** — Space Force's flagship multi-year program. LMT, NOC, RTX are co-primes; RKLB selected for Space Based Interceptor.
- **Sentinel ICBM** — NOC; ground-based but space-integrated targeting
- **B-21 Raider** — NOC; nuclear bomber + space-integrated mission systems
- **Commercial Augmentation Space Reserve** — 20 contracts by EOY 2026; formalizes commercial-DoD integration

### Direct-to-cell satellite (the disruptive bet)

- **[[ASTS]]** is the technical bellwether — 98.9 Mbps to unmodified smartphone is a real validated achievement
- **Starlink Direct-to-Cell with T-Mobile US** is already operational
- TAM: $300-500B if direct-to-cell achieves meaningful share of global cellular subscribers (Morgan Stanley + UBS scenarios)

## Risks / counter-thesis

- **Timeline slip is the base case for new-space.** Neutron, Starship, BlueBird constellation — all historically miss maiden / production targets by 1-3 quarters.
- **Commoditization risk at the launch layer.** If 5+ medium-lift providers emerge by 2030, margin compression is real for non-SpaceX players.
- **Spectrum / regulatory bottleneck for direct-to-cell.** FCC has approved ASTS's Supplemental Coverage from Space for 248 satellites; international regulatory varies by country. UK CMA / EU equivalents are slower.
- **DoD budget political risk.** Trump admin has signaled willingness to renegotiate defense prime economics (Jan 2026 executive order on pay + buybacks). Defense primes' valuations are sensitive to political environment.
- **SpaceX dominance is the elephant in the room.** Starlink at 9M subscribers and growing makes the satcom competitive landscape difficult for ASTS, IRDM, SATS. If Starlink Direct-to-Cell scales globally before ASTS reaches commercial scale, ASTS thesis weakens materially.
- **Capital intensity.** ASTS Q1 net loss $191M/quarter on $14.7M revenue. Even with $3.5B cash, runway depends on execution + ability to raise more capital.

## Open questions

- When does SpaceX IPO actually price? Confidential filing → public listing typically 6-9 months → Q4 2026 plausible.
- Does Neutron's debut succeed first-time? Maiden-flight failure rates historically ~50%.
- Does ASTS get a binding multi-billion commercial contract from AT&T or Verizon by 2027?
- How does Golden Dome contract structure split between LMT, NOC, RTX, RKLB, and (private) Anduril?
- Does Chinese Guowang/Qianfan reach Starlink-class operational scale by 2030? If yes, geopolitical bifurcation of the satcom layer becomes a hard reality.
