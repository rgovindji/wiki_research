# AI Value-Migration & Capex-Unwind Playbook

> The strategic map for the most important question hanging over the book: **when the AI-infrastructure trade unwinds, what falls, what holds, where does the money go — and what is the actual tradeable expression?** Built from a multi-day thesis the curator developed across June 2026, stress-tested against the wiki's own demand-side evidence.

## The thesis in one line
AI infrastructure value **migrates** — from the rented-shovel layer (commodity compute) toward (a) the durable chokepoints and (b) the application / distribution / proprietary-content layer — and the migration *accelerates* into any capex air-pocket. But **the trigger and the breadth are widely misjudged**: it is differential, not uniform; the catalyst is the private-AI *unlocks*, not the IPOs themselves; and a powerful demand-side counterweight (agentic traffic) means this is more likely a *rotation* than a *collapse*.

## Pre-committed view (conviction discipline)
- **View:** Don't short "AI." Own the **spread** — long the durable chokepoints + the app/distribution/content layer, underweight/short the pure rented-shovel layer (sub-scale neoclouds, commodity memory beyond its contracted floor, merchant power, pluggable optics). This is right whether the migration grinds slowly (it compounds) or a capex shock accelerates it (the spread blows out).
- **Confidence:** medium on the *structure* (the tiers, the spread); **low** on *timing* (when the unwind starts) — "inevitable" theses are notoriously early.
- **Falsifiers:** (1) a hyperscaler capex guide-down where the **chokepoints (TSM/ASML/NVDA) de-rate as hard as the commodity layer** → commoditization is *not* differential and the tiering is wrong. (2) The OpenAI/Anthropic listings price strong and the **whole complex melts up** for two quarters → the unwind read is early and the unlocks aren't the trigger. (3) Open-weight share of inference *stops rising* (OpenRouter) and frontier pricing power holds → the "LLM layer commoditizes" leg is wrong.

## 1. The two-scenario engine: *why* a capex cut happens is everything
A hyperscaler capex guide-down (next test: **Q2 prints, late July 2026**) does **not** mechanically mean "platforms rally / shovels fall." The market's reaction is conditional on the *reason*:

| | **Scenario A — Discipline / "harvest"** | **Scenario B — ROI / demand doubt** |
|---|---|---|
| Why capex falls | Enough built; ROI proving out; shift to harvesting FCF | Monetization isn't showing up; pull back because spend isn't paying |
| Platform FCF | Inflects **up** (the $80B+ drag lifts) | Irrelevant — the growth story they're valued on is cracking |
| Reaction | **Platforms re-rate higher** (rotation) | **Everything falls** — shovels most, platforms less, but no rally |
| Regime | Rotation | Risk-off |

**The signaling problem:** the first hyperscaler to cut gets read as **Scenario B regardless of stated reason** — the trade is too consensus, so any capex disappointment fires the bubble fear first. Base case is therefore **risk-off air-pocket first → rotation second, and only if the data confirms discipline.** The disambiguator is **cloud revenue growth + RPO/backlog** (Azure/GCP/AWS): capex down *with* backlog holding = discipline (bullish platforms); capex down *with* cloud decel = demand crack (bearish all). For names with giant backlogs ([[ORCL]] ~$500B, [[GOOGL]] ~$460B), a capex cut can even read *bad* ("can't fulfill backlog").

**The real trigger is the unlock, not the IPO.** The OpenAI/Anthropic listings are more likely a melt-*up* validation on the day (a $1T+ app layer retroactively justifies the infra). The repudiation comes from (a) the **post-IPO filings** exposing burn/ROI, and (b) the **lockup expiries** — today everyone long the AI *theme* via private stakes is locked and can't express a top; once they unlock, the theme's biggest believers get liquidity and rotate out of the *whole* complex (incl. public chip/memory/power longs) to rebalance. That mechanical, theme-wide deleveraging is the cleaner catalyst than "sell the news."

## 2. Differential commoditization — the three tiers (the heart of it)
"Everything the AI trade inflated falls together" is **true in a liquidity risk-off** (correlations → 1) and **false in the structural migration.** Commoditization is differential, and the tiering *is* the trade:

| Tier | Decommoditizes... | What it is | Names | In a risk-off | In the migration |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Chokepoints** | **last (if ever)** | Natural monopolies / process moats; value-capturers for years | [[ASML]] (EUV), [[TSM]] (leading-edge + CoWoS), [[NVDA]] (CUDA + reference-architecture standard), [[AVGO]] (custom-silicon franchise) | fall *with* everything | **survive / concentrate value** — own through |
| **Rented shovels** | **first and hardest** | Sub-scale, undifferentiated, pure capex-beta | sub-scale neoclouds ([[CRWV]]/[[NBIS]] ex-franchise portions), commodity DRAM/NAND beyond the contracted floor, merchant power, pluggable optics ([[AAOI]]), OSAT | fall **most** | **the source of the migrated value** — underweight/short |
| **App / distribution / content** | **captures the migrated value** | Owns the workflow, the distribution, or the proprietary data | the platforms with distribution ([[GOOGL]]/[[MSFT]]/[[META]]/[[AMZN]]), surviving software ([[VEEV]]/[[NOW]] usage-based), proprietary-data owners ([[RDDT]]), and OpenAI/Anthropic themselves | fall (high beta) | **the destination** — accumulate on the rotation |

The crucial correction to the curator's original framing: **"serve it at reasonable cost" usually means the *scaled incumbent* IS the low-cost winner** (TSMC is low-cost *and* high-moat). So commoditization kills the *second-tier*, it doesn't kill the leaders — and taken to its conclusion, the value-migration thesis is the **bull case for the lagging demand-side platforms + proprietary content** (see [[ai-software-disruption]], [[market-concentration]]).

## 3. The demand-side counterweight (don't be one-sided): agentic traffic
The bear's first leg ("models get efficient → aggregate compute demand falls → capex drops") has a serious rebuttal. Per [[2026-06-25-cloudflare-prince-agentic-web]] (Cloudflare CEO; CEO-bias-flagged but the crossover is a measured Radar number): **bots/agents passed humans online in H1 2026**, years ahead of forecast, with a 1,000×-in-5-years projection — an agent shopping a camera hits ~5,000 sites vs a human's 5. Agentic AI is a structural **compute/traffic multiplier** (the Jevons case made concrete; see [[inference-economics]], [[bottleneck-roadmap]] CPU leg). 

**Which means:** this *splits* the bear case rather than refuting it — the **"value migrates"** leg gets *stronger* (Prince also confirms the ad model breaking → licensing/micropayments), while the **"aggregate demand falls"** leg gets *weaker*. Net read: the unwind is more likely a **rotation within AI** (shovels → chokepoints + app/content) than a **collapse of AI**. The same logic the wiki has carried as [[ai-bubble-debate|Jevons paradox]] and the [[inference-economics|"open-weight commoditizes the lab layer while total compute rises"]] reconciliation.

## 4. The MU special case: pricing-top ≠ stock-top (the SCA decoupling)
A common error in the current bear chatter is "memory pricing tops → [[MU]] tops." The June 24 print broke that equivalence: Micron locked **~half its revenue into multi-year take-or-pay SCAs with price floors through 2030** ([[2026-06-24-MU-Q3-FY26-earnings]], [[mu-core-book-reconsideration]]). So the *commodity pricing cycle* can roll over while the *equity* de-rates far less than the 2018/2022 playbook implies — the contractual floor raises trough earnings. MU is the cleanest case of a "rented shovel" partially **migrating itself into the chokepoint tier** via contract structure. (Caveat preserved: floors struck at the apex, enforceability untested through a real bust — so it's a *partial* de-risk, not immunity.)

## 5. Positioning — the tradeable expression
- **The core trade: long the spread.** Long chokepoints ([[ASML]], [[TSM]], [[NVDA]], [[AVGO]]) + app/distribution/content ([[GOOGL]]/[[MSFT]]/[[META]] on the rotation, [[RDDT]] / proprietary data, [[VEEV]]/[[NOW]] survivors); underweight/short the rented-shovel layer (sub-scale neoclouds, commodity-memory beta beyond the contracted floor, merchant power, pluggable optics, OSAT). Right in both regimes.
- **Don't pre-position big into the binary.** The unwind's *timing* is the low-confidence variable; size for "grinds for quarters" while keeping dry powder for the air-pocket the unlocks/first-capex-cut could trigger.
- **Power names** ([[CEG]]/[[NRG]]/[[VRT]]): the one chokepoint-adjacent layer still cheap (filed as "utilities"), but they're rate-sensitive and partly capex-beta — own the *deployment* bottleneck, not as a hedge.
- **The hedge that actually hedges:** S&P puts do *not* hedge a high-beta AI book; if hedging the unwind, hedge the *factor* (SOXX/semis), not SPX (see [[hedging-risk]]).

## 6. The dashboard — tells to watch
| Tell | What it signals |
|---|---|
| **Hyperscaler capex guides (late-July Q2 prints)** | The master switch. Cut = unwind risk on; *raise* = the trade has another leg |
| **Cloud rev growth + RPO/backlog alongside any capex cut** | Disambiguates Scenario A (discipline, backlog holds) from B (demand crack) |
| **OpenAI / Anthropic lockup-expiry calendar** | The real deleveraging trigger (not the IPO date) |
| **Open-weight share of inference (OpenRouter token counts)** | Rising = LLM-layer commoditization confirmed; flat = frontier pricing power holds |
| **Memory contract prices + CoWoS lead times** | Decelerating/shrinking = the bottleneck rents are self-liquidating (the rotation has begun) |
| **Agentic traffic / CPU tightness (Cloudflare Radar, CPU ASPs)** | The demand-multiplier counterweight — is the "demand falls" leg actually wrong? |
| **Chokepoint vs commodity-shovel relative performance** | The thesis *in the tape* — if TSM/ASML start outperforming the neoclouds/commodity layer, the migration is underway |

## What would prove this wrong
- Chokepoints de-rate as hard as the commodity layer on a capex cut (commoditization is uniform, not differential).
- The mega-IPOs price strong and the whole complex melts up for two quarters (early; unlocks aren't the trigger).
- Open-weight inference share stalls and frontier labs hold pricing (the LLM-commoditization leg fails).
- Agentic demand keeps total compute compounding so fast that even the rented-shovel layer never gets a real air-pocket (the multiplier overwhelms the migration — bullish *everything*, the spread doesn't pay).
