# Does the SCA model make Micron a core holding? (post-FQ3-FY26 reconsideration)

## The question
We have deliberately kept [[MU]] **out** of the core long-term book, holding it as a *"rent, don't marry"* tactical position on one specific objection: memory is structurally cyclical (a 40–70% drawdown in every cycle since 1998), and buying near all-time highs on a cheap-looking forward P/E is the textbook **cycle-peak-P/E trap** — you pay a low multiple on peak earnings that then compress. The June 24 FQ3 FY26 print ([[2026-06-24-MU-Q3-FY26-earnings]]) introduced a genuinely new, *contractual* mechanism — **16 multi-year take-or-pay Strategic Customer Agreements (SCAs)** — that attacks that exact objection. This page asks: does it change the answer?

## Pre-committed view (conviction discipline)
- **View:** the SCAs are a real, material reduction in downcycle severity for ~half the book — enough to justify a **partial core allocation (a core slab + a tactical sleeve)**, but **not** enough to treat MU like a secular compounder ([[NVDA]] / [[TSM]] / [[ASML]]). Upgrade from "rent" to "own a core-sized position you intend to hold through normal volatility, plus a tactical sleeve you actively manage" — not to "marry and never sell."
- **Confidence:** medium.
- **Falsifier:** if any SCA counterparty publicly renegotiates or walks a take-or-pay floor in the next downturn, OR MU's next two prints reveal the contracted floor margin sitting *below mid-cycle* (i.e. the floor was struck so high relative to a normalized cycle that it's irrelevant when prices fall) — the de-cyclicalization thesis is wrong and MU reverts to pure rent.

## What actually changed
The SCA structure, verbatim from the prepared remarks:
- **16 agreements, take-or-pay, 5-year term (CY2026–2030)** — binding volume commitments, ~20% of DRAM volume + ~⅓ of NAND volume; **~half or more of total company revenue** once all are executed.
- The largest carry a **price floor held through the term** (and a ceiling at the current CQ2 market price). Management's claim: *"the floor price enables a very robust gross margin for Micron, well above our peak quarterly margins in any past cycle."*
- **14 of 16 = ~$100B cumulative revenue at the *minimum* price** over the term; **$22B of cash deposits/commitments** hitting the balance sheet starting FQ4.

The point: for the first time, a large fraction of MU's revenue has a **contractual margin floor** rather than pure spot-price exposure. That is precisely the variable the cycle-peak-P/E bear assumes goes to zero in a bust.

## The case FOR upgrading to a core slab
1. **A contractual through-cycle floor.** The bear's mechanism (prices collapse → margins crater → "E" evaporates → the cheap P/E was a trap) is *structurally muted for the contracted half.* If half the book can't fall below a floor that's above prior-peak margins, the trough earnings in the next downcycle are mechanically higher than any prior cycle — which is the entire reason the multiple should re-rate toward an infrastructure name rather than a commodity.
2. **Demand visibility the franchise never had.** ~$100B of cumulative minimum-price revenue + $22B of customer deposits is the demand side *pre-funding* MU's capacity — the "hyperscaler forward-funded capacity" signal (flagged since the May 26 UBS $1,625 note) now has signed paper behind it.
3. **Balance sheet is no longer a cyclical liability.** Net cash $24.4B, BBB+ from all three agencies, FCF a record $18.3B, and a stated intent to return ~100% of excess cash after Dec 2026. A core holding needs to survive its own downcycle; this balance sheet does.
4. **The bear's favorite line is now inverted.** "~70% of MU is commodity DRAM/NAND = the cyclical drag, the bull rests on ~30% HBM" was the standing objection. Post-print, commodity DRAM is the *margin engine* (SK Hynix throttled HBM4 to chase it — [[2026-06-23-sk-hynix-hbm4-throttle-commodity-dram]]) **and** it's increasingly contracted under the SCAs. The drag became the anchor.

## The case AGAINST a full core upgrade (why a slab, not the whole position)
1. **The floors were struck at the apex.** "Current CQ2 market price" is the most extreme pricing environment in 25 years (84.9% gross margin). "Above our past-peak margins" and "well below today's 85%" can both be true — the floor could still imply a painful step-down from current earnings, just a less catastrophic one.
2. **The rate of price increase is already rolling.** The FQ4 GM guide *"reflects a meaningful moderation in the rate of price increases"* — the second derivative is turning, consistent with Korea DRAM +3% MoM ([[2026-06-21-korea-exports-memory]]) and the SK Hynix throttle. The contracted floor matters *more* precisely because spot momentum is fading.
3. **Micron is funding the 2028 supply wave itself.** FY27 capex guided *above* FQ4 levels, >half the increase from construction pull-in. The bull's own roadmap still has a built-in cyclical end date around 2028 ([[bottleneck-roadmap]]), and MU is building toward it.
4. **Take-or-pay enforceability is untested through a real bust.** Binding contracts get renegotiated when counterparties are themselves distressed; the floor's value is a function of customer solvency in the exact scenario it's meant to protect against. We have never seen these specific contracts stress-tested.
5. **Half the book is still spot.** The SCAs de-risk ~half; the other half is the same cyclical commodity it always was.
6. **Entry timing is its own risk, separate from the franchise question.** Even granting the franchise upgrade, MU is being considered for core *after* a ~200% YTD run to all-time highs. The franchise can be more durable and the entry can still be poor.

## Synthesis / recommendation framing
*(Not advice — the bull case argues, the bear case argues.)* The SCAs justify treating MU differently than before: the bull case for **a core-sized slab held through normal volatility** is now legitimate, because the contractual floor genuinely raises the trough-earnings the next downcycle can produce. But the bear case against treating it as a **full secular compounder** is intact: floors struck at the apex, decelerating spot, a self-funded 2028 supply response, and untested enforceability mean exit discipline still applies to the portion above the slab. The clean expression of both: **a core slab (held like NVDA/TSM) + a tactical sleeve (managed like the old rent position).** Sizing below the secular compounders remains appropriate.

## What would settle it
- **Next 1–2 prints:** does the realized/guided *floor* gross margin sit above a normalized mid-cycle level? If yes, the de-cyclicalization is real; if the floor is only marginally above trough, it's marketing.
- **Do SK Hynix / Samsung adopt the same take-or-pay model?** Industry-wide adoption = structural de-cyclicalization of the whole complex (bullish all three). If instead they undercut MU on spot to take share, the SCA floor becomes a *share-loss trap* (MU holds price/margin but loses volume).
- **First real downturn:** do the take-or-pays hold, or do customers renegotiate? This is the only true test of the thesis, and it won't come until the cycle turns.
