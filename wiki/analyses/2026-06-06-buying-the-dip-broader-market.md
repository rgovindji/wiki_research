# Buying the dip: should you add, and is "the broader market" the right vehicle? (June 2026)

> **The question (asked June 5–6, 2026):** "So don't buy anything today? Could I buy the broader market?" — framed against a portfolio that's heavy AI/semis + Mag 7 and just took a hard hit. This page is the framework answer. It is a research synthesis, not a recommendation — the bull and bear cases are laid out so the decision stays the reader's.

## The setup (as of June 5, 2026 close)

The market just had its worst day of 2026 ([[2026-06-05-jobs-selloff-rate-fears]], [[overview]]). Closing levels and — critically — **drawdown from the June 1 record peak**:

| Index | June 5 close | Day | **Off June 1 peak** | Wiki 5% threshold? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| S&P 500 | 7,383.74 | −2.64% | **−2.8%** (peak 7,599.96) | **No — not yet** |
| Nasdaq Composite | 25,709.43 | −4.18% | **−5.1%** (peak 27,086.81) | **Yes — hit** |

**Which means:** the single most-quoted discipline rule in the wiki — *"treat any 5%+ pullback as a buy signal, not a panic signal"* ([[overview]]) — gives **two different answers depending on which "broader market" you mean.** That is the crux of the question.

## "The broader market" is three different bets

| Vehicle | What you're actually buying | Off-peak | Read |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SPY / VOO** (S&P 500) | ~35%+ Mag 7 / mega-cap tech — a *diluted version of the portfolio you already own* | −2.8% | Below the 5% buy-signal threshold. Less diversification than it sounds — same names that just fell, just watered down. |
| **QQQ** (Nasdaq-100) | The most concentrated AI/tech bet — the epicenter of this selloff | ~−5% | Hits the threshold, but it's the *most* exposed to the rate shock + the new AI-bubble-sentiment driver. Buying the thing that's falling because it's falling. |
| **RSP** (equal-weight) / value / financials | Genuine diversification *away* from AI concentration; financials actually *benefit* from higher-for-longer rates | n/a | The only "broader market" that's a different bet, not a leveraged echo of the existing book. |

> **What this means (plain English):** If the instinct behind "buy the broader market" is *"I want exposure to the dip without picking a single landmine like META or AVGO,"* the index is the right *vehicle* — it neutralizes the company-specific surprises (META's equity-raise scare, AVGO's whisper-miss) and just gives you the macro move. **But SPY is not an escape from AI concentration** — it *is* AI concentration, at ~35%+. If the goal is to *reduce* the AI tilt, SPY doesn't do that; equal-weight or a different sector does.

## The bull case for adding now

- **Nothing in the tape broke AI *demand*.** AVGO *raised* its full-year AI revenue guide ($56B FY26) two days before the stock fell 18%. The selloff is rates + positioning + sentiment — not a fundamentals downgrade. ([[2026-06-03-AVGO-Q2-FY26-earnings]])
- **Nasdaq has hit the wiki's own −5% threshold.** By the pre-committed rule, that's a buy-signal zone, not a panic zone.
- **Forced-selling signature.** ~$1T wiped from semis in a session with the VIX only at ~17 — this looks like fast-money de-grossing, not a fundamental re-rating with capitulation.

## The bear case for waiting

- **The character changed at the close.** Per the [[overview]] EOD update, a *third* driver joined jobs + AVGO + META: **AI-bubble skepticism itself** ("AI faith crumbling," "questioning Jensen Huang") was cited as an explicit catalyst — the first 2026 session where bubble doubt, not just rates, drove the tape. The burden of proof has visibly moved toward the bears for the first time in nine weeks. ([[ai-bubble-debate]])
- **The long end broke back above 5%** (20Y + 30Y). Duration repricing at the long end compresses growth multiples regardless of earnings — and that's a slower-moving headwind than a one-day flush.
- **You'd be buying ~2 days before a known binary.** The **June 10 CPI** is the highest-leverage scheduled event in the near-term framework ([[2026-06-10-cpi-binary]]). A hot print is a −3% to −7% S&P scenario; a cool print is +1.5% to +3%. Deploying full size the session before that is buying a coin-flip you know is coming.
- **S&P (the headline "broader market") is only −2.8% off its high** — below the threshold. By the rule, SPY hasn't even reached buy-signal territory yet.

## How the framework resolves it (not advice — the wiki's own logic, applied)

1. **Lump-sum, full-size, today** fights two pre-committed rules at once: *"don't add fresh size yet"* and the −5% threshold (which SPY hasn't hit). Hard to justify inside the framework.
2. **The framework-consistent path, if acting, is tranching:**
   - A **small first tranche** is defensible *now* — Nasdaq is at the threshold and the demand thesis is intact.
   - Hold **dry powder** for the two outcomes you can actually wait for: (a) a **hot-CPI flush** that drags the S&P past −5% (then the headline index is in buy-signal range too), or (b) a **cool CPI** that confirms the relief valve — at which point the [[2026-06-10-cpi-binary]] playbook says *add long-duration AI on the day*.
3. **The index over single names is the correct vehicle** for that tranche while the macro overhang resolves — it's the cleanest way to hold "rates dip, not demand break" without eating another META/AVGO-type idiosyncratic surprise.

## The June 10 CPI binary, condensed (full detail in [[2026-06-10-cpi-binary]])

| Scenario | Prob | S&P reaction | What it means for an index add |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cool** (core MoM ≤0.2%, core YoY ≤2.7%) | ~30-35% | +1.5% to +3% | Relief valve — playbook says *add* long-duration AI on the day. The dry-powder-deploy trigger. |
| **Stable hot** (the priced central case) | ~45% | ±1% | No major change; rotation > direction. A held tranche is fine; don't chase. |
| **Accelerating** (core MoM >0.4%, core YoY >2.9%) | ~25-30% | **−3% to −7%** | The flush. *This* is where S&P joins Nasdaq past −5% — and where the playbook trims, not adds. Waiting buys you a better entry. |

## Bottom line

- **Vehicle question:** index > single names *right now* — it sidesteps the company-specific landmines. But "broader market = SPY" is still ~35% the same AI names; if you want true diversification, that's equal-weight/value, not SPY.
- **Timing question:** the framework leans **patience or small tranches over a full-size buy**, because (a) the S&P hasn't hit the −5% rule, (b) a known binary (CPI) sits 2 sessions out, and (c) the selloff's *character* turned toward bubble-sentiment at the close — a new, slower-moving risk.
- **What the wiki can't tell you:** your cash level and time horizon drive the tranche-vs-wait call more than the market does. The wiki supplies the setup and the discipline; the sizing is yours.
