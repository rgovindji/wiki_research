# The Rotation Side Book — overlooked stocks for "the other side of the bottleneck"

*A complement to the AI-supply-chain–heavy [After Hours model portfolio](../../newsletter/portfolio.json). Built 2026-06-16 on the user's brief: now that the Iran war is over (oil cracked to ~$80, deal "complete") and money may rotate out of the crowded AI trade, find quality that **hasn't run yet and isn't in the masses' eyes.** The AI trade is not declared done — this is a parallel book of asymmetric, under-followed names.*

## The thesis in one paragraph
Three forces converge in June 2026: (1) **war over → risk-on breadth** + lower oil + the rate-cut conversation reopening; (2) **Gavin Baker's call** that the bottleneck trade is ending and the next game is "enduring franchise value on the other side" ([[2026-06-16-baker-bottleneck-rotation-boj-hike]]); (3) a **rotation already underway** — through 2026 leadership has broadened away from mega-cap tech into financials, industrials, utilities, materials and (lately) healthcare, with real assets the top sector YTD. This book owns the franchises the crowd left behind, not the picks-and-shovels it already bid to the moon.

## The selection filter (what makes the cut)
1. **Hasn't run** — trading meaningfully below its 52-week high, ideally de-rated on a *narrative*, not deteriorating fundamentals.
2. **Quality / franchise** — durable moat, real cash flow (no pre-profit story stocks; that's a different risk book).
3. **A rotation catalyst** — benefits from breadth, lower rates/oil, the IPO wave, M&A, or "money leaving crowded AI."
4. **Not in the masses' eyes** — under-followed angle or an out-of-favor sector, not the obvious trade.

## What I deliberately EXCLUDED (and why) — the discipline matters
- **Grid / electrification equipment** (ETN −6%, HUBB −11%, NVT, AGX) — great theme, but all **near 52-week highs**. Already run; fails filter #1.
- **Nuclear / uranium** (URA +120% past year) — structural AI-power story, but the trade is crowded and extended. Not under-the-radar anymore.
- **Copper majors at highs** (FCX −2.7% off high) — copper deficit is real, but FCX is priced for it. Took the cheaper, lower-cost long-life name instead (SCCO).
- **The run AI bottleneck names** (NVDA/MU/AVGO/AXTI/AAOI) — that's the After Hours book's job; this book is the complement.

## The book — 4 buckets, 10 names, ~20% cash
All entry prices = 2026-06-16 close. Conviction is honest; sizing reflects it.

### Bucket 1 — De-rated franchise software (~26%) · "left for dead quality"
The AI-disruption narrative crushed profitable, moaty SaaS that the story doesn't actually break. This is Baker's "franchise on the other side," on sale.
- **ADP** — 11% @ $222 (−30% off high). Payroll/HCM franchise, dividend aristocrat, ~30%+ recurring, fortress margins. Down on "AI disrupts payroll" fear; ADP is more plausibly *infrastructure* for AI-driven workforce management. **Conviction: high.** *Bear:* secular seat-count risk if AI genuinely compresses headcount.
- **PAYX** — 8% @ $100 (−34% off high). SMB payroll, high-margin, dividend. Same narrative overshoot, smaller-business cyclicality. **Conviction: medium-high.** *Bear:* SMB formation slows in a downturn.
- **PAYC** — 7% @ $129 (−48% off high). Higher-growth payroll; the deepest de-rate = the most beta if the narrative reverses. **Conviction: medium.** *Bear:* genuinely more AI-automatable than ADP; the discount is partly earned.

### Bucket 2 — Capital markets / the AI-IPO cycle (~24%) · "the non-obvious AI beneficiary"
2026 IPO proceeds ~$160B (≈4× 2025); SpaceX done, OpenAI (Sept) + Anthropic (Oct) ahead; private-market liquidity unlocking. The crowd thinks "AI = chips"; almost nobody owns the *plumbing of the listing wave* — and several have de-rated.
- **ICE** — 9% @ $141 (−25% off high). Intercontinental Exchange: NYSE + data + mortgage tech. Quality franchise on sale; data + IPO-cycle + eventual rate-driven mortgage thaw. **Conviction: high.** *Bear:* mortgage-tech drag if rates stay high.
- **NDAQ** — 8% @ $89 (−12% off high). Nasdaq: listings + index + market-data annuity; **direct** beneficiary as the mega-AI labs list on Nasdaq. **Conviction: medium-high.** *Bear:* listings are cyclical; if the IPO wave slips, so does the catalyst.
- **KKR** — 7% @ $99 (−36% off high). Alt-asset manager; the private-market liquidity unlock (AI-lab exits, IPO realizations) flows to fee + carry. Beaten down with rate-sensitive financials. **Conviction: medium.** *Bear:* most rate- and credit-cycle sensitive name in the book.

### Bucket 3 — Healthcare contrarian (~22%) · "multi-decade-low valuations + M&A + rate cuts"
Healthcare has lagged the S&P by a wide margin for 3 years; biotech P/Es at multi-decade lows; biotech M&A running ~one deal/week; a classic late-cycle defensive rotation destination that's also a rate-cut beneficiary.
- **STE** — 8% @ $206 (−23% off high). STERIS: sterilization + endoscopy, recurring razor-blade model, quality compounder simply on sale. **Conviction: high.** *Bear:* hospital capex softness.
- **MOH** — 7% @ $200 (−36% off high). Molina: managed care, deeply out of favor on medical-cost-ratio fears — the contrarian end. **Conviction: medium.** *Bear:* if MLR pressure is structural not cyclical, the cheapness is a trap.
- **CNMD** — 7% @ $34 (−39% off high). Small-cap surgical devices; beaten down, recurring disposables, plausible **M&A target** in the biotech/medtech deal wave. **Conviction: medium.** *Bear:* leverage + small-cap execution risk; deal optionality isn't a thesis on its own.

### Bucket 4 — Real assets, selectively (~8%) · "materials is the actual 2026 leader, still mid-innings"
- **SCCO** — 8% @ $194 (−12% off high). Southern Copper: lowest-cost, longest-life reserves; refined-copper deficit (~330kt 2026 per JPM) from electrification + grid + AI. Cleaner than FCX (which is at its high). **Conviction: medium-high.** *Bear:* China demand + a global growth scare hit copper hard and fast; Peru jurisdiction risk.

### Cash — ~20%
Rotations are choppy (June 15 tech +3%, June 16 tech −1.2% — the tape whipsaws on each headline). Dry powder to add on the inevitable head-fakes; this is a 6-18 month *tilt*, not an all-in.

## How this complements the After Hours book
After Hours owns the **AI build-out** (supply-side: NVDA/MU/AVGO/TSM/CRWV…). This side book owns **what the build-out's success rotates money toward** — the de-rated franchises, the listing-wave plumbing, the contrarian defensives, the real-asset deficit. If Baker is right and the bottleneck trade peaks, this book is the hedge that also makes money; if the AI trade keeps running, these are quality names bought cheap with limited downside. Deliberately low correlation to the main book.

## Honest risks on the whole book
- **Rotation head-fake** — the single biggest risk. Money has tried to leave mega-cap tech before and been pulled back by the next AI catalyst. This is why cash is 20%.
- **Rates don't fall** — BoJ just hiked to 1%, Warsh Fed is an unknown. Several names here (KKR, MOH, ICE-mortgage) are rate-cut-levered; sticky rates blunt the thesis.
- **Value-trap risk** — "cheap" healthcare/financials can stay cheap; the catalyst (M&A, rate cuts, IPO wave) has to actually show up. Each name has a named bear above.
- **Not yet validated** — this is a *new* book with zero track record; treat conviction labels as starting hypotheses to be scored, like the After Hours predictions.
