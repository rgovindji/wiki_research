# The Rotation Side Book — asymmetric, catalyst-driven names for "the other side of the bottleneck"

*A complement to the AI-supply-chain–heavy [After Hours model portfolio](../../newsletter/portfolio.json). Built 2026-06-16. **This is v2.** v1 was a de-rated large-cap value screen (ADP/ICE/KKR/MOH/SCCO…); the curator's verdict was blunt and correct — "trash picks: too boring / low upside, and down=broken." It solved the wrong problem (out-of-favor ≠ undiscovered; cheap-and-falling ≠ asymmetric). v2 is the rebuild: a **barbell of catalyst-driven asymmetric small/mid-caps + two overlooked-quality anchors.***

## What changed from v1 (the lesson)
- **v1 optimized for "quality on sale."** Crowded mega-caps down 30-48% on narrative. Defensible value, wrong brief.
- **v2 optimizes for "hard catalyst + real upside."** Every name has a *specific, recent* catalyst (a government award, a hyperscaler deal, an equity stake) and 2-3x potential on the asymmetric sleeve — not a 30% value rebound. "Down" is only acceptable when it's sector-beta on a name whose catalyst is intact, and those are sized as bets, not anchors.

## The selection filter (v2)
1. **A hard, recent, specific catalyst** — DOE money, a hyperscaler contract, a government stake. Not "it's cheap."
2. **Real upside** — 2-3x potential on the asymmetric sleeve; anchors with genuine growth, not just low multiples.
3. **Not a falling knife** — prefer names *working/inflecting*; catalyst names that pulled back on sector beta are allowed but flagged and sized down.
4. **Under-followed** — off the consensus AI-mega-cap and value-screen radar.
