## The biggest day for AI stocks this year (it's not the one you think)

*The bond market might steal the show on Wednesday.*

---

### Lede

This Wednesday — May 20 — Nvidia reports earnings. Every business news outlet will tell you it's the most important day of the year for AI stocks. They're half right. **The same afternoon, the US Treasury auctions $16 billion of 20-year bonds.** Whichever one goes badly probably decides the week.

### Why the bond auction matters

Most retail investors never think about Treasury auctions. They should. Here's why:

When the US government wants to borrow money for 20 years, it asks investors to buy bonds. The "interest rate" — the yield — is whatever investors are willing to accept. **If lots of investors want in, the auction goes well and yields stay low.** If few investors want in, the auction "tails" (clears above expected yield) and yields rise.

Higher long-term yields = bad news for growth stocks. Why? Because growth stocks are valued on cash flows years from now. When yields go up, future cash flows are worth less today. So **stocks like NVDA, MSFT, and the whole AI complex sell off when yields rise**.

Last Wednesday (May 14), the 30-year Treasury auction cleared at **5.046%** — the first auction above 5% since 2007. Bid-to-cover was 2.3x, well below the 2.42x average of recent auctions. **Translation:** demand was soft. Investors are demanding higher yields to lend money to the US government for 30 years.

If Wednesday's 20-year auction also tails, that's two strikes in a row. Markets will start pricing in a "structural" rate problem — not just a passing inflation scare.

### Why NVDA earnings matter (obviously)

NVDA is currently 7.5% of the S&P 500. That means **if you own VOO or any S&P 500 fund, you have meaningful NVDA exposure whether you chose it or not.** Wall Street expects $1.78 in earnings per share on $78.98 billion of revenue (78% year-over-year growth). The stock has run up to all-time highs going in.

Three things to watch on the call:

1. **Gross margin trajectory.** Above 73% = bullish. Below 72% = bearish.
2. **Vera Rubin demand commentary.** Vera Rubin is NVDA's next-generation AI chip platform. Specific customer commitments = bullish; vague language = neutral.
3. **China H200 update.** The US has approved NVDA to sell H200 chips to 10 Chinese firms. China hasn't approved purchases. If NVDA doesn't even mention China revenue in guidance, expect a sell-off.

### The compound shock scenario

Here's the case nobody is talking about: **what if the 20-year auction tails AND NVDA misses guidance, both on the same afternoon?**

Bond yields spike + AI stocks sell off + the broader market follows. You could see the S&P 500 down 2-3% in a single session. It's not the base case, but it's a real tail scenario.

What if the opposite happens? Strong auction + NVDA beats + Vera Rubin commentary is fire? You could see S&P 500 hit new all-time highs by Thursday close.

If you're long the AI trade (and if you own VOO, you are), **Wednesday is the highest-leverage single day of Q2**. You don't need to trade it — but you should know what's happening and why.

If you're considering buying NVDA at the highs into earnings, **don't**. Wait for the print, see how it reacts, then decide. The expected move is ±13% — that's $28 of stock price either way. The reward for being early isn't worth the risk for retail.
