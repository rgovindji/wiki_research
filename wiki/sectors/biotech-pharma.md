# Biotech & Pharma (stub coverage)

## Why this page exists

The wiki covers defensive healthcare (UNH, JNJ, MRK) at stub level in [[defensives]], but **biotech and weight-loss pharma have radically different risk/return profiles** that deserve their own page. Biotech is high-beta, binary-catalyst-driven, and largely uncorrelated to the AI capex curve — making it both a portfolio diversifier and a potential YOLO category.

## Why biotech is structurally different from the wiki's AI thesis

- **Not rate-sensitive in the same way** as AI duration plays — biotech moves on clinical readouts, FDA decisions, and M&A activity, not 30y Treasury yields
- **Catalyst-driven, not compounding** — single Phase 3 readout can 5x or -80% a stock
- **Different liquidity profile** — many speculative biotechs are sub-$5B cap with thin order books
- **Adverse selection in venture-backed names** — most clinical-stage biotechs underperform; the few winners drive the index

## The weight-loss pharma anomaly

**LLY + NVO are the biotech world's MEGA winners of 2023-2026** — but the wiki has no coverage despite both being top-10 most-discussed names in financial media. Quick framing:

| Variable | LLY | NVO |
|---|---|---|
| Drugs | Mounjaro (tirzepatide) + Zepbound | Ozempic (semaglutide) + Wegovy |
| 2026 sales (est) | ~$60-65B | ~$45-50B |
| Market cap (May 2026 est) | ~$900B-$1T | ~$550-600B |
| Forward P/E | ~50-55 | ~30-35 |
| Pipeline depth | Deep — orforglipron oral, retatrutide | Deeper — multiple oral + injectable |

**The bull case:** GLP-1 class is one of the most consequential drug classes in 40 years; addresses 700M global obesity sufferers + 530M diabetics + emerging indications (cardiovascular, kidney, sleep apnea, Alzheimer's potentially); 10-year revenue runway.

**The bear case:** Generic competition starts ~2031 (Ozempic) / ~2033 (Mounjaro); pricing pressure from insurance/governments; competitor biosimilars; LLY P/E ~50 prices in continued perfection.

**Pre-committed view (stub-level):** LLY and NVO deserve standalone company pages. Likely bull/medium stances pending deeper coverage. **Note for the user:** if you've considered weight-loss exposure, this is exactly the kind of category the wiki has structurally missed.

## When to look here

**Trigger conditions for adding biotech/pharma:**

1. **AI-correlation breakdown** — biotech historically rallies when AI capex story stalls; diversifier
2. **M&A reactivation** — large pharma facing patent cliffs (PFE, MRK) is acquisition-hungry; M&A premium expansion
3. **FDA / regulatory clarity** — Biden-era FDA was friendlier than current admin; watch for pipeline approvals
4. **Specific catalysts** — Phase 3 readouts, NDA submissions, label expansions

**Trigger conditions for trimming biotech:**

1. **AI catch-up trade** — periodic capital rotates back to AI mega-caps and biotech compresses
2. **Rate spike** — high-beta speculative biotech is rate-sensitive (long-duration cash flows)
3. **Single-name binary loss** — Phase 3 fail = -50% to -80% same-day; size carefully

## The "biotech YOLO" frame (a category worth flagging)

For risk-tolerant investors, **clinical-stage biotech is the highest-asymmetry public-market category** outside crypto. Single approvals can 10x positions; failures can wipe them out. Position sizing rule: **never more than 0.5-1% any single speculative biotech name**, with diversified basket approach (10-20 names) if pursued.

The wiki does not currently track speculative biotechs by name. **If the user wants exposure to this category, flag it explicitly** — wiki would need to build:
- Pipeline-tracker page
- Catalyst calendar
- Position-sizing rules specific to binary-outcome names

## Key questions / what to watch

1. **GLP-1 saturation** — when does growth slow from "explosive" to "linear"?
2. **CRISPR / gene therapy real-world approvals** — CRSP/NTLA/EDIT moving from clinical to commercial
3. **Alzheimer's drug class** — Eisai/Biogen lecanemab; LLY donanemab; uncertain commercial trajectory
4. **Inflation Reduction Act drug pricing** — first batch of negotiated prices in 2026; long-term margin pressure for Medicare-exposed drugs
5. **Trump admin FDA approach** — RFK Jr era FDA; uncertain how this affects approval cadence
