# Consumer Cyclicals & Autos (stub coverage)

## Why this page exists

The wiki covers [[TSLA]] (avoid/bear) and [[AAPL]] (bear) as individual names but has **no broader consumer cyclical or auto sector coverage** — leaving a structural gap for any user purchase in retail, travel, autos, brands, or restaurants. With the macro framework flagging **tariff pass-through, consumer rate sensitivity, and rising real costs**, the consumer cyclical sector is one of the wiki's largest coverage holes.

## When to look here

**Trigger conditions for adding consumer cyclicals:**

1. **Consumer confidence inflection** — University of Michigan / Conference Board surveys turning up
2. **Mortgage rates drop** — homebuilder + HD/LOW benefit
3. **Tariff easing** — TJX/NKE/LULU margin relief if reciprocal tariffs drop (per [[inflation-tariffs]] counter-bear relief valve)
4. **Trade-down trade reverses** — if consumer health strengthens, premium brands outperform discount
5. **Dollar weakening** — international consumer names (MCD, KO) benefit

**Trigger conditions for trimming:**

1. **Tariff escalation** — direct margin hit to brands/apparel
2. **Unemployment rises** — consumer cyclical sells off broadly
3. **Rate hike risk** — consumer financing tightens; auto + home demand weakens

## Special case: Amazon ([[AMZN]] — already covered)

The wiki currently treats AMZN as a structural AI bull (AWS narrative), not primarily as a consumer cyclical. **Both stories are true:** AMZN's retail business is its own consumer-cyclical thesis with margin expansion potential separate from AWS. **Pre-committed view:** the [[AMZN]] page should likely add a "retail margin story" section — flagged for next review.

## Special case: autos as policy plays

Autos in 2026 are unusually policy-driven:
- **EV credits / IRA** — Trump admin has signaled willingness to roll back; if executed, RIVN/LCID/TSLA all hit
- **Tariffs on Chinese EVs** — protect F/GM legacy ICE; benefit GM China JV less
- **Trump-Xi summit** delivered marginal de-escalation; full auto-tariff resolution still unclear

**Pre-committed stance (stub-level):**
- Legacy OEMs (F, GM): neutral — tariff-protected but EV transition risk
- EV pure-plays ex-TSLA: avoid (low conviction)
- Mobility (UBER): bull (low) — AV optionality + consumer durability
- TSLA already in wiki: avoid (bear) per [[TSLA]] page

## The consumer-rate-sensitive gap (this is why the page exists)

Per [[fed-policy]] / [[inflation-tariffs]], the macro framework explicitly flags:
- Higher-for-longer rates → mortgage demand soft → HD/LOW soft
- Tariffs → apparel/brand margin compression → NKE/LULU pressure
- Consumer financing rates elevated → auto demand mediocre → F/GM/RIVN headwinds

These macro signals predict bearish consumer cyclical outcomes — but the wiki had **no specific tickers** to anchor that view to. This page closes the gap so user purchases in this space (e.g., "I'm thinking of buying Costco" or "Should I add HD?") are evaluated within the wiki's macro framework.
