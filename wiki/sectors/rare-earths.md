# Rare Earths

## What this is

The 17-element rare-earth (REE) supply chain — extraction, separation, refining, alloying, and end-market magnet/optic/catalyst applications — and the U.S. policy-driven decoupling from China's dominant position. Rare earths are not literally rare; the bottleneck is the **metallurgical separation and downstream sintered-magnet capacity**, which China spent 30 years building and the West largely outsourced.

This is the **physical-stack analog to the AI capex cycle**: every modern hardware system that converts electrical energy to motion, or vice versa, depends on neodymium-iron-boron (NdFeB) permanent magnets or one of the heavy rare earth additives (dysprosium, terbium) that make those magnets stable at high temperature. AI ships into data centers; data centers run on motors, transformers, hard drives, and cooling systems that consume rare earths at the substrate layer. EVs, wind turbines, humanoid robots, fighter jets, and MRI machines are even more direct consumers.

> **What this means:** Rare earths are to the *physical* AI buildout what [[ASML]] EUV is to the *digital* one — a single-source bottleneck whose pricing power compounds as every downstream demand vector ramps simultaneously. The investable framework is the same as [[copper-thesis]] and [[bottleneck-roadmap]]: identify the constraint, own the constraint, expect re-rating as the demand vectors are priced in.

## Why it matters now

Four forces converged in 2025–2026:

1. **China formalized rare-earth export controls** through 2025 — the same playbook used for gallium / germanium / graphite, now extended to NdFeB magnets and heavy rare earths. Confirmed Tesla Optimus production was hit by these controls per Musk public commentary (May 2026, see [[MP]]). The "Chinese-monopoly" frame is no longer theoretical leverage; it's actively shaping Western production schedules.
2. **U.S. industrial policy went all-in.** DoD multi-billion-dollar partnership with [[MP]]. Apple $500M rare-earth commitment. Northlake TX magnet facility breaking ground 2026. DoE grants for separation R&D. Friend-shoring partnerships with Australia (Lynas) and Brazil. **The U.S. is treating rare-earth supply as a national security emergency, not a commercial procurement problem.**
3. **Humanoid robotics demand step-change.** Each humanoid actuator uses ~10× the NdFeB content of an EV motor. Tesla Optimus alone targets ~3.5 kg NdFeB/unit (per [[MP]] recent developments) — at 1M-units/year run-rate that's ~3,500 tonnes/yr, on top of EV + wind + defense baselines. See [[robotics]].
4. **AI infrastructure as second-order REE consumer.** Data centers consume rare earths through hard-disk drives (precision micro-magnets), VRM components, polishing compounds for semi wafers, and rare-earth phosphors in displays. Not the dominant demand line, but the strategic-criticality narrative attaches the REE story to the AI thesis — which is what unlocks the policy and capital response. See [[ai-capex-cycle]] and [[sovereign-ai]].

> **What this means:** Three years ago, rare earths were a cyclical commodity story with light political tailwind. Today they are an explicit pillar of US-China strategic competition with a fully-fledged policy apparatus (DoD direct investment, fast-track permits, defensive tariffs). The macro re-rating is *already* visible in [[MP]] price action; the second-tier extraction + processing names (the U.S. domestic supply build-out) are where the late-cycle upside lives if and when those projects actually reach commercial production.

## The 17 elements — quick orientation

Rare earths split into **light** (LREE: La, Ce, Pr, Nd, Pm, Sm, Eu, Gd) and **heavy** (HREE: Tb, Dy, Ho, Er, Tm, Yb, Lu, Y) families. The investable subset:

| Element | Symbol | Primary use | Strategic notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Neodymium | Nd | NdFeB permanent magnets | The single most demand-impactful element; EV, wind, robotics, defense |
| Praseodymium | Pr | NdFeB magnets (paired with Nd) | Co-mined with Nd; "NdPr" is the magnet-grade headline product |
| Dysprosium | Dy | High-temperature magnet stabilizer | China >85% — the binding HREE constraint |
| Terbium | Tb | High-temperature magnet stabilizer + phosphors | China ~90%+ |
| Cerium | Ce | Polishing compound (semi wafers, optics), catalysts | Most-abundant LREE; co-product of NdPr mining |
| Lanthanum | La | Catalytic cracking (oil refining), specialty glass | Oversupplied historically; flat demand |
| Samarium | Sm | SmCo magnets (defense — high-temperature niche) | Critical for missile / aerospace applications |
| Yttrium | Y | Laser crystals, ceramics, superalloys | Defense and semi adjacent |
| Gadolinium | Gd | MRI contrast agent, neutron-absorber alloys | Medical-critical |
| Europium / Terbium | Eu / Tb | Phosphors (displays, lighting) | Niche but no substitute |
| Scandium | Sc | Aerospace superalloys (Sc-Al) | Tiny market; critical for advanced airframes |

**Investable signal:** demand and pricing power concentrate around **NdPr (magnets)** and **Dy/Tb (high-temp magnet additives)**. La/Ce are co-product oversupply problems — they are the *cost* of NdPr mining, not the prize.

## The U.S. strategic pivot — policy framework

Per the May 2026 stack-post inventory:

| Lever | Mechanism | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Friend-shoring** | Bilateral partnerships with Australia (Lynas) and Brazil; co-investment in mine + processing | Active |
| **Capital injection** | DoD and DoE direct grants to domestic producers (MP, USA Rare Earth, Energy Fuels, NioCorp) | Active |
| **Trade protection** | Defensive tariffs / domestic-content rules shielding US producers from China dumping | Partially active; sector-specific |
| **Next-gen tech R&D** | DoE grants for REE recycling, coal-waste extraction, mine tailings | Early stage |

**What's missing from the policy stack (bear-watch):** *binding* offtake guarantees from major commercial customers beyond Apple/GM/DoD. The political will is in place; the commercial pull-through that would underwrite a 10-year capex cycle is still partial. Watch automaker + hyperscaler magnet contracts as the empirical signal.

## Risks / counter-thesis

This is the section the user's stack post does NOT cover — and the wiki house rule is to surface it.

### Substitution risk

- **Tesla pursuing rare-earth-free motors** since at least 2023. If Tesla and other automakers crack high-efficiency synchronous reluctance or wound-rotor designs at EV-grade efficiency, the NdFeB demand projection compresses materially. Watch for OEM disclosure on motor mix.
- **Ferrite magnet upgrades.** Recent Japanese R&D on grain-boundary-engineered ferrites has narrowed the performance gap. Not yet at EV-traction grade, but on the roadmap.
- **For LREE-heavy applications** (Ce polishing, La FCC catalysts) substitution is harder, but those are not the growth lines.

### Chinese price warfare

- China's actual leverage cuts both ways. They are the dominant *producer*, but also a dominant *buyer* of intermediate REE products. They can collapse NdPr prices to bankrupt Western producers BEFORE Western capacity reaches commercial scale — the same playbook used against U.S. solar manufacturers 2010–2015 and Western lithium 2015–2020.
- **MP's [[MP]] cap table risk:** ~7.7% Shenghe Resources (partly Chinese state-owned) ownership is awkward optics in an escalating export-control regime.

### Recycling supply

- NdFeB magnet recycling from end-of-life EVs, wind turbines, and electronics is **the most credible meaningful new supply source** with a 5–10 year ramp. If recycling reaches commercial scale faster than U.S. virgin-ore separation, the policy-driven investment thesis on virgin producers weakens.
- EU specifically has aggressive REE recycling policy. DoE grants in this space (per the policy stack above) could create technology winners that displace mining.

### Demand-side overstatement

- Humanoid robotics 1M-units/yr by 2027 is the bull-case anchor. If the humanoid ramp slips to 2029-30 (a reasonable downside), the demand step-change is delayed and Western producer balance sheets — which are negative or marginal cash flow today — get stressed.
- AI infrastructure REE exposure is real but second-order. Don't size on AI multiples expecting REE-direct earnings.

### Permitting and construction risk

- The U.S. has not built a new rare-earth separation plant in decades. Northlake TX, USA Rare Earth Stillwater OK, MP Fort Worth — all are first-of-kind for current management teams. Schedule slips of 12–24 months on first commercial production are the base-rate outcome, not the worst case.
- Mountain Pass ([[MP]]) is the only mine actually producing today. Every other listed name is *pre-commercial*. Conviction tiering should reflect this.

### Price volatility

- NdPr oxide pricing is set by China's State Reserve Bureau in practice. A coordinated Chinese price intervention could compress producer margins even with secured offtake contracts (as happened to MP in 2024).

## Open questions

- **When does the first commercial NdFeB magnet ship from MP Stillwater?** Single most material near-term industry catalyst. Slipped once already.
- **Does Tesla scale rare-earth-free motors in production volume before 2028?** Bear case-killer if yes.
- **Do hyperscalers (MSFT, AMZN, GOOG, META) sign multi-year REE-content contracts?** This would close the "commercial pull-through" gap in the policy stack.
- **Will the DoD push domestic-content rules into the FY27 procurement language?** Industrial policy lever that would force private commercial demand.
- **Does the EU recycling thesis reach commercial scale before US virgin capacity?** This determines who captures the policy-driven re-rating.
- **Chinese export-control escalation:** does Beijing extend controls to LREE (Ce, La)? Currently NdPr + heavy REE only; broader controls would force a more aggressive Western response.
