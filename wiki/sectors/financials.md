# Financials (stub coverage)

## Why this page exists

The wiki's [[fed-policy]] framework is the central rate-regime page, but the wiki has no coverage of the **most rate-sensitive equity sector**. Financials are bidirectionally exposed: banks benefit from net interest margin (NIM) expansion when rates rise but suffer from credit losses; insurers earn investment income on float; payments compound on consumer activity. This stub closes the gap.

**Stub coverage.** Deepen with proper company pages when user signals interest.

## When to look here

**Trigger conditions for adding financials:**

1. **Rate regime stabilizes high** — banks earn ~5% on cash/securities forever
2. **Yield curve steepens** — banks benefit on borrow-short, lend-long math (currently muted with inverted curve)
3. **M&A market reopens** — IB names (GS, MS) lever on deal volume
4. **AI IPO cycle continues** — IBs underwrite (CBRS already done; SpaceX pending) → fee income
5. **Consumer spending resilient** — V/MA compound on transaction volume
6. **Late-cycle alpha hunt** — payments compounders (V, MA, BRK) often outperform in tail of cycle

**Trigger conditions for trimming financials:**

1. **Credit cycle turns** — provisions for loan losses spike (look at JPM/BAC guidance commentary)
2. **CRE meltdown** — regional banks vulnerable
3. **Recession signal** — IB volumes collapse; payment volumes drop
4. **Tech disintermediation** — fintech (PYPL, SQ) competing with banks structurally

## Special case: BRK (Berkshire Hathaway)

Not really a financial — it's a holding company with insurance float (~$330B), Apple ($75B-ish position), railroad (BNSF), energy (BHE), and a $325B+ cash position earning ~$15B/yr at current rates. **Effectively a defensive compounder with embedded rate-sensitive cash optionality.** Worth its own page if user wants exposure.

## The AI-finance intersection (overlooked)

Two ways AI may show up in wiki-tracked financials:

1. **JPM** invests heavily in AI infrastructure — Jamie Dimon has called it "a transformational technology." Cost-takeout play; not yet showing up in margins materially.
2. **Asset managers** (BLK, BX, KKR) launch AI-themed funds and benefit from flow tailwind. **Apollo + Blackstone are positioning as private credit lenders to neoclouds** ([[CRWV]] equipment financing; [[NBIS]] data center development debt) — direct AI-capex exposure via debt rather than equity.
