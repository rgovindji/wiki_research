## Why precious metals specifically (the case the wiki hadn't made)

Gold has been in a **structural bull market since 2024** driven by:
- Central bank buying (China, India, Turkey, Russia) hedging against dollar weaponization post-Russia sanctions
- Real rates falling from peak as Fed cuts get priced (then unpriced May 2026)
- US deficit trajectory $1.8T+ annually = fiscal-dominance regime

As of May 2026, gold is **~$3,400/oz** — already up significantly from the $2,000-2,200 range of 2024.

**Pre-committed view:** GLD is the only commodity name the wiki currently recommends with conviction — see [[hedging-risk]] Tier 1 sizing (1-2% structural late-cycle position).
