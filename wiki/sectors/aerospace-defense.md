# Aerospace & Defense

## What this is

The combined sector covering **commercial space (launch + satellites + space infrastructure)** and **defense aerospace primes**. They overlap meaningfully — defense customers are the largest funding source for many "space" companies, and the major defense primes (Lockheed, Northrop, RTX) all have space-segment businesses. This sector treats them as one investable theme with internal tiers.

## Why it matters now

Three structural drivers converging in 2026:

1. **DoD / Space Force budget at $40.2B FY26 (+40% YoY).** The "Big Beautiful Bill" + Trump administration reconciliation gives Space Force its largest single-year increase ever. **Golden Dome layered missile defense architecture** is the new mega-program — sized comparable to F-35 in scope.
2. **Commercial space economy hit $470B in 2026 (78% commercial).** Morgan Stanley projects $1.1T by 2040; newer forecasts target $1.8T by 2035. **Launch costs are down 95% over the past decade**, driven by SpaceX reusability — opening up TAM that wasn't economical before.
3. **SpaceX IPO confidentially filed April 1, 2026 at ~$1.75-2T valuation.** Whenever it lists, it becomes the single largest space-economy public security. Index funds will rebalance materially; competitor valuations get pulled up by comp.

> **What this means:** The space economy isn't a "future" anymore — it's a $470B real sector growing 8%+ annually, with the largest defense buildout since the 1980s flowing through Space Force budgets. The investable question isn't "is space real?" — it's "which tier of the supply chain captures the most value as the cycle scales?"

## Key drivers

- **DoD modernization wave.** Golden Dome (missile defense), Sentinel (ICBM replacement), B-21 Raider (bomber) — three multi-decade programs in the build phase simultaneously
- **Satellite constellation deployment.** Starlink + Kuiper (Amazon) + AST SpaceMobile (direct-to-cell) + Eutelsat OneWeb — collectively launching 10K+ satellites this decade
- **Reusable launch cost compression.** SpaceX Falcon 9 reusability drove cost to ~$2,000/kg; Neutron + Starship targeting $500/kg by 2028
- **Geopolitical tail risk.** Iran conflict + Trump-Xi summit (see [[us-china-relations]]) elevate defense spending floor; Taiwan dynamics keep aerospace primes' valuations supported
- **Direct-to-cell satellite-to-phone** — ASTS is the speculative bellwether; if it works, terrestrial cellular gets disrupted

## Golden Dome detailed breakdown (NEW 2026-05-14)

Per [[2026-05-14-space-economy-budget-sources]] (Aerospace Corp FY26 budget brief), the $24.4B Golden Dome reconciliation funding splits into 4 categories:

| Category | $ | Primary beneficiaries |
|---|---|---|
| **Tracking threats from space** | **$9.2B** | [[NOC]] (MTI satellites + radar heritage), [[LMT]] (space systems), satellite primes |
| **Space-based missile interceptors** | **$5.6B** | [[RKLB]] (Space Based Interceptor program selection with Raytheon partner), RTX |
| **Launch and test range infrastructure** | $910M | SpaceX (private), [[RKLB]] |
| **Terrestrial elements** | $8.7B | [[LMT]], RTX, NOC (radars, hypersonic + ICBM defense) |

**Moving-Target-Indicator (MTI) satellites — underappreciated mega-program:**
- **$500M FY25 → $8.8B FY26 = 17.6x growth** (within tracking-threats category)
- Replaces E-3 Sentry AWACS + retired E-8C JSTARS
- NOC's E-8 legacy + radar/EW heritage = primary beneficiary
- Caveat: E-7 Wedgetail reinstatement push (House Appropriations + HASC) could delay ramp

## Commercial services gap pattern (CSIS, April 2026)

Despite $71B FY27 Space Force request, only **$1.4B clearly earmarked for commercial services — and $1.3B of that is launch (SpaceX).** Non-launch commercial space (TacSRT, SDA, Earth observation, weather) face the "Pentagon requests little → Congress adds money" cycle year after year.

**Why this matters for the wiki:**
- **BKSY (BlackSky)** and **PL (Planet Labs)** — Earth observation; exposed to TacSRT starvation. Not in wiki yet; flagging as cautious-coverage candidates.
- **IRDM, SATS** — commercial satcom is $1.2B annually but funded by other service branches, not Space Force directly.
- **[[ASTS]]** — partial exposure; $190M new proliferated LEO satcom line is mildly positive; broader pattern is structural headwind for Pentagon-dependent leg.
- **Structurally favored** (commercial gap = moat): SpaceX (private; captures $1.3B launch line), [[RKLB]] (HASTE + Space Based Interceptor).

## Risks / counter-thesis

- **Timeline slip is the base case for new-space.** RKLB Neutron debut "later 2026" has historical base-rate of 1-2 quarter slips. ASTS 45 BlueBirds is ambitious vs current Block 1 in orbit.
- **Defense prime valuations are not cheap.** NOC up 29% YTD 2026; LMT, RTX both near highs. Multi-decade backlogs are real but priced in.
- **SpaceX IPO is the dominant unknown.** When it lists, comp valuations get re-anchored. Could be bullish for RKLB/ASTS (rising-tide) or bearish (capital flow to SpaceX from comps).
- **Capital intensity for new-space.** ASTS net loss $191M/quarter on $14.7M revenue; even with $3.5B cash, runway requires execution.
- **Direct-to-cell competition.** Starlink Direct-to-Cell is already operational with T-Mobile; ASTS competes head-on, not in a vacuum.
- **Defense political risk.** Trump administration has signaled willingness to renegotiate defense prime contracts (Fortune Jan 2026 piece on RTX/LMT/Northrop pay + buyback executive order).

## Open questions

- When does SpaceX IPO actually price? Confidential filing → public listing typically 6-9 months. So ~Q4 2026 plausible.
- Does Neutron's debut launch succeed first-time? Maiden-flight failure rates historically ~50%.
- Does ASTS get a binding multi-billion commercial contract from a top-tier MNO (AT&T / Verizon) by 2027?
- How does Golden Dome contract structure split between LMT, NOC, RTX, RKLB, and Anduril? Material to relative tier-2 positioning.
- Does the rare-earth supply chain ([[MP]]) bottleneck defense magnet supply by 2027?
