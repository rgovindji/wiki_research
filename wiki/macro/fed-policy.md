# Fed Policy

## What this is
The path of US short-term interest rates as set by the FOMC. The single biggest macro variable for equity multiples.

## Current state (May 2026)
- **Federal funds target range: 3.50–3.75%** (held at the **April 2026** meeting)
- The April vote was **8–4** in favor of holding — the **largest dissent since October 1992**
- This was widely framed as **Powell's final meeting** as Chair
- FOMC statement: "Inflation is elevated, in part reflecting the recent increase in global energy prices"

### Post-PPI-CPI shock update (2026-05-13)

Following the April CPI + PPI hot prints (see [[2026-05-13-ppi-cpi-shock-positioning]]):

- **Incoming Fed Chair: Kevin Warsh** — confirmed taking the reins later in May 2026. Warsh has been **outspoken in favor of cutting** and aligns with President Trump's vocal preference for an easing central bank
- **CME FedWatch market pricing (per CNBC, May 12, 2026):**
  - **37% probability of a HIKE by EOY 2026** (up from ~0% pre-print)
  - **Cut probability through EOY 2027 = essentially zero** — a 24-month dovish bet erased in two prints
- **Moody's Analytics (Mark Zandi):** "I just don't see how he's going to get any kind of support for cutting interest rates in the current environment... If inflation expectations continue to move higher... Fed will likely focus on inflation and start raising interest rates"
- **Jefferies (Thomas Simons):** Still expects next move to be a cut rather than hike, but cuts "fading"; near-term expectation is hold

> **What this means:** The Fed just lost the ability to cut. A new Chair (Warsh) who was appointed *because* of his dovish tendencies is walking into an inflation regime that requires the opposite of what he and the President want. Markets now see a 1-in-3 chance the next move is actually a *hike*, not a cut. The single biggest macro variable for equity multiples has flipped from "rate-cut optionality" to "hike risk."

## The Warsh wildcard (NEW 2026-05-13)

The wiki's prior framing of "post-Powell era beginning" was too generic. Warsh's appointment introduces a **specific Fed-credibility risk vector** that wasn't priced into the original macro pages:

| Scenario | Probability | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Warsh aligns with the data → holds** | ~50% | Reluctant pause; modest credibility intact; rate-cut hopes deferred to 2027 |
| **Warsh aligns with Trump → cuts into hot inflation** | ~20% | **Fed credibility shock.** Long bonds sell off violently; dollar weakens; gold rallies; equity vol spikes. Worst outcome for the wiki's long-duration AI complex. |
| **Warsh capitulates → hikes** | ~15% | Bond rally on regained credibility; equity vol short-term but long-term constructive |
| **FOMC overrules Warsh / hawkish dissent dominates** | ~15% | Hawkish hold; Warsh's authority compromised; volatile communications regime |

**Which means:** the Fed's reaction function is materially less predictable than it was 30 days ago. **The 20% "cut into hot inflation" tail is the single worst outcome the wiki has not yet priced** — it's the closest analog to the late-1970s Burns Fed and would damage long-duration tech multiples severely.

## Key drivers
- **Energy-driven inflation** — Middle East tensions are a wildcard; oil & gas pass-through is the proximate inflation source
- **Tariff pass-through** — average US tariff rate up from 2% to 12%; Goldman estimates consumers absorb 67% of the cost (see [[inflation-tariffs]])
- **Soft labor market** — the dovish dissenters lean on this; the hawks lean on sticky inflation
- **Chair succession** — post-Powell era starts mid-2026; the new chair's reaction function is unknowable until policy is set

## Forward path (post 2026-05-13 PPI shock)
- **JPM Global Research:** Fed holds rates steady through the rest of 2026, with the next move likely a **+25bp HIKE** in Q3 2027 (was the hawkish outlier; now closer to consensus after PPI shock)
- **Market-implied (CME FedWatch, May 12, 2026):** **37% probability of a hike by EOY 2026**; cut probability through EOY 2027 essentially zero
- **Hawks (Hammack, Kashkari, Logan):** position vindicated by April prints; warning persistent inflation requires patience or hikes
- **Doves + Warsh:** awkward position — political bias toward cuts conflicts with data; June 10 CPI is the binary that determines if dove camp regains credibility

## Implications for equities
- A **higher-for-longer rates** regime compresses growth-stock multiples — a real risk for [[market-concentration]] in the Mag 7 at current valuations (see [[valuation-environment]]).
- AI capex is largely **internally funded** by hyperscaler cash flows, so direct rate sensitivity is limited at the project level — but multiple compression hits the same names.
- Defensives and value stocks (lower duration) outperform if rates stay elevated longer than expected.

## Open questions
- ~~Who is the next Fed Chair~~ → **Confirmed: Kevin Warsh**, taking reins May 2026. Reaction function still uncertain; political bias dovish but inflation data forcing realism.
- Does energy revert (Iran de-escalation) and free the Fed to cut, or does it stay sticky and force the next move to be a hike?
- How much of the 8–4 dissent reflects genuine policy disagreement vs. positioning ahead of the Warsh transition?
- **(NEW)** Does Warsh test the market by cutting into hot inflation? The "cut into 4% CPI" tail (~20% probability) is the single worst outcome for long-duration equities the wiki has not yet priced.
- **(NEW)** Does the FOMC committee structure constrain Warsh? Voting roster + dissent dynamics could blunt any dovish push.
