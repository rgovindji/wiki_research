# Valuation Environment

## What this is
The aggregate-level price the market is paying for a dollar of earnings, cash flow, and GDP. Frames "expensive" vs. "cheap" for everything.

## Current state (May 2026)

| Measure | Reading | Context |
|---|---|---|
| S&P 500 forward 12mo P/E | **20.9** | vs 5yr avg 19.9, 10yr avg 18.9 (late April 2026) |
| CAPE / Shiller P/E | **39.8** | Highest since 2000 dot-com peak (44.19); long-run avg ~17 |
| Buffett Indicator (mkt cap / GDP) | **217–228%** | Dot-com peak was ~150%; Buffett's "reasonable" range is 75–90% |
| Top 10 stocks share of S&P 500 | **>35%** | See [[market-concentration]] — exceeds 1929 and 2000 concentration |
| S&P 500 level | 7,209 (April close) | All-time high |

## Why valuations matter (and don't)

- **Forward P/E of ~21** is elevated but not historically extreme **if 2026 EPS growth (+21% expected)** comes through.
- **CAPE of ~40** uses a 10-year smoothed denominator and tells a different story — it's flashing dot-com-era warning levels.
- The two can coexist because forward P/E is "if earnings are right" and CAPE is "what if earnings mean-revert."
- High valuations don't predict 12-month returns well, but they correlate strongly with **lower 10-year forward returns**.

## Bull case (valuations are justified)
- **Earnings growth:** consensus 2026 EPS growth of **21%** outpaces multiple expansion. If realized, the index "grows into" its multiple.
- **Mag 7 quality:** the 35% concentration is in **highly profitable, capital-light businesses** with structural moats — not the telecoms/dot-coms of 1999-2000.
- **AI productivity:** if AI delivers on capex, productivity gains expand profit margins economy-wide → justifies higher steady-state multiples.

## Bear case (valuations are dangerous)
- **The 493:** ex-Mag 7 EPS growth is forecast to **double from 2025** to keep up — a "razor-thin margin for error" (FactSet/JPM).
- **CAPE 39.8 + Buffett 217%+ has only happened at the top of bubbles.** Both tools have a bad track record for timing but a great track record for warning.
- **AI capex is the single biggest source of EPS growth (~40%)** — if hyperscaler capex pauses, multiple AND earnings compress simultaneously.
- **Concentration is fragile** — a 20% drawdown in Mag 7 names alone takes the S&P 500 down ~7%.

## What this means for portfolio decisions
- Tilt toward names with **earnings catalysts** (e.g. [[NVDA]] hardware ramp, [[GOOGL]] cloud reaccel) and **cheap-relative-to-Mag-7 multiples** (GOOGL again).
- Be wary of **multi-multiple-expansion plus multi-EPS-expansion** stories priced for perfection (e.g. [[TSLA]] at 178x).
- Maintain dry powder / equal-weight exposure where possible, given Mag 7 concentration in cap-weighted indices.

## Open questions
- At what point does the **gap between forward P/E (modest) and CAPE (extreme)** resolve, and which way?
- How much of the multiple is **passive flow / index inclusion driven** vs. fundamentally earned?
- If 2026 EPS growth comes in below the 21% bar (e.g. 12-15%), what's the multiple compression risk?
