# Weekly Synthesis — Template

> **How to use:** Copy this template each Friday to `wiki/analyses/weekly-synthesis-YYYY-MM-DD.md`. Fill in the bracketed sections from wiki state + this week's prints. Strip internal annotations for the Substack version (the `[INTERNAL]` blocks).

## Purpose

A weekly distillation of the wiki's state — what changed, what matters, what to do — designed to be **both an internal review artifact and a Substack-publishable post.** Different from daily updates: weekly is for synthesis and trend-spotting, not blow-by-blow news.

Goal length: **800-1,500 words** for the publication version (Substack sweet spot). Internal version can be longer.

## Cadence

- **Friday EOD** — draft using wiki state of the week
- **Saturday AM** — light edit + publication-version strip
- **Saturday/Sunday** — publish (optional weekly Substack cadence) or hold for monthly cadence

## Structure

---

### [TITLE — narrative, not "weekly synthesis"]

*Examples:*
- *"The week the bond market spoke louder than the equity market"*
- *"AMAT raises CY26 to >30%: when the supply side validates the demand side"*
- *"5 of 6 indicators firing, but the wiki just bought hedges instead of selling"*

---

### Lede (2-3 sentences)

The one thing that mattered this week, framed as a hook. Avoid jargon in the first sentence. Lead with a concrete fact ("X happened") then implications ("which means Y for Z").

**[INTERNAL]** Tactical note: the Substack reader doesn't have the wiki context. The lede needs to stand on its own.

---

### The Big Picture (3-5 bullets)

Macro state of play, distilled. Each bullet should be one durable point + a "which means" clause.

Template:
- **[Macro variable]:** [current state] — which means [implication for portfolio / framework]
- **[AI capex curve check]:** [strongest single data point this week] — which means [bull thesis status]
- **[Bond market signal]:** [rate / spread / auction result] — which means [equity multiple implications]
- **[Geopolitical / policy]:** [key event] — which means [risk to specific sectors]

**[INTERNAL]** Limit to 5 bullets. If you have 7 ideas, the synthesis is too thin — go deeper on fewer points.

---

### What Changed in the Wiki This Week

The reader's "I trusted you to do the work, what did you find" section. Bullet what the wiki actually updated or added:

- **[Stance / conviction changes]** — if any (these are rare; flag prominently)
- **[New company / sector / theme pages]** — what was created and why
- **[Frameworks that were validated or contradicted]** — feedback-log entries
- **[Coverage gaps closed]** — process improvement items

**[INTERNAL]** This section is the unique value vs other financial Substacks. Most newsletters report news; the wiki reports what its analytical framework did with the news.

---

### One Idea Worth Holding On To

A single, durable thought worth zooming into. Could be:
- A framework refinement (e.g., "bottleneck cascade is on schedule")
- A counterintuitive observation (e.g., "memory cycle-peak P/E paradox")
- A pre-mortem on a binary catalyst
- A historical analog being misapplied

**Length:** 200-400 words. The reader's takeaway if they only remembered one thing.

## Publishing checklist (Substack version)

Before hitting publish:

- [ ] Strip all `[INTERNAL]` blocks
- [ ] Remove wiki-link syntax `[[X]]` → replace with markdown links to public sources where possible; otherwise drop
- [ ] Replace ticker mentions with `$TICKER` format (Substack convention)
- [ ] Add disclosure: "This is a research synthesis, not investment advice. Stances are research stances; verify before any decision."
- [ ] Add SEO-friendly subtitle (~120 chars)
- [ ] Set publication tags: ["Weekly synthesis", "AI capex", and event-specific tags]
- [ ] Schedule for Saturday 9:00 AM ET (highest open rate for finance Substacks per industry data)

## Voice & style notes

- **Third person, plural ("the wiki", "our framework") in the analysis sections.** Avoids hagiography while preserving the editorial voice.
- **First person plural ("we" for the synthesis author's POV) in the sign-off only.** Builds reader relationship without breaking the analytical frame.
- **Avoid hedging language.** "Could potentially possibly maybe" → "may." "It's worth noting that" → just say the thing.
- **No emojis in the body.** Standard Substack finance convention.
- **One graphic per post minimum.** Cap allocations / scenarios / catalyst calendar / before-after table — visual anchor.

## Substack ↔ Wiki feedback loop

After publishing:
- **Engagement signals worth tracking:** open rate, share rate, click-through on linked wiki pages
- **Reader questions** → feed into wiki Q&A backlog → become new analyses pages
- **Cross-citation:** if other Substacks engage, that's a framework-validation signal
- **Subscriber growth rate vs framework hit rate** in [[feedback-log]] — credibility compounds
