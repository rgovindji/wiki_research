## What's at stake

This is the single highest-leverage macro event in the wiki's near-term framework. The April CPI + PPI shock flipped market expectations from "rate cuts coming" to "1-in-3 hike." The May print decides whether that flip persists or reverses.

**The wiki's central tension:** AI capex curve is *accelerating* (AMAT >30%, CSCO $9B AI orders, Anthropic $9→$30B ARR) — but the bond market is pricing higher-for-longer at the long end (30y >5%). The CPI print breaks the tie.

## Pre-committed scenarios

### Scenario 1 — COOL (probability ~30-35%, refined up from 25%)

**Triggers (need 2+ to qualify):**
- Headline CPI YoY **≤ 3.6%** (vs 3.8% prior)
- Core CPI YoY **≤ 2.7%** (vs 2.8% prior)
- Core MoM **≤ 0.2%** (vs 0.4% prior)
- Energy index falls MoM (OPEC+ supply moderation visible)
- Shelter inflation moderates

**Macro implications:**
- 30y Treasury → back below 5%
- Hike probability collapses from 37% → <10%
- Cut probability re-emerges for 2027
- Dollar weakens; gold may correct
- USD weakens vs EUR/JPY
- Warsh keeps "cut bias" credibility

**Equity reaction:** S&P +1.5% to +3%; long-duration AI outperforms

**Pre-committed actions:**
- **Long-duration AI complex (NVDA, GOOGL, MSFT, META):** ADD on any pullback during the day — multiple expansion is the rate-cut trade
- **Neoclouds (CRWV, NBIS):** ADD modest — most rate-sensitive in the high-conviction bucket
- **Memory (MU, SNDK):** Hold; don't add at cycle-peak P/E even on rate-cut tailwind
- **Construction (FIX, EME, PWR):** Hold — relative underperformer in this scenario but locked backlogs protect
- **Rare earth (MP), Defense (NOC, LMT):** Hold
- **Hedges:** Close 50-75% of hedges by EOD. Keep small VIX call position rolled monthly through July.
- **Cash/BIL:** Deploy 1-2% into long-duration AI on the day

### Scenario 2 — STABLE HOT (probability ~45%, the central case)

**Triggers:**
- Headline CPI YoY **3.7–3.9%** (status quo)
- Core CPI YoY **2.7–2.9%**
- Core MoM **0.2–0.4%**
- Energy roughly flat
- Shelter sticky

**Macro implications:**
- 30y Treasury stays 4.95–5.10%
- Hike probability holds 30-40%
- Fed stays "hold" through summer; Warsh has cover
- Market reaction: muted

**Equity reaction:** S&P ±1%; sector rotation more important than direction

**Pre-committed actions:**
- **No major changes.** This is the priced scenario.
- **Long-duration AI:** Hold structural positions
- **Neoclouds:** Hold; do not chase
- **Hedges:** Keep current sizing; this is the world they're built for
- **Watch the next month** for whether stickiness becomes problem (June PPI mid-June, June CPI in July)

### Scenario 3 — ACCELERATING (probability ~25-30%, refined down from 30%)

**Triggers (any of):**
- Headline CPI YoY **> 3.9%** (re-acceleration from 3.8%)
- Core CPI YoY **> 2.9%**
- Core MoM **> 0.4%**
- Energy index rises materially MoM
- Shelter or services accelerating

**Macro implications:**
- 30y Treasury → **5.3%+** (real multiple-compression territory)
- Hike probability → **60%+** by EOY 2026
- Equity multiples compress regardless of earnings
- Warsh credibility tested (does he hold or cut into hot data?)
- **Worst case:** the "Warsh capitulates → cuts into hot inflation" 20% tail flagged in [[fed-policy]]

**Equity reaction:** S&P -3% to -7%; growth multiples compress more than value

**Pre-committed actions:**
- **Long-duration AI (NVDA, GOOGL, MSFT):** TRIM 15-25% of structural positions. These are the hardest hit by multiple compression.
- **Neoclouds (CRWV, NBIS):** TRIM 30-50%. Most rate-sensitive in the high-conviction bucket.
- **Memory (MU, SNDK):** TRIM 25% — cycle-peak P/E + rate shock = double whammy
- **Construction (FIX, EME, PWR):** HOLD — defensive backlog protects
- **Defense (NOC, LMT):** HOLD or modest ADD — relative outperformer
- **Rare earth (MP):** Hold — supply-side inflation story is *strengthened*, not weakened
- **Energy (none in wiki — coverage gap):** if energy index rising, the wiki should be long here; flag for adjacent-coverage build
- **Hedges:** ADD VIX calls (July expiry) + extend QQQ put spreads to August
- **Cash/BIL:** raise to 7-10% via the trims above
- **Watch for:** Warsh credibility shock if Fed signals cut into this. If that happens, **second wave of decisions** required — see Scenario 3b

### Scenario 3b — STAGFLATION SHOCK (within Scenario 3)

If accelerating CPI is paired with Warsh signaling cuts anyway in the days after:
- **Long-duration AI:** TRIM another 15% (compounding shock)
- **Gold/GLD:** ADD 2-3% — Fed credibility hedge
- **Dollar (UUP) short:** consider
- **TIPS (real return ETFs like SCHP):** consider
- This is the wiki's worst-case macro scenario (20% probability in [[fed-policy]] Warsh wildcard table)

## What to watch in the print

1. **Headline YoY (most-watched single number)**
2. **Core YoY (Fed-watched)**
3. **Core MoM annualized (cleanest signal of momentum)** — 0.4% MoM annualized = ~5% trend
4. **Energy: gasoline, fuel oil indices** — OPEC+ supply impact
5. **Shelter: rents, OER** — historically sticky
6. **Goods vs services split** — tariff pass-through visibility
7. **Supercore (core services ex shelter)** — Powell's preferred narrow measure (Warsh may keep this framing)

## Three questions to answer NOW (before June 10)

1. **At what core MoM % do I move from "hold" to "trim 25% of NVDA"?** → **Core MoM > 0.4% = trim 25% of NVDA core** (next-day execution, not in-the-moment).
2. **If headline cools but core stays hot, which scenario applies?** → **Treat as Stable hot.** Core is the Fed signal; headline alone doesn't move the regime.
3. **If the print is borderline (e.g., headline 3.7%, core MoM 0.3%), what do I do?** → **Stable hot actions.** Don't try to game the boundaries. The framework says: if it doesn't clearly hit Cool or Accelerating triggers, treat as the central case.
