# Daily Substack — Template & Style Guide

> **Audience:** Retail investors who are AI/stock-curious but lack good entry points. They've heard about NVDA, MSFT, the AI boom — but don't know how to think about it. No finance background assumed.

> **Cadence:** Daily, 5 days/week. Different format each day to avoid fatigue and build a rhythm.

> **Voice:** Plain English. Educational + transparent + honest about misses. Define every acronym first use. Build trust over months.

## Weekly format rotation

| Day | Format | Length | Title pattern | Voice |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Monday** | Macro What-Happened-and-Why | 300-500 words | *"What I'm watching this week"* | Explainer; sets the week's table |
| **Tuesday** | Single-name deep dive | 400-600 words | *"[Ticker]: the story in plain English"* | Story-driven; one company, one thesis |
| **Wednesday** | Sector / theme rotation | 300-500 words | *"Why [theme] matters"* | "Big picture" framing |
| **Thursday** | Second single-name deep dive | 400-600 words | *"[Ticker]: bull case vs bear case"* | Pro/con; balanced framing |
| **Friday** | Week-in-review + what's next | 500-700 words | *"This week in markets"* | Forward-looking; preview the weekend |

## Sectors to rotate Wednesdays through

1. AI / semis (highest reader interest; weekly cycle OK)
2. Crypto (BTC, ETH, regulation)
3. Healthcare / pharma (LLY/NVO weight loss is a popular hook)
4. Biotech (catalyst-driven; good story material)
5. Energy / commodities (oil, gold, copper — macro hooks)
6. Defense / aerospace
7. Hedging / risk (do this when markets are stressed)
8. Macro / Fed policy

## Required elements every post

- **One chart** (or simple table). Visual anchor. Use existing wiki data.
- **One quote** from a credible source (CFO, analyst, news article). With attribution.
- **One "What this means for you"** sentence at the end. Concrete, actionable.
- **One disclosure footer:** *"This is research, not investment advice. Stances are research stances. Verify before any decision. Track record at [link to feedback-log]."*
- **One link out** — to the wiki for deeper context (and to build the wiki as authority).

## Voice rules

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Define every acronym ("ASML — the Dutch company that makes the only machines capable of producing leading-edge AI chips") | Use jargon without explanation |
| Use specific dollar amounts, dates, percentages | Vague "huge potential," "could be massive" |
| Acknowledge what you don't know | Hedge with weasel words like "potentially possibly might" |
| Quote analysts and CFOs by name | Anonymous "sources say" |
| Show misses in the feedback log proudly | Hide losses or only highlight wins |
| Write like a smart friend explaining it over coffee | Write like a finance textbook |

## Beginner-friendly framing techniques

- **Use analogies:** "Think of NVDA as the company that sells the pickaxes during a gold rush"
- **Anchor on familiar things:** "Anthropic ARR went from $9B to $30B in 3 months. For context, Netflix took 8 years to hit $30B in revenue"
- **Sequence cause→effect:** "Banks lend to data centers → data centers can't pay if AI demand softens → bank losses → market sells off. We're tracking step 1."
- **Show the work:** "I changed my mind on X because Y data came in. Here's what convinced me."
- **Compare scales:** "$700B in AI capex is one Manhattan project every 3-4 weeks"

## Title formulas that work

- *"The $X billion you didn't know about [ticker]"* (data-led)
- *"Why [event today] matters for [reader's portfolio]"* (relevance)
- *"What I got wrong about [name] last [time period]"* (transparency builds trust)
- *"[Ticker] in 5 charts"* (visual hook)
- *"The case for / against [ticker / theme]"* (balanced framing)
- *"Three things to watch this week"* (Monday format)

## Title formulas to avoid

- *"You won't believe..."* (clickbait)
- *"Stock to buy now"* (recommendation language)
- *"Massive upside potential"* (vague hype)
- *"X is the next Nvidia"* (almost always wrong)

## Building trust over time

The reader should feel by month 3:

1. **You're honest.** Misses are documented. Track record is visible.
2. **You're useful.** They learned something they couldn't get from CNBC.
3. **You're a system, not a punter.** Stances change for reasons, not vibes.
4. **You're transparent on bias.** Disclose if you own a name. Disclose conflicts.

## Daily writing workflow

| Time | Task |
|---|---|
| Morning (15 min) | Scan overnight news + wiki updates; pick today's angle |
| Mid-day (45 min) | Draft post in `wiki/analyses/daily-YYYY-MM-DD-slug.md` |
| Pre-publish (15 min) | Strip wiki-links; add SEO title + subtitle; pick chart |
| Publish (5 min) | Schedule Substack post for 9:00 AM ET (highest open rate for finance) |
| Post-publish (10 min) | Monitor first-hour engagement; respond to comments |

**Total: ~90 minutes per post.** 5 posts/week = 7.5 hours. Fits within your 8 hours/day budget with room for other work.

```markdown

The one thing that mattered today / this week, framed as a hook.

## The story (300-500 words depending on day)

Body content. Use sub-headers (##) every ~200 words to break up scanning.

## Chart

[Embed image or table here]

## The numbers (if applicable)

| Variable | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| ... | ... | ... |

## What this means for you

One sentence. Concrete and actionable.

## Sources

- [Title](URL) — *one-line annotation*

---

**Disclosure:** This is research, not investment advice. Stances are research stances; verify before any decision. Track record at [Feedback Log](https://github.com/rgovindji/wiki_research/blob/main/wiki/feedback-log.md).

*Subscribe for daily AI / markets research.*
```

## First-week kickoff plan

If launching the Substack the week of May 18, 2026:

| Day | Topic | Why this works |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon May 18** | "The biggest day for AI stocks this year (it's not the one you think)" — NVDA earnings + 20-year Treasury auction same day | Sets up the week's macro and primes readers for Wednesday's NVDA event |
| **Tue May 19** | "AVGO: the company that's bigger than NVDA in a way you might not know" — Broadcom deep dive | Counterintuitive hook; introduces second-order AI plays |
| **Wed May 20** | "What to watch in NVDA earnings tonight" — Plain-English preview | Riding the day's catalyst |
| **Thu May 21** | "I was wrong about Ouster. Here's what I missed." — Feedback log entry as a post | Trust-building through transparency |
| **Fri May 22** | "This week in markets: NVDA, the bond market, and what comes next" | Recap + forward look |

This puts the **transparency-on-misses** post early to build trust before readers commit. By Friday they've seen 5 different formats and 5 different topics.
