# Redwire Corporation (RDW)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** 18-36 month Golden Dome buildout cycle + multi-year Edge Autonomy integration

## One-line thesis

Multi-domain space-and-defense platform built by **AE Industrial Partners** roll-up (2020 → SPAC 2021) and recently transformed via the **$925M Edge Autonomy acquisition (closed June 2025)** into a scaled, profitable space + tactical-drone supplier. Two strong niches: **VLEO + refuelable GEO spacecraft (SabreSat / Mako)** as a prime lead in the emerging Golden Dome architecture, and **combat-proven Penguin + Stalker tactical UAS** in the Edge Autonomy subsidiary. Real backlog ($498M record, book-to-bill 1.92), accelerating revenue (+58% YoY Q1 2026), and a 1,200-bp gross-margin expansion — but **146% share-count dilution in the last 12 months** from the Edge Autonomy stock consideration plus continuing AE Industrial-affiliate insider sales is the structural overhang on the stock.

> **What this means:** Redwire is the "scaled multi-domain mid-cap" of the public space-defense complex — bigger than the pure space pure-plays ([[RKLB]] / [[ASTS]]) on revenue, smaller than the defense primes ([[LMT]] / [[NOC]]), and now uniquely positioned across orbit-to-ground autonomy. The bull case rests on Golden Dome execution + Andromeda IDIQ ceiling expansion + Edge Autonomy revenue ramp. The bear case rests on the AE Industrial private-equity sponsor continuing to monetize the position via selling pressure on a stock that already trades above sell-side average PT.

## Business structure (post Edge Autonomy)

### Space segment ($52.7M Q1 2026; ~54% of revenue)

The original Redwire — a roll-up of small-but-storied space-component businesses (Adcole Space, Deep Space Systems, Made In Space, Roccor, Oakman Aerospace, Techshot, Deployable Space Systems, and others) that AE Industrial assembled starting 2020. Today operates as:

**Spacecraft platforms (the higher-margin growth vector):**
- **Mako** — high-agility GEO/MEO platform, rad-hard, RPO + docking provisions. **2 Mako spacecraft contracted for US Space Force / AFRL Tetra-5 program** validating on-orbit refueling. CEO: *"10 years in a limited competition pool to monetize this multibillion-dollar contract."* Third Mako on follow-on Space Force contract.
- **SabreSat** — VLEO platform; sensor performance + revisit rate + low latency for defense/intel/comms. **DARPA Otter program** ($44M Phase 2 awarded Q4 2025) is the lead VLEO development contract. SabreSat is Redwire's prime-lead asset in the [[space-economy|Golden Dome]] architecture.
- **Hammerhead** — LEO platform. ESA Σyndeo-3 satellite built on this. Workhorse for European satellite missions.
- **Thresher** + **Phantom** — additional platforms in the family.

**Heritage component product lines** (lower-margin, broadly-sold):
- Power generation (solar arrays)
- Antennas + deployables
- Star trackers + camera systems
- Docking systems (International Berthing and Docking Mechanism for ISS-compliant docking)
- Sun sensors, gyros, GN&C systems

### Defense Tech segment ($44.3M Q1 2026; ~46% of revenue) — **Edge Autonomy**

Acquired June 2025 for **$925M** ($150M cash + **$775M Redwire common stock** at $15.07 30-day VWAP). Edge Autonomy had ~600 employees across 6 locations in US, Canada, Europe at acquisition. Founded in San Luis Obispo, CA.

**Products:**
- **Penguin** family (Mk3 latest) — Group 2 tactical UAS; long-endurance ISR; **combat-proven** (supplied to Ukraine in US military aid packages). **NATO multi-year high-eight-figure contract** for Penguin Mk3 — anchor commercial validation.
- **Stalker** — US Army Group 2 tactical UAS; **$15M follow-on Q1 2026** is third order in 8 months; cumulative recent ~$24.8M.
- **VXE30** + other smaller UAS platforms.

**Strategic logic of the merger** (per Jan 2025 announcement): create a "multi-domain autonomous platform" combining Edge Autonomy's tactical drones (ground-based, atmospheric) with Redwire's "orbital drones" (VLEO satellites = SabreSat) — seamless network from ground to space. **Q1 2026 Defense Tech bookings: $72M.**

## Bull case

- **Golden Dome architecture positions RDW as a prime in TWO of three orbit tiers.** CEO: *"Prime lead in VLEO, prime lead in highly maneuverable, refuelable GEO, merchant supplier in LEO."* If [[space-economy|Golden Dome]] scales as the Aerospace Corp FY26 budget brief implies, RDW gets prime contracts (vs subcontractor revenue) for the VLEO + maneuverable-GEO buildout.
- **Andromeda IDIQ ceiling raising to $6B+** is the largest single-program upside vector. 1-of-14 vendor pool is competitive but RDW already has Mako heritage with USSF.
- **Edge Autonomy is the real surprise.** Combat-proven Penguin in Ukraine, repeat US Army Stalker buys (3 in 8 months), NATO multi-year contract. Tactical UAS is one of the fastest-growing defense categories globally.
- **Gross-margin expansion from 14.7% → 26.6% in one year** is striking. If the trajectory continues (Edge Autonomy is higher-margin than legacy space components), Adj EBITDA scales non-linearly with revenue.
- **FY26 revenue guide ($450-500M, +41.6%) is REAFFIRMED post-Q1** — credibility of management forecast intact.
- **Debt refinanced** to May 2029 at SOFR+375 (vs SOFR+700 before) materially reduces interest burden. $175M liquidity gives runway.
- **Multi-domain autonomy is an accelerating thematic** — Anduril (private), AVAV, and now RDW competing for the "ground + air + orbital autonomous platform" budget allocation. Defense buyer preference is consolidating around multi-domain primes vs single-platform vendors.
- **Mako on-orbit refueling demo (Tetra-5) is a category-defining program** — if successful, RDW becomes the de-facto on-orbit-servicing platform vendor.

## Bear case / risks

- **146% share count growth YoY** from Edge Autonomy stock consideration is the single biggest structural overhang. The deal economics ($925M for ~$200M-ish Edge Autonomy revenue base) require multi-year synergy + UAS market expansion to justify the dilution.
- **AE Industrial Partners continuing to sell down.** Form 4 filings April 2026 show steady selling pressure. The PE sponsor's exit cadence is itself a price-discovery overhang. Watch the next 13F + insider transaction cadence.
- **Net losses still material at $76.5M Q1**, even discounting the $42.5M one-time SBC. Until the EBITDA-positive guide actually shows up in GAAP results, the equity story is still "promised profitability, not earned profitability."
- **Stock at $17.49 trades ~22% above sell-side average PT ($14.33).** Recent +13.94% one-day move on Q1 print is momentum-driven; the fundamentals priced in are aggressive.
- **Andromeda IDIQ has 14 competing vendors.** Headline number ($1.8B ceiling, potentially $6B) is misleading until RDW captures meaningful task orders. Most IDIQ ceilings end up substantially under-utilized; RDW could end up with $50-100M not $400M+.
- **Tactical UAS is increasingly crowded.** [[AVAV]] Switchblade, Anduril Bolt, Skydio (private), AeroVironment Puma 3 AE, plus dozens of overseas competitors. Edge Autonomy's Penguin/Stalker face commoditization pressure as the category scales.
- **Reliance on US defense procurement cycle.** Continuing-resolution risk, budget shifts away from space programs, change in administration priorities — all hit RDW disproportionately because of the high government-revenue concentration.
- **Mako/SabreSat/Otter execution risk** — VLEO and on-orbit refueling are technically hard. A high-profile demo failure on Tetra-5 or Otter would damage the prime-lead Golden Dome narrative.
- **Edge Autonomy is AE Industrial Partners → AE Industrial Partners.** The seller of Edge Autonomy was AE Industrial-affiliated entities, and the buyer is AE Industrial's controlled portfolio company. **This is a related-party transaction.** Disclosed and presumably independently evaluated, but the optics + governance question is worth flagging.

> **What this means (bear synthesis):** RDW's operational momentum is real (revenue, margin, backlog all moving the right direction). The structural concerns are governance + dilution + multiple-already-stretched. The "good company, expensive stock with sponsor selling" pattern is a textbook setup that can keep working for 6-12 months and then de-rate sharply. Sizing should reflect the asymmetry: small-to-medium position with explicit trim rules on either valuation extension or accelerated AE Industrial selling.

## Investment framing

- **Compared to [[RKLB]]:** RDW has lower beta to launch outcomes (no Neutron-debut binary), more defense-revenue concentration, faster organic + inorganic growth on the topline. RKLB has cleaner equity story (no PE sponsor overhang).
- **Compared to [[ASTS]]:** completely different — ASTS is single-asset direct-to-cell constellation, RDW is diversified across orbital, ground, and air. Hold one or both depending on whether you want concentrated bet or diversified space-defense exposure.
- **Compared to [[KTOS]]:** more pure-play space (RDW) vs more pure-play defense-tech with rockets/satcom (KTOS). Similar mid-cap revenue base, similar Golden Dome adjacency.
- **Compared to [[AVAV]]:** Edge Autonomy directly competes with AVAV in tactical UAS. AVAV is more profitable and larger but RDW combines the UAS franchise with the much bigger space-prime narrative.
