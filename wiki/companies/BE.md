# Bloom Energy (BE)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** **high** (raised 2026-05-11 on Oracle Project Jupiter $2.45 GW deal) · **Time horizon:** medium-term

## One-line thesis
Solid-oxide fuel cell maker for data center primary power. **Dylan Patel / SemiAnalysis: "we've been very positive on them for a year and a half" because their payback period for production expansion is fast** — a structurally faster supply response than gas-turbine OEMs ([[GEV]] et al.) when AI data center power demand surges. **Conviction upgraded May 2026** on the Oracle Project Jupiter deal: BE is the **sole primary-power supplier** for [[ORCL]]'s New Mexico data center campus at **2.45 GW**, with up to **2.8 GW total committed across multiple Oracle deployments**.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Fuel cells** as primary power source for data centers (alternative to combined-cycle GT)
- **Behind-the-meter** AI factory deployments
- Operates outside the 3-vendor oligopoly bottleneck — **incremental supply at speed**
- Cost a little above combined-cycle but far better on capacity ramp

## Bull case
- **SemiAnalysis explicitly bullish** for 18+ months on production-ramp speed
- **Fast capacity expansion payback** — fewer multi-year-blade-lead-time issues than turbines
- AI capex cycle pulls fuel cells in alongside combined-cycle as supply response
- Cost premium absorbable: combined-cycle ~$1,500/kW vs. alternatives up to $3,500/kW translates to **+10c/hr Hopper TCO** — easy to pay
- Fits the 800V DC architecture push (NVIDIA Beam Rubin DSX ecosystem)
- **🔥 2026-05-17 PM update — Q1 2026 +130% YoY rev $751M; $5B Brookfield partnership; Oracle 2.8 GW supply** (per [[2026-05-17-quantum-energy-smr-accelerator-multi-agent]] Agent X). **2 GW annual capacity target**; **80% of first nine months 2026 fuel cell deal volume locked to Oracle alone**. SOFC tech delivers 60% electrical efficiency + 90% combined heat/power. Brookfield partnership = utility-scale deployment vehicle. **Which means**: BE has moved from "AI data center fuel-cell hopeful" to "operationally validated at hyperscale" — Oracle Project Jupiter 2.8 GW + Brookfield $5B = multi-year revenue visibility.

## Bear case / risks
- **Higher cost** than combined-cycle baseline — adoption depends on supply-chain urgency
- **Per Dylan: power is NOT the binding AI constraint** by EOD — many alternatives mean BE doesn't have monopoly pricing
- **Stock has been volatile** historically with multi-year periods of disappointment vs. promise
- **Customer concentration** — large hyperscaler / data center deals dominate revenue
- **Hydrogen / natural gas economics** subject to commodity / regulatory shifts
