# Micron Technology (MU)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term with cyclical awareness

## One-line thesis
Pure-play memory leveraged to the AI HBM supercycle — preferred [[NVDA]] HBM supplier, sold-out capacity through 2026, and the structural shift from DRAM/NAND to HBM is reshaping memory economics in Micron's favor.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **HBM3E (12-high stacks)** — Micron supplies with **30% lower power** vs. competitors → "preferred supplier" status with NVIDIA
- **HBM4 ramp** — Q2 2026 production start, scaling to **15,000 wafers/month in 2026**
- **NVIDIA's request to memory makers**: deliver 16-Hi HBM4 by Q4 2026

## Bull case
- **Sold-out capacity through 2026** across HBM industry (Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron)
- **HBM consumes ~3-4x wafer capacity per GB vs. DDR5** — AI demand is structurally pulling supply away from commodity DRAM, supporting all memory pricing
- **30% of Big 4 hyperscaler 2026 capex is going to memory** (per [[2026-05-09-dwarkesh-dylan-semianalysis]]) — ~$180B of demand pull
- **The demand-destruction mechanism (NEW).** Smartphone units forecast: **1.4B → 1.1B → ~800M (2026) → 500-600M (2027)**. Xiaomi/Oppo cutting low/mid volumes by half. Each iPhone has +$150 BOM impact from memory; that hit ripples through the entire consumer device universe and *releases* DRAM/NAND wafers to AI. Memory pricing has tripled and could double or triple again.
- **Stock up ~120% YTD 2026** on HBM supercycle thesis
- **Capacity expansion required** — but HBM ramps are slow; new fabs take 2 years to build, so the 2027-2028 supply response is set
- **Diversified beyond NVDA** — supplies all major AI accelerator makers + hyperscaler custom silicon
- **Bandwidth is what matters.** HBM4 stack: 2.5 TB/s vs. DDR5: 64-128 GB/s in same shoreline — order of magnitude advantage that no commodity DRAM substitute can deliver

## Bear case / risks

> **The single most important warning on MU:** the "undervalued on forward P/E" framing pushed by retail / Twitter / many sell-side notes is the textbook **cycle-peak P/E paradox** — explained in detail below. The wiki stays bull / high conviction on MU, but for "multi-year supercycle has 2-3 more years to run" reasons, **NOT** "undervalued long-term compounder" reasons. Sizing and exit discipline matter more here than for any other top pick.

> **What this means:** Micron is a great trade for the next 2-3 years because AI memory demand has another leg up. But it is **not** a "buy and hold forever" stock. Memory has crashed 40-70% in every prior cycle. The reason it looks cheap right now is the same reason it always looks cheap right before it crashes. Plan your exit.

## The right framing for MU

- **What it is:** a leveraged multi-year trade on the AI memory crunch through ~2028
- **What it isn't:** a "compounder" or "undervalued long-term hold." Memory is cyclical. Always.
- **Sizing:** smaller than NVDA / TSM / AMAT / ASML. The cyclicality means the drawdown when the cycle turns will be 40%+ even on the bull case.
- **Exit discipline (the most important variable):** When SemiAnalysis flags memory ASPs peaking AND new fab capacity coming online (currently signaled for 2028), that's the trim signal. **Don't ride it through the next cycle.**

## DRAM doubling/tripling thesis (NEW 2026-05-17 per [[2026-05-16-dylan-patel-invest-like-best]])

Dylan Patel (SemiAnalysis) on Invest Like the Best (recorded ~May 16, 2026 post-Opus-4.7 launch) made the most emphatic DRAM call the wiki has captured:

> *"DRAM will double or triple from here still because that's how much capacity is required... The only way to steal capacity from somewhere else in a capitalist economy is demand destruction via higher pricing. We're not rationing stuff here."*

Key supporting framework:
- Memory capacity growth limit: ~20-30%/yr DRAM, less for NAND (structural constraint)
- True incremental new capacity arrival: **"early-late 2027 at best, more realistically 2028"**
- Counter to consensus: *"People are like 'oh the memory storage is overplayed. Everyone gets it.' And it's like no no no. You don't get it."*

**Which means** for [[MU]]: the existing wiki framing (memory tightness through 2028) is *under-pricing the magnitude*. Dylan's call is for DRAM contract prices to 2-3x from current already-elevated levels, which would propagate to:
- MU revenue per bit doubling/tripling (offset by share dynamics with SK Hynix/Samsung)
- Gross margin expansion well beyond current 66% (HBM-driven) framing
- The 2028 catch-up window is the structural ceiling — but until then, pricing power compounds

**Caveat preserved from existing bear case**: this thesis assumes Samsung HBM4 doesn't disrupt MU's "preferred supplier" pricing premium, and that 70% commodity DRAM revenue holds up. The Dylan call sharpens *upside* but doesn't address the cycle-peak-P/E paradox that remains the primary sizing constraint.
