# Crusoe (Private — "Crusoe Energy Systems" / "Crusoe")

**Stance:** neutral (constructive on the business, cautious on the *seat* available to an individual) · **Conviction:** low · **Time horizon:** both (multi-year private hold until IPO/M&A)

## One-line thesis
Crusoe is a vertically integrated "AI factory" developer — it sources power, builds and operates gigawatt-scale AI data centers, and (secondarily) sells GPU compute via Crusoe Cloud — riding the AI-capex buildout as a fast-growing, deeply capital-intensive, debt-heavy private comp to [[CoreWeave|CoreWeave (CRWV)]]; the bull case is "the pick-and-shovel landlord of [[ai-capex-cycle|the AI capex cycle]]," the bear case is customer concentration, commoditizing GPU economics, and a brutal financing treadmill — and for an *individual*, the dominant question is whether any cap-table seat even exists below the institutional round.

## What it does (and why it could be huge)
Crusoe began (~2018) as a flared-gas-to-bitcoin miner — capturing stranded natural gas at oil wellheads to run mobile generators and crypto rigs ("Digital Flare Mitigation"). It has since pivoted hard to AI: it now designs, builds, and operates **gigawatt-scale AI data center campuses**, controlling the full stack from power generation (on-site gas turbines, contracted GE Vernova and PROENERGY fleets) through data-center construction to a GPU cloud (Crusoe Cloud). The flagship is the **Abilene, TX "Stargate" campus** built for the OpenAI/Oracle/SoftBank [[stargate|Stargate]] program (~1.2 GW, Phase 1 live on Oracle Cloud Infrastructure, up to ~400k Nvidia GB200). Crusoe is among the largest independent AI-infra developers, frequently framed as the #2 "neocloud"/AI-factory comp behind [[CoreWeave]]. If AI compute demand keeps compounding and power is the binding constraint, a vertically integrated developer that *controls power* is structurally advantaged. That is the bull dream.

## Bull case (load-bearing)
1. **Power is the binding constraint on AI, and Crusoe controls power.** Vertical integration (generation → DC → cloud) is the genuinely differentiated asset; energy origins (flared gas, gas turbines, plus Form Energy / Redwood Materials storage deals) give a plausible cost/speed edge over neoclouds that only rent space.
2. **Capacity is real and contracted, not vapor.** ~4.9 GW contracted with **hyperscaler-grade counterparties** (Microsoft, Meta, Oracle/OpenAI). Abilene Phase 1 is **live** — execution, not just slides.
3. **Top-tier, strategic cap table.** Nvidia + Supermicro (supply chain), Founders Fund/Valor/Mubadala/T. Rowe/Tiger/Altimeter/Fidelity/Blue Owl (capital + IPO sponsorship). Nvidia's $150M deposit shows a vendor willing to backstop Crusoe's lease-up.
4. **Steep, fast growth + clear liquidity path.** Revenue scaling fast; a 2026 IPO would be a relatively *near* exit by private-market standards — unusual and valuable for a private holding.
5. **Comp anchor exists.** [[CoreWeave]]'s public listing gives a live read on how the market values an AI-infra landlord, aiding eventual price discovery.

## Bear case / risks (load-bearing)
1. **Customer concentration + counterparty fragility.** A handful of mega-tenants drive the book. The Abilene episode shows how fast a single anchor (OpenAI) can pull back, and how dependent Crusoe is on others (Nvidia/Meta) to backfill. One renegotiated or cancelled mega-lease moves everything.
2. **Crushing capital intensity + layered debt.** Multi-GW buildouts financed with a **~$7B+ (cited up to ~$11.6B) construction-loan stack**. High rates, construction/permitting/turbine-delivery risk, and interest expense make this a **financing-treadmill business**: it must keep raising equity *and* debt to grow. Equity holders sit behind a large preference and debt stack.
3. **GPU/compute commoditization.** Per-hour GPU rental reportedly fell from ~$8 → ~$2; if the cloud layer commoditizes, Crusoe is left as a leveraged real-estate/power developer at REIT-like (not software) multiples — hard to justify a $30–40B mark.
4. **Operational reliability is now a known weakness.** The multi-day Abilene cooling outage is a concrete, reputation-damaging execution failure at the flagship site — exactly the thing hyperscaler tenants underwrite against.
5. **Valuation velocity outruns fundamentals.** ~14x mark expansion in ~15 months on a business with **undisclosed margins, burn, and concentration**. The $30–40B figure is a *target*, not a clearing price; an individual buying secondary at an implied $30B+ is paying a marketing mark for a leveraged, cyclical, single-theme bet.

## The pivotal unknown(s)
1. **What seat is actually on offer to an individual — and does one even exist?** Almost certainly **NOT primary preferred** (that round is institutional: Valor, Mubadala, Blue Owl, T. Rowe, etc.). Realistic individual access is **secondary common/early-preferred via Forge/Hiive/EquityZen, or an SPV/feeder** — the worst seats: behind the full preference stack, often layered fees/carry, no info or pro-rata rights, and frequently blocked by company ROFR/transfer restrictions. **If the only access is an SPV at a rumored $30–40B mark, that is itself close to a pass-level finding.**
2. **Real 2025 revenue and gross margin.** Sources diverge $500M–$998M and margin is undisclosed. Without this you cannot tell if the $30–40B target is ~30x or ~80x revenue, or whether the cloud business earns software-like or thin developer margins.
3. **Customer concentration + lease durability.** How much of contracted GW/revenue is OpenAI vs Microsoft vs Meta, and are the leases take-or-pay/NNN with creditworthy guarantors? The Abilene pullback makes this the question that most moves the downside.
4. **The debt covenants and equity cushion.** Where does common sit after the preference + ~$7B+ debt stack in a sideways/down exit?

## Cap-table seat / how to invest
- **Primary round:** Institutional only; an individual is very unlikely to get primary preferred. (Round leads: Valor, Mubadala; Blue Owl closing tranche.)
- **Secondary marketplaces:** Crusoe is *listed* on **Forge Global, Hiive, EquityZen, Nasdaq Private Market** for **accredited investors**. **But:** listings are typically **bid/ask indications, not confirmed trades**; no verified clearing price was found. Expect **company ROFR / transfer restrictions** common to hot pre-IPO names, plus marketplace fees. What you'd buy is most likely **common or early preferred**, behind the entire institutional preference stack.
- **SPV / feeder:** The most likely retail-accessible structure for a sub-institutional check — and the **worst seat**: extra management fee + carry (sometimes stacked across multiple SPV layers), no direct cap-table rights, no info rights, and you're often a unit-holder several layers removed from the shares. **Confirm the all-in fee load and how many SPV layers before considering.**
- **Public route (cleanest):** If the **2026 IPO** materializes [rumored], an individual can simply buy the listed stock at known terms with full disclosure — almost always a better seat than a late secondary/SPV at a marketing mark. **Waiting for the IPO is a legitimate strategy here.**

## Questions to ask the seller / broker
1. **Security type:** Common or preferred? Which series? Exact **liquidation preference** (1x non-participating? participating? multiple?) ahead of these shares.
2. **Direct vs SPV:** Am I buying shares **on the cap table** or **a unit in an SPV/feeder**? If SPV: how many layers, and the **full fee + carry** load?
3. **Price & implied valuation:** Exact $/share and implied post-money. How does it reconcile with the **last primary ($10B, Oct 2025)** vs the **rumored $30–40B**? Is this an **executed trade** or an **indication**?
4. **Transfer mechanics:** Does Crusoe's **ROFR / board approval** apply? Will the transfer actually clear, and in what timeframe?
5. **Information rights:** Will I receive financials, cap table, or 409A? (Likely **no** for secondary/SPV common.)
6. **Last 409A valuation** and date.
7. **Last audited financials:** 2025 **revenue, gross margin, EBITDA, net debt, cash, burn**. (If unavailable, you are buying blind.)
8. **Customer concentration:** % of contracted GW / revenue from the top 1–3 tenants; lease structure (take-or-pay? NNN? term? guarantor credit?).
9. **Insider behavior:** Is the seller a **founder/early employee/early VC** taking liquidity? Why now? (Insider selling into a hot mark is a signal.)
10. **Cap-table/preference stack & total debt** ahead of my shares; pro-rata/anti-dilution rights (likely none).

## Verdict
**Lean: neutral, tilting cautious for an individual — conviction LOW (data-limited).** The *business* is one of the more credible private AI-infra stories: real contracted gigawatts, hyperscaler counterparties, a strategic cap table (Nvidia/Supermicro/Founders Fund/T. Rowe), a live flagship, and the June 2026 Meta deal that backfilled the Abilene scare. **But the investable proposition for an individual is weak**: the attractive seat (primary preferred) is institutional-only; the accessible seats (secondary common / SPV) are subordinated, fee-laden, and likely priced off a **rumored $30–40B marketing mark** against a business whose **margins, burn, and customer concentration are undisclosed**, financed on a heavy debt stack in a commoditizing-compute, capital-intensive, single-theme sector.

**Scenario framing:** (a) *Bull* — IPO 2026 at/above the rumored mark, multi-GW lease-up holds → strong outcome, but most of that is best captured by **buying the IPO**, not a late secondary/SPV. (b) *Base* — IPO slips or prices below the $30–40B target as the market discounts leverage/concentration; secondary buyers at the top mark are flat-to-down. (c) *Bear* — a major tenant renegotiates/cancels, a financing window slams shut, or compute margins compress → leveraged equity is impaired and common sits behind a deep stack.

**What would change the view (falsifiers):** verified 2025 financials showing healthy gross margin + diversified contracted revenue; a **direct primary or clean direct-secondary preferred** seat (not an SPV) at a sensible entry; or a confirmed IPO at known terms (which would route the decision to the public page). **What would kill it:** SPV-only access at a $30B+ implied mark, or evidence of >50% revenue/GW concentration in a single wobbling tenant.
