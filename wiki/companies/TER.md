# Teradyne (TER)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
**Every advanced AI chip needs to be tested before it ships** — and Teradyne is the dominant Western semiconductor test equipment vendor. Q1 2026 was a step-change: **revenue +87% YoY to $1.282B (record)**, **Semi Test segment >$1B for the first time**, and **70% of revenue is now AI-related**. Largely overlooked because TER sits outside the WFE bucket (AMAT/LRCX/KLAC) — but the same fab buildout that drives WFE also drives test equipment. **+66% YTD 2026.**

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Semiconductor test equipment** — system-on-chip (SoC) testers used at chip-final-test by [[TSM]] / [[NVDA]] / [[AMD]] / [[AVGO]] / hyperscalers' custom silicon foundry partners
- Adjacent to [[KLAC]] (process control) and [[KEYS]] (design verification), but distinct from both
- **Test intensity per chip is rising** as nodes shrink, packaging stacks grow, and HBM integration adds complexity
- **Robotics segment** (Universal Robots, Mobile Industrial Robots) — collaborative robots; lower-growth but separate from semi cycle

## Bull case
- **Step-change in scale** — Semi Test segment > $1B for the first time, doubling YoY
- **AI mix shift** — compute (AI) now ~75% of SoC product revenue (vs. mobile-dominant prior)
- **Q1 2026 revenue +87% YoY** — explosive growth driven by AI chip test complexity
- **Test intensity per chip is structurally rising** — more cores, more memory channels, advanced packaging means more test time per die = more equipment per fab
- **Aligned with [[bottleneck-roadmap]] 2027 fab buildout** — every new fab line needs test equipment
- **70% AI revenue exposure** is one of the highest among public equipment names
- **Margin leverage** — flow-through on incremental revenue is high
- **Robotics call option** — Universal Robots / MiR provide non-semi optionality

## Bear case / risks
- **Cyclical at the equipment level** — test orders boom and bust with semi cycle
- **GuruFocus flags overvalued** by their GF Value model (167% above fair value)
- **Q2 guide ($1.15-1.25B) is BELOW Q1 print of $1.282B** — sequential deceleration after the spike
- **Customer concentration** in advanced foundry customers
- **Robotics segment growth** has been disappointing relative to expectations
- **China exposure** — semi test trade subject to export controls
- **Competition from Advantest (Japan)** in semi test; Advantest is arguably the larger player by share
- **AI-specific test demand could be lumpy** as new chip generations design-cycle through

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Semi Test revenue trajectory** — does the $1B threshold hold?
2. **AI / compute mix** — does the 70-75% share keep expanding?
3. **Q2 print vs guide** — does sequential deceleration prove cyclical or transient?
4. **Operating margin** — incremental margin on AI-test revenue
5. **Advantest share dynamics** — Western vs. Japanese share
6. **Robotics segment** — turnaround or continued drag?
7. **China revenue mix** under export-control regime
