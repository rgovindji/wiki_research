# Air Products & Chemicals (APD)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium (slow-moving industrial; multi-decade contracted revenue) · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
The most overlooked specialty-gases play in AI infrastructure. **Every advanced semiconductor fab needs industrial gases** (nitrogen, oxygen, argon, hydrogen + specialty gases like NF3 for chamber clean, neon for DUV lasers, helium for cryogenics). Air Products is one of two large Western specialty-gas suppliers (Linde the other), with **multi-decade build-own-operate gas-supply contracts** bundled into new fab construction. As the [[bottleneck-roadmap]] 2027 fab build-out kicks in, APD's contracted revenue base grows in lockstep — but the stock trades like a boring industrial, not an AI play.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Bulk gases** at fab sites — nitrogen, oxygen, argon, hydrogen (cryogenic & gaseous)
- **NF3 (nitrogen trifluoride)** — the world's leading chamber-cleaning specialty gas; 4 transfill facilities globally (Japan, Taiwan, Korea, Belgium)
- **Specialty gases** — silanes, dopants, etch chemistries
- **Build-Own-Operate (BOO) model** — APD constructs and operates onsite production facilities; customer signs 15-20 year off-take contracts
- Indirect — sells to fabs ([[TSM]], Samsung, Intel, [[MU]], SK Hynix), not [[NVDA]] — but every advanced wafer consumes APD gas

## Bull case
- **Multi-decade contracted revenue** — BOO model locks in revenue tied to specific fab off-take
- **The Pyeongtaek expansion is genuinely huge** — Samsung's next-gen fab, APD's largest single electronics investment
- **NF3 is mission-critical and consumable** — every wafer pass needs chamber clean; sold at high margin per kg
- **Helium & neon shortages** create pricing power for the few large suppliers
- **Aligned with [[bottleneck-roadmap]] 2027 fab buildout** — gas plant capacity comes on with new fabs 2028-2030
- **Slow but durable** — dividend grower with industrial-grade balance sheet
- **Underexposed in AI narratives** — could re-rate if investors discover the AI-fab link

## Bear case / risks
- **Slow-moving industrial growth profile** — not the explosive growth of TER / ENTG / MU
- **Customer concentration in fabs** — TSMC + Samsung + Intel + memory makers
- **Capital-intensive BOO model** — large up-front capex; payback over 15-20 years; rate-sensitive
- **Net Zero / hydrogen project execution** — APD has been spending heavily on green-hydrogen mega-projects with execution challenges; non-semi business can drag
- **Long-duration revenue** = slow to inflect upward
- **Activist investor focus** historically on capital discipline
- **Competition from Linde, Air Liquide** in specialty gases globally
- **Helium / neon market volatility** can affect margins

## Key questions / what to watch
