# Ouster (OUST)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** **low** (downgraded from medium 2026-05-13 on Hesai competitive gap) · **Time horizon:** long-term / 12-24mo inflection

## One-line thesis

Highest-quality Western lidar pure-play with a credible 2026 profitability inflection, expanding into stereo cameras for robotic manipulation via the Stereolabs acquisition — earnings story validated by Q1 2026 +49% YoY, but **OUST is structurally outscaled by Chinese competitor Hesai** (already profitable, 4M unit capacity 2026 vs OUST ~50-70K) and the most direct US peer (Luminar) just filed Chapter 11. Real business, not pure speculation — but conviction is low because the path to profitable scale requires either a sustained Western tariff/sanctions regime against Chinese lidar OR a humanoid OEM design win that hasn't materialized.

> **What this means:** Ouster is *not* a story-stock — they have $200M of real revenue, real customers (Google, Volvo, BMW), and 12,600 sensors shipped last quarter. But they're losing badly to a Chinese competitor (Hesai) that is already profitable and roughly 25x larger. The most similar US competitor (Luminar) just went bankrupt. The bull case essentially requires either US-China policy decisively favoring Western lidar makers, or a clear humanoid robot design win. Both are plausible but neither is in place yet.

## Bull case

- **Q1 2026 record results.** Revenue $49M (+49% YoY) vs. $46.15M consensus. Record 8,300 lidar units of 12,600 total sensors shipped. Gross margin improved 200bps to 43%. Adjusted EBITDA loss narrowed by $1M to -$7M.
- **Stereolabs acquisition** brings ZED stereo cameras — directly applicable to robotic manipulation, imitation learning, and high-throughput data collection. Repositions OUST from pure lidar to "sensing and perception for Physical AI."
- **Rev8 OS family launch.** Native color lidar, double the range and resolution of prior gen, designed for functional safety, reliability, and 10-year production lifespan. Qualified to run on NVIDIA DRIVE Hyperion.
- **BlueCity smart-infrastructure deployments.** Atlanta rollout demonstrates lidar + AI software winning real-world multi-intersection contracts. Recurring revenue smoothing.
- **Path to operating cash flow positive H2 2026** per management guidance. Analyst narrative model projects $414.8M revenue and $8.9M earnings by 2029.
- **Customer roster** includes Google and Volvo Autonomous Solutions.
- **Largest lidar patent portfolio** post-Velodyne merger.

## Bear case / risks

- **Hesai is winning the category (NEW — biggest structural risk).** Chinese pure-play Hesai became the **world's first profitable lidar company in 2024** and shipped **1M+ units in 2025**. They're doubling production capacity to **4M units in 2026** vs OUST's ~50-70K. Operating margin **+9.73% for Hesai vs -61% for OUST**. Cash position $394M (Hesai) vs $175M (OUST, before $100M ATM dilution). **Which means:** OUST is competing against a Chinese champion that is roughly 25x larger by volume and already profitable. The "OUST as Western lidar leader" thesis only works if Western policy decisively bars Hesai from Western markets — possible (see [[us-china-relations]]) but not guaranteed.
- **Luminar (LAZR) filed Chapter 11 in early 2026.** Most direct US lidar pure-play peer **bankrupt** — this happened ~1 month before Hesai announced its capacity doubling. **Which means:** Western lidar as a standalone business model has been tested and one major player failed. Category mortality is not theoretical; it just happened.
- **Dilution overhang.** Launched $100M At-The-Market offering May 2026. Recent $97.5M follow-on already completed earlier in the year. **~$200M raised in 2026 alone against a $1.71B market cap = ~12% dilution** on a still-loss-making business. Continued capital raises likely if H2 2026 OCF-positive guide slips.
- **Camera-only autonomy alternative — and Tesla is winning at the price-sensitive OEM.** Tesla Optimus = **vision-only, no lidar at all**; targets $20-30K mass-production price vs Figure $30-50K. If the cost-leader OEM rejects lidar entirely, the addressable humanoid lidar TAM is capped before it scales. The vision-only philosophy is also gaining ground in autonomous vehicles (Tesla FSD, Mobileye SuperVision).
- **Even where lidar wins humanoids, OUST may not be the supplier.** Figure 02 uses a 3D lidar (MID-360) — **that's a Livox/DJI sensor, not Ouster**. The "humanoid attach" framing in OUST's narrative is aspirational; no Western humanoid OEM has publicly named OUST as a supplier.
- **Two Cantor downgrades in 4 days (May 7 + May 11, 2026).** May 7: Overweight → Neutral. **May 11: Neutral → Hold with $33 PT** (vs current ~$26.80). Consensus PT $39 (range $33-50) — has come DOWN from earlier highs.
- **Insider selling.** Company insiders collectively sold US$2.5M more than they bought over the past 12 months. Mark Frichtl filed intent to sell 40k shares.
- **Humanoid attach is still narrative.** Direct lidar use on humanoid form factors is not yet a confirmed large-volume use case — most humanoid OEMs (Tesla, Figure) currently favor camera-only or competing-vendor lidar setups.

> **What this means (bear case synthesis):** Three of OUST's bear arguments compound — Hesai is structurally bigger and profitable, the closest US peer (Luminar) just went bankrupt, and the most efficient humanoid OEM (Tesla) explicitly rejects lidar entirely. If any two of these dynamics worsen simultaneously, OUST could see a 30-50% drawdown even without an earnings miss. The stance stays bull because the Stereolabs/Physical-AI pivot is real strategic optionality — but the conviction is low because the path to profitable scale is narrow.
