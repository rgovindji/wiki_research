# Amkor Technology (AMKR)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
US-based OSAT making the most aggressive bet in the industry — **2026 capex $2.5-3B (~3x prior years)** to build out an end-to-end advanced packaging campus in Peoria, Arizona. Direct beneficiary of [[TSM|TSMC]]'s growing reliance on external partners for CoWoS-S networking packaging.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **OSAT partner for TSMC** — handles CoWoS-S networking packaging that TSMC outsources
- **Peoria Arizona campus** (groundbreaking late 2025) — creates US end-to-end advanced packaging ecosystem alongside TSMC Arizona fabs
- Part of NVDA's CoWoS-S/R packaging supply chain alongside [[ASX]] and SPIL
- Advanced products now **>80% of total revenue**

## Bull case
- **2026 capex of $2.5-3B**, nearly 3x previous spending levels — unambiguous demand-driven build
- **JPMorgan TSMC CoWoS report explicitly highlights AMKR** as a beneficiary of TSMC outsourcing
- **US geographic positioning** — Peoria reduces "geographic bottleneck" of Taiwan-only advanced packaging
- **CoWoS oversubscribed** through 2026 — pricing power
- **CoPoS pilot production in 2026** — next-generation packaging tech in their roadmap

## Bear case / risks
- **Capex risk** — $2.5-3B is a large bet that must earn returns; underutilization is expensive
- **Capex arms race with [[ASX]]** — risk of industry overbuild for 2028+
- **Customer concentration** — TSMC + a small number of mega-customers drive most revenue
- **Cyclicality** — OSAT margins have been historically cyclical
- **Execution risk on Arizona ramp** — labor, construction, qualification all on critical path

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Peoria construction milestones** — on time, on budget?
2. **Advanced packaging revenue mix** — direction quarter-over-quarter
3. **CoWoS-S capacity** — how much does AMKR actually capture vs. ASX?
4. **Operating margin expansion** — does the build-out flow through?
