# Qualcomm (QCOM)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** 6-18mo

## One-line thesis
Cheap (~23x forward) edge-AI / on-device-inference proxy with a real datacenter-AI optionality narrative (Tenstorrent talks, AI200/AI250 server chips) layered on a durable handset + auto franchise — the value-priced way to own the "AI leaves the cloud" theme, with Apple-modem loss as the known offsetting overhang.

## Bull case
- **Cheapest large-cap AI-adjacent name** — ~23x forward vs. peers at 35-55x.
- **On-device / edge AI** — Snapdragon as the inference engine for AI phones, PCs, autos; 40+ AI-enabled devices in pipeline.
- **Datacenter optionality** — reported Tenstorrent acquisition talks ($8-10B) plus AI200/AI250 datacenter inference chips; a genuine new TAM if it lands.
- **Automotive + IoT diversification** reduces handset dependence; auto design-win backlog growing.

## Bear case / risks
- **Apple modem loss** — Apple's in-house modem ramp removes a major QCT revenue stream over the next 1-2 years; the central overhang.
- **Handset maturity / China exposure** — core market is cyclical and geopolitically exposed.
- **Datacenter AI is unproven** — QCOM has tried and retreated from server silicon before; execution risk is high.
- **Licensing (QTL) legal/renewal risk** — high-margin licensing is the profit engine and faces periodic disputes.

## Key questions / what to watch
1. Apple-modem revenue cliff timing and magnitude.
2. Tenstorrent deal close + datacenter chip traction (AI200/AI250 design wins).
3. Auto + IoT revenue mix climbing.
4. Capital return (buyback pace) supporting the multiple.

## Verdict
Bull / medium conviction. **Book-fit: core-add candidate** — the value/contrarian edge-AI slot. The de-rate (~13% off highs) + cheap multiple + free datacenter optionality is the most asymmetric setup in Cluster A. Apple cliff is the falsifier.

## Recent developments
- **2026-06-29 — The data-center stack gets a 3rd piece: Qualcomm reportedly in talks to buy Tenstorrent for $8–10B** ([[2026-06-29-semiwiki-daily]]). Following Ventana (RISC-V CPU IP) and the $2.4B Alphawave (interconnect) buys, Tenstorrent would add the two things Qualcomm's inference-only, scaled-up-mobile **Hexagon** can't do: **BUDA** — a software stack that runs PyTorch/TensorFlow models on non-NVIDIA silicon *without a code rewrite* (a direct chip at NVDA's CUDA moat, same play as the [[2026-06-24-semiwiki-daily|Modular ~$4B]] buy) — and **Tensix cores built for AI training**, not just inference. All of it slots under the new **Dragonfly** data-center brand. **Which means:** Qualcomm is buying its way to a credible *third* merchant AI-DC platform (after NVDA and [[AVGO]]) — and doing it on RISC-V, diversifying off both handsets *and* [[ARM]] in one move. The bull read: the declaration is being backed with real M&A and a software-portability story, not just slideware. The skeptic read (echoed on the SemiWiki forum): Tenstorrent's *products* are unimpressive — the value is the IP/talent + BUDA, possibly a defensive acquire-before-someone-else move, and the silicon is still years from shipping. Stance held **bull / medium**; the DC optionality is widening, but it stays optionality until Dragonfly ships.
- **2026-06-25 — Investor-day "we're an AI data-center company now" declaration; stock sells the news (+3.8% but faded from +12%)** ([[2026-06-25-memory-cost-device-makers-fade]]). At its June 24 investor day Qualcomm formally entered the AI data center: a server CPU (Dragonfly C1000, 250+ cores), an inference accelerator, in-house memory tech to undercut HBM, and the [[2026-06-24-semiwiki-daily|Modular ~$4B]] software buy to chip at CUDA — with **Meta and Microsoft named as customers** and a target of **$5B data-center revenue in FY27, $15B in FY29** (plus $40B non-handset revenue and $18 adjusted EPS by FY29). **The tape's verdict was sell-the-news:** the stock opened June 25 at $219.43 (the day's high, gapping the after-hours pop) and closed **$204.90** — +3.8% vs the prior close but ~6.6% off the open. **Which means:** the market will pay for a *declaration* but discounts a multi-year promise — most of the hardware doesn't ship until 2028, and a CUDA rival is years of execution. The bull read for the thesis: demand for merchant AI silicon is now deep enough to pull in a *third* credible vendor (after NVDA and [[AVGO]]) and the customers showed up to sign. Stance held **bull / medium**; the data-center optionality just got a number and two marquee customers, but it's still optionality until silicon ships.
