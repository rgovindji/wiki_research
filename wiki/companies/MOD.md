# Modine Manufacturing (MOD)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium-high · **Time horizon:** 2026-2030 cooling supercycle + 2026 H2 spinoff catalyst

## One-line thesis

**The pure-play data center cooling story that's about to emerge from a multi-segment industrial conglomerate.** Modine has gone from 4% data center revenue to **25% in 18 months**, generating **>$640M data center revenue in FY25**. FY26 guide: **20-25% total sales growth, with Data Center segment specifically guided 60%+ YoY** and a target of **>$2B data center revenue by FY28** (3x current). **Management announced plans to spin off the Performance Technologies segment** (the legacy automotive thermal business), leaving behind a pure-play Climate Solutions + Data Centers business. Created a dedicated Data Centers segment April 1, 2026 with Art Laszlo as president, supported by >$100M of capacity expansion including new Franklin, Wisconsin cooling plant. **The spinoff is the catalyst** — it converts MOD from "industrial with cooling exposure" to "cooling pure-play with optionality."

> **What this means:** Most cooling stories are "buy [[VRT]] or [[NVT]] for the supercycle." Modine is "buy the company *about to become* a cooling pure-play, before the spin-driven re-rating." The math: a pure-play cooling business at 60%+ growth deserves a much higher multiple than an industrial conglomerate at 20-25% growth. The spin unlocks that delta.

## Role in the AI / data center infrastructure stack

- **Climate Solutions segment**: data center cooling (CDUs, cold plates, immersion cooling, chillers), HVAC for industrial + commercial
- **Performance Technologies segment** (TO BE SPUN OFF): automotive + off-highway thermal management — the legacy business
- **Data Center segment (created Apr 1, 2026)** under Climate Solutions: dedicated leadership (Art Laszlo as president); >$100M capacity expansion in motion; new Franklin Wisconsin cooling systems plant
- Adjacent to [[VRT]] (broader portfolio incumbent), [[NVT]] (liquid-cooling pure-play), [[ETN]] (power-focused)
- Customers: hyperscalers + colocation operators + AI-specific data center operators

## Bull case

- **The spinoff catalyst converts MOD from conglomerate to pure-play cooling.** Post-spin Modine = Climate Solutions + Data Centers — exactly the high-multiple business mix the market wants. **Spin-driven re-rating is a well-documented value-unlock mechanism** when the remaining business is structurally higher-growth and -margin.
- **Data center revenue went from 4% to 25% in 18 months.** That's a structural transformation, not a cyclical pop. Combined with FY28 $2B+ target, MOD is mid-flight in a multi-year revenue-mix shift.
- **60%+ FY26 Data Center growth is among the strongest in industrials.** Even with conservative haircut, the segment outpaces NVDA in revenue growth rate (NVDA Q1 FY27 guide is +XX% which is lower than 60%).
- **Dedicated Data Centers segment + named president** = real organizational commitment, not just marketing. Art Laszlo's appointment from April 1 is the kind of governance signal that often precedes investment + strategic clarity.
- **>$100M capacity expansion + Franklin WI plant** = real capex, real conversion. Capacity is what converts demand into revenue; Modine is executing.
- **Liquid + air cooling diversified portfolio.** Unlike NVT (heavily liquid-focused), Modine spans the full cooling stack — air-cooled, liquid-cooled, immersion, hybrid — giving customers single-source options.
- **Climate Solutions ex-data-center grows too.** HVAC industrial + commercial market provides downside support that pure-play cooling specs don't have.
- **Multiple expansion delta is the biggest near-term catalyst.** Current MOD trades at industrial multiple; pure-play cooling co. would trade at NVT-class multiple. **Spinoff re-rating optionality.**

## Bear case / risks

- **Spinoff execution risk.** Performance Technologies separation requires complex accounting, banking, and operational decoupling. Timeline could slip; spin-execution mistakes have cost shareholders in adjacent cases.
- **NVT competitive pressure.** nVent has been investing in liquid cooling for a decade with >1 GW deployed; Modine is the newer entrant despite the strong growth.
- **VRT scale advantage.** Vertiv is much larger ($XX B revenue) with enterprise sales relationships; could squeeze Modine in larger hyperscaler tenders.
- **Customer concentration in hyperscalers.** Same risk as all data center infrastructure names.
- **Performance Technologies left behind.** The remaining post-spin entity (PT-only) needs its own thesis; if PT trades at a discount, total spin-value could disappoint.
- **AI capex digest risk.** Same Damodaran-scenario vulnerability. Modine's data center growth is the most cyclical leg of the business.
- **Capacity ramp execution at Franklin WI** + other expansion needs to happen on schedule + at yield.
- **History of cyclical thermal business with mixed margins.** Modine's automotive thermal legacy created uneven margin track record; investors may discount until the pure-play financials demonstrate sustained margin.
- **Insider selling or share issuance during spin process** could create overhangs.

> **What this means (bear synthesis):** The spinoff catalyst is genuinely real but introduces execution complexity. The data center growth is real but Modine is competing against larger incumbents (VRT) and stronger pure-plays (NVT). The bull thesis works if the spin clarifies value + Data Center growth maintains 60%+ pace; it weakens materially if spin slips or growth decelerates.
