# Jabil (JBL)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
The "silent giant" of AI infrastructure — Intelligent Infrastructure segment **+52% YoY**, AI-related revenue projected **$13.1B FY26 (+46%)**, and a $500M North Carolina AI hardware facility coming online mid-2026. Hyperscaler-served contract manufacturer with concentrated customer exposure and outsized AI mix shift.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Contract manufacturer** for hyperscaler AI hardware (Amazon, Microsoft, Google, Meta)
- **Liquid-cooled AI server racks** + high-density power racks
- New **$500M NC AI facility** mid-2026 — focused on liquid cooling and AI infrastructure
- Customer concentration to 4-5 hyperscalers

## Bull case
- **Q2 FY26 revenue $8.3B (+23% YoY)** with Intelligent Infrastructure +52%
- **AI revenue trajectory $13.1B FY26 (+46%)** — direct read on hyperscaler demand
- **Mid-2026 NC capacity** comes online into demand
- **Infrastructure Margin expansion** as AI mix grows
- **Quiet compounder narrative** — less analyst coverage than Mag 7 names
- **🔥 Apptronik humanoid manufacturing partner (NEW 2026-05-17 PM)** per [[2026-05-17-robotics-multi-agent-batch]] Agent C: **Jabil announced as Apptronik's manufacturing partner** for the Apollo humanoid robot. Apptronik just raised Series A extension to $5B valuation (Feb 2026; $935M total Series A) with Google + Mercedes-Benz + AT&T Ventures + John Deere + Qatar Investment Authority as backers. Apollo runs on NVIDIA Jetson AGX Orin (275+ TOPS); deployed at Mercedes-Benz factories + GXO Logistics. **Which means**: JBL gains direct humanoid-manufacturing exposure beyond the AI-server thesis — a second-leg revenue vector at the most concrete edge of physical AI buildout. Production scale data not yet disclosed but Apptronik's 2026-2027 commercial roadmap implies meaningful volume contribution to JBL Intelligent Infrastructure segment.

## Bear case / risks
- **Hyperscaler concentration** — 4-5 customers drive most AI growth
- **Contract manufacturing margins** are thinner than design houses or branded OEMs
- **Capex-heavy** business — execution risk on NC facility
- **AI capex cycle exposure** — slows when hyperscalers pause
- **Less brand defensibility** vs. Dell or SMCI

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Intelligent Infrastructure segment growth** quarterly
2. **NC facility ramp** — milestones and utilization
3. **Customer concentration disclosures**
4. **Operating margin** on AI mix
5. **Backlog visibility**
