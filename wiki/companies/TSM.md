# Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing (TSM)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term · **Listing:** ADR (NYSE: TSM); primary listing TPE: 2330

## One-line thesis
The single point of truth for advanced AI chip manufacturing — every leading-edge AI accelerator (NVDA Blackwell, AMD MI series, hyperscaler custom silicon) flows through TSMC fabs. Picks-and-shovels-of-the-picks-and-shovels.

## Bull case
- **Monopoly economics on leading-edge** — only foundry that can produce 3nm/2nm at AI-compute scale
- **All major AI customers funnel through it** — NVDA, AMD, AAPL, MTK, plus hyperscaler custom silicon (TPU, Trainium, MTIA)
- **Capex commitment ($52-56B)** signals confident demand visibility from customers
- **Margin discipline visible in allocation behavior (NEW).** Per [[2026-05-09-dwarkesh-dylan-semianalysis]]: TSMC is *more* excited to give allocation to **CPU customers** (Graviton, AMD CPUs) than to GPU customers — because they view CPU demand as **stable long-term growth** vs. GPU as **fast-but-cyclical**. This is conservative supply-chain management, not greedy. It also means TSMC will sell out as much as they choose to make available.
- **A16 (N2 variant) first customer is AI, not Apple (NEW).** A historic shift — Apple has lost first-customer status at the leading node. This is a structural negative for [[AAPL]] but bullish for TSMC pricing power across the whole AI customer base.
- **Geographic diversification** — Arizona, Japan, Germany fabs reducing single-island concentration risk over multi-year horizon
- **Pricing power** — process node leadership lets TSMC raise wafer prices and pass through inflation

## Bear case / risks
- **Geopolitical (Taiwan).** The single largest tail risk in global tech. PRC actions (invasion, blockade, escalation) would catastrophically disrupt operations and supply chains.
- **US export controls** to China limit some addressable market and create policy risk in both directions.
- **Customer concentration** — heavy dependence on a small number of mega-buyers (NVDA, AAPL).
- **Cyclicality** — foundry has historically been deeply cyclical; current capex cycle could over-shoot.
- **Currency** — TWD volatility affects USD-reported figures.
- **Capex risk** — $50B+ annual capex requires continuous demand validation; under-utilization in a downturn is expensive.

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Monthly revenue prints** — leading indicator for advanced-node demand
2. **Capex utilization commentary** — overbuild / underbuild signals
3. **2nm ramp** — production yields and pricing at the next node
4. **Geopolitical headlines** — Taiwan / China / US developments
5. **Customer concentration disclosures** — NVDA share of revenue

Capital appropriations: **US$31,284.30M ("$31.3B")** confirmed. **🔥 MAJOR NEW DATA POINT: capital injection into TSMC Arizona of up to US$20 billion** — roughly **1/3 of full-year 2026 CapEx earmarked for Arizona alone**. Rationale: *"focus its resources on core business activities"*. **Which means** TSMC continuing to recycle legacy mature-node capital into the leading-edge AI capex cycle. Strategic relations (VIS interposer production, GaN tech licensing) preserved.
- **2026-05-17 — Mii (TSMC SVP R&D) roadmap: A14 by 2028 + CFET + COUPE photonics + SoW** ([[2026-05-17-semiwiki-nenni-blog-sweep]] Article 2). **A14**: 2nd-gen nanosheet with NanoFlex Pro; **10-15% speed gain or 25-30% power reduction** vs N2; **production by 2028**. **A13** (post-A14): 6% die size via optical shrink, backward compatible. **N2 family** (N2/N2P/N2X/N2U) all in customer use. **CFET** (vertical stacking nFET/pFET) demonstrated; next scaling beyond GAA. **CoWoS**: reticle sizes expanding + HBM through 2030. **SoW (System on Wafer)**: entire systems integrated on a wafer for massive AI workloads — new platform. **COUPE** (Compact Universal Photonic Engine) explicitly confirmed = TSMC's CPO integration platform is at-scale program, NOT speculative — third independent confirmation of [[SOITEC]] substrate thesis on the wiki. **Which means**: TSMC's roadmap depth justifies the multi-decade pricing-power framing; A14 timing in 2028 aligns with [[bottleneck-roadmap]] EUV constraint period.
- **2026-05-17 — N2 "effectively sold out through 2026"** per Nenni (March 2026 article via [[2026-05-17-semiwiki-nenni-blog-sweep]] Article 1). Anchor customers: **Apple, Nvidia, Qualcomm, AMD**. Nenni: *"TSMC will again dominate 2nm as it did 3nm without question."* Capacity targeted to "well into six figures by 2026-2028" monthly wafer starts.
- **2026-05-17 — Apple's flagship 5G modem (C2) stays at TSMC on N2** ([[2026-05-17-semiwiki-forum-sweep-2]] Thread C). Even with the Apple-Intel diversification narrative, marquee Apple work (5G modem replacing Qualcomm in 2027) is going to TSMC. TSMC subsidiary Jingcai (3374) urgently purchased **600 testing machines** for the volume. Daniel Nenni's verdict: *"What foundry at 2nm has the capacity and yield to support Apple's iPhone orders? I can only think of one... TSMC!!!!! Not to mention trust with that important of a chip."* TSMC backend testing capacity also reported "almost fully booked" with AI demand from NVDA/GOOGL/MSFT/META/AMD — independent confirmation of [[bottleneck-roadmap]] cleanroom-space thesis.

Nenni's call: *"TSMC's current 2026 CapEx framework is already becoming too conservative"* — probability of a 2Q26 earnings CapEx upward revision is "increasing." **Which means** Dylan's qualitative "$100B by 2028" thesis now has hard tooling-order data backing it. Specialty Devices + Advanced Packaging $0 approvals in Q1 are attributed to *digestion* of prior massive allocations (silicon photonics, CoWoS, SoIC), not demand weakness — but worth monitoring [[AMKR]] / [[ASX]] / [[CAMT]] if Q2 also prints zero.
