# Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing (TSM)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term · **Listing:** ADR (NYSE: TSM); primary listing TPE: 2330

## One-line thesis
The single point of truth for advanced AI chip manufacturing — every leading-edge AI accelerator (NVDA Blackwell, AMD MI series, hyperscaler custom silicon) flows through TSMC fabs. Picks-and-shovels-of-the-picks-and-shovels.

## Bull case
- **Monopoly economics on leading-edge** — only foundry that can produce 3nm/2nm at AI-compute scale
- **All major AI customers funnel through it** — NVDA, AMD, AAPL, MTK, plus hyperscaler custom silicon (TPU, Trainium, MTIA)
- **Capex commitment ($52-56B)** signals confident demand visibility from customers
- **Margin discipline visible in allocation behavior (NEW).** Per [[2026-05-09-dwarkesh-dylan-semianalysis]]: TSMC is *more* excited to give allocation to **CPU customers** (Graviton, AMD CPUs) than to GPU customers — because they view CPU demand as **stable long-term growth** vs. GPU as **fast-but-cyclical**. This is conservative supply-chain management, not greedy. It also means TSMC will sell out as much as they choose to make available.
- **A16 (N2 variant) first customer is AI, not Apple (NEW).** A historic shift — Apple has lost first-customer status at the leading node. This is a structural negative for [[AAPL]] but bullish for TSMC pricing power across the whole AI customer base.
- **Geographic diversification** — Arizona, Japan, Germany fabs reducing single-island concentration risk over multi-year horizon
- **Pricing power** — process node leadership lets TSMC raise wafer prices and pass through inflation

## Bear case / risks
- **Geopolitical (Taiwan).** The single largest tail risk in global tech. PRC actions (invasion, blockade, escalation) would catastrophically disrupt operations and supply chains.
- **US export controls** to China limit some addressable market and create policy risk in both directions.
- **Customer concentration** — heavy dependence on a small number of mega-buyers (NVDA, AAPL).
- **Cyclicality** — foundry has historically been deeply cyclical; current capex cycle could over-shoot.
- **Currency** — TWD volatility affects USD-reported figures.
- **Capex risk** — $50B+ annual capex requires continuous demand validation; under-utilization in a downturn is expensive.

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Monthly revenue prints** — leading indicator for advanced-node demand
2. **Capex utilization commentary** — overbuild / underbuild signals
3. **2nm ramp** — production yields and pricing at the next node
4. **Geopolitical headlines** — Taiwan / China / US developments
5. **Customer concentration disclosures** — NVDA share of revenue

## Recent developments
- **2026 Q1** — Revenue grew ~35% YoY; capex guidance raised to $52–56B
- **2026 (ongoing)** — Arizona fabs progressing toward N3/N2 production milestones
- **2026-05-17 — 2026 capex now tracking $57.4B** (above prior guide); per Dylan Patel SemiAnalysis ([[2026-05-16-dylan-patel-invest-like-best]]): **TSMC 2028 capex potentially $100B**. Quote: *"3 years from now TSMC is going to spend $100 billion on capex... 2028... a real real possibility."* If realized, this is roughly 2x 2026 spend and represents the largest single-vendor capex bet in semiconductor history. **Direct read-through to [[ASML]] (EUV), [[AMAT]], [[LRCX]], [[KLAC]] equipment vendors** — they all see proportional revenue if TSMC capex reaches that level.
- **2026-05-15 — SemiWiki (Daniel Nenni): Q1 2026 board capital appropriations $31.3B** ([[2026-05-15-semiwiki-tsmc-tool-orders-capex]]). **Composition is the story**: $21.0B specifically for **Advanced Node equipment — the highest single-quarter authorization since tracking began Q4 2019**. **Trailing-twelve-month Advanced Node equipment approvals ≈ $55B**, nearly consuming TSMC's full 2026 $56B CapEx guide. Nenni's call: *"TSMC's current 2026 CapEx framework is already becoming too conservative"* — probability of a 2Q26 earnings CapEx upward revision is "increasing." **Which means** Dylan's qualitative "$100B by 2028" thesis now has hard tooling-order data backing it. Specialty Devices + Advanced Packaging $0 approvals in Q1 are attributed to *digestion* of prior massive allocations (silicon photonics, CoWoS, SoIC), not demand weakness — but worth monitoring [[AMKR]] / [[ASX]] / [[CAMT]] if Q2 also prints zero.
