# NextEra Energy (NEE)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term compounder (regulated utility + largest renewables developer)

> **Why a page now (2026-06-20):** NEE is the largest US utility + the world's largest wind/solar/storage developer — the scaled, lower-beta way to play data-center load growth. Cluster B power priority.

## One-line thesis
NEE pairs **Florida Power & Light** (regulated, steady) with **NextEra Energy Resources** (largest renewables + storage developer in the US), giving it the biggest pipeline to add new generation as data-center load surges. **Which means** NEE is the defensive, dividend-growing way to own electricity demand from the [[ai-capex-cycle]] — slower torque than the nuclear names but far lower risk.

## Role in AI / datacenter power
- **Largest US renewables + storage developer** — the deepest backlog to add the new MW data centers need fastest (solar + wind + battery come online quicker than nuclear/gas).
- **FPL** serves fast-growing Florida load, including data-center demand, under a constructive regulated framework.
- Storage + grid services position it for the firming/balancing that AI 24/7 load requires (complements [[GEV]]/[[ETN]] grid equipment).
- Less directly "nuclear-AI" headline-driven than CEG/TLN — more a broad electrification compounder.

## Bull case
- **Scale + pipeline** — biggest renewables developer = most able to monetize load growth.
- **Regulated FPL** provides stable, growing earnings base = lower beta than merchant power.
- **Dividend grower** with a long track record of high-single-digit EPS/dividend growth.
- **De-rated ~12% off highs** — more reasonable entry than the merchant nuclear names near their tops.
- Electrification + data-center demand is a multi-decade tailwind regardless of which generation tech wins.

## Bear case / risks
- **Rate sensitivity** — utilities are bond proxies; higher-for-longer rates pressure the multiple and financing costs ([[fed-policy]]).
- **IRA / tax-credit policy risk** — renewables economics lean on federal credits; policy changes under a Trump admin are a real overhang for the Resources segment.
- **Slower torque** — won't move like a pure nuclear-AI play; modest upside if the thesis is data-center-power capture.
- **Leverage** — large balance sheet and capex program; financing-cost exposure.
- **Execution** on the massive renewables backlog (interconnection queues, supply chain).

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Interest-rate path** — the dominant driver of the multiple.
2. **Tax-credit / IRA policy** under the current administration.
3. **Data-center load wins** at FPL and Resources.
4. **Backlog conversion** — renewables + storage additions per year.
5. **Dividend growth guidance** sustainability.
