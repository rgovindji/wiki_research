## Bull case
- **Aerospace aftermarket supercycle** — aging global fleets + delayed OEM deliveries (Boeing/Airbus) push airlines to keep older CFM56-powered jets flying longer, driving demand for FTAI's engine module swaps and parts.
- **High growth + improving margins** — +48% revenue, EPS up sharply; the asset-light MRO/parts pivot lifts returns vs. pure leasing.
- **Strong-Buy analyst consensus** (avg PT ~$350.60 cited as of 2026-06-18 — analyst figure, not a wiki target).

## Bear case / risks
- **Cyclical + rate-sensitive** — leasing/financing model exposed to air-travel demand and interest rates ([[fed-policy]]).
- **Rich multiple** — 55x trailing / 34x forward prices in continued execution; a fleet-renewal acceleration (new OEM deliveries) would erode the CFM56 thesis.
- **Past short-seller scrutiny** of accounting/segment disclosure is a known overhang for the name.
- **Outside circle of competence** — no AI/tech linkage; the wiki has no edge here.

## VERDICT
- **Book-fit: pass / watchlist-only.** A legitimate aerospace-aftermarket compounder, but no AI/tech/infra relevance and outside the wiki's focus. Track for diversification curiosity, not as a core thesis.
