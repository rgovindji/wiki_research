# Salesforce (CRM)

**Stance:** bear (tactical) / neutral (deep-value) · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** 12mo

## One-line thesis
The poster child of the [[ai-software-disruption|agent-eats-seats]] fear: a seat-priced CRM whose own AI is already shrinking the seat count it sells — cheap on paper (fwd P/E ~11) precisely because the market doubts the seat model survives the agentic transition.

## What they do
The largest CRM vendor — sales, service, marketing clouds, plus Data Cloud, Slack, Tableau, and MuleSoft. The canonical "system of record" SaaS, sold per-seat to revenue and service teams. Agentforce is its agentic-AI push.

## AI angle — the cluster's clearest disruption *target* (with an agentic counter-bet)
- **Agent-eats-seats, named and dated:** Salesforce's *own* service customers are already seeing **~10% reduction in seats/headcount** because AI makes service agents more efficient — a direct hit to the seat-based model, disclosed by the company itself. This is the cleanest hard datapoint behind the SaaSpocalypse thesis ([[ai-software-disruption]]).
- **The counter-bet:** Benioff is racing to reprice the threat as the opportunity — Agentforce now runs **three pricing models simultaneously** (per-conversation/per-resolution consumption, Flex Credits at ~$0.10/action, and a per-user "Agentic Enterprise License Agreement" at $125+/seat that wraps a "digital workforce"). The bet: sell the agents that replace the seats, capturing the labor budget instead of the software budget.
- **The bind:** if agents do the work, consumption pricing must grow *faster* than seat revenue shrinks — and the CIO cost-backlash ([[inference-economics]]) is squeezing consumption pricing at the same time. Forrester is publicly skeptical of Agentforce ROI claims.

## Bull case
- Fwd P/E **~11** — value-stock multiple on a $43B-revenue franchise with huge installed base and switching costs; a lot of bad news is priced.
- If Agentforce consumption monetization works, CRM converts the agent threat into a larger wallet (labor > software budget) — the optionality the cheap multiple ignores.
- Still growing +11% with strong FCF and a buyback; not a melting ice cube yet.

## Bear case / risks
- **The seat model is structurally challenged** — confirmed by CRM's own ~10% seat-reduction disclosure. This is the mechanism the whole SaaSpocalypse derate is built on.
- +11% growth is the slowest in the cluster — decelerating into the exact headwind.
- Three competing pricing models = pricing confusion / channel friction; consumption ramp is unproven and Forrester-doubted.
- Laffont (AI long) explicitly bearish SaaS; software down 9 days running into the SaaSpocalypse marker ([[ai-bubble-debate]] June 20). CRM was cited at 52-week lows in the June 11 software-derate ([[ai-bubble-debate]]).

## Key questions / what to watch
- Is Agentforce consumption revenue growing *fast enough to offset* seat erosion? (The single number that decides the thesis.)
- Net seat additions: do they go negative, the falsifying tell?
- Does the ~11x multiple re-rate up (monetization works) or is it a value trap (seats melt faster than agents monetize)?

## Where it sits in the SaaSpocalypse debate
**The defining disruption TARGET of the cluster.** CRM is the cleanest case study of agent-eats-seats — its own data shows the seat erosion. The investable question is purely whether Agentforce consumption monetization out-runs seat decay. Until that's proven, it's the structural short/avoid candidate the bears point to ([[ai-software-disruption]], [[inference-economics]], [[ai-bubble-debate]]).

## VERDICT
**Bear (tactical) / medium conviction / PASS for now (deep-value WATCHLIST).** The cheapest name in the cluster, but cheap for the most fundamental reason — its core pricing unit is the thing AI substitutes. Not a core-add until Agentforce consumption revenue is visibly out-growing seat erosion; until then the ~11x is a value trap risk, not a bargain. The worst-positioned of the enterprise three on "AI eats software." A successful Agentforce monetization print is the bull trigger to revisit.
