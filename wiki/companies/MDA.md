# MDA Space (MDA.TO / MDA)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term (10yr)

## One-line thesis

The **only public pure-play space robotics business globally** — MDA built the Canadarm legacy (Shuttle, ISS), is delivering Canadarm3 for lunar Gateway/lunar surface under a $1B+ Canadian Space Agency contract, and is commercializing the underlying robotics IP via SKYMAKER for on-orbit servicing + satellite assembly. **Q1 2026 revenue $464M CAD (+32% YoY), backlog $4.6B, robotics segment +18.5% YoY** — visibility most space stocks would kill for. Different thesis than humanoid robotics (no overlap with [[TSLA]] / [[MBLY]] / [[SONY]]) but if your robotics thesis includes orbital, this is the only credible name.

> **What this means:** If you want clean space-robotics exposure, MDA is essentially the only public game in town. Most space stocks are launch (RKLB, SpaceX via [[GOOGL]] stake), Earth-observation ([[PL]]), or constellation (SPIR) — none of them do robotics. MDA's competitive moat is decades of orbital-robotics IP that simply does not exist elsewhere outside of NASA labs. The risk is *Canadian government concentration* — most revenue traces back to CSA, ESA, or NASA contracts; you're indirectly owning federal procurement risk.

## Role in the AI / robotics stack

- **Canadarm3 / Lunar Gateway robotics** — $1B+ Canadian Space Agency contract (held with CSA, NOT NASA — so March 2026 Gateway pause did not cancel the program); design is being *adapted* for lunar surface applications
- **SKYMAKER** — commercial product line offering scalable + modular space robotics, interfaces, tooling, ground control stations; addressable for on-orbit servicing, satellite assembly, refueling
- **Robotics & Space Operations segment** — Q1 2026 revenue $91.6M (+18.5% YoY), driven by Canadarm3 program ramp
- **Adjacent businesses** — Geo-intelligence (satellite imaging tasking, EO satellites), Satellite Systems (Telesat constellation, smallsat manufacturing)
- Adjacent to [[RKLB]] / SpaceX (launch infrastructure, not direct competitor), [[PL]] / SPIR (Earth observation), [[NOC]] (defense space), [[LMT]] (defense space, hypersonics)

## Bull case

- **Backlog visibility is rare in space.** $4.6B backlog on $1.7-1.9B 2026 revenue guide = **2.4-2.7x book-to-bill on a current-year basis**. Most space stocks ([[RKLB]], [[PL]]) trade with single-digit-quarter visibility; MDA has multi-year visibility into 2028-2030.
- **Canadarm3 is locked in.** Contract with Canadian Space Agency, not NASA — March 2026 Gateway pause did not cancel the program. Mike Greenley (CEO) public statement: design can be *adapted* for lunar surface operations rather than the original lunar-orbit role. **Which means** the contract value is preserved even as NASA's mission concept evolves; MDA's IP is fungible across orbit and surface use cases.
- **Q1 2026 +32% YoY revenue growth** is a clear acceleration; Robotics & Space Ops segment specifically +18.5% YoY. Most growth-acceleration stories in space are pre-revenue (Intuitive Machines, ASTS); MDA is accelerating on $464M of real revenue.
- **SKYMAKER is the commercialization vector.** MDA is the only company globally with three decades of orbital robotics flight heritage — Canadarm (Shuttle), Canadarm2 (ISS), Dextre (ISS). SKYMAKER packages that IP for commercial customers (private space stations Orbital Reef / Starlab / Vast; satellite servicers Orbit Fab / Astroscale; potential lunar surface operators).
- **Trump 2.0 + Project Artemis pivot to lunar surface = actively beneficial for MDA.** NASA's pause on Gateway-as-orbital-outpost in favor of lunar-surface-base means more emphasis on surface mobility, surface manipulation, and surface infrastructure — exactly MDA's IP wheelhouse. The CSA + NASA dynamic is uncomfortable politically but technically advantageous.
- **Geo-intelligence + Satellite Systems are real businesses too.** Telesat constellation work + smallsat manufacturing diversify away from pure robotics single-point dependency.
- **Sell-side fair value range CA$49-58** vs CA$37 spot = 32-57% upside to consensus before any blue-sky moonshot.

## Bear case / risks

- **Customer concentration is the structural risk.** Most MDA revenue traces back to CSA / NASA / ESA / Telesat. If Canadian federal budget tightens (Liberal-Conservative election outcome 2026), Canadarm3 funding could be reprioritized. **Which means** the bull case has political tail-risk on top of normal business risk.
- **NASA Gateway pause is *not* fully de-risked.** The Canadarm3 contract survived because it's with CSA — but if Canada eventually aligns its lunar program scope with NASA's revised mission concept, scope could shrink. The "Mike Greenley adapt for lunar surface" framing is reassuring but not contractual.
- **SKYMAKER commercialization is *early*.** Private space stations (Orbital Reef, Starlab, Vast) are years away from operational; commercial on-orbit servicing market (Orbit Fab, Astroscale) is sub-$200M today. Most MDA revenue will trace back to government contracts through 2028-2030 regardless of SKYMAKER pitch.
- **Currency / Canadian listing makes US-investor access friction.** MDA trades on TSX; the US-listed shares (also under MDA) work but tax treatment for US investors adds complexity. Not a deal-breaker but a friction point.
- **Telesat capex risk.** Telesat's Lightspeed LEO constellation has had repeated funding + program scope issues; MDA is a key contractor. If Telesat fails to fund, MDA's Satellite Systems segment takes a multi-quarter hit.
- **Not a humanoid play.** If your robotics thesis is humanoid, MDA does not address it. Space robotics is a parallel TAM, not the same TAM as Optimus / Figure / Apptronik.
- **Limited operational leverage post-Canadarm3.** Beyond the $1B Canadarm3 contract burn, organic growth from SKYMAKER + new geo-intelligence wins must replace the program-completion runoff. Visibility past 2030 is genuinely uncertain.

> **What this means (bear synthesis):** MDA's setup is structurally favorable — record backlog, accelerating revenue, surviving a major NASA program change. But the bull case requires (a) Canadian political stability supporting CSA budget through 2030, (b) SKYMAKER commercial validation in 2026-2028, (c) NASA lunar surface roadmap actually advancing. Any one of these slipping doesn't kill the story; two of them slipping compresses the multiple by 30-40%.
