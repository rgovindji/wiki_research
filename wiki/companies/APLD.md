# Applied Digital (APLD)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium (lower than IREN — earlier cash flows but thinner balance sheet) · **Time horizon:** 6-18mo tactical + long-term

## One-line thesis
A purpose-built **AI data-center developer/landlord** — designs, finances, builds, and leases large "AI Factory" campuses (Polaris Forge, Delta Forge) to investment-grade hyperscalers on long-term leases. The thesis is **build-to-suit AI real estate with hyperscaler credit behind the leases**; the risk is heavy project debt against an unprofitable, capital-hungry developer.

> **What this means:** APLD is less a "cloud" than an AI-specific REIT-like developer. It doesn't (primarily) buy GPUs and rent compute the way [[CRWV]]/[[NBIS]] do — it builds the energized buildings and signs multi-billion-dollar leases with hyperscalers who put their own GPUs inside. The bull case is the durability of long leases backed by investment-grade tenants; the bear case is that funding each campus requires stacking expensive secured debt while the company still loses money.

## Role in the AI stack
- **AI data-center developer / landlord (build-to-suit)** — campuses leased to hyperscalers, who deploy their own compute. Distinct from GPU-renting neoclouds ([[CRWV]], [[NBIS]], [[IREN]]).
- **Delta Forge 2** (Jun 8 2026) — new 210 MW lease with a US-based, high-investment-grade hyperscaler; APLD's 5th AI Factory campus.
- **Polaris Forge 1** — anchor campus; 4th building funded via the June 2026 notes offering.
- **Held long by Situational Awareness LP** (Aschenbrenner) — named among the fund's longs alongside [[IREN]], Core Scientific, Cipher, Bloom Energy. See [[2026-06-20-aschenbrenner-situational-awareness]]. Same "own the buildout bottleneck" logic.

## Bull case
- **Investment-grade hyperscaler leases = high-quality, long-duration revenue.** The Delta Forge 2 tenant is explicitly "high investment-grade." **Which means:** APLD's forward revenue is underwritten by hyperscaler credit, not by spot GPU-rental rates — structurally more durable than a pure neocloud's customer book if executed.
- **Demand is pulling capacity forward.** 5 campuses now; serial 200 MW+ lease signings; +66% revenue growth. **Which means:** APLD is supply-constrained on energized capacity, not demand-constrained — the favorable side of the [[datacenter-construction]] / [[ai-capex-cycle]] imbalance.
- **Landlord model insulates from GPU obsolescence.** Because tenants own the GPUs, APLD avoids the Blackwell→Rubin depreciation write-down risk that hangs over CRWV/NBIS/IREN. **Which means:** one of the sharpest neocloud bear risks largely doesn't apply here.
- **Smart-money validation** — Situational Awareness LP long, consistent with the owned-infrastructure thesis.

## Bear case / risks
- **Unprofitable and capital-intensive.** ‑$192.5M net loss; each campus needs large secured debt. **June 9 2026: priced $1.59B senior secured notes due 2031** to fund a single building. **Which means:** APLD is layering on expensive project debt while burning cash — a rising-rate or AI-sentiment shock ([[fed-policy]], [[ai-infrastructure-debt]], [[ai-bubble-debate]]) makes the next financing far more painful.
- **Thinner balance sheet than peers.** Smaller revenue base ($319M TTM) and no profits vs. CRWV's scale or NBIS's $9.3B cash. Less margin for error if a lease slips or a tenant pushes timelines.
- **Lease-counterparty / single-tenant concentration.** Each campus leans on one hyperscaler; delays in tenant fit-out or any renegotiation hit the project economics directly.
- **Execution / construction risk.** Build-to-suit means revenue lags the capex by quarters; power-energization and construction delays compress returns.
- **Valuation prices the pipeline.** ~$13B cap on $319M revenue with losses — much of the multi-campus pipeline is already in the stock.

> **What this means (bear synthesis):** APLD's bear case is balance-sheet fragility. The landlord model is genuinely attractive (IG tenants, no GPU-depreciation risk), but funding the campuses requires a continuous stack of high-coupon secured notes against an unprofitable developer. The model works beautifully in a strong financing tape and breaks fast in a weak one.

## How APLD compares to [[IREN]] / [[CRWV]] / [[NBIS]]
- **Different model, not just smaller.** CRWV/NBIS/IREN rent *compute* (buy GPUs, take depreciation risk, capture GPU-rental upside). APLD rents *buildings* (no GPU risk, no GPU-rental upside — it's lease spread). Cleaner risk profile on obsolescence, lower ceiling on per-MW revenue.
- **Lower quality than CRWV/NBIS today** — unprofitable, far smaller contracted base, no NVDA equity tie. Roughly peer-tier with IREN on maturity, but IREN owns its power and captures GPU-cloud economics while APLD captures lease economics.
- **Read:** the more conservative *expression of the same bottleneck trade if you believe in IG-tenant leases over compute resale — but the weakest balance sheet of the cluster, so financing risk is the swing factor.

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Financing cadence & cost** — coupon and size of each secured-notes tranche; the market's appetite is the leading indicator of stress.
2. **Lease-up pace** — new campus/MW signings and tenant credit quality.
3. **Path to profitability / FCF** — when do completed campuses turn cash-generative net of debt service?
4. **Tenant concentration** — disclosure of single-tenant exposure per campus.
5. **Power-energization timelines** — the binding constraint; any slip pushes revenue right.
