# KLA Corporation (KLAC)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
Process-control monopoly in semiconductors — as AI chip complexity drives up yield costs, KLA's metrology and inspection tools become more valuable per wafer. Jefferies upgraded to **Buy with $1,500 PT** (Dec 2025).

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Process control / yield management** for advanced AI nodes (3nm, 2nm) at [[TSM]]
- KLA tools are critical to keeping yield high enough for the economics of leading-edge AI chips to work
- Indirect — sells to fabs, not to NVDA — but every advanced-node wafer NVDA buys flows through KLA-controlled lines

## Bull case
- **Process control share is gaining** as chip complexity rises (heterogeneous integration, advanced packaging, ever-smaller nodes)
- **Jefferies $1,500 PT** (raised from $1,100, Dec 15 2025)
- **WFE forecast: $145B in 2026, $156B in 2027** — record territory for the broader equipment cycle
- **Highest gross margin** among the major equipment names
- **Advanced packaging momentum** — KLA is also a beneficiary of the CoWoS / HBM trend

## Bear case / risks
- **Cyclical** — equipment orders boom and bust
- **China exposure / export controls** cap addressable market
- **Customer concentration** — TSMC, Samsung, Intel, key memory makers
- **Already priced for the cycle** — multiple expansion has occurred alongside the equipment cycle thesis

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **WFE guidance** — quarterly directional reads
2. **China revenue mix** under tightening export controls
3. **Service revenue** — recurring / installed-base annuity
4. **Process control share** — does KLA continue gaining?

## Recent developments
- **🔥 2026-06-01 — GOOGL $80B raise + Berkshire $10B placement read-through:** KLAC is the indirect (one step up the chain) beneficiary — every incremental TPU wafer at TSMC AND every HBM stack at SK Hynix / Micron / Samsung funded by the GOOGL raise lands in fabs running KLAC process-control + metrology + inspection on every advanced node lot (per [[2026-06-01-googl-80B-equity-raise-berkshire]]). The $30-50B incremental AI capex tranche above the 2026 guide is additive WFE demand on top of the $145B 2026 → $156B 2027 industry trajectory the Jefferies $1,500 PT was already pricing. **Which means**: process-control intensity rises with chip complexity AND volume — GOOGL just equity-funded both. The "already priced for the cycle" bear bullet is partially refuted by externally-funded demand validation; bull thesis holds.
