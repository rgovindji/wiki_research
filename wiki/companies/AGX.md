# Argan Inc. (AGX)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** 2026-2030 natural-gas power-generation buildout

## One-line thesis

**The cleanest gas-plant EPC contractor in the AI-power-buildout era — debt-free balance sheet + record $1.9B+ backlog (growing) + 1.2 GW Sandow Lakes natural gas plant full notice to proceed.** Argan engineers + procures + constructs natural-gas combined-cycle power plants (and increasingly renewables) — exactly the form of generation that hyperscalers + governments are reaching for to fill the gap between current grid + 2030 AI-driven demand. Q1 FY26: revenue **$193.7M (+23% YoY)**, EBITDA $30.3M, EPS $1.60. Backlog grew 36% YoY to record $1.9B (with $1.26B gas + $532M renewables); management expected >$2B by year-end. **$546.5M cash + $315M net liquidity + ZERO DEBT** is unusual for an EPC. $150M buyback authorized; dividend raised to $0.375/qtr.

> **What this means:** AI capex needs power. Hyperscaler campuses need MW-scale generation that can't wait for utility-scale solar + storage timelines. Natural gas combined-cycle is the only proven baseload technology that can be built in 24-36 months at gigawatt scale. **Argan does that work** — and they have no debt, lots of cash, and a record backlog growing in the right direction.

## Role in the AI / power-buildout stack

- **Gas plant EPC**: combined-cycle natural-gas power plant engineering, procurement, construction (typically 0.5-2 GW per project)
- **Renewables EPC**: solar + wind + battery storage construction
- **Industrial fabrication** (smaller): metals + alloys for power generation
- **Telecom + power services** (smaller): legacy operations
- Adjacent to [[GEV]] (GE Vernova — supplies the gas turbines Argan installs), [[STRL]] (campus civil — parallel construction sequence), [[PWR]] (specialty electrical contracting — competes in some power-line work)
- Sequencing: Argan builds the power plant *upstream* of the data center campus that needs it; often a separate but coordinated project

## Bull case

- **Debt-free balance sheet is unusual + powerful for an EPC.** Most EPCs run with significant working capital debt + project finance lines. Argan's $546M cash + zero debt = ability to take on larger contracts, weather project cycles, and return capital to shareholders.
- **Natural gas is the only baseload power available at gigawatt scale in 24-36 months.** Nuclear takes 7-15 years (with [[OKLO]]-class SMRs being the only faster option but still pre-revenue); utility-scale solar+storage is still energy-limited; geothermal is too small. **Gas combined-cycle is the structural answer to AI power demand 2026-2030.**
- **1.2 GW Sandow Lakes notice to proceed** is enormous for Argan. A single project at this scale anchors the next 3-5 years of revenue.
- **+36% YoY backlog growth to $1.9B record** with $1.26B gas + $532M renewables = diversified across both energy transition tracks.
- **Mgmt projected >$2B year-end backlog** + trade-press reporting of $2.9B in later quarters = sustained order intake.
- **$546M cash + $150M buyback + raised dividend** = capital return commitment that's rare in growth-EPC names.
- **Hyperscaler power demand is real + accelerating.** Trump-era + Biden-era energy policies both favor natural gas for grid stability + AI economic competitiveness. Bipartisan tailwind.
- **MISO + ERCOT capacity needs.** Texas (Sandow Lakes location) is the most undersupplied grid for the AI capex curve; ERCOT specifically needs gigawatts of dispatchable generation by 2027-2028.
- **EPC margin profile is project-execution-driven; debt-free balance sheet protects against project-financing tightening cycles.**

## Bear case / risks

- **EPC margins are project-execution-volatile.** Cost overruns, weather delays, materials price changes, permitting delays = quarter-to-quarter margin variance. Argan has historically had volatile margins through cycles.
- **Project concentration in 2-3 mega-projects.** Sandow Lakes + a few other large projects drive most revenue; problems on any one project compresses overall results.
- **Natural gas pricing + policy risk.** While bipartisan-friendly today, future regulatory regime could pivot against gas (carbon tariffs, hyperscaler clean-energy commitments).
- **Cancellation risk.** Mega-projects can be canceled or restructured by customers; backlog isn't always firm.
- **Renewable EPC competition.** Solar + storage EPC is more crowded + competitive than gas EPC; renewables margins are tighter.
- **Hyperscaler in-house power.** Microsoft + others increasingly negotiating direct PPAs + in-house power generation; could disintermediate EPC players over time.
- **Customer concentration in a small set of utility / IPP / industrial customers** = vulnerable to specific customer capex decisions.
- **Stock has run substantially in 2025-2026** as AI power thesis has been recognized; valuation is no longer cheap.
- **AI capex digest risk = power demand digest risk.** If AI capex slows, power-plant order book softens.

> **What this means (bear synthesis):** Argan is genuinely high-quality with the right business model at the right time, but EPC is structurally lumpy. Returns from here depend on continued backlog growth + clean execution on mega-projects. Position sizing should reflect execution + project concentration risks, not just upside.
