# Harmattan AI (private)

**Stance:** neutral (research only — no investable seat identified) · **Conviction:** low · **Time horizon:** pre-IPO / multi-year illiquid

> **Why a page now (2026-06-22):** User asked for a work-up on Harmattan AI, a name the orchestrator did not recognize. Verification result: **it is a real, well-documented company** — a Paris-based defense-tech drone/autonomy startup, dubbed the "European Anduril," that closed a **$200M Series B led by Dassault Aviation in Jan 2026 at a ~$1.4B valuation**. Coverage is credible and corroborated (TechCrunch, Bloomberg, Sifted, DefenseNews, PitchBook). **But this is a hot, oversubscribed primary round in a sovereign-sensitive French defense champion — there is no obvious way for an individual investor to get a seat, and that is the pivotal unknown.** Private financials (ARR, burn) are NOT disclosed. Data confidence on the *company* is medium-high; on anything *investable* (price, available shares, security type) it is LOW.

## One-line thesis
Harmattan AI is a fast-rising European "autonomous forces" defense startup — interceptor/counter-drone, ISR, and strike systems plus mission-autonomy software — riding the EU rearmament wave with a strategic anchor investor (Dassault) and early government orders. **Which means** the *company* may be a genuine winner, but for a private outside investor the question is not "is it good?" — it is "can you even buy in, and on what terms?" — and right now the answer to that is unverified and probably no.

## What it does + why it could be huge
Founded **April 2024** in Paris, Harmattan AI builds **vertically integrated autonomous defense systems**: hardware drones + onboard AI autonomy + mission/command-and-control software, explicitly modeled on **Anduril** ("the European Anduril"). Product families (per the company site, named after deserts):
- **Gobi / Gobi Tempest** — VSHORAD (very short-range air defense) **interceptor drones**. GOBI is a <2kg AI interceptor, up to ~250 km/h, designed to kill hostile drones within ~5km in under a minute. *(army-recognition, dronexl)*
- **Sahara** — persistent ISR / surveillance.
- **Barkhan** — precision strike / autonomous engagement.
- **Sonora** — operator training / force readiness.
- **Kalahari** — mission management / command-and-control layer spanning the above.

The bull frame: Europe is rearming, counter-drone is the most acute live battlefield need (Ukraine), and Harmattan is moving from stealth to government orders in ~18 months with a top-tier strategic backer. **Which means** if it executes, it could become a core node of European sovereign defense-AI — a category the US (Anduril, [[AVAV]]-adjacent) is currently defining and Europe wants its own version of.

## Founder strategy (2026-06-22 — learning) · ⚠ NO English YouTube video exists
A YouTube search returned **no usable video** (no founder interview, conference talk, or English coverage on YouTube). The best learning resource is the **text** interview with [Le Grand Continent (Feb 15 2026, French)](https://legrandcontinent.eu/fr/2026/02/15/entretien-harmattan-ai-mouad-mghari/), CEO **Mouad M'Ghari**. Its thesis:
- **"The true strategic weapon is the factory."** Mass-production capacity, not individual system quality, wins modern wars — **100,000 units at 80% capability beats 1,000 units at 100%.** Speed/volume over perfection; "saturation by intelligent, coordinated robotic swarms" ("Napoleonic robotic armies").
- **Vertical integration for supply-chain sovereignty:** designs its own radios, radar sensors, optics, motors, flight/motor controllers in-house; developing **rare-earth-free motor magnets (targeted end-2026)** to de-risk the China supply chain. **Invested early in mass-production infrastructure** rather than proofs-of-concept — consistent with the "factory is the weapon" thesis.
- **Deployment-speed as the moat/credibility flywheel:** claims it "sold more drones in one month than most European drone makers in 15 years"; deployment timelines — France ~14 months ("fastest in DGA history"), UK ~15 months, **Estonia <7 months**; needs **long-term government commitments** to justify the production-capacity capex.
- **Investment read-through:** the model is *capex-heavy, mass-manufacturing, government-contract* — high future capital intensity (→ dilution) and revenue gated by multi-year state offtake. Reinforces both the bull (right thesis, fast traction) and the bear (capital-hungry, sovereignty-bounded) below. Note: founder talking his book; unit economics still undisclosed.

## Cap-table seat / how to invest (the part that actually decides the outcome)
**This is where confidence collapses to LOW.** Nothing found indicates an open avenue for an individual investor:
- The Jan 2026 round was a **primary preferred** raise led by a strategic (Dassault) plus institutional VCs. Those seats are taken; the protected preferred lines are with the funds and the strategic.
- **No secondary offer, SPV, or marketplace listing was found.** If the user is being shown a deal, it is almost certainly **secondary common** (an employee/early-investor selling) or an **SPV/feeder** — the two worst seats: behind every preferred dollar, no info rights, and (for SPV) extra fees/carry.
- **Sovereignty risk is structural here:** a French defense champion with a Rafale-maker as strategic anchor and EU/UK government contracts is exactly the kind of company where **foreign ownership is restricted (French FDI screening, classified-program clearances)** and the obvious exit (sale to a US megacap) is likely **foreclosed**. Exit is plausibly IPO or sale to a *European* strategic — years away.

## Bull case (if a real, fairly-priced primary/preferred seat existed)
- **Right category, right moment:** counter-drone + autonomy is the most acute live defense need; EU rearmament is a multi-year secular tailwind. See [[AVAV]] and US defense-AI comps for the public-market version of this thesis.
- **Strategic anchor de-risks demand and capital:** Dassault as lead + Rafale F5 integration is real industrial pull, not just a check.
- **Early government orders** (France, UK, a NATO gov) in <18 months from founding is unusually fast for defense procurement.
- **Strong VC syndicate** (FirstMark, Atlantic, Motier) and senior team (Safran, Isar alumni).

## Bear case / risks
- **No investable seat identified** — the binding constraint. A great company you can only buy as fee-laden SPV common is often a poor *investment*.
- **$1.4B post-money on undisclosed revenue** — likely a steep multiple on *orders/units*, not booked revenue. Defense procurement converts to cash slowly; unit orders ≠ revenue ≠ margin.
- **Capital intensity + dilution:** drone manufacturing at "10,000/month" ambition is capex-heavy → expect more dilutive rounds; a secondary-common buyer today could be diluted hard before any exit.
- **Sovereignty / exit foreclosure:** French FDI rules and classified programs likely block a US acquirer; narrows the exit set and timeline.
- **Hype-cycle / "European Anduril" framing** is a marketing posture; execution risk (production yield, contract conversion, key-person around founders Mouad M'Ghari / Martin de Gourcuff) is unproven.
- **Founder/early-investor selling**, if that's the deal on offer, is itself a signal — ask why.

## Pivotal unknowns (would flip the decision)
1. **What seat is on offer?** Primary preferred vs secondary common vs SPV/feeder — and at what price vs the $1.4B mark. *Unverified — almost certainly the deciding factor.*
2. **Is foreign / individual ownership even permitted** given French defense FDI screening and classified programs? *Unverified.*
3. **Real economics:** booked revenue/ARR, contract dollar values (not unit counts), gross margin, burn, runway. *Not disclosed anywhere public.*

## Questions to ask the seller / broker
- Security type: **primary preferred, or secondary common, or an SPV unit**? If SPV: how many layers, what management fee + carry, who's the GP?
- **Liquidation preference** and where you sit in the stack; **pro-rata / info rights**? (Common = none.)
- Price per share vs the **Jan 2026 $1.4B post-money**, and the **last 409A**.
- **Is the sale ROFR'd** by the company / Dassault, and is **foreign-buyer approval** required (French FDI / defense clearance)?
- **Last audited financials**, revenue/ARR, and the *euro value* (not unit count) of the France/UK/NATO orders and how much has been recognized.
- Cap table & total preference overhang; how many rounds of dilution are expected to fund the manufacturing ramp.

## Verdict
**Lean: pass / cannot assess as an investment — pending the cap-table answer.** The *company* is real, credibly funded, and in a hot category (medium-high confidence). But as a *private investment* this is currently **unrateable**: no investable seat was found, the round was a closed strategic-led primary, sovereignty rules likely restrict foreign ownership, and the only realistic retail entry (SPV / secondary common) is the worst seat at a strategic-set $1.4B mark with undisclosed economics. **What would change it:** confirmation of a primary/preferred allocation (or a fairly-discounted secondary with clean terms), proof foreign ownership is permitted, and actual revenue/contract-value disclosure. **Confidence: company = medium-high; investability = LOW.**
