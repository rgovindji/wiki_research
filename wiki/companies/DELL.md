# Dell Technologies (DELL)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
The most credible enterprise AI server channel — **first OEM to ship the GB300 Grace Blackwell Ultra Desktop**, comprehensive Vera Rubin (XE9812 NVL72) platform, and a deep enterprise + storage + services bundle that hyperscalers and SMCI can't match.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Top-tier AI server OEM** — PowerEdge XE9812 (Vera Rubin NVL72) flagship, plus XE9880L / XE9882L / XE9885L on HGX Rubin NVL8
- **First OEM to ship NVIDIA GB300** desktop superchip
- "Dell AI Factory with NVIDIA" — joint go-to-market for enterprise AI

## Bull case
- **Enterprise channel + storage + services** = harder-to-replicate moat vs. SMCI's pure-server play
- **First-mover on Vera Rubin** form factors → captures highest-margin early-cycle deals
- **Liquid-cooled rack-scale systems** at scale — technical complexity favoring Dell
- **AI servers + traditional infrastructure cross-sell** to enterprise IT
- **Diversified business** (PCs, storage, services) beyond AI

## Bear case / risks
- **AI server margins are thin** — Dell's growth is volume-led, not margin-led
- **Hyperscaler bypass** — large hyperscalers buy direct from ODM (Foxconn, Quanta) not Dell
- **PC business macro sensitivity** — consumer/commercial PC demand volatile
- **Inventory risk** during fast hardware transitions (Blackwell → Rubin)
- **SMCI / HPE competition** — share-shift battle ongoing

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Infrastructure Solutions Group (ISG)** revenue trajectory
2. **AI server backlog** disclosure
3. **Operating margin** on AI server mix
4. **Dell Pro Max GB300 desktop adoption**

## Recent developments
- **2026 Q4 FY26 print** — **AI-optimized server revenue $8.95B, +342% YoY**. Management **FY27 outlook ~$50B AI server revenue** (roughly doubling YoY). **$43B backlog** entering FY27. **Stock +67% YTD in 2026.**
- **2026-Apr** — Best month of the year (stock $163.64 → $208.95).
