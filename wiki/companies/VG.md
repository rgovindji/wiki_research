# Venture Global (VG)

**Stance:** neutral · **Conviction:** low · **Time horizon:** 6-18mo deep-value / catalyst trade

> **Why a page now (2026-06-20):** Venture Global is a fast-ramping US LNG exporter — only an indirect, second-order play on AI-datacenter power (via natural-gas demand). Deeply de-rated (~43% off highs) but carries contract-dispute and leverage baggage. Watchlist, not a core power add.

## One-line thesis
Venture Global is a high-growth US **LNG exporter** (Calcasieu Pass, Plaquemines) ramping rapidly with a low-cost, modular build model. **Which means** it benefits from rising global gas demand — including the natural-gas piece of US power generation for data centers — but the AI link is indirect, and customer arbitration disputes plus heavy leverage make it a speculative deep-value name, not a clean power-bottleneck pick.

## Role in AI / datacenter power
- **US LNG export** — primarily a global-gas-demand story, not a direct datacenter play.
- **Indirect AI link**: rising US power demand (incl. data centers) supports domestic gas demand + prices; gas peakers (e.g. CEG/Calpine, VST/Cogentrix) are part of the 24/7 power stack.
- Best viewed as an **energy-cycle / gas-demand** position that rhymes with the power theme rather than a core [[datacenter-construction]] beneficiary.

## Bull case
- **Explosive growth** — rev +140% YoY as new trains ramp; fwd P/E ~7 is cheap on forward earnings.
- **Low-cost modular build** model = structural cost advantage vs peers.
- **De-rated ~43% off highs** — deep-value setup if disputes resolve and ramps execute.
- Long-term global LNG demand (Europe diversification, Asia growth) is a multi-year tailwind.
- Sell-side "Buy" consensus with upside to avg PT ~$16.26 (per stockanalysis.com, 2026-06-20) — cited, not invented.

## Bear case / risks
- **Indirect to the AI thesis** — owning VG for "AI power" is a stretch; it's a gas-export story.
- **Customer arbitration disputes** — disagreements with long-term offtakers over early cargoes/commissioning are a real overhang and reputational risk.
- **Heavy leverage** — capital-intensive build financed with significant debt ([[fed-policy]] rate exposure).
- **Commodity price exposure** — LNG/gas spreads are volatile.
- **Execution** on remaining trains; commissioning risk.

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Resolution of offtaker arbitration disputes** — the key sentiment swing.
2. **Train ramp + commissioning** schedule.
3. **Leverage / financing** trajectory.
4. **Long-term SPA signings** with creditworthy buyers.
5. **Global LNG price spreads.**
