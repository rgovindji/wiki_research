# Coinbase Global (COIN)

**Stance:** neutral · **Conviction:** low · **Time horizon:** 12mo

## One-line thesis
The leading US crypto exchange and emerging crypto-infrastructure platform — a *crypto-beta* name, not an AI-software name: it's largely orthogonal to the SaaSpocalypse, with its swing factor being crypto-cycle volume/regulation, not "AI eats software."

## What they do
Crypto exchange (retail + institutional trading fees), plus growing non-trading revenue: custody, staking, stablecoin economics (USDC via Circle relationship), and crypto infrastructure (Base L2). Revenue is highly levered to crypto trading volumes, which are cyclical and volatile.

## AI angle — mostly orthogonal (the cluster outlier)
- COIN is **not a SaaS / app-disruption story** — it is crypto-cycle infrastructure. The agent-eats-seats / model-is-the-product framework that governs [[CRM]]/[[DUOL]]/[[NOW]]/[[VEEV]] largely does not apply here. Including it as "AI/enterprise software" is a category stretch.
- **Tangential AI tailwind:** "agentic commerce" / AI-agents-transacting narratives (autonomous agents needing wallets, stablecoin rails, machine-to-machine payments) could route volume through crypto infra like Base — a real but speculative optionality, not a current revenue driver.
- **Crypto-infra-as-picks-and-shovels:** the bull frame is "Coinbase is the on-ramp/rails regardless of which token wins" — analogous to the inference-infra "sells shovels" thesis, but for crypto, not AI.

## Bull case
- Dominant US-regulated exchange with widening non-trading revenue (custody, stablecoin/USDC economics, staking) that diversifies away from pure trading-fee cyclicality.
- Base L2 + stablecoin rails = optionality on "crypto as payment infrastructure," including the agentic-commerce angle.
- -63% drawdown reflects a crypto-cycle downdraft; high operating leverage means volume recovery flows hard to the bottom line.

## Bear case / risks
- **Revenue is *shrinking* (-5.4% YoY)** yet it trades at fwd P/E ~70 — the most expensive name in the cluster on declining revenue. Earnings are a leveraged bet on crypto-volume recovery.
- Pure crypto-cycle beta: drawdowns are violent (-63% off high) and largely macro/sentiment-driven, not fundamentally controllable.
- Regulatory and competitive (fee-compression) overhangs persist.
- Does not fit a "tech/AI software" book thesis — owning it is a crypto-cycle call, which is outside this wiki's stated AI/tech focus.

## Key questions / what to watch
- Crypto trading volume + price cycle (the dominant driver) — where are we in the cycle?
- Non-trading revenue mix: can subscription/services + stablecoin economics grow through a volume trough?
- Whether agentic-commerce / stablecoin-rails volume becomes a real revenue line (long-dated optionality).
