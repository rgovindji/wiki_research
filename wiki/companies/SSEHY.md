# Samsung Electro-Mechanics (SSEHY / 009150.KS)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium-high · **Time horizon:** long-term (2026-2030 acute window)

## One-line thesis

**The rising AI substrate challenger taking share at NVDA and AMD simultaneously.** Samsung Electro-Mechanics (SEMCO) just secured **"first vendor" status for NVDA Groq3 LPU FC-BGA** — the substrate for the LPU (Large Processing Unit) inside NVIDIA's next-generation **Vera Rubin** platform, with full mass production starting Q2 2026. Separately, SEMCO has an active **AMD data center processor + accelerator substrate partnership** (Busan + new Vietnam factory). This is the *only* major ABF substrate maker taking share from Ibiden + Unimicron in 2026 rather than maintaining position. Korean-listed (009150.KS); ADR available as SSEHY though OTC and thinly traded — but the Korean exchange is far more accessible than Taiwan for US investors.

> **What this means:** Ibiden + Unimicron + Nan Ya are the *incumbent* ABF substrate makers riding the rising AI tide. Samsung Electro-Mechanics is the *challenger* actively winning new business — Groq3 + AMD design wins in the same 6-month window. If you believe new entrants take share during demand inflections (the supply-constrained pattern), SEMCO is the higher-beta version of the ABF substrate thesis. The catch is conglomerate ties to Samsung Electronics parent + foundry process dynamics + Korea-specific risks.

## Bull case

- **Groq3 LPU first-vendor confirmation is a major design win.** The Groq3 LPU is the LPU partner-chip inside NVIDIA's Vera Rubin platform (the H2 2026 / 2027 generation after Blackwell). SEMCO winning first-vendor status for the FC-BGA means a multi-year revenue tailwind from a brand-new NVDA platform — not just incremental share of existing Blackwell volume. **Which means** SEMCO is locked in to the next-gen NVDA generation in a way that Ibiden is locked in to Blackwell/Rubin.
- **AMD partnership independently de-risks the customer mix.** Most ABF substrate makers are heavily concentrated on one customer (Ibiden 80% NVDA). SEMCO has *two* hyperscaler-customer wins (NVDA Groq3 + AMD MI series) in 2024-2026. **Diversified customer beta with the same AI capex tailwind** is structurally better than single-customer concentration.
- **Samsung 4nm foundry symbiosis is a structural advantage.** Groq3 LPU is fabbed at Samsung Foundry on 4nm; SEMCO substrate completes the package. **Samsung Group ecosystem integration** (Samsung Electronics, Samsung Foundry, SEMCO) gives SEMCO a unique advantage on any chip designed for Samsung Foundry production — which Korea's national champion strategy is actively expanding.
- **Vietnam factory adds geographic + cost optionality.** New Vietnam capacity diversifies away from concentrated Busan production; provides labor + cost advantages; positions SEMCO well for tariff-driven supply chain realignments.
- **AMD MI350/MI400 series ramping** — AMD's data center GPU roadmap is accelerating; SEMCO is one of two primary FC-BGA suppliers (with AT&S in Europe). Ramping AMD volumes = ramping SEMCO substrate revenue.
- **Tesla / xAI substrate potential** flagged in trade press — Samsung 4nm fab handles Tesla AI5 chip (rumored); SEMCO substrate logical fit.
- **MLCC division ride-along.** Multi-layer ceramic capacitors are critical components in AI servers; SEMCO is a top-3 MLCC supplier globally. Separate revenue line that benefits from the same AI capex.
- **Korean Won weakness can be a translation tailwind** (or headwind) depending on cycle.

## Bear case / risks

- **SSEHY ADR is OTC and thinly traded.** US-investor access is friction-heavy; primary listing is 009150.KS on Korean exchange. **Which means** institutional position sizing is constrained without Korean-equity broker access.
- **Samsung Foundry execution risk on Groq3 LPU.** SEMCO's Groq3 win is contingent on Samsung Foundry successfully delivering 4nm production for the LPU. **Samsung Foundry has historically had yield issues** (lost AMD share to TSMC, lost some NVDA mainline to TSMC). If Samsung Foundry stumbles on Groq3, SEMCO's substrate win is moot.
- **First-vendor ≠ sole-vendor.** SEMCO is *one* supplier of Groq3 LPU FC-BGA; Ibiden or others may also supply. The "first vendor" wording specifies key-supplier status but not 100% allocation.
- **Korean geopolitical tail.** Korea-US-China dynamics are sensitive; Korean foundry / package supply chain is positioned at multiple geopolitical hot points. Less severe than Taiwan but non-zero.
- **Cyclical exposure to smartphone camera + MLCC.** SEMCO's non-FC-BGA revenue ties to Samsung Electronics smartphone cycle + broader consumer electronics; these segments cyclically pressure consolidated results.
- **Ajinomoto 30% film price pass-through risk.** Same as Ibiden + Unimicron — SEMCO must pass through film cost increases to maintain margin.
- **Conglomerate / cross-shareholding governance.** Samsung Group has complex cross-shareholdings; minority shareholders of SEMCO are exposed to Samsung Group strategic decisions on inter-company pricing, capital allocation, and resource sharing.
- **Won / yen / USD currency translation across customer base.** Multiple FX exposures simultaneously (USD revenue from US customers, KRW costs in Korea, VND costs in Vietnam, JPY costs for Ajinomoto film).
- **AMD MI series may shift to TSMC.** AMD has historically dual-sourced foundry between TSMC + Samsung. Any AMD pivot back to TSMC packaging puts SEMCO's AMD substrate revenue at risk.

> **What this means (bear synthesis):** SEMCO's risk-reward is leveraged to two specific design-win execution paths — Samsung Foundry delivering Groq3 LPU at yield, and AMD MI series continuing at SEMCO. If both pan out, SEMCO is a multi-year compounder taking share from incumbents. If either falters, SEMCO loses the differentiating thesis vs Ibiden. The conviction is medium-high (not high) because the design wins are confirmed but the execution paths haven't yet de-risked at volume.
