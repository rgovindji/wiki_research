## One-line thesis
Soitec is the **only qualified volume supplier of photonics-SOI substrate wafers** to all three major silicon-photonics foundries (TSMC, GlobalFoundries, Tower Semiconductor) — meaning every NVDA Spectrum-X / Quantum-X CPO switch, AVGO Davisson chiplet, and MRVL Celestial chiplet is fabricated on a Soitec substrate. **Which means** it captures the upstream substrate-layer rent in the 2026-2028 co-packaged optics transition that the wiki has been tracking via [[COHR]] / [[LITE]] / [[GLW]] / [[FN]]. **But** the stock has already rallied ~6x off its December 2025 low, sell-side targets sit at €31-€35 (vs. spot ~€148-171), and ~52% of revenue is still smartphone RF-SOI that's structurally declining.

## Bull case
- **Photonics-SOI monopoly position.** Per industry sources, Soitec is the only supplier *qualified for volume production* of photonics-grade SOI at TSMC, GlobalFoundries, and Tower Semiconductor — the three foundries shipping silicon photonics for CPO. **Which means** Soitec is the substrate-layer equivalent of what TSMC is for advanced logic — there is no second source at volume today.
- **Direct customer exposure** (via foundries) to: **NVDA Spectrum-X / Quantum-X switches** (launched March 2025 — first volume CPO products), **AVGO Davisson chiplet**, **MRVL Celestial chiplet** (Celestial AI partnership). **Which means** Soitec is plugged into the [[NVDA]], [[AVGO]], and Marvell CPO ramps simultaneously without being a single-customer story.
- **Patent moat.** ~3,500 patents around **Smart Cut** wafer-bonding technology. Shin-Etsu has a license but limited volume; GlobalWafers' license was terminated 2025; SUMCO is not in this segment. **Which means** the technology moat is real and legally enforced.
- **Optical TAM expansion.** Optical interconnect market projected $14B → $73B by 2030 (~39% CAGR). Silicon photonics share of AI optical units rising from ~38% → 84% by 2030. **Which means** the Photonics-SOI base (~€100M today) has 5-10x runway if mix and unit volumes follow trajectory.
- **Operating leverage.** Soitec built ~€1.1B of factory capacity that's currently running ~60% utilization. Capex declining to ~€100M/yr post-build-out. **Which means** Photonics-SOI revenue growth flows through to margin at a high incremental rate — gross margin recovers as the AI mix fills the underutilized fabs.
- **New optionality: LNOI** (lithium niobate on insulator) — Soitec is developing for ultra-wideband data communication beyond silicon. Early stage but potential 2028+ revenue line.
- **Pristine balance sheet** — €808M cash, €145M net debt (~0.5x leverage). Self-funded through the smartphone downturn.

## Bear case / risks
- **Stock has already moved a lot.** €23 (Dec 2025) → €78 (Apr 2026) → ~€148-171 today. **Which means** much of the photonics-revaluation has already happened; new entry is into momentum rather than dislocation.
- **Sell-side targets are FAR below spot.** JPMorgan €33, HSBC €31, Morgan Stanley €35 — all Hold/Equal Weight. **Which means** either the analysts haven't caught up (bull view) or the market is pricing in optimistic execution that hasn't been delivered (bear view). Tension worth flagging.
- **Mobile Communications = 52% of revenue and declining.** RF-SOI for smartphone 5G is the cash cow but H1 FY26 was -30% YoY. Total revenue still down 22-29% YoY. **Which means** the "AI photonics story" has to grow fast enough to offset a structurally shrinking core — a race against time, not a clean growth story.
- **Photonics-SOI is small in absolute terms.** ~€100M revenue today (~17% of total). Even at 30% growth, it's a 5-7 year buildout to drive the bull math. **Which means** near-term EPS and revenue numbers will continue to be dragged by mobile/auto weakness, masking the secular photonics story.
- **Customer concentration.** 3 customers each >10% of revenue (unnamed — likely Qualcomm, Apple, NXP/STM exposure on RF-SOI; foundry customers on photonics). **Which means** any single foundry slowdown or design loss is a meaningful revenue event.
- **Patent moat is contestable.** Shin-Etsu has the Smart Cut license; capacity expansion at Shin-Etsu in photonics-SOI would erode the monopoly framing. Per [[2026-05-16-dylan-patel-invest-like-best]], the broader SOI/substrate industry sold-out theme is real, but Soitec's specific share is undisclosed.
- **Currency / liquidity risk for US holders.** EUR-denominated; Paris-listed; ADR-less; lower daily volume than US semis. **Which means** position sizing should reflect this.
- **CPO ramp execution risk.** NVDA Spectrum-X / Quantum-X are first-generation volume CPO products. If they slip or ramp slower than expected, Photonics-SOI growth slows. **Which means** the thesis is partially dependent on a small number of customer programs running on schedule.

## Why this fits the wiki framework
Soitec is **the substrate-level equivalent of the bottleneck-pricing-power pattern** that the wiki tracks throughout [[bottleneck-roadmap]]:
- 2023: CoWoS packaging → [[AMKR]] / [[ASX]] / [[CAMT]]
- 2024: Power → [[VRT]] / [[ETN]] / gas turbines
- 2025-26: Memory → [[MU]] / SK Hynix / Samsung
- **2026-28: Photonic substrates → Soitec** (for the CPO transition)
- 2028-30: EUV tooling → [[ASML]]

The CPO transition is *upstream* of the optical-module layer ([[COHR]] / [[LITE]] / [[FN]]) and downstream of fab equipment ([[AMAT]] / [[KLAC]] / [[LRCX]]). Soitec captures the substrate rent that doesn't flow to either of those layers.

**Per [[2026-05-16-horace-he-ml-systems]]** (Horace He, Meta PyTorch): the trillion-FLOP scaling problem is bottlenecked by data-shuffling between SRAM, VRAM, and across nodes. CPO is the architectural answer to inter-node bandwidth — and Soitec is the substrate layer that makes CPO physically possible at AI data center scale.

## iPhone 17 mmWave commercial validation (NEW 2026-05-17 PM)

Per SemiWiki / Yole Group / TechInsights teardown analysis ([[2026-05-17-semiwiki-nenni-blog-sweep]] companion article):

- **Apple iPhone 17, iPhone 17 Pro, iPhone 17 Pro Max** mmWave antenna-in-package (AiP) module is the **Qualcomm QTM565 (HG11-34443-2)**
- Manufactured on **GlobalFoundries' 22FDX RF process** — which is **built on Soitec FD-SOI substrates**
- Independent confirmation from BOTH Yole Group and TechInsights (industry intelligence firms with teardown labs)
- Daniel Nenni: *"The findings highlight a major industry shift toward highly integrated 5G mmWave Systems-on-Chip (SoCs), where phased array element spacing and area constraints are highly critical."*

**Which means**: Soitec's Mobile Communications segment (52% of H1 FY26 revenue, currently flagged in bear case as "declining -30% YoY") is **NOT** in pure secular decline. **Content per device is rising** as mmWave moves from bulk CMOS to FD-SOI — partly offsetting smartphone unit-volume decline.

**Strategic significance**:
- Apple iPhone 17 = **hundreds of millions of units of high-volume consumer 5G validation** for Soitec FD-SOI
- Mobile RF transition from bulk CMOS → FD-SOI on mmWave is a per-device content-growth dynamic the wiki had not fully captured
- Apple already validates Soitec FD-SOI = strong industry credibility *also* for the photonics expansion (related substrate platform)
- GlobalFoundries (GFS) is the foundry partner here — not directly on the wiki but worth noting as a Soitec channel partner

**Adjust bear case**: Mobile Communications volume decline is real, but FD-SOI mmWave content growth provides a partial offset. The "race against time" framing on the page should be moderated.

## Position sizing considerations (for a small starter position)
This is a **low-medium conviction starter**, not a high-conviction allocation. The thesis is genuinely interesting *and* the entry-point is uncomfortable.
- **The May 27 earnings is a binary event** that will clarify whether the Photonics-SOI ramp justifies the multiple. **Which means** a reasonable approach is to take a small starter pre-earnings (to participate in any upside surprise) and add only on confirmation post-print.
