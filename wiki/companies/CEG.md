# Constellation Energy (CEG)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium-high · **Time horizon:** both (long-term nuclear compounder + 6-18mo re-rating off the dip)

> **Why a page now (2026-06-20):** CEG was already in the book and referenced by [[VST]] but had no page. It is the largest US carbon-free / nuclear generation fleet and the original "nuclear-for-AI" PPA story (Microsoft / Three Mile Island restart). With the stock ~34% off its 52-wk high, it is now a **de-rated** core power name — the most important page in this cluster.

## One-line thesis
CEG owns the **largest US nuclear fleet** (~21 reactors) plus the Three Mile Island (Crane) restart contracted to **Microsoft on a 20-yr PPA**, and is acquiring Calpine to add gas + geographic reach. **Which means** CEG is the most direct, scaled public proxy for 24/7 carbon-free baseload feeding the [[ai-capex-cycle]] — now available ~34% cheaper than the 2025-26 peak.

## Role in AI / datacenter power
- **Largest US nuclear fleet** (~21 reactors, ~32 GW carbon-free capacity) — exactly the 24/7 baseload hyperscalers want for [[datacenter-construction]].
- **Three Mile Island / Crane Clean Energy Center restart** — ~835 MW contracted to **Microsoft** under a 20-year PPA; targeted restart ~2027-2028.
- **Calpine acquisition** (~$26-29B announced 2025) adds large gas fleet + Texas/West reach = round-the-clock + peaking complement to nuclear baseload.
- Nuclear uprates + relicensing extend cash-flow runway into the 2050s.

## Bull case
- **Largest carbon-free fleet in the US** = scaled, hard-to-replicate baseload the AI buildout structurally needs.
- **Microsoft 20-yr TMI PPA** is the marquee nuclear-for-AI contract; validates the entire data-center-power thesis.
- **De-rated ~34% off highs** — better entry than at the 2025 peak when the nuclear trade was crowded.
- **Calpine deal** diversifies fuel (gas peakers) and geography, smooths the all-nuclear concentration.
- **+23% revenue growth** and a strong nuclear production tax credit (PTC) floor under merchant power prices.
- Policy tailwind: bipartisan + Trump-admin support for nuclear life-extension and restarts.

## Bear case / risks
- **TMI restart execution / timeline risk** — first US reactor restart of its kind; cost overruns or schedule slip would dent the marquee narrative.
- **Customer concentration** on the marquee deal (Microsoft) — though the broader fleet sells into wholesale markets, so less acute than single-asset plays.
- **Merchant power price exposure** — earnings swing with PJM/ERCOT power prices; a soft-power-price year compresses upside.
- **Calpine integration + leverage** — large acquisition adds debt and execution risk; regulatory approval conditions.
- **Valuation** — fwd P/E ~24 is rich for a utility; the AI-power premium can compress in any tech/data-center capex scare (see [[ai-bubble-debate]], [[ai-infrastructure-debt]]).
- **Rate sensitivity** — utility-style balance sheet is exposed to higher-for-longer rates ([[fed-policy]]).

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **TMI/Crane restart milestones** — NRC approvals, capex tracking, in-service date.
2. **Calpine close + integration** — approval timeline, synergy realization, pro-forma leverage.
3. **New hyperscaler PPAs** beyond Microsoft — does CEG sign a second/third anchor?
4. **Power price trajectory** in PJM and the merchant book.
5. **Multiple** — does the AI-power premium hold or compress vs [[VST]]/[[TLN]]?
