# IREN Limited (IREN)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** 6-18mo tactical + long-term

## One-line thesis
Ex-Bitcoin miner ("Iris Energy") executing a credible pivot into AI cloud — owns its own ~low-cost power and data-center shells, and just landed a **$9.7B multi-year Microsoft AI cloud contract** (GB300) that re-rates it from "miner" to a [[CRWV]]/[[NBIS]]-class neocloud. The thesis is the **owned-power-into-GPU-cloud conversion**; the risk is a large funding gap to get there.

> **What this means:** IREN spent years building cheap, powered data-center capacity to mine Bitcoin. That same powered land is now the scarcest input in the [[ai-capex-cycle]] — energized shells. By retrofitting existing Bitcoin sites (British Columbia, Childress TX) into GPU halls and signing Microsoft, IREN is monetizing infrastructure it already owns at far higher revenue-per-MW than mining. The catch: the full buildout needs billions more capital it doesn't yet have on the balance sheet.

## Role in the AI stack
- **Neocloud / GPU cloud, miner-pivot variant** — converts owned, energized Bitcoin-mining capacity into NVDA GPU cloud. Peer set: [[CRWV]], [[NBIS]], [[APLD]], Core Scientific, Cipher.
- **Microsoft $9.7B multi-year AI cloud contract** (NVDA GB300) — capacity at the Childress, TX campus, phased through 2026. This is the single fact that re-rates the equity.
- **~50,000+ NVIDIA B300 GPUs** purchased (Mar 2026); ~150,000 GPU fleet targeted; **AI Cloud ARR target >$3.7B by EOY 2026**, back-end-weighted as Microsoft + the extra GPUs ramp in Q3 2026.
- **Owned power is the moat** — sites in British Columbia and West Texas; additional Texas + Oklahoma land secured. Differentiated from lease-based neoclouds by controlling the power stack.
- **Held long by Situational Awareness LP** (Leopold Aschenbrenner) — a top-5 long alongside [[CRWV]] and Core Scientific. See [[2026-06-20-aschenbrenner-situational-awareness]]. His fund's thesis is explicitly to own the *bottlenecks* (power, neoclouds) while shorting the GPU complex.

## Bull case
- **Microsoft $9.7B contract is the de-risking event.** A hyperscaler-grade, multi-year offtake on GB300 takes IREN from "Bitcoin miner with AI optionality" to a contracted neocloud with forward revenue visibility. **Which means:** the market can now underwrite IREN on AI-cloud ARR (>$3.7B target EOY 2026) rather than on volatile mining economics.
- **Owned power + existing energized shells = speed and cost edge.** Retrofitting mines is faster and cheaper per MW than greenfield, and IREN controls the power contracts — the binding constraint in the buildout (per Aschenbrenner's [[ai-capex-cycle]] framing). **Which means:** IREN can stand up GPU capacity on a timeline lease-dependent peers can't match.
- **Revenue mix is inflecting the right way.** Per Q-March data: AI cloud revenue grew to $33.6M (from $17.3M prior quarter) while Bitcoin mining fell $167.4M → $111.2M. **Which means:** the higher-quality, contracted revenue line is compounding while the commodity line shrinks — a genuine business-model upgrade, not a side bet.
- **Smart-money validation.** Situational Awareness LP holding IREN as a top-5 long is a credible (if not infallible) signal that the owned-power-neocloud thesis has institutional conviction behind it.

## Bear case / risks
- **Large funding gap.** Coverage cites a **~$21B funding gap** to fully execute the AI infrastructure transition; IREN has raised ~$3B in convertible notes (upsized $2.6B → $3.0B, closed May 14 2026). **Which means:** IREN must repeatedly tap debt + equity markets to deliver the Microsoft buildout — serial dilution and refinancing risk, acute if rates stay high (see [[fed-policy]]) or AI sentiment cracks (see [[ai-bubble-debate]], [[ai-infrastructure-debt]]).
- **Customer concentration.** Microsoft dominates the contracted AI book. Loss or renegotiation re-rates the equity. Same structural risk as [[CRWV]]/[[NBIS]] but with a thinner balance sheet to absorb a shock.
- **Bitcoin still material.** Mining is still a large revenue share and drags volatility/sentiment; a crypto drawdown hits cash flow during the most capital-intensive phase of the pivot.
- **Execution at the pace.** ARR target is back-end-weighted into H2 2026 — any GB300 supply, power-energization, or construction slip pushes the re-rating out and strains financing.
- **Valuation already prices a lot.** 110x trailing P/E and ~$21B cap on $757M TTM revenue means the AI pivot is substantially priced in; the Microsoft deal is known, not a surprise.

> **What this means (bear synthesis):** IREN's bear case is financing, not demand. The Microsoft contract proves demand; the open question is whether IREN can fund a multi-billion buildout without crippling dilution or a refi squeeze — while still carrying a shrinking-but-volatile Bitcoin business. It is structurally more fragile than CRWV/NBIS, which have deeper balance sheets and broader customer rosters.

## How IREN compares to [[CRWV]] / [[NBIS]]
- **Lower quality than both, today.** CRWV has ~$99B backlog and prints $2B/qtr revenue; NBIS has ~$44-50B contracted and turned Adj-EBITDA-profitable. IREN's contracted book (~$9.7B Microsoft) and revenue base ($757M TTM, much of it still mining) are a tier below.
- **But shares the best structural feature with NBIS: owned power.** IREN's owned-shell/owned-power model echoes NBIS's owned-capacity edge over lease-based CRWV — front-loaded capex, no recurring lease drag.
- **Read:** IREN is the *higher-beta, earlier-stage* expression of the same neocloud thesis. Right entity if you want the owned-power-pivot trade with more upside and more funding/execution risk than the established names.

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Microsoft revenue timing** — when does the $9.7B start hitting the P&L vs. sitting as backlog? Q3 2026 ramp is the tell.
2. **Funding cadence** — how is the ~$21B gap closed, and how much dilution? Each raise is a datapoint.
3. **AI ARR trajectory toward >$3.7B EOY 2026** — quarterly progress; back-end-weighting means H2 is decisive.
4. **GB300 / B300 deployment pace** — supply and power-energization at Childress.
5. **Bitcoin drag** — how fast does mining shrink as a share of revenue, and what happens to it in a crypto drawdown?
