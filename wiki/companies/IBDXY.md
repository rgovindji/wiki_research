# Ibiden Co. (IBDXY / 4062.T)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term (2026-2030 acute window)

## One-line thesis

**The single most NVDA-leveraged public stock outside of NVDA itself.** Ibiden is the primary ABF substrate supplier to NVIDIA AI GPUs — **80%+ of Ibiden's ABF packaging substrate revenue comes from NVDA**. Q1 FY26 board approved a **¥500B (~$3.3B) three-year capex plan FY2026-2028 — the largest investment program in Ibiden's history.** Capacity expanding **60-70% in 2024-2026**. **BofA models 35% CAGR operating profit growth over the next five years.** Two new Japan plants (Gifu + Ono) coming online. **The ADR (IBDXY, OTC) solves the access friction problem that [[UNIMICRON]] has** (no US ADR). If you can own only one ABF substrate maker, Ibiden is the cleanest US-investor-accessible high-conviction pick.

> **What this means:** Most "NVDA-adjacent" stocks have NVDA as one of many customers. Ibiden has NVDA as 80%+ of its highest-margin segment. Owning Ibiden is closer to "owning the right to manufacture for NVDA" than any other public company — and you get it at a Japanese-mid-cap multiple rather than NVDA's 280x forward earnings. The catch is concentration risk: if NVDA's AI capex digests, Ibiden takes the worst of it.

## Role in the AI / semiconductor stack

- **FC-BGA ABF substrate manufacturing** — the substrate that connects every NVDA Blackwell / Rubin / Vera Rubin AI accelerator to the motherboard
- **Primary NVDA substrate supplier** — 80%+ of Ibiden's ABF substrate revenue is NVDA AI GPUs
- **Intel server CPU substrate** historical heritage — Ibiden was Intel's primary ABF supplier for decades (Xeon, server CPUs); deep manufacturing-process IP
- **AI ASIC opportunity** — AI ASICs from hyperscalers (Trainium, Maia, TPU, Tomahawk) expected to approach **20% of Ibiden's revenue by 2030** (current single-digit)
- Upstream: buys ABF film from [[AJINY]] (Ajinomoto, 95%+ film monopoly)
- Adjacent: [[UNIMICRON]] (3037.TW, ~25% share but Taiwan-only access), [[SSEHY]] (Samsung Electro-Mechanics, Groq3/AMD wins), [[ATSAY]] (AT&S, AMD partner)
- Customer concentration overlap with [[TSM]] (TSMC fabs the chip; Ibiden builds the substrate it sits on)

## Bull case

- **80%+ NVDA concentration is the highest-leverage AI exposure outside of NVDA itself.** Every Blackwell, Vera Rubin, Vera Rubin Ultra ships on an Ibiden ABF substrate. **Which means** Ibiden's revenue trajectory IS NVDA's AI shipment trajectory — but at a Japanese mid-cap multiple instead of NVDA's premium.
- **¥500B FY26-28 capex plan is the largest in company history** — board-approved Feb 2026. This is a structural commitment, not a cyclical guess. **Which means** Ibiden's management is underwriting the same multi-year AI capex curve that NVDA + TSMC + Micron are.
- **60-70% capacity expansion in 2024-2026** is enormous — and AI ABF orders are still sold out. The market is so supply-constrained that even this massive build doesn't oversupply demand. **The shortfall projections (10% H2 2026 → 21% 2027 → 42% 2028) assume Ibiden's expansion happens on schedule** — without it, the shortfall would be far worse.
- **BofA's 35% 5-year operating profit CAGR** is an exceptional forecast for a Japan-listed industrial. For comparison: that's higher than most US large-cap semiconductor names. Even with conservative haircuts on execution, the operating leverage on capacity is substantial.
- **IBDXY OTC ADR solves the access problem.** Unimicron is TWSE-only; Ibiden has a US-listed ADR (OTC), which makes it accessible to US brokerage accounts without international-trading capabilities. **Which means** Ibiden is the practical default ABF substrate maker for US-account investors.
- **Intel CPU substrate heritage is structural moat.** Decades of FC-BGA process IP, customer qualification cycles, equipment optimization. Hard to replicate even with cash.
- **AI ASIC diversification underway.** Current heavy NVDA concentration is the bear case — but mgmt explicitly targets AI ASICs to be ~20% of revenue by 2030 (with major cloud customers). **The diversification is starting, which softens the customer-concentration tail risk.**
- **NVDA equity-investment / LTA pattern likely.** NVDA's track record of locking up critical supply via equity ($2B COHR + $2B LITE + $3.2B GLW + $2B NBIS + $2B SNPS + $1B NOK) makes Ibiden a likely next target. **Ibiden's 80%+ NVDA revenue concentration creates strong mutual interest in formal lock-up arrangements.**
- **Ajinomoto 30% film price pass-through is margin tailwind.** Since Ibiden is the supply-constrained party in the chain, they can pass through film cost increases more readily than non-AI substrate makers. **Which means** rising film costs are actually a moat-reinforcing event for Ibiden's bargaining position.

## Bear case / risks

- **80%+ NVDA concentration cuts both ways.** If NVDA's AI capex digests (Damodaran's "worse than dotcom" scenario) or hyperscalers blink on 2027 spend, Ibiden's revenue falls faster than any peer. **Which means** Ibiden has higher beta to NVDA than even most NVDA suppliers.
- **¥500B capex commitment is hard to unwind if demand slows.** If NVDA demand softens in 2027-2028, Ibiden has committed massive capacity that can't easily redirect. Margin compression in the capacity overbuild scenario would be severe.
- **Ajinomoto 30% film price increase compresses substrate-maker margin** unless Ibiden fully passes it through. While Ibiden's pricing power is good, full pass-through isn't guaranteed in every customer-by-customer negotiation.
- **Currency / yen translation risk.** USD-reported AI revenue translates back to ¥ at currency-pair-dependent levels. If USD/JPY moves toward 145 on BoJ hawkish action, JPY-reported revenue gets a tailwind — but if USD/JPY moves to 165, it hurts.
- **IBDXY ADR is OTC, not exchange-listed.** Some institutional accounts can't hold OTC securities. Spread and liquidity is materially worse than NYSE-listed ADRs. The 4062.T primary listing is more efficient if you have Japan-equity access.
- **Capacity expansion execution risk.** Two new plants (Gifu + Ono) need to ramp on schedule + at yield. Substrate manufacturing has historically had yield-ramp issues during capacity step-changes. **The 60-70% expansion is what the bull case requires; any slippage matters.**
- **Customer-poaching risk from competitors.** Unimicron (Taiwan), Samsung Electro-Mechanics (Korea), AT&S (Europe), Nan Ya PCB, Kinsus all have AI substrate ambitions. NVDA could re-balance suppliers to reduce Ibiden dependency over time.
- **No A14/sub-A14 process visibility.** Substrate makers ramp on TSMC's process generations. If TSMC delivers A14 sooner or differently than expected, substrate technical requirements shift.
- **Japan geopolitics tail.** Lower than Taiwan but non-zero. Japan-US-China dynamics affect Ibiden's customer mix.

> **What this means (bear synthesis):** Ibiden's risk is *not* AI being wrong — AI is right. The risk is *Ibiden being too perfectly concentrated on NVDA*. If you believe in NVDA's AI capex trajectory through 2028, Ibiden is among the highest-leverage public plays. If you believe NVDA's AI capex is going to digest, you should own Ibiden less than you own diversified peers.
