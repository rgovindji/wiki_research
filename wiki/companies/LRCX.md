# Lam Research (LRCX)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
Deposition and etch equipment leader, especially well-positioned for the HBM build-out — HBM consumes ~3x the wafer capacity of DDR5 per gigabyte, making it disproportionately Lam-content-rich. **Systems revenue forecast +26% YoY to $14.5B in FY26.**

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Deposition / etch tools** for HBM stacking and advanced logic nodes
- Indirect — sells to fabs, not NVDA — but HBM ramp is a direct revenue driver
- **HBM is wafer-intensive** = more Lam tools per GB of memory shipped to NVDA

## Bull case
- **Lam's Systems revenue guide: $14.5B FY26 (+26.2% YoY)**
- **HBM mix-shift** is structurally favorable — HBM consumes ~3x wafer capacity vs. DDR5 per GB
- **WFE cycle: $145B in 2026 → $156B in 2027** — broad-based capex strength
- BofA expects LRCX, KLAC, AMAT to all benefit from AI-driven capex
- **Service revenue** is a high-margin annuity from large installed base

## Bear case / risks
- **China exposure / export controls** — significant historical revenue from Chinese memory + logic build-out
- **Cyclicality** — orders can swing sharply
- **Customer concentration** — [[TSM|TSMC]], Samsung, Intel, memory makers
- **Already-elevated multiple** — much of the AI capex thesis priced in

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Systems revenue trajectory** — does it hit $14.5B FY26?
2. **China revenue** under export-control regime
3. **HBM-attributable revenue** — leading indicator
4. **Service / installed-base growth**

## Recent developments
- **🔥 2026-06-01 — GOOGL $80B raise + Berkshire $10B placement read-through:** LRCX is the indirect (one step up the chain) beneficiary on the HBM-wafer-intensity vector — every incremental HBM stack required to feed GOOGL's TPU + GCP NVDA fleet pulls disproportionate deposition + etch content (HBM consumes ~3x wafer capacity per GB vs DDR5) per [[2026-06-01-googl-80B-equity-raise-berkshire]]. The $30-50B incremental AI capex tranche above the 2026 guide flows into the same SK Hynix / Micron / Samsung HBM lines where LRCX's $14.5B FY26 Systems guide is already gated on HBM mix-shift. **Which means**: the structural HBM bull pillar that anchors LRCX's +26% YoY Systems growth just got externally-funded — GOOGL has now equity-funded a meaningful slice of the 2027 HBM4 / 16-Hi / 20-Hi capacity build the wiki's [[bottleneck-roadmap]] thesis depends on. Bull thesis holds.
