# Texas Instruments (TXN)

**Stance:** neutral · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
The blue-chip analog/embedded compounder is finally riding an industrial + auto recovery on top of AI-server content gains, but the stock has run back to within ~3% of all-time highs at a rich multiple, leaving the 12-month setup more "fairly priced cyclical recovery" than mispriced opportunity.

## Bull case
- **Industrial + auto cyclical recovery** is inflecting up after a multi-year digestion; revenue back to +14.9% YoY.
- **AI-server analog content growth** — power management, signal chain content per rack rising as power architectures get harder.
- **In-house 300mm capacity** (Sherman, Lehi) plus CHIPS Act support — structural cost/margin advantage as utilization recovers.
- **Highest-quality analog franchise** — wide catalog, sticky sockets, strong FCF and dividend track record.

## Bear case / risks
- **Rich multiple** — ~39x forward / ~55x trailing for a mid-teens grower; little margin of safety near all-time highs.
- **Heavy capex digestion** — the 300mm buildout has weighed on FCF; payback depends on sustained recovery.
- **AI is a small mix** vs. industrial/auto/personal-electronics; not a pure AI play.
- **China competition** in commodity analog; pricing pressure risk.

## Key questions / what to watch
1. Industrial recovery durability quarter-over-quarter.
2. FCF inflection as capex rolls off and utilization rises.
3. AI/datacenter content disclosure.
4. Gross-margin trajectory through the recovery.

## Verdict
Neutral / medium conviction. **Book-fit: watchlist.** Great franchise, fully-valued setup near highs; not a 12-month edge.
