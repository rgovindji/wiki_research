# MP Materials (MP)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term

The only fully integrated rare-earth producer in the US, with H1 2026 commercial NdFeB magnet ramp arriving precisely as humanoid robotics, EV, and defense magnet demand inflects — and Chinese export-control risk is the binding supply-chain bottleneck.

## Bull case

- **Only fully integrated US NdFeB producer.** Owns and operates Mountain Pass, CA — the only operating rare-earth mine and processing facility in the United States. Stillwater plant ramp expected H1 2026 produces sintered NdFeB magnets used in F-35 fighter jets, EV motors, and missile guidance systems.
- **Anchor customers Apple and General Motors.** GM magnet production ramping through 2026. Apple committed $500M to a rare-earth partnership.
- **DoD multi-billion-dollar partnership** announced 2026 establishes MP as a strategic asset. US government has explicitly shifted toward direct investment in domestic rare-earth capacity.
- **Humanoid robotics demand multiplier.** Each humanoid actuator requires NdFeB magnets at roughly 10× the per-unit content of an EV. A scaled humanoid buildout in 2027–2030 is a structural step-change in NdFeB demand on top of existing EV / wind / defense use cases.

> **What this means:** Every electric car uses some rare-earth magnets. Every humanoid robot uses about ten times as much. So even a modest humanoid buildout (say, 100,000 units in 2027) creates magnet demand equivalent to a million EVs — on top of existing demand from cars, wind turbines, and defense. MP Materials owns the only US factory that can supply this.
- **Northlake TX 10X magnetics facility** backed by $200M incentive package expected to break ground 2026 — adds long-cycle capacity.
- **Wedbush $90 PT** (Dan Ives initiation, April 2026) frames MP as not yet fully priced for vertical integration; consensus runs lower at ~$62 (Morgan Stanley Overweight).
- **"Balanced robotics ecosystem" framing reinforces strategic-asset status (NEW 2026-05-16 per [[2026-05-16-sergey-levine-physical-intelligence]]).** Sergey Levine (Physical Intelligence, Berkeley): "Something really important to get right here is a balanced robotics ecosystem… we need to think about how to balance our priorities, our investment." Dwarkesh on this episode pulls the exact pattern the wiki has been tracking: "within a few years the key bottleneck to every single part of the supply chain here will be something that China is the 80% world supplier of." **Which means:** as the consensus among leading robotics researchers converges on "US needs domestic robotics-components capacity," the political/strategic justification for MP's DoD-partnership trajectory deepens. Each new researcher commentary makes the path to a 2027-2028 industrial-policy lever (e.g., domestic-content rules for DoD humanoid procurement, tariffs on Chinese magnet/actuator imports) marginally more likely. Direct upside catalyst for MP's pricing power on top of base-rate humanoid demand growth.

## Bear case / risks

- **China retaliation risk.** ~7.7% owned by Shenghe Resources (Chinese, partly state-owned via Ministry of Natural Resources as of December 2021). Cap table optics are awkward in an export-controls scenario.
- **NdPr price volatility.** Rare-earth prices are Chinese-set in practice. A coordinated price collapse would compress margins even with secured offtake contracts.
- **Execution risk on Stillwater ramp.** Magnet manufacturing at the required quality and volume is not the same problem as ore processing. First commercial production was already pushed once.
- **Multiple has run on narrative.** +27% YTD on partnership news rather than realized magnet shipments. The next 4 quarters need to deliver against the H1 2026 ramp guide.
- **Humanoid timeline slip.** If the 2027 ramp narrative slips, second-order magnet demand pushes out with it. MP's primary thesis still holds on EV + defense + wind, but the upside multiple compresses.

## Key questions / what to watch

- First commercial NdFeB shipment from Stillwater. Single most material near-term datapoint.
- GM magnet offtake volumes by end of 2026.
- DoD investment terms — equity component, offtake provisions, price floor.
- Any export-control announcement (US or China) affecting rare-earth flows.
