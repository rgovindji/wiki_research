# AT&S (ATSAY / AT.VI)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term (2026-2030 capacity ramp window)

## One-line thesis

**The cleanest non-Asian ABF substrate exposure available in public markets.** AT&S is Austria-headquartered with the most geographically diversified manufacturing footprint of any ABF substrate maker (Austria, China, Malaysia/Kulim, India/Nanjangud, plus European R&D in Leoben). **Confirmed AMD data center processor + accelerator substrate supplier** — the primary non-Asia FC-BGA partner for AMD MI series. **Deutsche Bank upgraded to Buy with €50 PT** on AI substrate demand February 2026. **Kulim Malaysia + Leoben Austria facilities ramping in 2026**, with the unused K2 building at Kulim drawing customer interest (signal of tightening supply). **Also pioneering glass-core substrates** as a next-gen ABF alternative for ultra-large AI packages — positioning for the 2028-2030 architecture transition. Vienna-listed (AT.VI); OTC ADR (ATSAY) available though liquidity is thin.

> **What this means:** If you want ABF substrate exposure but don't want all-Asia-Pacific concentration, AT&S is the only credible Western pure-play. Smaller share than Ibiden / Unimicron / SEMCO (~8% global per industry data) but cleanest geographic diversification + clearest AMD AI partner story + glass-substrate optionality. The catch is smaller scale and lower NVDA exposure — AT&S is more AMD-leveraged than NVDA-leveraged.

## Role in the AI / semiconductor stack

- **FC-BGA ABF substrate manufacturing** for high-end IC packaging — same product class as Ibiden + Unimicron + SEMCO
- **AMD data center processor + accelerator substrate supplier** (announced 2024; ramping 2025-2026) — substrate for AMD MI300/MI350/MI400 series
- **Glass-core substrate development** for AI, high-performance computing, and photonics — pioneering the next-gen alternative to traditional ABF substrates for ultra-large packages
- **Geographic diversification** — Austria (Leoben R&D + production, Fehring production), China (Shanghai + Chongqing), Malaysia (Kulim Hi-Tech Park, new facility + K2 idle building), India (Nanjangud)
- Upstream: buys ABF film from [[AJINY]] (Ajinomoto)
- Adjacent: [[IBDXY]] (Ibiden — NVDA incumbent, Japan), [[UNIMICRON]] (Taiwan), [[SSEHY]] (SEMCO — Korea, AMD partner Asia), [[AMD]] (primary customer)
- Photonics adjacency: glass-core substrate development could position AT&S for the photonic-substrate ramp alongside [[SOITEC]]

## Bull case

- **Cleanest non-Asian ABF substrate exposure.** Every other top-5 ABF substrate maker is either Japan (Ibiden), Taiwan (Unimicron, Nan Ya, Kinsus), or Korea (SEMCO). **AT&S is the only Western public play** with material AI substrate exposure. **Which means** for portfolios concerned about Asia-Pacific concentration / Taiwan tail risk / Korea tail risk, AT&S provides genuine geographic diversification.
- **Confirmed AMD MI series partnership.** AMD's data center AI accelerator (MI300/350/400) ramp is one of the few credible NVDA alternatives in production. **AT&S has the primary non-Asia AMD substrate slot** — making it directly leveraged to AMD's AI capex share gains.
- **Deutsche Bank Buy upgrade with €50 PT** February 2026 — major European sell-side validation. PT vs current trading levels implies material upside on AI substrate demand realization.
- **Kulim Malaysia high-volume manufacturing started** — confirmed by DigiTimes May 2025 + reinforced by ongoing customer engagements. The **K2 building (currently idle) drawing customer interest** is a signal that AT&S has spare capacity that customers want — a luxury few substrate makers have in the current sold-out market.
- **Leoben Austria facility ramping in 2026** — European production capacity adds to operational leverage. Per industry analysis: "Two massive new factories, Kulim (Malaysia) and Leoben (Austria) are now active and ramping up, expected to drive significant revenue growth next year and operating leverage as capacity utilisation increases."
- **Glass-core substrate development is differentiated long-term option.** As AI packages scale beyond what traditional ABF can support (ultra-large dies, multi-die packages), glass-core substrate is the leading next-gen alternative. **AT&S is publicly developing glass substrates for AI + HPC + photonics** — positioning ahead of the architecture transition.
- **Geographic diversification = tariff resilience.** Current US-China-Taiwan tariff dynamics make Asia-only suppliers vulnerable; AT&S can route through 5 different countries depending on customer / tariff requirements.
- **Smaller scale = higher growth leverage.** ~8% market share with 4 production facilities ramping = if share moves to 10-12%, revenue trajectory is materially more positive than for share-stable incumbents.
- **Ajinomoto film price-pass-through tailwind** — same dynamics as peers, but AT&S has more pricing room as a smaller player gaining share.

## Bear case / risks

- **Smaller scale than incumbents.** ~8% market share means less negotiating leverage with Ajinomoto upstream + less relative volume commitment from customers. **Which means** AT&S is exposed to share-loss-during-shortage risk if AMD / others reallocate to higher-volume partners.
- **AMD customer concentration is the inverse of Ibiden's NVDA problem.** AMD is the smaller AI accelerator vendor (vs NVDA dominance). If AMD's AI share doesn't grow in 2026-2028, AT&S's revenue ramp is structurally limited. **AT&S is essentially levered to AMD share-gain at NVDA's expense** — a thesis that's plausible but not yet proven.
- **ATSAY ADR is OTC and very thinly traded.** Primary listing AT.VI on Vienna is the efficient access route for most investors but requires European broker capability.
- **Glass-core substrate is early-stage.** Multi-year qualification cycles; technical risks; commercial revenue from glass-core won't materialize until 2028-2030. **Which means** glass-core is optionality, not 2026-2027 revenue.
- **European cost base + currency translation.** USD AI revenue translates to EUR; EUR cost base. If EUR strengthens (already happened 2025-2026), margins compress. AT&S has had recurring profitability issues historically.
- **Profitability has been inconsistent.** AT&S has historically posted operating losses + cash burn during capacity build phases. **The K2 idle building + multi-country capex commitments are structural cost drags** that only pay off if capacity utilization climbs.
- **Tariff exposure on Chinese facilities.** Two Chinese facilities (Shanghai + Chongqing) face tariff regime uncertainty; mitigated by Malaysia + India + Austria capacity but not eliminated.
- **Indian Nanjangud facility execution.** New facility; ramp execution risk; Indian semiconductor supply chain is nascent.
- **Less NVDA exposure than peers.** Ibiden 80% NVDA, SEMCO Groq3 wins, AT&S primarily AMD = AT&S is the LEAST leveraged to the NVDA-specific AI cycle.

> **What this means (bear synthesis):** AT&S is genuinely the only Western ABF substrate pure-play but with the trade-off of smaller scale + AMD-primary customer mix + historical profitability inconsistency. The bull case requires AMD AI share to grow + AT&S capacity ramps to execute + glass-substrate future optionality to materialize. Any of these slipping reduces the differentiation vs incumbents.
