# Mobileye (MBLY)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium-high · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis

The most credible **two-pillar physical-AI platform** in public markets — Mobileye's core AV business is at scale (Q1 2026 revenue **$558M, +27% YoY**, full-year guide raised to **$1.94-2.02B**) AND the **$900M Mentee Robotics acquisition (Jan 6, 2026)** instantly inserts MBLY into the humanoid platform race under Amnon Shashua, one of the most credentialed founder-CEOs in computer vision. Treat MBLY as the cleanest *public* humanoid-platform exposure that isn't [[TSLA]] — with the difference being that MBLY's humanoid optionality sits on top of a real revenue base, a $250M buyback, and Tier-1 OEM relationships across [[VW]]/Porsche/MOIA, Mahindra, Audi, BMW, and Ford.

> **What this means:** Most retail-pitched "humanoid stocks" are pre-revenue private (Figure, 1X, Apptronik) or are sensor/component plays ([[OUST]], [[MP]]). MBLY is different: it is a profitable-trajectory AV business that just bought a third-generation vertically-integrated humanoid robot company for $900M. If 2028 commercial humanoid deployment hits, MBLY is one of two or three public stocks that capture the platform-economics layer, not just the component layer. The catch is that Intel still owns ~88%, which creates a perpetual supply overhang.

## Role in the AI / robotics stack

- **Autonomous driving compute** — EyeQ chips (currently EyeQ6 + EyeQ Ultra) power L2+/L2++/L3/L4 across 50+ OEMs; the system-on-chip is fabbed at [[TSM]] and architecturally distinct from [[NVDA]] DRIVE Hyperion
- **SuperVision** — eyes-off urban driving platform launching with Porsche Macan EV (pre-series production confirmed Q1 2026 at VW Hanover)
- **DRIVE robotaxi** — L4 platform powering VW/MOIA fleet; Orlando launch with Beep (driverless); LA on-road validation with Uber
- **Mentee Robotics (acquired $900M Jan 2026)** — Israeli humanoid OEM; third-generation vertically integrated humanoid robot; founded by Amnon Shashua, Lior Wolf (ex-FAIR director), Shai Shalev-Shwartz; first commercial deployment targeted **2028 at fulfillment centers / automotive suppliers**, **2030 for home use**
- Adjacent to [[NVDA]] (competing AV compute), [[ARBE]] (radar complement), [[OUST]] (lidar complement), [[SONY]] (image sensor likely inside MBLY designs)

## Bull case

- **Mentee Robotics deal is genuinely strategic, not tourist M&A.** Shashua founded both Mobileye and Mentee — he is *literally* the founder-CEO of two AI platforms in two different physical-AI verticals. The Mentee team (Wolf ex-Facebook AI Research director; Shalev-Shwartz, ML researcher) is the kind of talent density usually only available privately. **Which means** MBLY is the rare case where a tier-1 humanoid platform IP comes inside a public AV business at a defensible price.
- **AV business is finally scaling.** Q1 2026 +27% YoY revenue + raised full-year guide is the first quarter in two years where the underlying ADAS/SuperVision execution caught up to the bull narrative. Mahindra design wins + Porsche SuperVision pre-series + Ford EyeQ wins suggest a clean break from the 2024-2025 inventory destock that crushed the stock.
- **Robotaxi platform optionality.** VW/MOIA partnership is the closest thing to a Waymo-equivalent at scale outside of US tech ecosystem. Orlando driverless launch + LA Uber validation establishes a US market footprint. Goldman Sachs has explicitly flagged robotaxi as the upside vector.
- **$250M buyback** signals management confidence post-Mentee deal. Buyback funded from existing + future cash flows, designed to offset stock comp + Mentee dilution.
- **Israel-tier engineering moat.** Mentee + Mobileye both Israeli-headquartered, both Shashua-pedigree. Israeli AI/CV talent depth is structurally a top-3 global hub (NVDA Mellanox legacy, [[ARBE]] founder pool, AI21 Labs).
- **Vision-only AV viability.** If Tesla's vision-only thesis is proven correct at scale (FSD v13+ + Optimus), Mobileye's REM/SuperVision crowdsourced-mapping + vision-first stack is similarly positioned — and MBLY has 50+ OEMs vs Tesla's 1.

## Bear case / risks

- **Intel ownership overhang is permanent until it isn't.** [[INTC]] owns ~88% of MBLY post-IPO. Every quarter Intel could choose to monetize via secondary offering. **Which means** the stock has a structural ceiling until the ownership cap-table normalizes — likely a 2-3yr unwind, not weeks.
- **Q1 GAAP loss was -$3.8B**, largely on non-cash charges tied to the Mentee deal (purchase accounting, goodwill, share comp). This is well within Israeli M&A norms but optically ugly for retail.
- **2028 humanoid commercial deployment is a target, not a contract.** No publicly-named OEM/customer commits to Mentee robots in 2028. Compare to [[TSLA]] Optimus (Tesla itself is the launch customer for ~10K units 2026), Apptronik (Mercedes-Benz, Google, AT&T pilot), Figure AI (BMW X3 production line, 240/month April 2026). Mentee is *behind* these on customer validation despite the $900M sticker.
- **$900M for a pre-revenue humanoid startup is expensive even by 2026 standards.** Comparable: Apptronik raised at $5B in Feb 2026 — but that's a Series F private valuation, not an acquisition price. MBLY effectively paid public-style multiple for private-stage product. Goodwill writedown risk if 2028 deployment slips.
- **AV competition from NVDA DRIVE Hyperion.** NVIDIA's full-stack offering ([[OUST]] Rev8 just qualified) is winning end-to-end at the high end. MBLY's "EyeQ-only" pitch works for cost-sensitive L2+/L3 — but if NVDA dominates premium L4 robotaxi, MBLY's robotaxi TAM compresses.
- **Tesla Optimus + Figure + Apptronik are 1-2 years ahead** on factory-floor demonstration. Mentee will need to leapfrog generationally to be competitive by 2028. Possible but not de-risked.
- **Currency / Israeli geopolitical risk** — substantially all R&D in Israel; ongoing regional security situation is a non-zero tail.

> **What this means (bear synthesis):** MBLY's risk-reward turns on three things in 2026-2027: (1) Intel ownership unwind path, (2) Mentee Robotics deployment milestones (first customer pilot disclosure), (3) AV margins as VW/MOIA robotaxi ramps. If any two of these go sideways, the stock could give back 30-40% even with the raised guide intact. The thesis is bull/medium-high conviction *because* the underlying business is real — but the humanoid leg of the story is 24-36 months from product validation.

## Key questions / what to watch

1. **Intel ownership** — secondary offering timing + size; any change to lockup terms
2. **Mentee deployment milestones** — first named customer; pilot disclosure timing; integration with MBLY's Tier-1 OEM relationships
3. **VW/MOIA robotaxi** — Orlando launch passenger numbers; LA Uber on-road validation results; revenue contribution timeline
4. **Porsche SuperVision** — Macan EV production volumes; consumer feedback on eyes-off urban driving
5. **AV ASP trends** — EyeQ Ultra vs EyeQ6 mix shift; competitive pressure from NVDA DRIVE
6. **REM crowdsourced mapping** — fleet size and competitive moat versus Waymo / Google street-level data
7. **Goodwill review** — Mentee acquisition purchase accounting tested in 12-18 months
8. **Operating margin trajectory** — incremental margin on +$200M of guidance raise
