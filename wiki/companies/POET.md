## Bear case (the wiki's view)

1. **Trades on narrative, not earnings.** Partnership announcements (Marvell, Lumilens, others) drive 30-100%+ swings without underlying commercial inflection. Production ramp is consistently 12-18 months out.
2. **Massive dilution structurally.** May 15 $400M raise = 38M potential new shares (19M direct + 19M warrants). On a company with prior share count ~95-100M, this is **~30-40% dilution.**
3. **Dilute-into-narrative pattern (NEW 2026-05-15).** Two-day sequence: (a) May 14 Lumilens partnership announcement + 22.9M-share warrant grant to Lumilens at $8.25 pumped stock +38% to $20+; (b) May 15 POET sells $400M of stock + warrants at $21 to an undisclosed "single institutional investor." **The Lumilens deal created the pop; the financing monetized it.** This is the canonical small-cap-photonics playbook the wiki has been documenting.
4. **Undisclosed buyer.** "Single institutional investor" not named. Strategic partners are usually publicized. Anonymous = often structured finance vehicle / opportunistic hedge fund.
5. **Generic use of proceeds.** "Manufacturing, corporate development, acquisitions, R&D, light source, operations, working capital." Translation: no specific high-leverage use case identified for $400M.
6. **CFO retirement timing.** After 10 years AND right before a $400M dilutive close = classic exit-before-issues pattern. Whether deliberate or coincidental, the signal contributes to the bearish read.
7. **Q1 missed while diluting.** Adjusted loss $0.08 vs $0.05 expected = wider than consensus by $0.03. **Missing operational milestones AND raising capital is not a confident posture.**
8. **Asymmetric downside grows with the stock.** At $8 with $8.20 PT = roughly fair. At $18 with $8.20 PT = 2.2x consensus → if the stock returns to consensus, that's a 55% downside. **Asymmetric downside is biggest at peak narrative, not smallest.**

## Bull case (steelmanned)

- **Real technology platform.** POET Optical Interposer™ is patented; the wafer-level integration concept addresses real bandwidth-density challenges in AI clusters.
- **Lumilens partnership is real** ($50M PO + warrant for $189M strike-value if exercised). If Lumilens is a substantive customer (not yet verified), the $500M cumulative-purchase-over-5-years framing implies real demand.
- **$930M+ cash now (post-financing).** Materially more capital than at any point in company history. If management executes, runway is long.
- **AI-photonics is a real category.** [[COHR]] / [[LITE]] / [[GLW]] are wiki-tracked AI-photonics winners with hard revenue. POET could in principle convert technology into commercial traction.

**Why we don't buy the bull case:** Production ramp 2027 = at least 12-18 months of zero revenue impact from the Lumilens deal, during which the stock will trade on narrative. The dilution + warrant structure + undisclosed buyer + CFO exit + missed Q1 collectively signal management is monetizing the bubble, not capitalizing on a structural inflection.

## Position sizing guidance

For anyone considering a position:

- **Honest framing:** This is a speculation, not an investment. Position sizing must reflect that the stock could reasonably retrace to the $8 consensus PT zone (~55% downside) on any of: capacity wall, partnership cancellation, dilution overhang, broader AI-bubble pop.
- **Maximum position size:** **1-2% of net worth for risk-tolerant speculators.** Anything above 5% is concentration risk inconsistent with the speculative nature of the position.
- **Don't average down on dilution drops.** A pullback after a dilutive financing is not a discount — it's the market pricing in the new share count. Adding into structural weakness compounds risk.
- **Exit triggers if holding:**
  - Production ramp slips beyond 2027
  - Customer concentration (one large customer >50% of revenue)
  - Going-concern language in 10-K (unlikely with $930M cash but watch)
  - Any of the bull catalysts (named customer wins, revenue ramp) failing to materialize on schedule

## Key questions / what to watch

1. **Who is the May 15 anonymous institutional investor?** This will surface eventually in 13F filings.
2. **What is Lumilens' actual financial capacity?** The $500M cumulative purchase commitment is only meaningful if Lumilens can fund it.
3. **Production timeline slips.** Any push from 2027 to 2028 = bearish thesis confirmation.
4. **CFO successor profile.** Internal promotion vs external CFO with specific turnaround / pre-IPO experience tells you what the board thinks the next phase is.
5. **Cash burn rate.** $930M cash sounds like a lot but burn rate accelerates with manufacturing scale. Watch quarterly OpEx and capex.
