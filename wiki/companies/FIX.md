# Comfort Systems USA (FIX)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
The pure-play **mechanical / HVAC contractor** for AI data center construction — **45% of 2025 revenue was data centers**, Q1 2026 backlog hit a **record $12.46B (+81% YoY)**, and mechanical segment gross margins expanded to **26.9%** (from 21.7%) on AI-driven pricing power. The single cleanest "labor bottleneck" play in the wiki.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Mechanical / HVAC / liquid cooling installation** inside the data center hall
- Closest to the rack itself — installs the cooling systems that keep [[NVDA]] Blackwell + Vera Rubin chips running
- Captures pricing power as liquid-cooled rack densities (megawatts per rack) make cooling more complex
- Customers include the major hyperscalers + neocloud operators

## Bull case
- **Backlog up 81% YoY** to record $12.46B — multi-year visibility
- **Margin expansion** as liquid cooling adds complexity and pricing power
- **Pristine balance sheet** — net cash position; flexible for tuck-in M&A or dividends
- **Pure-play AI infrastructure construction** at scale
- **Labor as a moat** — recruiting and retaining 24,000+ skilled trade workers across a national footprint is hard to replicate
- **Service / maintenance recurring revenue** layered on top of installation
- **Per [[bottleneck-roadmap]]: 2030+ binding constraint is labor + modularization** — FIX captures both labor pricing AND modularization integration revenue

## Bear case / risks
- **+47% YTD already** — much of the AI thesis priced in
- **Cyclical at the core** — when AI capex pauses, bookings drop fast even if backlog stays large
- **Labor inflation cuts both ways** — wage pressure could compress margin if customer escalators lag
- **Hyperscaler self-perform** — Google / Amazon have in-housed certain construction; not zero risk
- **Geographic concentration** in specific hyperscaler campuses (Virginia, Texas, Phoenix)
- **Modularization** could shift revenue toward factory assemblers in Asia long-term
- **Mid-cap valuation** — premium multiple now demands strong execution to maintain

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Quarterly bookings rate** — direction matters more than absolute backlog
2. **Mechanical segment margin** — does 26.9% hold or grind higher?
3. **Data center % of revenue** — direction; how high before concentration risk?
4. **Hyperscaler capex commentary** — direct read-through
5. **Operating cash flow** — backlog quality vs. accounts receivable
6. **M&A appetite** — net-cash balance sheet enables consolidation
