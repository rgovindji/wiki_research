# SanDisk (SNDK)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** **high** (raised 2026-05-11 on HBF product + data center traction; valuation caveat applies — high YTD beta) · **Time horizon:** medium-term tactical

## One-line thesis
Pure-play NAND flash storage, recently spun out from [[WDC|Western Digital]] (Feb 2025), riding the AI "data exhaust" trade — AI workloads generate enormous flash storage demand, and supply is constrained. **Conviction upgraded May 2026** on (1) **HBF (High-Bandwidth Flash) product development** — vertically stacked NAND with **8-16x the capacity of HBM** in the same footprint at lower cost; first samples 2H 2026 — and (2) Q3 datacenter revenue **$1.47B** validating the AI-storage thesis.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **NAND flash for AI training data and inference caches** — every AI server needs SSDs
- Indirect beneficiary, not contracted to NVDA, but rides the same hyperscaler capex wave
- Storage tier of the AI infrastructure stack alongside [[MU]] (HBM/DRAM) and [[WDC]] (HDDs)

## Bull case
- **Stock up ~528% YTD in 2026** — extreme momentum on AI memory supercycle
- **NAND market revenue forecast $174.1B in 2026** (IDC)
- **Limited NAND capacity expansion** projected through 2028 → tight supply, higher pricing
- **2026 EPS estimates: $110** on revenue ~$29.3B; **2027: $125 EPS, $34B revenue**
- **Pure-play exposure** — unlike Samsung/SK Hynix/Micron which mix NAND with DRAM

## Bear case / risks
- **NAND rises *less* than DRAM (NEW NUANCE).** Per [[2026-05-09-dwarkesh-dylan-semianalysis]]: as smartphone volumes collapse (1.4B → ~500-600M by 2027), the destruction *releases more NAND than DRAM* back to AI demand. So NAND prices go up, but **the price increases for DRAM will be larger than those of NAND.** Net: SNDK still benefits from the AI memory supercycle, but the *pure-play NAND* story is structurally less leveraged than [[MU]]'s HBM-attached DRAM story.
- **YTD move is enormous.** 528% leaves no room for execution misses; mean reversion risk is acute
- **NAND has historically been MORE cyclical than DRAM** — peak-to-trough swings are violent
- **No HBM exposure** — the highest-margin memory product is DRAM/HBM, not NAND
- **Recently spun out** — limited public history; less analyst visibility on stand-alone economics
- **Concentrated customers** — hyperscaler buying patterns drive a large share of revenue

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **NAND pricing trend** quarter-over-quarter
2. **Hyperscaler CapEx commentary** — storage allocations specifically
3. **Capacity expansion announcements** from SNDK and competitors
4. **Operating margin expansion** — does the up-cycle translate to earnings leverage?

## Recent developments
- **2026-05-11** — SNDK **+91% in past month**. **Q3 revenue $5.95B**, **datacenter revenue $1.47B**. Most importantly: **HBF (High-Bandwidth Flash) product** — vertically stacked NAND, **8-16x HBM capacity** in the same footprint at lower cost; first samples expected 2H 2026. If HBF ships and scales, this **creates a new memory category** that could partially substitute for HBM in cost-sensitive inference workloads. **Conviction upgraded medium → high.** Caveat: stock is up 91% in 1 month; valuation no longer cheap.
- **2026-05** — Customer conversations point to NAND/storage tightness **extending into 2028** (matching SK Hynix's 2026 booking and Samsung's "shortages through 2027" comments).
