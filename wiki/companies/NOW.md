# ServiceNow (NOW)

**Stance:** neutral · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** both (12mo tactical + long-term)

## One-line thesis
The enterprise workflow platform best-positioned to *sell* agentic AI into its own installed base (system-of-action for IT/HR/customer ops) — but its consumption/seat model is exactly what the [[ai-software-disruption|SaaSpocalypse]] derate is repricing, and the stock has already lost more than half its value waiting to see which way it cuts.

## What they do
Cloud platform for digitizing enterprise workflows — IT service management (its origin and moat), plus HR, customer service, security, and a low-code app-build layer. Sells to large enterprises on subscription; sticky "system of action" that sits on top of other systems of record.

## AI angle — tailwind *and* target (net: more tailwind than most SaaS)
- **Tailwind:** ServiceNow is a natural *distribution channel* for agentic AI — its Now Assist / agentic offerings let it upsell AI into a captive installed base that already runs the workflows agents would execute. It is closer to "system of action" (where agents do work) than "system of record," which is the layer best-placed to monetize agents rather than be replaced by them.
- **Target:** the [[inference-economics|agent-eats-seats]] risk is real. If agents collapse the headcount that drives seat expansion, NOW's net-revenue-retention engine (land-and-expand on seats) weakens. NOW's pricing is migrating toward consumption/credits, which the CIO cost-backlash ([[inference-economics]]) explicitly targets ("relitigating ROI," value-maxing not token-maxing).
- Still growing **+21.7%** at $14B TTM — the fastest grower in this cluster ex-DUOL, which is the bull's main rebuttal to "SaaS is dead."

## Bull case
- Highest revenue growth in the cluster (+21.7%) at meaningful scale — not yet showing the deceleration the SaaSpocalypse thesis predicts.
- System-of-action positioning = monetize agents, don't get displaced by them.
- Fwd P/E ~22 after a -55% drawdown is the cheapest NOW has traded in years relative to its growth.

## Bear case / risks
- **Agent-eats-seats** ([[ai-software-disruption]]): the seat-expansion flywheel is the exact mechanism AI labor-substitution threatens. Salesforce already disclosed ~10% seat reduction at AI-using service customers ([[CRM]]) — a read-through risk for any seat-priced enterprise SaaS.
- CIO cost-backlash ([[inference-economics]]) pressures consumption pricing.
- -55% drawdown signals the market is pricing a real growth derate, not just multiple compression — the tape is "guilty until proven innocent" on enterprise SaaS.
- Laffont (AI long) is *bearish SaaS* — "AI models are digital 3D printers" ([[ai-bubble-debate]]).

## Key questions / what to watch
- Does NRR hold, or does seat growth roll over as agentic deployments scale at customers?
- Agentic-AI ARR disclosure: is Now Assist a real, growing line or <2%-of-rev (the ADBE pattern, where AI ARR was real but immaterial — [[ai-bubble-debate]] June 11)?
- First sign of consumption-pricing pushback from CIOs.

## Recent developments
- {2026-06-20} — Trading $95.04, -55% off 52-wk high; fwd P/E ~22, still growing +21.7%. The SaaS derate is in the price; growth has not yet broken.

## Where it sits in the SaaSpocalypse debate
**Tailwind-leaning, but the most "show-me" name of the enterprise three.** NOW is the cluster's best *agent-distribution* story (system of action) and its fastest grower, but it carries full seat-model exposure. The -55% drawdown means the market has already assigned heavy disruption odds. See [[ai-software-disruption]], [[inference-economics]], [[ai-bubble-debate]].

## VERDICT
**Neutral / medium conviction / WATCHLIST.** Best-positioned of the SaaS names to monetize agents, cheapest it's been, still growing 20%+ — but the seat-model overhang and the unproven agentic-ARR ramp keep it off core-add until NRR durability is demonstrated. The cleanest "survives AI-eats-software" candidate in the enterprise trio, but survival isn't yet proven in the numbers.
