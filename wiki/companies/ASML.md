# ASML Holding (ASML)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high (multi-year), medium (near-term) · **Time horizon:** long-term · **Listing:** Amsterdam (AMS: ASML) and ADR (NASDAQ: ASML)

## One-line thesis
Monopoly supplier of EUV lithography — and per the May 2026 [[2026-05-09-dwarkesh-dylan-semianalysis]] analysis, **ASML is THE single binding constraint on the entire AI buildout by 2028-2030**. No advanced semiconductor exists without ASML; tool output (~70/yr now → ~100/yr by 2030) physically caps AI compute scaling. **Conviction upgraded from medium to high on multi-year horizon.** Near-term still has the 2026 China-pull-forward digestion.

## Bull case
- **EUV monopoly.** No competitor exists for the machines required to produce 3nm and below.
- **The 2028-2030 binding constraint of all AI compute.** Per Dylan Patel/SemiAnalysis: 3.5 EUV tools = 1 GW of AI capacity. Cumulative ~700 tools by EOD = ~200 GW/yr theoretical AI capacity.
- **High-NA EUV** is the next-gen platform; ASML's roadmap stays ahead.
- **Long-term TAM expansion** with every advanced AI chip generation.
- **Service revenue** is a growing high-margin annuity from the installed base.
- **Direct beneficiary of [[TSM|TSMC]]'s $52-56B capex** and similar investments by Samsung, Intel.
- **Tools are physically supply-constrained, not commercially.** Carl Zeiss (lenses, ~18 multilayer mirrors per tool) and Cymer (San Diego, EUV source) can't ramp faster — supply chain is 10,000+ people of specialized labor.

## Bear case / risks
- **2026 lumpiness.** China pull-forward demand in 2025 (ahead of export controls) leaves a 2026 air pocket — quarterly numbers will be uneven.
- **The "generous monopolist" problem.** Per Dylan: "ASML has never raised the price more than they've increased the capability of the tool ... they've always provided net benefit to their customers." This is *atypical* monopoly behavior. **The biggest unknown of the 2028-2030 thesis is whether ASML finally takes pricing power commensurate with its position** — or whether shareholders capture the bottleneck rents at all. If they don't, the upside is capped by tool ASPs.
- **Export controls.** US restrictions on selling to China cap a meaningful addressable market.
- **🔥 MATCH Act passage risk (NEW 2026-05-17 per [[2026-05-17-semiwiki-forum-sweep-3]])** — pending US legislation (House + Senate, introduced April 2026; House Foreign Affairs committee advanced April 22) specifically targets ASML, Tokyo Electron, and US tools makers. Mechanism: coerces allied countries (Netherlands, Japan) to limit equipment exports to China; **requires licenses to service equipment**. **Which means**: even if ASML sells equipment to China, the high-margin recurring service revenue could be license-gated. China responded with April 13 "Malicious Entity List" decree creating retaliation mechanism; MOFCOM summoned US embassy diplomats in China; US chip industry reps summoned to Chinese embassy in Washington. Trump admin has NOT publicly taken position. **Bipartisan committee advance + China's preemptive countermeasures** both treat passage as plausible. ASML China revenue historically ~40% of sales; service revenue is the highest-margin segment. **MATCH Act passage = material structural headwind not currently in the bull thesis.**
- **Customer concentration** — TSMC, Samsung, Intel are 3 customers at most for leading-edge tools.
- **Equipment cyclicality.** Capital equipment is famously cyclical; orders can be delayed or pulled in.
- **Long sales cycles + delivery times** mean revenue can de-couple from order book in either direction.
- **3D DRAM (late decade)** could reshape memory-side EUV demand — fewer EUV passes per bit if memory architecture pivots.
- **Snake-eats-tail Taiwan risk** — EUV tools themselves use Taiwan-made chips; a Taiwan disruption doesn't just hit TSMC, it hits ASML's own supply chain.

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Order book replenishment** post the China pull-forward
2. **Tool output trajectory** — does ASML push past 80/yr to 100+ by 2030?
3. **Pricing discipline** — does ASML *finally* take price commensurate with monopoly position? **The single highest-leverage variable for the multi-year thesis.**
4. **High-NA EUV adoption** by TSMC / Samsung / Intel
5. **Service revenue mix** — is the recurring base growing?
6. **Export-control updates** — any US policy moves
7. **TSMC capex confirmation** — read-through to ASML order book
8. **Carl Zeiss + Cymer ramp signals** — supply-chain bottleneck of the bottleneck
