# nVent Electric (NVT)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium-high · **Time horizon:** 2026-2030 liquid-cooling supercycle

## One-line thesis

**The cleanest liquid-cooling pure-play in public markets at the moment when the supercycle begins.** Q1 2026 revenue **$1.242B (+53% YoY, +34% organic)** powered by "very strong data center sales." Backlog **$2.3B (3x prior year)** built primarily from large hyperscale liquid-cooling orders. Infrastructure is now 45% of sales, targeting >50% by FY26. **Liquid-cooling penetration in data centers is still under 10%** — the runway is structurally long. Market projected $6B 2026 → $27.1B 2035 at 18.2% CAGR. nVent has deployed over **1 GW of liquid-cooling capacity** with more than a decade of experience, ahead of [[VRT]] (which is broader-portfolio) on the cooling-specific specialization. **FY26 guide: 26-28% sales growth, $4.45-4.55 adj EPS.** The catch is that this is a thesis everyone now knows; the valuation already reflects much of the multi-year story.

> **What this means:** Every Blackwell/Rubin rack-scale system in 2026-2028 increasingly requires direct-to-chip liquid cooling because air-cooling caps at roughly 60kW per rack. The standard NVDA NVL72 rack is 120kW. **Air cooling is physically insufficient for next-gen AI hardware** — this isn't a preference, it's a constraint. nVent is at the front of the line as that conversion happens.

## Role in the AI / data center infrastructure stack

- **Liquid-cooling hardware**: direct-to-chip cold plates, manifolds, CDUs (coolant distribution units), heat exchangers, cooling distribution infrastructure
- **Infrastructure segment**: enclosures, racks, power distribution, thermal management — now 45% of total nVent revenue
- **Electrical connection products** (legacy business): fastening, grounding, bonding for electrical infrastructure
- **Acquired Trachte 2024** + multiple bolt-on acquisitions in liquid cooling
- Adjacent to [[VRT]] (Vertiv — broader cooling + power; bigger), [[ETN]] (Eaton — power management focused), [[MOD]] (Modine — emerging data center cooling pure-play post-spin), [[STRL]] (Sterling — campus construction)
- Customers: hyperscalers + colocation operators + data center developers building rack-scale AI infrastructure

## Bull case

- **Liquid-cooling is now a physical requirement, not a preference.** NVDA NVL72 racks are 120kW; air-cooling tops out near 60kW per rack. **Which means** every NVDA rack-scale AI deployment from 2026 forward requires direct-to-chip liquid cooling. Hyperscalers can't opt out — the math forces conversion.
- **<10% market penetration today.** Even doubling penetration to 20% by 2028 represents enormous runway. nVent is positioned as one of two-three primary suppliers (with VRT, plus emerging MOD post-spin).
- **53% YoY revenue with 3x backlog** is among the strongest growth profiles in industrial AI infrastructure. The growth isn't degrading — it's accelerating against an expanding TAM.
- **Decade-plus liquid-cooling experience is moat-grade.** Liquid cooling is operationally complex (leak detection, redundancy, fluid management, mixed-environment integration). nVent's incumbent expertise gives qualification advantages over newer entrants.
- **>1 GW of deployed cooling capacity** is a credibility marker hyperscalers value. New entrants have to build trust through pilots; nVent already has production references at scale.
- **Infrastructure segment growing toward 50%+ of revenue** — the mix shift from legacy electrical connection products to high-growth infrastructure compounds operating leverage.
- **FY26 guide raises** are coming on top of already-elevated expectations — the operational momentum is sustained.
- **Acquisitions are expanding the cooling portfolio.** Trachte (2024) + others fill product gaps; cross-sell opportunity to existing customer base.

## Bear case / risks

- **Valuation already reflects much of the story.** Stock has had a substantial run; multiple expansion from here requires continued operational beats. **Which means** at current levels you're not buying optionality — you're paying for the bull thesis.
- **Customer concentration risk.** Top-5 hyperscaler customers likely 50%+ of data center revenue. If AI capex digests (the Damodaran scenario) or if a major hyperscaler defects to a competing cooling vendor, revenue impact is direct.
- **VRT competitive response.** Vertiv has scale advantages, deeper enterprise relationships, and is actively investing in liquid cooling. Could compress nVent's pricing power over multi-year.
- **MOD's emerging pure-play post-spin** creates a new specialized competitor with strategic clarity (no Performance Technologies drag).
- **Liquid cooling commoditization risk.** As designs standardize (open compute, MGX), differentiation could shrink and margins compress.
- **Backlog conversion risk.** $2.3B backlog needs to convert at expected margins; execution at scale is non-trivial for hardware-heavy delivery.
- **AI capex digestion is the macro risk.** If hyperscaler 2027 capex slows, the multi-year liquid-cooling growth curve gets pushed right — same AI-capex-correlated drawdown vulnerability as VRT/ETN/MOD/STRL.
- **Recurring revenue % is lower than software comps.** Hardware-heavy mix means revenue is project-driven, not subscription-driven.

> **What this means (bear synthesis):** The bull thesis is right but no longer secret. The stock has already absorbed most of the 2026 narrative; what's left to capture is execution + continued backlog growth + a sustained 18%+ CAGR through 2030. Position sizing should reflect the diminished asymmetry vs 12-18 months ago.
