# Alibaba Group (BABA)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** both (12mo AI-cloud re-rating + long-term China AI champion)

> **Why a page now (2026-06-20):** Created during the Cluster E fantasy-draft evaluation. BABA is the **single clearest accessible China AI/cloud play** — Qwen is the #1 enterprise LLM in China and Aliyun (Alibaba Cloud) is the #1 China AI-cloud at ~36% share, with a **$55.4B FY26-FY28 AI capex commitment**. This is the China analog to the US hyperscaler-capex thesis ([[ai-capex-cycle]], [[cloud-hyperscalers]]) and genuinely on-mission for an AI/tech book.

## One-line thesis
BABA is a **two-engine bet**: a slow/mature China e-commerce cash machine funding an **accelerating AI-cloud + LLM (Qwen) franchise** that is the domestic leader in the world's second-largest AI market. **Which means** investors get China-AI exposure at a single-digit-after-cash earnings multiple — but only if Beijing, margins, and ADR-delisting risk cooperate.

## Bull case
- **China AI champion with two leading assets**: Qwen ranked #1 at ~32% share of enterprise large-model invocation in China 2H2025; Alibaba Cloud #1 at ~35.8% of China AI-cloud. **Which means** BABA captures both the model layer and the infrastructure layer of China AI — a position no other single US-listed China name holds.
- **$55.4B FY26-FY28 capex** signals genuine hyperscaler-scale commitment; Jefferies expects Aliyun to capture the **majority of incremental China AI-cloud revenue** in 2026. Mirrors the [[ai-capex-cycle]] dynamic that has driven US hyperscalers.
- **Cloud reacceleration**: AI-cloud at ~$5B run-rate and growing; the slow-growth e-commerce optics mask a fast-growing, strategically critical cloud segment.
- **Cheap optionality**: ~16x trailing / ~16x forward with a large net-cash position and listed-equity stakes; the AI/cloud business is arguably under-valued inside the conglomerate.
- **Sovereign-AI tailwind** ([[sovereign-ai]]): China's domestic-AI push structurally favors domestic providers over US hyperscalers inside China.

## Bear case / risks
- **AI push is crushing near-term profit**: FY26 Q3 non-GAAP net profit fell ~67%, adjusted EBITA -57%, and FCF plunged ~71% to RMB1.1B as capex outpaces revenue absorption. **Which means** the bull case requires patience while reported earnings deteriorate — a classic "spend now, earn later" leap of faith.
- **Top-line is barely growing** (+2.7% TTM): the consolidated growth profile is unattractive vs US hyperscalers; the AI story has to carry the whole stock.
- **ADR / delisting tail risk** (HFCAA / PCAOB): all China ADRs carry structural delisting risk; consider H-share / Stock-Connect access.
- **Beijing / regulatory risk**: data, antitrust, and strategic-industry intervention remain live; AI is a national-priority sector that cuts both ways.
- **Competitive intensity**: ByteDance/Doubao, Tencent, Baidu, DeepSeek all contest the China model + cloud markets; share is not guaranteed.
- **Currency / geopolitics**: RMB exposure for a USD ADR; US-China decoupling and chip export controls constrain access to leading-edge compute ([[semiconductors]]).
- **Chip-access constraint**: US export controls limit H100/B-class GPU access; reliance on domestic accelerators may cap training competitiveness.

## Key questions / what to watch
- **Aliyun revenue growth + AI-cloud run-rate** each quarter — is the $5B run-rate compounding fast enough to justify $55.4B capex?
- **When does the capex curve inflect to FCF recovery** — FY27? The whole re-rating depends on it.
- **Qwen monetization** — does model leadership convert to cloud-revenue capture, or stay a loss-leader land-grab?
- **Domestic accelerator supply** — can BABA secure enough non-NVIDIA / domestic compute to train at frontier scale under export controls?
- **Capital returns** — does management keep buying back stock while spending on AI?
