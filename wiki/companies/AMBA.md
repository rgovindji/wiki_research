# Ambarella (AMBA)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis

Best-quality edge AI vision SoC business of the small-cap robotics screen — returned to profitability in FY2026 with Edge AI now 80% of revenue, but the "AI vision brains for humanoids" framing is somewhat aspirational; most revenue is still automotive ADAS and IoT security, and FY27 guide is 10–15% growth, not robotics hypergrowth.

## Bull case

- **Returned to profitability in FY2026.** Non-GAAP net profit $26.9M vs. -$6.8M FY2025 — meaningful inflection.
- **Edge AI is 80% of revenue and grew ~50% YoY.** The pivot from legacy video compression to edge AI inference is structurally complete.
- **CV7 5nm chips ramping** with revenue contribution starting Q4 FY2026. CV3-AD family for L2+ autonomy expected revenue in calendar 2026.
- **9 Buy / 5 Hold / 0 Sell** across 14 Wall Street analysts. Median PT $100, high $115 (Rosenblatt, +57.9% vs. ~$72).
- **Strategic optionality.** Company explored a sale and engaged with potential buyers (rivals + PE) in June 2025. Embedded takeout premium.
- **Unified Edge AI platform + Developer Zone** launched at CES 2026 — expanding ecosystem reach into drones, edge infrastructure, robotics.

## Bear case / risks

- **Customer concentration.** Heavily dependent on a limited number of customers; US-China trade tensions add concentration risk.
- **FY27 revenue guide is 10–15% growth, not hypergrowth.** Disconnect between the "robotics SoC leader" narrative and the actual guide.
- **Trailing P/E -26x.** Returned to non-GAAP profitability but GAAP losses persist (-$75.87M FY2026, -21.3% margin).
- **Roth lowered PT to $65 from $85** (Neutral). BofA lowered to $85 from $92 (Neutral).
- **AMBA -16% on a single day** earlier in 2026 illustrates the volatility — narrative-driven repricing in both directions.
- **Humanoid attach is currently aspirational.** No named humanoid OEM design wins. Automotive ADAS and IoT security cameras remain the actual revenue base.
- **Competition from larger semis** in autonomous driving (Mobileye, NVIDIA Drive, Qualcomm Snapdragon Ride).
- **Slower-than-expected CV processor adoption** is a real risk per analyst commentary.

## Key questions / what to watch

- Q1 FY2027 print (May 2026) — does the FY27 10–15% guide get revised?
- CV3-AD design wins — first publicly named automotive program would re-rate the multiple.
- Any humanoid OEM design win disclosure.
- M&A activity. The 2025 sale exploration may resurface.
- China customer exposure trend — does trade tension force a customer loss?
