# Super Micro Computer (SMCI)

**Stance:** neutral · **Conviction:** low · **Time horizon:** tactical

## One-line thesis
Specialty AI server builder with deep Blackwell support and a 4U liquid-cooled HGX B300 portfolio — AI hardware tailwinds remain real, but the **lingering accounting / legal overhang** plus **March 19, 2026 DoJ indictment of cofounder + sitting board member Wally Liaw for conspiring to illegally export ≥$2.5B of Nvidia AI chips to China** keep conviction at low until federal investigation resolves.

> **What this means:** Supermicro's underlying AI server business is real — they make some of the best liquid-cooled Blackwell racks. But the company has a senior executive under federal indictment for one of the largest export-control cases ever, the stock dropped 33% on the news, and the SE Asian diversion network the DoJ is investigating could produce more indictments. The bull case requires the legal cloud to clear; the bear case is that more is yet to surface. Stay neutral; don't catch the falling knife.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **AI server OEM** — HGX B300 8-GPU 4U liquid-cooled systems; full Blackwell + Vera CPU portfolio
- **Reference designs** with NVIDIA RTX PRO Blackwell Server Edition GPUs
- Historical leader in dense GPU server racks for AI training

## Bull case
- **Deep Blackwell + Vera support** across multiple form factors
- **Liquid cooling expertise** in DLC-2 capturing 98% of heat
- **Faster product time-to-market** historically vs. Dell/HPE on bleeding-edge configurations
- **Stock has remained volatile** — leveraged exposure to AI capex cycle
- **Hyperscaler edge / RTX PRO** systems expand TAM

## Bear case / risks
- **DoJ indictment of cofounder Wally Liaw (March 19, 2026) — SHARPEST DATA POINT.** Liaw + SMCI Taiwan GM Steven Chang (fugitive) + fixer Willy Sun indicted for conspiring to illegally export ≥$2.5B in NVDA AI tech to China via SE Asian intermediaries. **Stock fell -33% on the news.** Liaw was placed on administrative leave and subsequently left the board. SMCI is not a named defendant but is under federal microscope. See [[2026-05-13-culper-nvda-short-thesis]]. **Which means:** the "accounting / legal overhang" framing the wiki has used since the 2024 Hindenburg / EY events is now sharper — it's a specific federal export-control prosecution targeting senior leadership, not just back-office accounting. Multiple additional DoJ indictments of intermediaries are plausible; any that name additional SMCI employees would re-rate the stock further.
- **Accounting / legal overhang (broader)** — Hindenburg short, EY resignation, restatement → lingering trust deficit (predates the March 2026 indictment)
- **Share shift to Dell/HPE** as enterprise customers prefer governance certainty (now sharpened by export-control case)
- **Margin compression** as AI server market becomes more competitive
- **Customer concentration** — small number of large buyers
- **Inventory + receivables risk** during fast hardware transitions

> **What this means (bear case synthesis):** SMCI now has the worst risk-reward profile in the AI server OEM cohort. Dell is winning share with first-mover Vera Rubin / GB300 (+67% YTD). HPE is participating with new product launches (+20% YTD). SMCI is -7% YTD, has a cofounder under federal indictment, and faces ongoing DoJ investigation of the SE Asian diversion network. The legal cloud has to clear before any multiple expansion is possible — and "clear" likely means more indictments before resolution, not fewer.
