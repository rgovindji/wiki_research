# Keel Infrastructure (KEEL)

**Stance:** neutral (speculative) · **Conviction:** low · **Time horizon:** event-driven (lease signatures) · **Verdict:** a real powered-land asset and a real lease-signature catalyst — but the circulating "10x to $30B" math is several optimistic assumptions stacked. Size as a speculative catalyst ticket, not a 10x you underwrite.

## One-line thesis
KEEL controls a multi-year ~2.2 GW power/data-center development pipeline (PA / WA / Quebec) into the AI power bottleneck; the stock is a **bet on signing hyperscaler leases** that convert speculative megawatts into contracted cash flow. The asset is real and the first signed lease is a genuine re-rating catalyst — but it has already run ~9x, trades ~17x sales, and just raised $458M of convertible debt to *fund a capital-intensive buildout*, which contradicts the "capital-light, just-sign-leases" pitch.

## What they do
Develops "power-rich" data-center campuses — securing megawatts and grid interconnection, then moving sites through permitting toward leasing to AI/HPC tenants. The pitch is that **secured, interconnected power is the scarce asset** in the AI buildout (interconnection queues run years), so KEEL's value is turning controlled MW into signed, long-term leases.

## Bull case (the circulating "10x" thesis, represented fairly)
- **Power is the AI bottleneck; KEEL controls 2.2 GW with interconnections** — a genuinely scarce, hard-to-replicate asset right now.
- **Near-term leases (Panther Creek 350MW + Sharon 110MW + Moses Lake 18MW ≈ 478MW)** at colocation rates (~$150-200/kW/mo) ≈ **$850M-1.1B ARR** vs a $3.8B cap.
- **Full 2.2 GW leased ≈ $4B+ ARR**; at a 6-7× sales data-center multiple ≈ **~$30B company** (~8× from today).
- **"Simple, not a tech race"** — no new product, just sign leases; CEO has named 3 signings as the 2026 priority.
- **The first hyperscaler lease re-rates the whole pipeline overnight** — comp: [[DGXX]] popped on its Cerebras lease.

## Bear case / why the 10× math breaks
- **🔴 It conflates two different businesses.** "Powered-land lessor" (tenant builds; KEEL earns a *fraction* of colo rates) vs "build-and-operate colocation" (KEEL earns the full ~$163/kW/mo — *because* the rate includes the building, cooling, power distribution, redundancy KEEL would have to **build**). The pitch takes the *revenue* of the second and the *capital-light story* of the first. You can't have both.
- **🔴 The capex hole.** Build cost is **~$11-12M/MW** (standard shells); AI-grade at GW scale is **$45-55B/GW**. So the 478MW near-term piece needs **~$5-6B of capex** to earn that $1B ARR — they raised **$458M** (~10% of it). The full 2.2 GW needs **~$25B (shells) to ~$100B+ (AI-grade)**. The $30B-company math silently assumes all of it gets built and leased at peak rates while ignoring the $25-100B of debt/dilution that would gut the equity holder's slice.
- **🔴 The $458M convert raise is the tell** — they're funding a buildout because this is **capital-intensive**, directly contradicting "they already own the power, just sign leases."
- **🟠 Multiple sleight-of-hand.** "Operators trade 6-7× sales" describes *built, contracted, cash-flowing* companies, and that multiple is on EV that *includes the debt that funded the build*. You can't apply 6-7× equity-to-sales to ARR that requires $25-100B of borrowing to exist. (And KEEL *already* trades ~17× *current* sales.)
- **🟠 Already run ~9× and at its highs** ($0.70 → $6.29). Much of "secured power is valuable" is priced; the "10x from here" follows the re-rate.
- **🟠 Stacked optimism / promotion pattern.** 100%-of-a-multi-year-speculative-pipeline × peak rates × premium multiple × ignoring capex × after a 9× run = fake precision. The cashtag-pitch structure ("the signatures are coming") fits the late-cycle retail-FOMO / narrative-re-rate pattern (cf. [[POET]] as the wiki's anti-canonical reference).
- **🟡 Comp caution:** [[DGXX]] (the "first lease re-rates it" example) is itself now **−27% off its high** after the pop — the catalyst is real but not a durable 10×.

## The realistic framing
Powered land with interconnection *is* a scarce, valuable asset, and **a signed, credible hyperscaler lease is a real catalyst that could pop the stock 50-150%.** That makes KEEL a **lease-signature catalyst trade, not a clean path to $30B.** The decisive unknowns are the **lease terms** — who funds the capex (tenant build-to-suit vs KEEL builds), the $/kW, the tenant's credit. Those determine whether KEEL captures colo-like economics or thin ground-lease economics, and they're unknowable until a lease is actually signed.

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Lease terms when signed** — build-to-suit (tenant funds capex) vs KEEL-builds? $/kW? tenant credit (true hyperscaler vs smaller operator)? *This is the entire investment.*
2. **Capital plan** — how much more debt/equity beyond the $458M converts; dilution trajectory.
3. **How much of the 2.2 GW is energized/interconnected-ready vs "potential"** and on what timeline.
4. **Panther Creek first** — the lead campus; first signature is the catalyst.
5. **Revenue mix** — what is the $218.6M TTM revenue today (legacy vs data-center)?
