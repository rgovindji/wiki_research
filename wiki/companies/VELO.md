# Velo3D (VELO)

**Stance:** neutral · **Conviction:** low · **Time horizon:** 6-18mo (speculative watch only)

## One-line thesis
A real margin-inflection turnaround in defense-grade metal 3D printing — but a near-bankruptcy survivor whose stock currently trades on war narrative and squeeze mechanics, not on its $60–70M revenue base.

## What they do
Laser Powder Bed Fusion (LPBF) metal additive manufacturing — Sapphire printers (parts up to 600mm dia / 1m height, US-assembled) for rocket engines, jet engines, fuel delivery. Historically a SpaceX supplier; emerged from near-bankruptcy via restructuring.

## Bull case
- **DLA JAMA IDIQ contract** ($9.8M / 5yr, awarded 2026-03-30): the military printing spare parts on demand is a structural demand story, and a kinetic US–Iran conflict accelerates the replenishment narrative.
- Gross-margin inflection (+970bps y/y) plus 48% revenue growth suggests the restructured cost base works.
- Balance sheet repaired: ~$9M debt after the April raise.
- Aurelia partnership extends Sapphire XC into small gas-turbine systems.

## Bear case / risks
- **The June 2026 run is narrative, not fundamentals** — see [[2026-06-11-velo-war-squeeze]]: no new 8-K since May 15; +93% in four sessions on squeeze mechanics.
- **Shelf on file + doubled stock = raise risk.** A 424B into this pop would complete the POET-style dilute-into-narrative signature. (Rational for the company; painful for chasers.)
- Sub-scale: ~$65M revenue, EBITDA-negative until (targeted) H2 2026; competition from much larger AM players and conventional machining.
- Near-bankruptcy history; customer concentration questions remain.

## Key questions / what to watch
- Does an equity raise land within days of this squeeze? (EDGAR 424B/8-K watch — the decisive near-term signal.)
- Does DLA JAMA convert from $9.8M pilot to programs of record?
- H2 2026 EBITDA-positive target — hit or pushed?
