# Sterling Infrastructure (STRL)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium-high · **Time horizon:** 2026-2030 hyperscaler campus build cycle

## One-line thesis

**The cleanest hyperscaler-campus civil construction pure-play.** Q1 2026: total revenue **+92% YoY**, E-Infrastructure segment **+174% YoY** to $597.7M, mission-critical projects (data centers + semis + manufacturing) now **>90% of E-Infrastructure backlog** (up from 84% YE 2025), pool of work approaching **$6.5B** between signed backlog + future-phase opportunities. Per management: hyperscalers are "screaming to get into these markets faster with what they see coming in capital spending." **E-Infrastructure projected to grow 80%+ in 2026.** This is the company that does the actual site work — earth moving, grading, foundation prep, underground utilities, concrete — for multi-thousand-acre hyperscaler campuses before any building goes up. The catch is post-run valuation: stock is +88.7% on the recent print, raised guidance, and the easy-money entry is past.

> **What this means:** Every hyperscaler $20B+ data center campus needs ~12-24 months of site preparation before the first server room can be poured. Sterling is the contractor doing that work at the front of the construction sequence. As hyperscalers move from single-building data centers to massive multi-thousand-acre campuses, Sterling's TAM grows faster than the data center vertical itself — they're capturing more share of dirt per dollar of compute.

## Role in the AI / data center infrastructure stack

- **E-Infrastructure Solutions** (the AI capex engine): site development, earthwork, grading, foundations, underground utilities for hyperscale data centers + semiconductor fabs + advanced manufacturing
- **Transportation Solutions**: legacy heavy civil construction (highways, bridges, airports)
- **Building Solutions**: residential + commercial concrete foundations
- **CEC acquisition** (2025): geographic + capability expansion
- Adjacent to [[FIX]] / [[EME]] / [[PWR]] (mechanical/electrical contractors — they install after Sterling preps the site), [[AGX]] (gas plant EPC — adjacent power-side construction), [[GEV]] (power-side equipment that gets installed)
- Sterling sits *upstream* of MEP contractors in the construction sequence — first to site, first to revenue

## Bull case

- **+174% E-Infrastructure YoY growth** is exceptional even by AI-infrastructure standards. The growth is real, customer-driven (hyperscalers actively pulling Sterling into new markets), and supported by signed backlog rather than narrative.
- **>90% mission-critical backlog mix** = moat-grade revenue quality. Mission-critical projects are higher-margin, lower-cancellation-risk, and require specialized qualifications. The mix shift from 84% → 90%+ in 2 quarters is a structural quality improvement.
- **$6.5B total work pool** gives multi-year visibility most construction names don't have. Civil contractors typically run 6-12 months of visibility; STRL has 2-3 years.
- **First-to-site advantage in the construction sequence.** Hyperscalers commit Sterling earliest in project timelines — meaning Sterling captures cash flow + revenue ahead of MEP contractors (FIX, EME, PWR). **Cash flow comes first in the AI capex curve.**
- **Hyperscaler campus shift = TAM expansion.** Data centers are moving from single buildings to multi-thousand-acre campuses (Stargate, Trump-Saudi data center, Texas mega-campuses). Per acre, site prep costs more for campus-scale projects than single buildings — Sterling's revenue per project compounds.
- **Geographic expansion via CEC acquisition** + organic — Sterling is growing into Texas, Arizona, Southeast where hyperscaler campus development is concentrated.
- **Raised 2026 guidance** validates execution on the growth path.
- **Management commentary on hyperscaler urgency** — "screaming to get into these markets faster" — is the kind of qualitative signal that often precedes upward revisions.

## Bear case / risks

- **The stock has already run +88.7%** on the Q1 print + raised guide. The asymmetric setup that existed pre-Q1 is largely consumed. From here, returns require continued execution at high pace + sustained AI capex environment.
- **Construction is cyclical and margin-volatile.** Weather, labor, materials cost, permitting all create unpredictable margin variance quarter-to-quarter. The +174% growth came with above-baseline margins that may not sustain.
- **Customer concentration in 4-5 hyperscalers.** If any major hyperscaler delays or cancels a campus (Microsoft Stargate scope changes, Meta capex digestion), Sterling's pipeline visibility compresses fast.
- **Acquisition execution risk.** CEC integration + future M&A introduce execution risk that pure-play organic growth doesn't have.
- **AI capex digestion risk.** Same Damodaran-scenario vulnerability as VRT/ETN/MOD/NVT — if hyperscalers blink on 2027 capex, Sterling's E-Infra backlog growth flattens.
- **Labor constraints.** Civil construction labor (heavy equipment operators, concrete workers, foreman) is supply-constrained in the hyperscaler markets. Wage inflation could compress margins faster than revenue grows.
- **Site availability constraints.** Hyperscaler campus development requires permitted, transmissible-power-served, water-served sites. The site bottleneck is increasingly the constraint upstream of Sterling's services.
- **Multiple compression risk on a stretched valuation.** At elevated multiples, any softness in growth rate triggers disproportionate stock-price reaction.

> **What this means (bear synthesis):** Sterling is a high-quality business at the right place in the AI capex curve, but the easy money has been made. The risk-reward from here is more about whether the +88.7% post-Q1 run already discounts 2026-2027 growth — if so, returns depend on execution and AI capex sustaining beyond what's priced in.
