# Howmet Aerospace (HWM)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term compounder with hypersonic upside

> **Why a page now (2026-05-17 PM):** Howmet is **the cleanest exposure to (a) commercial engine spares supercycle, (b) hypersonic structural materials, and (c) 787 ramp**. Q1 2026 commercial engine spares **+48% YoY**; titanium owns 78.4% global aerospace share (with ATI/VSMPO/TIMET/Kobe); +19% Q1 revenue with 32% EBITDA margin. **$1.8B Consolidated Aerospace acquisition closing H1 2026** signals capex confidence. Surfaced via [[2026-05-17-space-aerospace-multi-agent]] Agents R + T.

## One-line thesis
Howmet is the **structural-materials chokepoint** for both commercial aerospace recovery AND the US hypersonic + missile-defense buildout. **Which means** HWM captures the rare cross-cycle position where commercial engines (787 ramp, GE LEAP service margins) and defense (ARRW/HACM/GPI hypersonic structural Ti-Al / Ti-Si for 2000°C+ heat) both pull on the same supply base.

## Role in AI / physical-AI / defense
- **Commercial engine spares**: revenue from aging-fleet replacement parts; structurally high-margin recurring
- **Hypersonic structural Ti-Al / Ti-Si**: critical for 2000°C+ heat in ARRW (AGM-183A), HACM, GPI nose cones
- **787 ramp**: HWM is in the supply chain at 8/month target

## Bull case
- **Commercial engine spares +48% YoY** Q1 2026 — clearest demand signal in the cycle
- **787 production ramp to 8/month** by EOY 2026 (from current ~5/month) — direct HWM revenue
- **Hypersonic structural demand**: FY26 defense budget allocates **$3.9B hypersonic R&D**; ARRW/HACM/GPI structural Ti-Al/Ti-Si embedded in volumes that aren't yet fully modeled by sell-side
- **Pratt & Whitney GTF recovery**: 835 aircraft grounded; spare-engine + replacement-part demand bridges the gap
- **$1.8B Consolidated Aerospace acquisition** signals capex confidence + capacity consolidation
- **Aggressive M&A** in a fragmented market = HWM positioning as the "TransDigm of titanium"
- **Cross-cycle resilience**: commercial weakness AND defense weakness both face structural under-supply — HWM benefits either way
- 32% EBITDA margin = software-like economics on aerospace materials

## Bear case / risks
- **787 production catch-up risk**: Boeing slipping production targets again pushes spares revenue out
- **Titanium prices cyclical**: HWM passes through but FY27-28 normalization could compress margins
- **Customer concentration**: Boeing + Airbus dominate; loss of any program is material
- **Consolidated Aerospace integration**: M&A execution risk; first 6 months post-close are sensitive
- **Higher Russian VSMPO titanium supply** if Ukraine resolves — would re-pressure spot prices
- **ATI competition**: ATI also expanding titanium capacity for Boeing
- **Multiple expansion limit**: HWM already trades at premium to peers on quality narrative
