# Vishay Precision Group (VPG)

**Stance:** neutral · **Conviction:** low · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis

Precision force-torque sensing is a real humanoid bottleneck and CEO Ziv Shoshani is explicitly pivoting the narrative to "Physical AI," but the stock has 4×'d in 12 months and now trades at P/E 135–215 against a 2026 revenue guide of mid-to-high-single-digit growth — the multiple has done most of the asymmetric work already.

## Bull case

- **CEO pivoting narrative to "Physical AI and humanoid robotics."** Called 2026 "a pivotal year" for these segments on Q4 2025 earnings call. Strategic intent is clearly toward the robotics buildout.
- **Force-torque sensing IS a real humanoid bottleneck.** Dexterous manipulation requires sub-Newton force feedback — VPG's strain-gauge and load-cell IP base is genuine.
- **30% pop on Q1 2026 earnings** (May 12, 2026) confirms market is rewarding the narrative pivot if execution begins to match.
- **Strong cash position** $87.4M with manageable debt ($64M).
- **Diversified end-market base** (test & measurement, steel, medical, construction, agricultural) provides a floor under the existing business while the humanoid pivot plays out.
- **Lake Street raised PT to $54** from $44 (still below current price — note this).

## Bear case / risks

- **Multiple already prices in transition success.** P/E 134–215 implies the humanoid pivot delivers on every dimension. Any execution stumble compresses the multiple sharply.

> **What this means:** VPG's stock has gone up 4x in a year on a *story* about humanoid robotics — but the company's actual 2026 revenue is only growing in the mid-single digits, and one sell-side firm's price target is *below* where the stock trades today. If the humanoid pivot doesn't deliver real revenue quickly, the stock could drop sharply. It's the most fragile name in the robotics group.
- **2026 revenue guide is mid-to-high single digits** — not humanoid-hypergrowth territory. Disconnect between narrative and near-term financials.
- **Stock up 4× in 12 months** ($24.90 → $89.70 high). Most of the asymmetric upside has been realized.
- **Q4 2025 EPS missed by 67%.** Gross margin declined to 37% from 40.5% prior quarter. Adjusted operating margin only 2.3%.
- **Lake Street price target $54 is below current $89 price.** Sell-side has not caught up to the recent rally — meaning either analysts will revise upward (bullish) or the price corrects (bearish).
- **No named humanoid OEM design wins disclosed.** Narrative pivot has not yet been backed by customer announcements.
- **CapEx $14–16M planned for 2026.** Real investment in the pivot — but cash flow drag during the transition.

## Key questions / what to watch

- Q2 2026 earnings print — does revenue accelerate above the mid-single-digit guide? Single most important near-term datapoint.
- Any named humanoid OEM design win disclosure (Tesla, Figure, Apptronik, Agility).
- Gross margin recovery trajectory back toward 40%+.
- Lake Street and other analyst PT revisions post-Q1 2026.
- Insider activity. The 30% pop creates obvious sell-pressure incentives.
