# Quanta Services (PWR)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
The **upstream-of-the-data-center play** — Quanta builds the **transmission lines, substations, and grid infrastructure** that get power TO the AI data centers. Q1 2026 backlog hit a record **$48.5B** (up from $39.2B three quarters earlier). FY26 adjusted EPS guide raised to **$13.55-$14.25**. Less pure-AI than [[FIX]]/[[EME]] but more durable because grid modernization + renewables + AI are all tailwinds in the same direction.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Transmission lines** — the high-voltage backbone connecting generation to data center campuses
- **Substations** — stepping voltage down to data-center-usable levels
- **Generation projects** — including NiSource engagement to build **~3 GW of generation capacity** for a major load customer
- **Underground utility** — fiber + conduit + power buried alongside data center build-outs
- **Renewable integration** — solar, wind, battery storage projects
- Customer base = utilities + hyperscalers + sovereign agencies

## Bull case
- **$48.5B record backlog** — the largest in the cluster; multi-year visibility
- **Three tailwinds in the same direction**: grid modernization + renewable energy integration + AI data center load growth
- **Less AI-cycle-dependent** than FIX/EME — utility customers buy regardless of AI sentiment
- **Self-perform model** — Quanta does the work in-house (vs. subcontracting) → margin capture
- **Transmission is the *next* binding constraint after data centers** themselves are built
- **Long-duration projects** — transmission has 2-5 year build cycles; backlog is genuinely sticky
- **Sovereign / utility customer mix** provides stability even in hyperscaler capex pauses

## Bear case / risks
- **Diluted pure-AI exposure** — only a fraction of backlog is direct AI; majority is utility / renewable
- **Stock has already run** — premium multiple reflects the bull case
- **Permitting timelines** can delay project starts (regulatory risk)
- **Utility customer concentration** — top customers drive a large share of revenue
- **Renewable energy policy risk** — federal incentive changes affect part of backlog
- **Labor and equipment costs** — wage + raw material inflation
- **Project execution risk on multi-billion-dollar transmission programs**

## Key questions / what to watch
