## Role in AI robotics
Surgical robotics is the **only category where robots reliably generate cash today**. ISRG's da Vinci is the platform; recurring revenue from instruments + services compounds with installed base. AI integration is real but downstream:
- **NVIDIA Holoscan** + **GR00T-H** + **Open-H (700-hour surgical video dataset)** + **Cosmos-H** synthetic-data generation power AI-enabled intraoperative planning
- ISRG's **da Vinci 5 Force Feedback** + integrated digital architecture capture 85% of US placements
- AI augmentation extends moat — surgical training data + workflow integration are reinforced by AI rather than displaced

## Bull case
- **Structural recurring revenue (86%)** — instruments, services, accessories per procedure; compounds with installed base
- **Da Vinci 5 acceleration** — 232 placements Q1 (+58% YoY); displaces da Vinci Xi/X in US hospitals
- **Procedure mix expansion**: urology 80%+ robotic; **general surgery only 15% — massive runway**
- **Pricing power**: hospitals don't switch platforms; surgeon training is multi-year sunk cost
- **AI moat extension**: NVIDIA Holoscan + Open-H surgical video dataset integrated with ISRG's data flywheel
- **Threat assessment** — Medtronic Hugo + JNJ Ottava are **greenfield threats, NOT incumbent-capture** — agent verdict
- **Ion lung biopsy growth** — separate da Vinci platform, expanding TAM
- **40% EBIT growth Q1** — operating leverage real and scaling

## Bear case / risks
- **Premium valuation** — typically 50-70x forward earnings; high-multiple compression risk in [[bank-of-japan]] / carry-trade unwind scenario
- **China procedure slowdown** — exposure to Chinese hospital procurement cycles + tariff risk
- **Bariatric / GLP-1 headwind** — Ozempic/Wegovy reduce bariatric surgery volumes (real but partially offset by Ion lung biopsy)
- **Medtronic Hugo competitive entry** — FDA cleared Feb 2026; long-term share-loss risk if Hugo wins at price-sensitive hospitals
- **JNJ Ottava** — in development; could leverage J&J's distribution into hospitals
- **Stryker Mako handheld (Feb 2026)** — orthopedic-specific but signals robotic competition expanding into procedure categories
- **Regulatory risk** — FDA changes to robotic surgery approval pathways
- **Surgeon adoption fatigue** — eventually limits new system placements; procedure growth still uncapped
- **Reimbursement** — Medicare/CMS coverage changes could affect hospital ROI on robot acquisition
