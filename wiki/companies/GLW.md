# Corning (GLW)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
Near-monopoly position in high-performance optical fiber — **direct $3.2B NVDA investment** (May 6 2026) accompanied by **3 new US factories** focused on AI fiber. Fiber demand grows regardless of which optical technology (CPO vs. pluggable) wins.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Optical fiber supplier** for AI data center fabric
- NVDA investment expands Corning's optical capacity **tenfold**, with 3 new US factories (NC + Texas)
- Joint participation in NVIDIA Spectrum-X photonics ecosystem
- Diversified portfolio (display glass, automotive, life sciences) gives stability beyond AI

## Bull case
- **NVDA $3.2B direct investment** = capital + offtake commitment
- **10x optical capacity expansion** — direct response to AI fiber demand
- **Most defensive optics name** — "fiber demand grows regardless of which optical tech wins" (industrial analyst commentary)
- **Diversified business** — display glass, environmental, life sciences provide non-AI ballast
- **US manufacturing footprint** — geopolitically advantaged

## Bear case / risks
- **Capex execution** — building 3 new factories takes time and money
- **Optics trade volatility** — fiber pricing can move on supply-demand mismatches
- **Customer concentration** — NVDA share growing
- **Display glass cyclicality** — non-AI segments still material

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **NVDA partnership rollout** — factory milestones, first shipments
2. **Optical Communications segment growth**
3. **Capacity utilization** as new factories come online
4. **Display glass demand** under macro pressure
