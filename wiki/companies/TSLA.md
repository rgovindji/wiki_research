# Tesla (TSLA)

**Stance:** bear (avoid) · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** any

## One-line thesis
Trading at **~178x 2026 expected earnings** — valuation depends almost entirely on unproven robotaxi and Optimus optionality, with the underlying EV business facing margin pressure and slowing volume growth. Consensus avoid for 2026.

## Bull case (for completeness)
- **AI-platform pivot.** Tesla pitches itself as an AI/robotics company, not a carmaker; if Optimus or robotaxi ships, valuation framework changes entirely.
- **FSD progress** could unlock high-margin software revenue from existing fleet.
- **Energy storage** business growing fast and high margin.
- **Brand + leadership.** Cult-of-CEO premium that doesn't apply to other Mag 7.
- **Samsung Texas robot-chip deal (NEW).** Per [[2026-05-09-dwarkesh-dylan-semianalysis]]: Elon signed a major deal with Samsung to fab robot chips in Texas — gives Tesla **(a) Taiwan-risk diversification** and **(b) a separate, less-contended supply chain** that doesn't compete with the AI-data-center wallet. Real strategic positive for the robotics thesis if Optimus actually ships.
- **Dojo restart.** Tesla Dojo (wafer-scale chip with 25 chips on it) was cancelled and restarted; per Dylan, "still probably the best chip for running convolutional neural networks." Niche but live.

## Bear case / risks (the dominant view)
- **Valuation disconnect.** 178x 2026 EPS is priced for a robotaxi/Optimus outcome that has no commercial-scale validation. Any slip in launch dates is a re-rating event.
- **EV core is contracting in growth and margin.** Lower deliveries or lower ASPs hit the actually-existing business.
- **Competitive intensity** in EVs (Chinese manufacturers; legacy auto EV ramps) — Tesla's first-mover lead is eroding.
- **CEO concentration risk.** Operational and brand risk tied to a single individual.
- **Space data center thesis is wrong this decade (NEW).** Per Dylan: same chip-supply constraint applies, plus 6+ months added deploy time = 10% of useful life lost, plus 15% of Blackwell GPUs need RMA on initial deploy. Verdict: space data centers don't make sense until ~2035. So if part of Elon's bull narrative depends on space data center economics, that's a multi-year-out story at best.
- **Tariff exposure.** Both inputs and demand sensitivity (see [[inflation-tariffs]]).
- **Mag 7 multiple compression** would hit TSLA hardest given valuation extremity.

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Robotaxi commercial launch** — actual revenue, not pilot
2. **Optimus production milestones** — units shipped at scale, not demos
3. **EV gross margin** — direction quarter-over-quarter
4. **FSD Pro / V13 take rate** — software revenue actualizing?
5. **China EV share** — Tesla's competitive position vs. BYD et al.
