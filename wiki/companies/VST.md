# Vistra Corp (VST)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium-high · **Time horizon:** long-term compounder with 20-yr PPA visibility

> **Why a page now (2026-05-17 PM):** Vistra has **2,609 MW Meta 20-year PPA** + 3 operating nuclear plants + 433 MW of uprate capacity. **The cleanest near-term operational nuclear-for-AI-data-center play in public markets**. Vistra owns **3 operating nuclear plants** (Beaver Valley PA, Perry, Davis-Besse OH) with **Meta locking in 2,609 MW over 20 years** + uprate capacity + license extensions into 2050s. **Which means** VST captures the structural shift of AI data center demand to existing nuclear baseload at PPAs that compound for 20+ years — without first-of-a-kind SMR execution risk.

## Role in AI / nuclear power
- **Beaver Valley**: 1,900 MW PA — Meta PPA core asset
- **Perry / Davis-Besse OH**: 760 MW + ~900 MW — Meta + Amazon supply
- **Uprate capex**: incremental output at existing assets = highest-margin growth
- **Cogentrix gas asset acquisition** $4.7B: diversification into natural gas peakers for data center backup

## Bull case
- **Meta 2,609 MW 20-yr PPA** = multi-decade revenue visibility; CPI-linked pricing typical
- **Operational TODAY** — no construction / regulatory / FOAK risk vs OKLO / X-energy
- **433 MW uprate capacity** = highest-margin growth (incremental output at existing reactors)
- **License extensions into 2050s** = 25+ years of cash flow runway
- **Multi-hyperscaler customer base**: Meta + Amazon (via Cogentrix) = customer diversification (vs CEG single-customer Microsoft)
- **Reaffirmed 2026 outlook** in Q1 earnings = management credibility
- **Gas peaker capacity** (via Cogentrix) = data center backup power = additional revenue stream
- **Nuclear baseload + gas peaker combination** is exactly what hyperscaler PPAs demand (24/7/365 + peak-shaving)

## Bear case / risks
- **Customer concentration**: Meta accounts for bulk of growth narrative; any Meta delay or PPA renegotiation = material
- **Capex for uprates** — 433 MW costs $2-3B; execution risk on schedule
- **Interest rate sensitivity**: utility-style balance sheet has rate exposure
- **Regulatory risk**: NRC license extensions need renewal cadence
- **Counterparty risk**: hyperscaler bankruptcy / business model shift over 20-yr horizon
- **Texas weather event risk**: legacy ERCOT gas exposure (Cogentrix) faces winter storm tail risk
- **Trump admin defense prime renegotiation tendencies** could extend to utility PPAs (low probability but tail)
- **Macro multiple compression**: utilities have rerated higher on AI capex thesis; could compress in any tech sell-off
- **Cogentrix integration risk**: $4.7B acquisition only recently closed; synergy realization period

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Meta PPA ramp pace** — late 2026 start, multi-year ramp through 2034
2. **Uprate execution**: 433 MW timeline + capex realization
3. **License extension applications**: which plants, which timelines
4. **Amazon volume** via Cogentrix gas peakers
5. **2027 PPA pricing escalators** (CPI vs fixed)
6. **Any new hyperscaler PPA wins** beyond Meta + Amazon
