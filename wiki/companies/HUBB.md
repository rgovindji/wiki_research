# Hubbell (HUBB)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term grid/electrification compounder

> **Why a page now (2026-06-20):** Hubbell makes the utility T&D and electrical infrastructure (transformers, switching, grid-protection gear) that every data-center and grid-buildout dollar flows through. Cluster B power/grid pick — a "picks and shovels" complement to the generation names.

## One-line thesis
Hubbell's **Utility Solutions** segment supplies grid hardware — transformers, switchgear, metering, grid automation — exactly the bottleneck equipment utilities must buy to connect new generation and data-center load. **Which means** HUBB is a diversified grid-electrification compounder levered to the same [[ai-capex-cycle]] + [[datacenter-construction]] demand as [[GEV]]/[[ETN]]/[[VRT]], without merchant-power risk.

## Role in AI / datacenter power
- **Utility Solutions** — transformers, switching, grid protection, metering: the gear utilities need to interconnect new generation and growing data-center load. Transformers in particular are a multi-year supply bottleneck.
- **Electrical Solutions** — wiring, connectors, enclosures used in data centers and industrial/commercial buildout.
- Direct beneficiary of grid-modernization + reconductoring capex regardless of which generation tech wins.
- Same structural tailwind as [[GEV]] (grid) and [[ETN]]/[[VRT]] (electrical/power management) but more pure-play on utility T&D hardware.

## Bull case
- **Grid bottleneck exposure** — transformer/switchgear backlogs and pricing power as utilities race to add capacity.
- **Diversified** across utility + electrical end-markets = less single-driver risk.
- **Pricing power** through the supply-constrained transformer cycle has driven margin expansion.
- **Steady compounder** — consistent execution, dividend grower.
- Picks-and-shovels: benefits from data-center buildout without merchant-power or PPA-counterparty risk.

## Bear case / risks
- **Slower top-line growth (+7% YoY)** vs the hyper-growth generation names — this is a steady compounder, not a torque play.
- **Valuation** — fwd P/E ~25 is full for mid-single-digit growth; prices in continued T&D strength.
- **Cyclicality** — electrical/industrial segment is economically sensitive; a capex slowdown hits orders.
- **Transformer cycle normalization** — once supply catches up, pricing power fades.
- **Near highs (-7%)** — limited de-rating cushion.

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Transformer backlog + pricing** — the key margin driver.
2. **Utility capex trends** — grid-modernization spending pace.
3. **Data-center order contribution** within Electrical Solutions.
4. **Margin sustainability** as supply normalizes.
5. **M&A** — Hubbell has grown via bolt-ons.
