# Rocket Lab (RKLB)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term (multi-year)

## One-line thesis

The only US-listed pure-play small/medium-lift launch + space-systems vertically-integrated company — **Q1 2026 record $200M revenue (+63.5% YoY) with $2.2B backlog (~2x YoY)**, Neutron medium-lift debut later 2026, and a **Department of War Golden Dome (Space Based Interceptor) partnership with Raytheon** that converts RKLB from "space startup" to "Tier-2 defense contractor with launch capability."

> **What this means:** Rocket Lab is the rare new-space company that's both growing fast AND winning defense contracts. Q1 2026 was a record quarter, they sold more launches in 3 months than all of 2025, and they just won a slot on the Pentagon's biggest new program (Golden Dome missile defense). If their Neutron rocket debuts successfully later this year, they become the only credible Western alternative to SpaceX in the medium-lift launch market.

- **Pure-play small-lift launch** (Electron — 60+ launches to date)
- **HASTE hypersonic test platform** — $190M deal for 20 test flights; multiple new DoD contracts
- **Neutron medium-lift** — debut "later 2026"; positioned to compete with SpaceX Falcon 9
- **Space Systems segment growing FASTER than Launch** — $136.7M (Space Systems) vs $63.7M (Launch) Q1 2026
- **Vertical integration via acquisitions:** Closed Mynaric (laser inter-satellite links); definitive agreement on Motiv Space Systems
- **Department of War Space Based Interceptor selection (Golden Dome program)** — partnership with [[RTX|Raytheon]]

## Bull case

- **Q1 2026 record print validates the thesis.** Revenue $200.3M (+63.5% YoY); backlog $2.2B (~2x YoY). **More launches sold in Q1 2026 than full year 2025.** GAAP gross margin 38.2% record; adj EBITDA loss narrowing rapidly.
- **Neutron is the next leg.** Medium-lift entry into a market where SpaceX Falcon 9 is the only credible Western alternative. Even partial market share at debut creates a step-change in TAM.
- **Space Systems is the underappreciated moat.** Mynaric + Motiv + Gauss positions RKLB as a vertically-integrated space supplier — they sell components AND launches. Higher-margin recurring revenue.
- **Defense pivot is durable + Golden Dome SBI is a $5.6B program tier (NEW 2026-05-14).** Per [[2026-05-14-space-economy-budget-sources]] (Aerospace Corp FY26 budget brief): the **Space Based Interceptor category within Golden Dome receives $5.6B in FY25-29 reconciliation funding** — first space-based interceptor program since Reagan SDI ended. RKLB-Raytheon partnership is the named selection. Plus $190M HASTE hypersonic deal + ongoing DoD Department of War contracts. **Which means:** the RKLB defense pivot isn't a single contract — it's positioning within a multi-billion-dollar, multi-decade program category that has presidential-level priority. The CBO 20-year cost range for Golden Dome is $161-542B; even at the low end, RKLB's positioning is structurally durable.
- **Cash + liquidity strong.** $1.21B cash + $2B+ liquidity = no near-term dilution risk despite ongoing capex for Neutron.
- **Confidential largest-ever contract signed in Q1** for Neutron + Electron through 2029 — implies named customer disclosure when allowed.
- **SpaceX IPO comp tailwind.** When SpaceX IPOs at $1.75-2T valuation, RKLB as the only public pure-play launch competitor gets re-rated.

## Bear case / risks

- **Neutron timing risk is the single biggest variable.** "Debut later 2026" has historical base-rate of 1-3 quarter slips. First-flight failure rates ~50% industry-wide. A 2027 slip + first-flight failure could compress the stock 30-40%.
- **Stock has run.** RKLB is up materially YTD and from 12 months ago. Wall Street PT $120 implies upside but the easy money may be gone.
- **Cash burn is real even with profitability narrowing.** $11.8M adj EBITDA loss Q1 + heavy capex for Neutron infrastructure. Not break-even yet.
- **Backlog conversion timing.** $2.2B backlog is multi-year; revenue conversion depends on launch cadence and customer readiness. Not all $2.2B hits 2026-2027.
- **Defense political risk.** Trump administration's Jan 2026 executive order on defense contractor pay + buybacks signals willingness to renegotiate primes' economics. RKLB is small enough to be less affected, but the political backdrop matters.
- **Competition from new entrants.** Stoke Space, Astra (recovered?), Relativity Space, Firefly (Alpha) — even if none catches up by 2027, the medium-lift category will have 4+ credible Western players by 2030.

> **What this means (bear case synthesis):** Rocket Lab is winning the new-space race today, but the next 18 months are a binary on Neutron. If it debuts on time and works, the stock has another structural leg up. If it slips a year AND fails first-flight, the stock could lose 30-40% of value. Position-size accordingly — this is a high-quality story but not a low-risk one.
