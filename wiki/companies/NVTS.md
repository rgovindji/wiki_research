# Navitas Semiconductor (NVTS)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** low (high beta / extreme valuation) · **Time horizon:** medium-term tactical

## One-line thesis
Pure-play GaN (gallium nitride) and SiC power-semiconductor specialist — announced as **NVIDIA partner for next-gen AI factory platforms** with co-development of 800V HVDC architectures. Stock **+88% YTD** but trading at **~74x sales**; revenue inflection management points to **2027** as Blackwell / Rubin rack systems ramp Navitas content.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **GaN power devices** for AI server power delivery
- **Co-developing 800V HVDC architectures** with NVDA for the next-gen AI factory
- **800V→50V DC-DC platform** for AI servers (10kW; 98.5% peak efficiency; 2.1 kW/in³ density)
- Partner in NVIDIA 800V power initiative alongside [[STM]], [[ADI]], [[ON]], Infineon, MPS, etc.

## Bull case
- **Direct NVDA partnership** for next-gen AI factory power architecture
- **GaN structurally superior** to silicon for high-efficiency power conversion at high voltages
- **Revenue inflection in 2027** as Blackwell racks deploy Navitas content (per management)
- **Smaller-cap leverage** — incremental wins move financials more
- **Product roadmap depth** in monolithic GaN integration

## Bear case / risks
- **Power is NOT going to be the binding AI constraint (NEW).** Per [[2026-05-09-dwarkesh-dylan-semianalysis]]: 16+ power-generation vendors; behind-the-meter alternatives plentiful. NVTS's 800V→50V pitch is real, but the *bottleneck premium* in power electronics is bounded — it's a feature, not a binding scarcity.
- **~74x sales multiple** — extreme valuation; structural reality vs. promise mismatch (Trefis)
- **Revenue trough late 2025** — turn coming through 2026, but financially weak in interim
- **Competitive intensity** from Infineon, Texas Instruments, [[STM]], [[ON]] — all of whom have manufacturing scale and existing customer relationships
- **High beta / volatility** — optics-style sentiment swings
- **Execution risk** on production ramp at scale
- **AI capex cycle exposure** — slows with hyperscalers

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Quarterly revenue inflection** — when does it actually turn?
2. **NVDA-related revenue** disclosure
3. **Gross margin trajectory** under competitive pressure
4. **Customer concentration** as ramp builds
5. **Execution on 800V→50V product family**
