# Hewlett Packard Enterprise (HPE)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
The third leg of the enterprise AI server OEM stool with [[DELL]] and [[SMCI]], now significantly stronger after the **$13.4B Juniper Networks acquisition** (closed July 2, 2025) — networking + servers + GreenLake (hybrid cloud) + Cray HPC heritage = a full-stack AI infrastructure vendor with a uniquely deep enterprise relationship base.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **AI server OEM** — full Blackwell + Vera Rubin portfolio across the HPE AI Factory line
- **HPE ProLiant** servers integrating NVIDIA RTX PRO 4500 / 6000 Blackwell Server Edition GPUs (rolling out Q1-Q2 2026)
- **HPE Juniper Networking** — coherent optics + routers connecting distributed AI deployments
- **HPE AI Grid Solution** — edge AI with NVIDIA
- **Cray HPC heritage** gives credibility for sovereign + research deployments (Frontier, Aurora supercomputers)

## Bull case
- **Juniper closes the networking gap.** HPE was the only major AI server OEM without a serious networking story; Juniper changes that overnight (151.5% YoY networking revenue growth in Q1 FY2026 reflects acquisition + organic).
- **Sovereign AI is a perfect fit.** HPE has a long government/research customer base; the sovereign AI factory wave (UAE, Saudi, India, EU) plays directly to HPE's enterprise/government strengths
- **Full-stack story** — servers + networking + GreenLake hybrid cloud + Cray HPC = harder-to-replicate moat than SMCI's pure-server play
- **Catching up to Dell** — HPE was a laggard in 2024-2025; 2026 portfolio (RTX PRO + Vera Rubin) closes the gap
- **Less SMCI risk** — clean accounting, established governance vs. SMCI's lingering legal overhang
- **GreenLake recurring revenue** provides ballast against cyclical hardware

## Bear case / risks
- **Late mover** — Dell already captured first-mover Vera Rubin / GB300 deals; HPE has to displace, not just win
- **AI server margins are thin** — like the rest of the OEMs, growth is volume-led, not margin-led
- **Hyperscaler bypass** — large hyperscalers buy direct from ODM (Foxconn, Quanta) not OEMs
- **Juniper integration risk** — $13.4B acquisitions take 2-3 years to fully integrate; cultural + product roadmap consolidation is non-trivial
- **Cisco competition in networking** — Juniper enters HPE into a head-on fight with Cisco that wasn't on prior threat list
- **Storage business cyclicality** — drag from non-AI segments

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Networking segment growth** — does the +151% YoY momentum continue ex-acquisition?
2. **AI server backlog disclosure** — direct comparison to Dell ISG
3. **GreenLake ARR** — recurring revenue trajectory
4. **Sovereign AI deal flow** — HPE's distinct edge vs. Dell
5. **Operating margin** as AI mix grows
6. **Juniper integration milestones** — synergy realization
