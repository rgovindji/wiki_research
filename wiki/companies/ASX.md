# ASE Technology Holding (ASX)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term · **Listing:** NYSE ADR (primary: TWSE 3711)

## One-line thesis
Largest OSAT (outsourced semiconductor assembly and test) globally — direct beneficiary of the **CoWoS bottleneck**, with advanced packaging sales **doubling in 2026** as [[TSM|TSMC]] outsources more capacity to keep up with AI demand.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **OSAT for advanced packaging** — receives wafer-level overflow from TSMC's CoWoS line
- Part of NVIDIA's CoWoS-S/R packaging supply chain alongside [[AMKR]], SPIL (an ASE subsidiary)
- NVDA's CoWoS-S/R volume **outside TSMC**: 65k wafers in 2026, 140k in 2027

## Bull case
- **Advanced packaging revenue doubling in 2026** per management
- **CoWoS oversubscribed** through 2026 — favorable pricing environment
- **TSMC outsourcing increases** with capacity constraints — direct read-through
- Owns SPIL — second OSAT entity expanding capacity
- Diversified beyond AI (memory, automotive, industrial) for cycle hedging

## Bear case / risks
- **Capex arms race** with [[AMKR]] — both massively scaling capacity; risk of overbuild
- **Geographic concentration** — Taiwan exposure (geopolitics, see [[TSM]])
- **Customer concentration** — small number of leading-edge clients drive most advanced packaging revenue
- **Cyclicality** — OSAT margins are historically cyclical
- **Currency** — TWD volatility in USD-reported figures

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Advanced packaging revenue growth** quarterly
2. **CoWoS capacity additions** — both at TSMC and at OSATs
3. **Capex discipline** — is the industry building too much for 2027-28?
4. **Geopolitical headlines** affecting Taiwan operations
