## What this company is

Enphase makes **microinverters** (the small box that converts DC from each individual solar panel into AC) plus **IQ Batteries** for residential and small-commercial solar systems. Microinverter-per-panel architecture is differentiated vs. string inverters — better yield in shading, panel-level monitoring, safer wiring. Q1 shipped 1.41M microinverters (≈627.6 MW DC) + 103 MWh of batteries.

This is **not data-center power** — it's residential rooftop solar economics. Different category from the wiki's [[VRT]] / [[ETN]] / [[GEV]] / [[BE]] data-center power coverage.

## Bull case
- **Bombed-out valuation.** Down ~85% from the 2022 peak (~$340) — most of the consumer-rate-cycle and ITC pessimism is already in. Any moderation in either variable = mean-reversion candidate.
- **Q1 2026 beat both lines** (Rev $282.9M vs $281.89M cons; non-GAAP EPS $0.47 vs $0.44) with $83M free cash flow and $930M cash on balance sheet — survivor mode, not distress mode.
- **Non-GAAP GM held at 44%** with ~3pp reciprocal-tariff drag already absorbed — implies underlying 47% structural GM if/when tariffs ease.
- **New microinverter launch May 14, 2026** — fresh product cycle drove ~+15% intraday spike. Stocks at this valuation level move on incremental product wins.
- **ITC safe-harbor pipeline strong:** $843.6M of TPO agreements YTD ($89.6M under 5% safe-harbor method + $754M physical-work-test method) — locks in ITC eligibility before any future policy change.
- **Battery list-price cut 12-14% in March** signals demand response, but margin held in Q1 = pricing power is bigger than feared.

## Bear case / risks
- **Reciprocal tariffs are explicit and ongoing.** Management quantified ~3pp of GM impact in Q2 guide. [[inflation-tariffs]] documents the average US tariff regime moving from 2% → 12% under current admin; ENPH is directly exposed.
- **GAAP gross margin only 35.5% in Q1** — the gap between GAAP (35.5%) and non-GAAP (44%) is wide because of large discounted PTC sales + tariff drag. GAAP is the better cash-pricing proxy.
- **GAAP net loss of $7.4M in Q1** — even after a beat, the company is not yet GAAP-profitable. Cash is declining.
- **ITC policy risk.** The Trump administration's general posture toward IRA-era credits is hostile; residential solar economics depend heavily on the 30% ITC. Any rollback compresses demand. Safe-harbor mechanics preserve some pipeline but don't immunize future demand.
- **Consumer rate sensitivity.** Residential solar TPO / financing is sensitive to mortgage rates and consumer financing rates. [[fed-policy]] documents the higher-for-longer regime: with the 30y at 5.02%, residential rate-sensitive demand stays soft.
- **Hold consensus.** 45% of analysts at Hold, 23% at Sell, only 32% at Buy. Average PT $59 vs current $42 looks generous but the **median is closer to current price** — the bull camp is small.
- **Late-cycle warning indicator overlap.** The wiki's [[ai-bubble-debate]] framework flagged "bombed-out cyclicals bouncing on narrative without earnings inflection" as a late-cycle pattern (POET being the canonical example). ENPH is not POET — it has real revenue and free cash flow — but the **bounce pattern is similar**: −85% from peak, then volatile sentiment trades on incremental product news.
- **First Solar lift was a sympathy trade.** Some of the recent strength was sector-sympathy from First Solar's strong Q1 print — not company-specific.

## Key questions / what to watch
- **Q2 2026 print (late July):** Does non-GAAP GM stay 44–47% guide? Does GAAP loss narrow or widen? Does the new microinverter launch show in the safe-harbor pipeline?
- **ITC policy actions:** Any executive-order or Treasury rule-making attacking residential-solar safe-harbor or 30% credit = stock drops. Any clarity that ITC stays intact = base case improves.
- **Tariff path:** Q2 guide bakes in ~3pp reciprocal tariff GM drag. If reciprocal tariffs ease (per Trump-Xi de-escalation framing in [[us-china-relations]]), that's a ~3pp GM tailwind = direct EPS lift. If they harden, more drag.
- **Battery competitive position:** 12–14% list-price cut in March suggests Tesla Powerwall + LG Chem competition is biting. Watch Q2 attach rates and Q3 GM trajectory.
- **Cash burn:** $930M cash is the runway. If GAAP losses persist and FCF turns negative, cash buffer compresses fast.
