# Kratos Defense (KTOS)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis

The mid-cap defense / aerospace specialist that sits between Tier 2 primes and Tier 1 pure-plays — **XQ-58 Valkyrie tactical drone (loyal wingman to F-35), Mako hypersonic drone**, tactical missile systems, satellite ground systems, and Space Force training systems. Smaller cap (~$5B) makes it more torque-y than primes but with material defense backlog.

> **What this means:** Kratos is the "tactical drones + space + missiles" specialist that the big primes can't easily replicate. They make autonomous fighter wingmen, hypersonic test platforms, and satellite ground systems — all categories the Pentagon is funding aggressively. Smaller than Lockheed/Northrop so more upside per contract win, but also more execution risk.

## Role in aerospace-defense

- **XQ-58 Valkyrie** — autonomous "loyal wingman" combat drone partnered with F-35 / NGAD
- **Mako hypersonic drone** — high-speed test platform
- **Tactical missile systems** — solid rocket motors via Stratolaunch acquisition target rumored
- **Satellite ground systems** — software + hardware for Space Force C2
- **Training systems** — Space Force + Air Force pilot training
- **Cybersecurity** — defense cyber operations

## Bull case

- **Autonomous combat drone tailwind.** Valkyrie + competing Anduril Fury programs validate the "loyal wingman" thesis. Pentagon CCA (Collaborative Combat Aircraft) program is multi-billion-dollar opportunity.
- **Hypersonic test demand** — Mako + competing platforms benefit from DoD hypersonic spending wave
- **Space Force training + ground systems** — recurring revenue subset of the $40.2B Space Force budget
- **Mid-cap torque.** ~$5B market cap means contracts move the needle in a way they don't for $100B+ primes.
- **Defense / aerospace exposure without the political pay-and-buyback headwinds** that hit Tier 2 primes (Trump Jan 2026 EO targets primes specifically)

## Bear case / risks

- **Competition from Anduril** (private). Anduril Fury + Roadrunner are direct competitors for the autonomous combat drone slot. Anduril's defense-AI positioning has been highly successful in winning Pentagon attention.
- **Execution risk.** Mid-cap defense specialists historically have spotty execution; KTOS has missed multiple guidance prints in past years.
- **Margin pressure.** Defense specialty hardware tends to run lower margins than primes' software-enabled platforms.
- **CCA program structure uncertainty.** Pentagon hasn't committed to specific KTOS Valkyrie procurement quantities.
- **Capital intensity.** Hypersonic and autonomous-drone development requires capex.

## Key questions / what to watch

1. **CCA program contract structure** — does Pentagon commit to volume on Valkyrie variants?
2. **Mako hypersonic flight test milestones**
3. **Space Force training contract renewals**
4. **Q2 2026 print** — margin and backlog trajectory
5. **Anduril competitive dynamics** — does Anduril win share?
