# SpaceX (SPCX)

**Stance:** neutral (lean bearish near-term on valuation + post-IPO float dynamics; the draft "short" is defensible tactically) · **Conviction:** low · **Time horizon:** 6-18mo tactical

## One-line thesis
The dominant launch + satellite-broadband (Starlink) franchise, public since **June 12, 2026** in the largest IPO in market history. Phenomenal asset, but it IPO'd at a **~$1.77T valuation**, spiked to ~$226, and trades near $185 on ~$19B revenue — i.e. priced for near-flawless Starlink + Starship execution. The 12-month *stock* setup, not the company, is what's debatable.

> **What this means:** SpaceX the company is arguably the best space asset on Earth. SPCX the stock is a different question: it came public at a multi-trillion-dollar valuation against ~$19B of revenue, in a euphoric IPO, with a large new float still settling. The draft treats it as a SHORT — and on valuation + post-IPO supply/lockup dynamics that's a coherent *tactical* stance, even if shorting a structurally dominant compounder is dangerous.

## What it is
- **Two businesses:** (1) **Space access** — reusable Falcon 9 / Falcon Heavy / Starship launch, the dominant global launch provider; (2) **Starlink** — satellite broadband + direct-to-cell, the revenue + growth engine.
- **IPO:** priced $135, offered ~555.6M shares (~$75B raise — the largest IPO ever), debuted Jun 12 2026, +19% day one, ran to ~$226, since pulled back to ~$185.
- **Relation to the wiki:** anchor of the [[space-economy]] theme; not an AI/tech-stack name. Included because it's in the Cluster C draft and is a major new public mega-cap.

## Bull case
- **Structural dominance in launch.** Reusability gives a cost/cadence moat no competitor (incl. [[RKLB]] Neutron, Blue Origin) is close to matching at scale. Per [[space-economy]], SpaceX drove ~95% launch-cost compression over a decade.
- **Starlink is a real, fast-growing broadband + direct-to-cell business** — recurring revenue, global TAM, +33% total-company revenue growth.
- **Optionality:** Starship unlocks heavy-lift economics (sub-$500/kg targeted by 2028) and entirely new categories (orbital compute, large constellations, lunar/Mars contracts, expanded DoD work — Space Force budget $40.2B FY26, +40% YoY).

## Bear case / risks (why the draft short is defensible tactically)
- **Valuation leaves no margin.** ~$1.77T+ on ~$19B revenue (unprofitable) prices in years of flawless Starlink scaling + Starship success. **Which means:** even a great company can be a poor *stock* from this entry; multiple compression can swamp fundamental progress.
- **Post-IPO float / lockup dynamics.** Fresh mega-IPO with a huge raise; the run to $226 and fade to $185 shows the price is still discovering its level, and **lockup expiries** will add supply. **Which means:** classic post-IPO drift/volatility is a real tactical short setup over 6-18 months.
- **Execution lumpiness.** Starship is iterative and failure-prone by design; any high-profile setback or Starlink saturation/competition (Amazon Kuiper, AST SpaceMobile [[ASTS]]) dents the narrative.
- **Key-man / governance.** Musk concentration, multi-company entanglements (Tesla, xAI), and unusual governance are real discount factors.
- **Shorting risk is asymmetric.** Borrow cost, squeeze risk, and the danger of shorting a structural winner mean this is a *tactical/valuation* short, not a thesis short.

> **What this means (bear synthesis):** The short case is about the *stock at this price and this moment in its IPO lifecycle*, not about SpaceX being a bad business. Valuation + lockup overhang + post-IPO discovery make a 6-18mo tactical short coherent — but shorting a dominant compounder is high-risk; size and stops matter more than the thesis.

## VERDICT
- **As a long:** pass at ~$185 / ~$1.77T+ — no margin of safety; revisit on a meaningful drawdown or post-lockup reset.
- **As the drafted short:** defensible *tactical/valuation* short on post-IPO supply + stretched multiple — but low conviction and asymmetric risk; manage as a trade, not a core position.

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Lockup expiry schedule** — the dominant near-term supply catalyst.
2. **Starlink ARPU / subscriber + direct-to-cell ramp** — does growth justify the multiple?
3. **Starship cadence & milestones** — successes/failures move the narrative hardest.
4. **Path to GAAP profitability.**
5. **Float settling** — where does the stock base after IPO euphoria fades?
