# Apple (AAPL)

**Stance:** bear · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
**Stance flipped from neutral to bear in May 2026 ingest.** Two structural compressions hitting at once: (1) [[TSM|TSMC]] is structurally **deprioritizing Apple** as Nvidia + hyperscalers prepay for capacity ("just like any old customer" by A16 node, per [[2026-05-09-dwarkesh-dylan-semianalysis]]); and (2) the memory crunch is set to **halve global smartphone unit volumes** (1.4B → 500-600M by 2027) and add ~$150 to iPhone BOM. The cash-return engine still works, but the secular setup is materially worse than it was at the start of 2026.

## Bull case
- **Services flywheel** — App Store, iCloud, Apple Care, Music, TV+ — a high-margin annuity growing faster than hardware
- **Hardware moat** — Apple silicon (M-series, A-series) is genuinely industry-leading; product integration deepens lock-in
- **Capital return discipline** — multi-year buyback program reduces share count; dividend growing
- **Apple Intelligence** is differentiated on **privacy-by-design** (on-device + Private Cloud Compute) — unique positioning
- **Defensive characteristics** — relatively less AI-cycle exposed than Mag 7 peers
- **iPhone as "pocket world scanner" for robotics (NEW 2026-05-16, small option value).** Per NVIDIA's Jim Fan ([[2026-05-16-jim-fan-nvda-robotics]]): iPhone 3D scans feed NVIDIA's real-to-sim-to-real pipeline for robot training. As humanoid robotics scales, the iPhone becomes ambient data-collection infrastructure for the entire industry. This is **second-order option value** — Apple doesn't capture the robotics market, but every iPhone becomes a data ingestion node. Doesn't flip the bear stance (TSMC deprioritization + smartphone volume collapse still dominate) but adds a small structural hedge.

## Bear case / risks
- **TSMC deprioritization (NEW).** Per [[2026-05-09-dwarkesh-dylan-semianalysis]]: Apple is being squeezed off TSMC's leading edge. 2 nm in 2026 is the first time Apple is **not** the first customer to a leading node (excluding Huawei pre-ban). At A16 (N2 variant), the **first customer is AI, not Apple**. Apple "will become a smaller and smaller percentage of TSMC's revenue, and therefore be less relevant for TSMC to cater to their demands ... they are just not TSMC's best bud like they have been historically."
- **Memory cost crunch (NEW).** iPhone BOM impact: **+$50 → $150 just on memory** (DRAM tripled; NAND also up). Net price impact ~$250 retail given Apple margin discipline. Apple has 3-12 month long-term contracts so the hit lags but is coming.
- **Smartphone volume collapse (NEW).** Global smartphone units: **1.4B → 1.1B → projected 800M (2026) → 500-600M (2027)**. Xiaomi/Oppo already cutting low/mid volumes by half. The volume collapse is concentrated in low/mid-end, but high-end (where Apple lives) still gets a smaller drop — Apple's revenue exposure is real even if not the worst hit.
- **AI narrative lags peers.** Apple Intelligence rollout has been slower and more cautious than MSFT/GOOGL/META AI products. The market may pay AI-laggards less.
- **iPhone unit growth** is largely flat globally; growth depends on services + ASP mix — but the BOM increase squeezes that calculus.
- **China exposure** — both demand (iPhone share losses to local champions) and supply (manufacturing concentration). Geopolitical risk.
- **Antitrust** — App Store fees under pressure in EU (DMA) and US courts.
- **Buyback ceiling** — at some point, capital return can't drive EPS growth alone; underlying revenue growth has to step up — and the structural setup just got harder.

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **iPhone unit volumes** — does the smartphone volume collapse spare premium tier?
2. **Memory cost pass-through** — how much of the BOM increase does Apple absorb vs. pass to consumers?
3. **TSMC allocation** — what % of N2 / A16 does Apple actually get year-by-year?
4. **Services growth rate** — the moat metric; resilient if hardware compresses
5. **China iPhone share** — direction (vs. Huawei, Xiaomi)
6. **Apple Intelligence adoption** — silent AI revenue contributor or a non-event?
7. **App Store regulatory outcomes** — financial impact of EU DMA, US Epic remedies

Apple lost **5 percentage points of TSMC wallet share in a single year**. Extrapolating linearly (a strong assumption), Apple share continues compressing toward sub-10% by 2028-2029. The structural argument: TSMC has more pricing power and capacity allocation flexibility with AI customers willing to pay $2-3/wafer-hour premiums; Apple is paying the historical lower rate. Stance reinforced — bear / medium conviction.
- **2026-05-17 — Apple = TSMC's #1 customer at 22% of 2024 revenue (NT$624.3B, +14.2% YoY); booked 50%+ of TSMC 2026 N2 capacity** ([[2026-05-17-semiwiki-forum-sweep-4]] Thread C). Apple dominates N2 through 2027 but loses A16 priority to AI customers. Time-horizon distinction: Apple is at peak TSMC dominance NOW, declining from here.

Apple's self-developed **C2 5G modem** (replaces Qualcomm in 2027) is going to **TSMC on N2**, not Intel. Hundreds of millions of units anticipated. **C2 features**: full mmWave support (improvement over Sub-6 only); satellite communication. TSMC subsidiary Jingcai bought 600 testing machines for backend volume. Forum interpretation (rinoali): Apple is splitting **BB (TSMC N2) + AP (multi-foundry, possibly some Intel share like Samsung's historical role)** rather than integrating into one SoC. **Which means**: Apple-Intel diversification narrative from [[2026-05-17-semiwiki-kuo-apple-intel]] is *real* but for *secondary* work — flagship modem stays at TSMC. Bear thesis intact: Apple's smartphone-volume + TSMC priority dynamics unchanged; modem strategy is intra-Apple optimization not a TSMC pivot. Tim Cook accompanied Trump to Xi summit; Apple's China political exposure remains elevated.

Kuo's 10-point industry checks add precision to the Apple-Intel timeline: **small-scale testing 2026 → ramp 2027 → growth 2028 → decline 2029** at Intel 18A-P. Critically: **"Even if Intel's initial shipments go smoothly, TSMC will still retain over 90% of supply share"** AND **"Apple began discussions with Intel well before TSMC's advanced-node capacity became tight"** — this is long-planned strategic diversification, NOT reactive.
