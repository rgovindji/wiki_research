# Northrop Grumman (NOC)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis

The defense prime with the deepest space + strategic-deterrence portfolio — **B-21 Raider bomber + Sentinel ICBM** are the two largest modernization programs in DoD history, plus **record $95.7B backlog at FY25 close, +29% YTD 2026, 2026 sales guide $43.5-44B**. Higher-quality growth profile than [[LMT]] or [[RTX]] in this cycle.

> **What this means:** Northrop Grumman has the two biggest defense programs in modernization (B-21 stealth bomber and Sentinel ICBM replacement) plus the largest backlog in its history. The stock has already run 29% YTD, so the easy money is gone — but the multi-decade revenue visibility is unmatched among defense primes.

## Role in aerospace-defense

- **B-21 Raider** — next-generation nuclear-capable stealth bomber (replacing B-2)
- **Sentinel ICBM** — replacing Minuteman III; ~$200B program over decades
- **Mission Systems** — radars, electronic warfare, C5ISR
- **Space Systems** — military satellites, JWST-class instruments
- **Golden Dome layered missile defense** — co-prime alongside [[LMT]], [[RTX]]

## Bull case

- **B-21 Raider** is the most expensive defense program currently in build phase — multi-decade revenue runway. First flight already achieved; entering low-rate production.
- **Sentinel ICBM** is a $200B+ program over its life. NOC is the prime.
- **Moving-Target-Indicator satellites — $500M → $8.8B FY26 (17.6x growth, NEW 2026-05-14).** Per [[2026-05-14-space-economy-budget-sources]] (Aerospace Corp FY26 Budget Brief). Two new capabilities funded: (1) **ground-moving-target-indicator satellites** replacing E-8C JSTARS (retired 2023) — tracking adversary surface targets; (2) **air-moving-target-indicator satellites** replacing E-3 Sentry AWACS + cancelled E-7 Wedgetail — tracking airborne/maritime threats. **NOC's radar + EW heritage + E-8 JSTARS legacy positions them as a primary beneficiary.** Caveat: House Appropriations and HASC propose reinstating E-7 Wedgetail, which could push MTI satellite revenue ramp out by 1-2 years. **Which means:** NOC is now positioned to capture material share of THREE mega-programs simultaneously (B-21 + Sentinel + MTI satellites), each multi-decade — strongest backlog-quality narrative among the primes.
- **Golden Dome layered missile defense** — co-prime in $24.4B reconciliation program; tracking-threats-from-space category alone is $9.2B; space-based missile interceptors $5.6B (first since Reagan SDI). NOC's space systems + radar IP positions them as a Tier 1 beneficiary.
- **Record $95.7B backlog at end of FY25** — multi-year revenue visibility
- **2026 sales guide $43.5-44B** with margin expansion expected
- **FCF +26% YoY to $3.3B in FY25** — cash returning machine
- **Stock +29% YTD 2026** — market is recognizing the modernization-cycle leadership
- **Space Force tailwind** — FY27 Space Force request **$71B (+77% over FY26)** per CSIS

## Bear case / risks

- **Stock has run +29% YTD.** Most of the modernization-wave re-rating has happened. Wait for pullback.
- **Sentinel program cost overruns** are a recurring concern. Pentagon has flagged Sentinel as a Nunn-McCurdy breach candidate in prior cycles.
- **B-21 production rate ambiguity** — Pentagon hasn't committed to a final fleet size; could be 100 vs 200 vs 145.
- **Political risk** on defense pay + buybacks (Trump Jan 2026 EO).
- **Concentration risk** — Sentinel + B-21 = ~40%+ of forward revenue. Program slip is material.

## Key questions / what to watch

1. **Q2 2026 print** — does FY26 guide hold or get raised?
2. **B-21 production rate disclosure** — Pentagon decisions on fleet size
3. **Sentinel program milestones** — cost-overrun risk
4. **Backlog conversion** — Q-over-Q book-to-bill
5. **Golden Dome contract structure** — NOC share vs LMT, RTX
