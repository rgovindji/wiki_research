# Camtek (CAMT)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term · **Listing:** NASDAQ

## One-line thesis
Specialty inspection and metrology equipment for the **advanced packaging** layer (CoWoS, HBM, 2.5D/3D stacking) — small-cap pick-and-shovel exposure to the same CoWoS bottleneck that drives [[AMKR]] and [[ASX]].

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **AOI (automated optical inspection)** for advanced packaging lines
- Critical for HBM stack inspection and CoWoS interposer quality
- Sells to OSATs ([[ASX]], [[AMKR]]) and to fabs running advanced packaging in-house ([[TSM]])
- Indirect, but every unit of CoWoS capacity expansion pulls Camtek tools

## Bull case
- **Direct exposure to CoWoS / HBM capex** — content per wafer increases as packaging gets more complex
- **Smaller-cap execution leverage** — incremental orders move the financials more than at large peers
- Inspection/metrology is a structural growth area as nodes shrink and packaging stacks grow
- Adoption curve aligned with HBM4 / CoPoS / 16-Hi stacking ramps

## Bear case / risks
- **Customer concentration** — small set of OSAT/foundry buyers
- **Cyclicality** — equipment orders are lumpy; quarterly revenue volatile
- **Competitive landscape** — [[KLAC|KLA]], ONTO, others can compete in inspection adjacencies
- **Smaller market cap** — higher beta to the AI capex narrative
- **Limited public coverage** vs. mega-cap equipment names

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Order momentum** quarterly — leading indicator for CoWoS/HBM capex
2. **Win rates** at [[TSM|TSMC]], Samsung, SK Hynix, Amkor, ASE
3. **Gross margin direction** — pricing power as advanced packaging tightens
4. **Geographic mix** — China vs. ROW
