# CoreWeave (CRWV)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** **high** (upgraded from medium 2026-05-13 after Q1 2026 blow-out) · **Time horizon:** long-term + medium-term tactical

## One-line thesis
The largest "neocloud" — leases out high-performance GPU compute to AI labs, hyperscalers, and enterprises. **NVDA anchored the IPO with $250M + recently placed $2B more in Class A** for a 5+ GW AI factory partnership by 2030. **Q1 2026 print decisively re-rates the thesis: revenue $2.078B (+112% YoY), backlog $99.4B (~50% QoQ growth, ~2x [[NBIS]]'s $44.4B), Adj EBITDA $1.157B (56% margin), Anthropic multi-year Claude contract NEW disclosure, Meta added $21B in March.**

> **What this means:** CoreWeave has the largest contracted backlog ($99.4 billion) of any AI infrastructure pure-play in public markets. They run Anthropic's Claude (frontier-grade workload), they have 1+ gigawatt of active power, and they're building toward 8+ gigawatts by 2030. The previous "speculative AI infra" framing is wrong — this is a hyperscaler-quality scaled infrastructure business now. The remaining bear case is execution at the capex pace ($7.7B in one quarter) and debt service ($24.86B total debt).

## Bear case / risks
- **Capex burn rate is extreme.** **Q1 2026 capex $7.695B** in a single quarter — ~$31B annualized pace. **Cash went from $3.127B → $2.244B**; **total debt rose $21.4B → $24.9B (+$3.5B)**. Financed via DDTL 4.0 $8.5B facility + $2B NVDA Class A placement + operating cash flow. **Which means:** CRWV can fund this pace for ~12-18 months but **rising rates (see [[fed-policy]] and [[2026-05-13-ppi-cpi-shock-positioning]]) materially increase refinancing risk**. The 5.9% fixed rate on DDTL is fine; if 10y goes to 5%+ and the company needs to refi at higher spreads, FCF compresses.
- **Operating margin compressed despite EBITDA scaling.** Adj EBITDA margin 62% → 56% (Y/Y); Adj operating income only $21M (vs $163M Y/Y) due to capex-driven D&A. Margin trough per management guidance — needs Q2 sequential expansion to validate.
- **Customer concentration extreme.** MSFT + Meta + OpenAI + Anthropic = effectively the entire backlog. Loss of any one re-rates valuation.
- **Capital-intensive model** — high debt loads, capex cycles, fragile if rates rise / GPU prices fall
- **GPU resale risk** — older GPUs depreciate fast as Blackwell → Rubin → next ramps
- **Margin pressure** — competing against hyperscalers and other neoclouds (NBIS, Lambda, etc.) on price
- **Hyperscaler insourcing** — MSFT/META could pull workloads back in-house

> **What this means (bear case synthesis):** CRWV's bear case is no longer "does the business work?" — Q1 proved it does. The bear case is **financial leverage in a rising-rate regime**. They're funding ~$31B of annual capex via cash + debt + equity, and if rates stay higher-for-longer (per the morning's [[2026-05-13-ppi-cpi-shock-positioning]] analysis), refinancing risk compounds. Customer concentration is the second-order risk; rate sensitivity is first-order.
