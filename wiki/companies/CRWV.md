# CoreWeave (CRWV)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** medium-term tactical

## One-line thesis
The largest "neocloud" — leases out high-performance GPU compute to AI labs, hyperscalers, and enterprises. **NVDA anchored the IPO with $250M**; recently signed a Meta deal valued at **~$35B total** through 2032.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Neocloud / GPU cloud** — Tier-2 buyer of NVDA hardware, reselling GPU-hours to AI customers
- **NVDA $250M IPO anchor order** — strategic alignment + offtake signaling
- Customer base includes Microsoft, Meta, OpenAI, and others
- 2026 revenue projected **~$12.5B** (~4x [[NBIS]])

## Bull case
- **Pure-play GPU compute** supplier with hyperscaler-scale contracts
- **Meta contract ~$35B** through 2032 — material long-duration revenue
- **First-mover in neocloud** with NVDA-blessed positioning
- **Capital-intensive moat** — building data centers fast at scale is hard to replicate
- **NVDA partnership** = priority on GPU allocations

## Bear case / risks
- **Capital-intensive model** — high debt loads, capex cycles, fragile if rates rise / GPU prices fall
- **Customer concentration** — large hyperscaler contracts dominate revenue
- **GPU resale risk** — older GPUs depreciate fast as Blackwell → Rubin → next ramps
- **Margin pressure** — competing against hyperscalers and other neoclouds (NBIS, Lambda, etc.) on price
- **Hyperscaler insourcing** — MSFT/META could pull workloads back in-house

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Meta contract revenue ramp** — schedule and amount
2. **Utilization rates** on installed GPU fleet
3. **Customer concentration** disclosure
4. **Debt / capex structure** as fleet expands
5. **GPU residual value** assumptions vs. actual depreciation
