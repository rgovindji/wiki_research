# Planet Labs (PL)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term compounder with defense backlog visibility

> **Why a page now (2026-05-17 PM):** Planet Labs is **the leading public-market Earth Observation pure-play** with daily-revisit imagery + $900M defense backlog from NGA + DIA. Q1 2026 revenue $66.3M (+10% YoY); non-GAAP gross margin 59% and climbing. Surfaced via [[2026-05-17-space-aerospace-multi-agent]] Agent Q.

## One-line thesis
Planet Labs operates the **largest commercial Earth Observation constellation** (~200+ satellites) providing daily-revisit imagery to defense + climate + commercial customers. **Which means** PL captures the structural shift of satellite imagery from one-off purchases to subscription-data-feeds — the SaaS-ification of space data.

## Role in AI / EO
- **AI-on-satellites**: Tasking + analytics; ML for imagery analysis at scale
- **Daily-revisit imagery**: highest cadence commercial EO available
- **Defense customer base**: NGA (National Geospatial-Intelligence Agency), DIA (Defense Intelligence Agency) drive recurring revenue
- **Climate / sustainability**: agriculture, deforestation, carbon credits

## Bull case
- **$900M defense backlog** = multi-year revenue visibility (NGA + DIA)
- **59% gross margin climbing** — software-style economics on hardware-heavy stack
- **Daily-revisit moat**: ~200 satellite constellation is meaningfully larger than competitors (Maxar/Vantor, BlackSky)
- **NGA Luno A contract** ($290M, signed 2023) — recurring AI-imagery service contract
- **Climate / ESG tailwind**: carbon credit verification + agricultural monitoring drive secular demand growth
- **AI imagery analysis layer**: PL has built ML pipelines on its own data — captures both data AND analytics revenue
- **Public-market liquidity**: NYSE-listed pure-play vs private Maxar/Vantor

## Bear case / risks
- **Slow revenue growth (+10% Q1 2026)** vs other space-related names — capex-heavy expansion ratio
- **Constellation refresh capex**: 5-7 yr satellite lifespan = continuous launch cadence
- **SpaceX as launch supplier dependency**: needs ride-share access
- **Customer concentration**: government deals are lumpy; loss of NGA contract = material
- **Competitive pressure from cheaper imagery**: BlackSky, Maxar, ICEYE (SAR), and private players
- **Lumpy revenue recognition** — defense contracts can be back-end loaded
- **Stock has been volatile** vs other space names
- **No clear path to GAAP profitability** through 2026; cash burn continues

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Q2 2026 revenue growth** — accelerating from +10% or steady?
2. **Defense backlog conversion** — multi-year NGA + DIA contracts ramping
3. **Climate contract wins** — any major agriculture / carbon-credit deals
4. **Constellation refresh cycle**: capex pace + launch cadence
5. **AI imagery analytics ARR** — Planet Insights Platform adoption
6. **NGA Luno A renewal** in 2026-2027
