# MediaTek (2454.TW / MDTKF)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium-high · **Time horizon:** long-term (multi-year) with tactical 6-18mo window through fall 2026 RTX Spark launch · **Listing:** TWSE 2454 (primary), OTC ADR MDTKF (illiquid, ~1:1 sponsored)

> **Why this page was opened 2026-06-01:** the wiki had a gap at the **Arm-licensee + NVDA-co-design-partner** layer below [[ARM]]. The Grace CPU partnership for RTX Spark — formally unveiled today at Computex Taipei but rooted in **17 months of public co-design** dating to CES January 2025 — promotes MediaTek from "smartphone SoC + connectivity pure-play" to **"data-center-adjacent + Windows-AI-PC platform partner + custom AI ASIC merchant for one named US hyperscaler"**. Three independent revenue legs (smartphone Dimensity, automotive Dimensity Auto + NVIDIA DRIVE Thor, custom hyperscaler ASIC) now sit alongside the legacy connectivity moat. Wiki promotion is structurally overdue.

## One-line thesis
The Taiwanese SoC house quietly accumulating **three NVIDIA co-design partnerships at once** (GB10 DGX Spark → RTX Spark Windows AI-PC → Dimensity Auto Cockpit + DRIVE Thor) while also winning a **$2B Q4 2026 first-revenue ramp** on its first hyperscaler custom AI ASIC — all on top of a #1-share Wi-Fi 7 connectivity moat and 40%+ premium-tier Dimensity share in China. The "boring" Arm-licensee that's increasingly the picks-and-shovels under the picks-and-shovels.

## Role in the NVDA stack / AI capex cycle

MediaTek is the rare Asian SoC house with **three concurrent NVIDIA co-design touchpoints**, each at a different layer of the stack:

| Layer | Product | What MediaTek contributes | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Personal AI supercomputer** | **NVIDIA DGX Spark** (originally "Project DIGITS") with **GB10 Grace Blackwell Superchip** | 20-core Arm v9 Grace CPU primary IP + power-efficiency + connectivity (NVIDIA contributes C2C + Display IP + Blackwell GPU) | Unveiled CES Jan 2025; **shipping mid-2026** |
| **Windows AI-PC** | **NVIDIA RTX Spark superchip** (formerly N1X) — 20-core Grace CPU + Blackwell RTX GPU 6,144 CUDA cores + NPU + 128GB unified LPDDR5X on TSMC 3nm | Co-designed Grace CPU + chassis-level platform IP | **Computex Taipei 2026-06-01 reveal**; ship fall 2026 in laptops from MSFT Surface, [[DELL]], HP, ASUS, Lenovo, MSI |
| **Automotive cockpit** | **Dimensity Auto Cockpit C-X1 / C-Y1 / C-M1 / C-V1** (TSMC 3nm) paired with **NVIDIA DRIVE AGX Thor** | Cockpit SoC silicon + DriveOS integration + cabin-driver fusion | C-X1 unveiled Shanghai Auto Show April 2025; design wins ramping 2026-2027 |

**Which means**: every wiki bull pillar around NVIDIA platform expansion outside the data center — PC TAM, automotive AI, personal supercomputer — has MediaTek as the silicon-partner counterpart. The relationship is **structural and bilateral**, not one-off. The Grace CPU IP relationship in particular is now a **17-month-deep co-design partnership** (Jan 2025 CES → June 2026 Computex), which is the strongest signal NVIDIA has chosen MediaTek as its long-term Arm CPU partner outside its own Vera/Olympus design work. (See [[ARM]] for the v9 royalty pull-through; [[NVDA]] for the platform context; [[TSM]] for the 3nm foundry under every product.)

## Bull case

- **Three concurrent NVIDIA platform partnerships is the cleanest single non-NVDA AI bet on the Taiwanese exchange.** GB10 (DGX Spark, shipping mid-2026) + RTX Spark (Windows AI-PC, ship fall 2026) + Dimensity Auto Cockpit (with DRIVE Thor) — all on **TSMC 3nm**, all using MediaTek's Arm-IP CPU expertise, all anchored by 17 months of co-design history. **Which means**: MediaTek is now the *de facto* "AI-PC + automotive AI" Arm CPU vendor for NVIDIA, parallel to how AMD owns the x86 Ryzen AI side. The PC TAM that NVIDIA is opening fall 2026 has MediaTek's name on the silicon.

- **First custom AI ASIC ramping to $2B revenue Q4 FY2026 — first hyperscaler customer named (US).** CEO Rick Tsai on Q1 FY2026 call: *"our first AI accelerator ASIC project for a US Hyperscale customer is progressing fairly well... we now expect AI ASICs business to contribute around $2 billion in revenue in the fourth quarter of this year."* A second ASIC project targeted for mass production by end of FY 2027, with "several new data center ASIC opportunities in late-stage discussion." **Which means**: MediaTek now has a tangible custom-XPU revenue line competing in the same market [[AVGO]] dominates with its 6-hyperscaler portfolio. A second leg of growth that is *not* correlated to the smartphone cycle and *not* on the same depreciation curve as Dimensity. The $2B Q4 number is already 5-7% of quarterly revenue at the scale MediaTek runs.

- **Dimensity 9500 has narrowed the Snapdragon performance gap AND captured >40% of Chinese premium-tier flagship sockets.** Per multiple 2026 benchmark reviews, the Dimensity 9500 now operates "in the same performance class" as Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 on CPU/GPU, with MediaTek ahead on GPU efficiency and thermals. MediaTek has **>40% share in Chinese-brand super-premium phones** (OPPO Find X9 Ultra, Vivo X-series flagships, Xiaomi premium tier). **Which means**: the historical "MediaTek is the cheap-phone chipmaker" framing is structurally out of date — the Dimensity premium-tier ramp is a material ASP-mix-shift story even as global smartphone unit volumes contract 15% in FY2026.

- **#1 share in Wi-Fi 7 connectivity is the under-discussed structural moat.** Filogic platform spans consumer routers → enterprise APs → client devices. **Which means**: as Wi-Fi 7 transitions from early-adopter to mainstream 2026-2027, MediaTek captures the share across the entire stack. This is a connectivity-royalty business with stable margins that hedges the cyclical Dimensity smartphone leg.

- **Smart Edge Platforms +13% YoY in Q1 FY2026** while smartphones declined 15% YoY — proves the diversification is real, not narrative. Segment mix at Q1 was Smartphone 49% / Smart Edge 46% — the closest to parity in MediaTek's history. **Which means**: even with the smartphone cyclical drag, total revenue declined only 2.7% YoY because edge platforms are now 46% of the business and growing. The "smartphone pure-play" framing is empirically dead.

- **TSMC 3nm leading-edge access is a competitive moat.** Every flagship MediaTek product (Dimensity 9500, GB10 Grace, RTX Spark Grace, Dimensity Auto C-X1) is on TSMC N3 family. Per [[TSM]] Q1 2025 disclosure, NVDA at 19% is now TSMC's #1 customer — and MediaTek's N3 designs share priority allocation with NVDA via the co-design relationship. **Which means**: in a 3nm-constrained world, MediaTek has structural supply priority that Qualcomm (still mostly on Samsung Foundry for some flagship tiers) does not.

- **Long-term Arm v9 royalty lever stacks with [[ARM]] bull thesis.** Every premium Dimensity SoC, every Grace CPU MediaTek co-designs, every Dimensity Auto SoC pays Arm a v9 royalty. **Which means**: MediaTek is one of the largest single Arm royalty *contributors* in the world — a high-volume, high-mix-shift partner whose growth directly translates to [[ARM]] revenue. Holding both is a correlated bet; the wiki should treat the pair as one Arm-ecosystem position.

## Bear case / risks

- **Smartphone-cycle exposure remains a real overhang.** Smartphone was 49% of Q1 FY2026 revenue and is **declining 15% YoY** with global handset shipments forecast to drop ~15% in FY2026. **Which means**: even with Smart Edge offsetting, the largest single segment is in cyclical decline through at least H1 FY2026. Management guides recovery in H2; if that slips, the FY2026 mid-to-high single-digit growth guide is at risk. (Tied to [[AAPL]] / global smartphone TAM bear thesis surfaced in [[2026-05-09-dwarkesh-dylan-semianalysis]] — memory rationing taking smartphone units to 500-600M by 2027.)

- **Operating margin compression is the headline concern: 15.8% Q1 FY26 vs 20.0% Q1 FY25** (−420 bps YoY). Driven by elevated smartphone costs *"driven by industry resource concentration in data centers"* — MediaTek pays more for memory, substrates, and CoWoS-adjacent packaging because the data-center buildout is sucking capacity out of consumer supply chains. **Which means**: the same AI-capex cycle that is bullish for MediaTek's data-center ASIC + AI-PC leg is *cannibalizing* its margin profile on the smartphone leg. Net effect on FY2026 EPS is mixed; mid-to-high single-digit revenue growth doesn't translate cleanly to operating leverage.

- **Qualcomm remains the global flagship Android leader and US-market gatekeeper.** Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 is the default flagship socket in Samsung Galaxy S-series globally + every Android flagship in the US carrier channel. MediaTek's 40%+ share in Chinese-brand premium is real but **does not extend to the US carrier-distributed flagship market** — the highest-ASP, highest-margin smartphone segment in the world. **Which means**: the Dimensity premium-tier story is regional (China + EMEA), not global. Geopolitical risk: if US-China decoupling escalates, MediaTek's premium-tier China exposure becomes a liability, not an asset.

- **The "AI ASIC business" is one named customer.** $2B Q4 FY2026 revenue is concentrated in a single US hyperscaler. A second project targets end-FY2027 mass production. **Which means**: customer concentration risk is real until the second ramp lands; MediaTek's custom-XPU business is *one quarter* of disclosed scaling, vs [[AVGO]]'s 6-hyperscaler portfolio that is already $20B+ run-rate.

- **OTC ADR (MDTKF) is illiquid; primary listing is TWSE 2454.** Daily MDTKF ADR volume is typically <10,000 shares — institutional sizing requires the Taiwan listing, which has its own access friction (currency, Taiwan account, TWSE settlement). **Which means**: most US retail and many US institutional investors cannot easily size into the position. This is a structural valuation discount that may persist regardless of fundamentals. (Compare [[IBDXY]] which has the same problem; [[ATSAY]] partially solved via ADR liquidity.)

- **Valuation is full at TWD 4,310 vs TWD 3,327 consensus PT (13% above PT).** The stock has rallied from 52-week low TWD 1,130 to a peak TWD 4,690 — **+281% from trough** in ~12 months. Even with the bull thesis fully intact, entry-price discipline matters. **Which means**: the easy money has been made; current position-sizing should weight the "AI ASIC + RTX Spark optionality already in the price" risk.

- **Taiwan geographic concentration.** Headquartered in Hsinchu; primary listing TWSE; the [[TSM]] geographic risk transmits to MediaTek directly. **Which means**: any Taiwan Strait escalation re-prices MediaTek alongside [[TSM]] and [[ASML]]'s Taiwan exposure.

- **No NVIDIA equity stake.** Unlike [[CRWV]] / [[NBIS]] / [[IREN]] (warrant) / [[NOK]] / [[COHR]] / [[LITE]] / [[GLW]] / [[SNPS]], MediaTek does *not* have NVIDIA equity investment confirming the partnership at the balance-sheet level. The Grace co-design is a contractual / IP relationship only. **Which means**: NVIDIA could pivot to an alternative Arm partner (custom Olympus / Vera in-house, or a second supplier) without ownership friction. Bull case "structural partnership" is contingent on continued contract renewal.

## Key questions / what to watch

1. **RTX Spark commercial unit volumes H2 2026 → 2027** — the first hard test of MediaTek's PC-platform revenue line. OEM mix (Surface, [[DELL]], HP, ASUS, Lenovo, MSI) is broad enough to support a multi-million-unit run-rate; the test is sell-through at the premium AI-PC SKU price points.

2. **Whether Grace CPU partnership extends to Vera CPU follow-on.** Vera CPU is "in full production" per Jensen at Computex 2026-06-01 with Anthropic / OpenAI / SpaceX AI as customers. If MediaTek co-designed Grace, the natural progression is to participate in Vera. Confirmation event would be the highest-leverage data point for the bull thesis.

3. **Dimensity 9500 + 9600 socket share at premium tier in China and EMEA.** Watch OPPO Find X10 series, Vivo X300/X300 Pro, Xiaomi 16 Ultra design-win disclosures through 2H 2026.

4. **Automotive Dimensity Auto C-X1 design wins.** The platform launched April 2025 paired with NVIDIA DRIVE Thor. First production vehicle ramps expected 2027 model year. Track which automakers pick the C-X1 + Thor combo vs Qualcomm Snapdragon Ride or in-house silicon (Tesla, BYD).

5. **AI ASIC second-customer disclosure timing.** CEO said "several new data center ASIC opportunities in late-stage discussion" on Q1 FY2026 call. Confirmation of a second named hyperscaler would de-concentrate the ASIC revenue line and validate that MediaTek's custom-silicon design service is a competitive offering vs [[AVGO]].

6. **Q2 FY2026 print (expected late July 2026)** — gross margin trajectory (46.0% ±1.5pp guide), smartphone segment recovery into H2, AI ASIC milestone updates.

7. **Wi-Fi 7 adoption rate in enterprise + consumer.** Filogic platform is positioned to capture the Wi-Fi 7 transition; track quarterly connectivity revenue disclosure.

8. **OPEX trajectory.** Q2 FY26 guide is OpEx ratio 31.0% ±2pp — already elevated. Watch whether GPM expansion to mid-50s offsets, or whether 2027 needs an OPEX-funded second-product-cycle catch-up.
