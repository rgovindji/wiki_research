# TransDigm Group (TDG)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term compounder

> **Why a page now (2026-05-17 PM):** TransDigm is **the "boring" aerospace aftermarket compounder** with private-equity-style M&A discipline. Q1 2026 EPS $8.23 (+5% adj); aftermarket +7% organic; **Steve Cohen's Point72 added TDG to Q1 2026 13F** per [[2026-05-17-robotics-multi-agent-batch]] context. 50%+ EBITDA margins. ## One-line thesis
TransDigm captures the **aerospace aftermarket rent** — proprietary parts (fasteners, seals, hydraulics, ignition systems) sold into 22,000+ aircraft installed base at recurring, high-margin economics. **Which means** TDG compounds revenue at 10%+ CAGR with 50%+ EBITDA margins because fleet aging + limited airframe deliveries + structural aftermarket shortage = pricing power.

## Role in AI / aerospace
TDG is NOT an AI-native play. But it benefits from:
- **Defense ramp** (Patriot/PAC-3, missile production scaling) — supply-chain components into Lockheed/Raytheon programs
- **AI predictive maintenance at GE/RTX** — drives parts-replacement frequency optimization but doesn't reduce TOTAL parts demand
- **Aging fleet thesis**: commercial aircraft flying 95%+ load factors with limited new deliveries (787 ramping slowly, 777X 2027+) = aftermarket structural shortage

## Bull case
- **Structural aftermarket shortage thesis**: commercial aircraft utilization + aging fleet = compounder
- **50%+ EBITDA margins** — best-in-class for aerospace
- **Private-equity-style M&A discipline**: $3.16B acquisitions 2026 with disciplined returns (Calspan, etc.)
- **Aftermarket +7% organic** + ~10%+ inorganic (M&A) = double-digit total growth
- **Defense ramp tailwind**: Patriot/PAC-3 scaling 600→2,000/yr by 2030 → component demand
- **Sticky customer base**: proprietary parts have high switching costs
- **Cohen (Point72) Q1 2026 13F position** — tier-1 smart-money endorsement
- **34.5x P/E premium but 21.1x EV/EBITDA at 10-yr median** — premium for earnings *quality*, not over-extension on EV multiples

## Bear case / risks
- **34.5x P/E** is the most-stretched aerospace multiple — multiple compression risk in any broader sell-off ([[bank-of-japan]] carry-trade scenario)
- **Commercial cycle exposure**: 60% revenue commercial; cycle compression hits aftermarket lag
- **Acquisition integration risk**: $3.16B 2026 M&A volume = execution risk
- **Customer concentration**: Boeing/Airbus/Lockheed top customers
- **Higher interest rates compress LBO-style M&A returns**: TDG model relies on debt-financed acquisitions
- **AI/automation in aerospace MRO** — could compress aftermarket frequency if predictive maintenance reduces replacement cycles (longer-term thesis)
- **Macro multiple compression** — TDG has historically traded as a quality compounder; any sentiment shift hits multiple first
