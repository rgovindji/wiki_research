# Celestica Inc. (CLS)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium-high · **Time horizon:** 2026-2028 hyperscaler 1.6T networking + AI/ML compute cycle

> **Why this page was opened 2026-05-29:** the wiki had a gap at the **hyperscaler contract-manufacturing / ODM layer for networking specifically** — separate from [[JBL]] (Jabil, diversified across AI servers + humanoids + medical) and [[FLEX]] (Flex, diversified contract mfg). Celestica is **the cleanest pure-play public name on hyperscaler networking ODM** — specifically the 1.6T switch ramp that powers [[ANET]] / [[AVGO]] / [[CSCO]] / NVDA Spectrum-X. Q1 2026 print on April 28 raised FY26 guide by $2B to $19B with **10 active 1.6T networking programs** disclosed. Per [[2026-05-28-DELL-Q1-FY27-earnings]] read-through: DELL's $51.3B AI backlog pulls networking via ANET, which pulls Celestica via the 1.6T contract.

## One-line thesis

**The single most direct hyperscaler-networking-ODM beneficiary the wiki has identified.** Q1 2026: rev **$4.05B (+53% YoY)**; HPS (Hardware Platform Solutions) $1.7B (+63% YoY, now 42% of revenue); CCS (Connectivity & Cloud Solutions) 80% of total. **FY26 guide raised by $2B to $19B**; adj EPS $10.15. **10 active 1.6T networking programs ramping** = direct read-through to ANET / AVGO networking volume + Google + Meta hyperscale AI/ML compute. **2025 revenue $12.39B (+28.46%); 2025 earnings +94.51% to $832.5M.** Stock had ATH closing **$422.21 on April 27, 2026** — the AI-CM trade has worked exceptionally well but the market is now pricing in the explosive momentum. **Which means** CLS gives you "DELL-upstream-networking exposure" with structurally lower beta than ANET itself, plus diversification through HPS into AI/ML compute platforms.

> **What this means:** Celestica builds the actual hardware that Arista designs and that Meta + Google deploy. The 1.6T networking ramp doesn't happen without Celestica. The risk is that contract manufacturers historically have low margins and high customer concentration — and CLS at 8% adj op margin is now at a multi-year high that's likely to compress as the AI build-out matures. But the next 12-18 months of 1.6T volume ramp is structurally locked in.

## Role in the AI infrastructure stack

- **Hyperscaler ODM contract manufacturer** — Google + Meta are the two largest customers; AWS exposure smaller; Microsoft minimal historically
- **Connectivity & Cloud Solutions (CCS)** segment = 80% of revenue; covers networking switches, storage, optical networking, communications infrastructure
- **Hardware Platform Solutions (HPS)** subsegment within CCS = $1.7B Q1 2026 (42% of total revenue); ramping fast on AI/ML compute platforms (custom server designs for hyperscalers — competes with Foxconn / Quanta / Wiwynn at the disaggregated-rack layer)
- **10 active 1.6T networking programs** disclosed Q1 2026 — likely Arista 7800R4 + AVGO Tomahawk 6 + Cisco / Juniper next-gen platforms
- **800G + 1.6T optical networking + AI/ML compute** — three concurrent ramps
- **Advanced Technology Solutions (ATS)** segment — aerospace + defense + industrial + healthcare; less AI-correlated but provides margin diversification
- Upstream: [[AVGO]] (Tomahawk + Jericho ASICs), [[ANET]] (Arista EOS-based switches), various semis
- Downstream: [[GOOGL]] (custom networking + TPU support), [[META]] (custom networking + MTIA support), [[AMZN]] (smaller), increasingly hyperscaler-direct on AI/ML
- Competitors: Foxconn (private; Apple + NVDA partner), Quanta (Taiwan-listed), Wiwynn (Taiwan-listed; Meta + Microsoft focus), Inventec, Compal, [[JBL]] (Jabil — Apptronik humanoid + medical + diversified), [[FLEX]] (Flex — diversified)

## Bull case

- **The 1.6T networking ramp is contractually locked in.** 10 active programs disclosed = multi-year volume commitments from hyperscaler customers, not speculative pipeline. **Which means** the 2026-2027 revenue trajectory is structurally supported even if hyperscaler capex moderates at the margin.
- **HPS segment +63% YoY to 42% of revenue** — Celestica has successfully transitioned from "low-margin contract assembler" to "AI infrastructure platform partner." **Which means** the historical CM gross margin compression argument is less binding as long as HPS keeps growing.
- **8% adj op margin = multi-year high** at the company that historically ran 4-5% margins. **Which means** AI hyperscaler programs have **structurally higher** margin profiles than the legacy CM business — and the mix shift is intact.
- **Google + Meta = two of the strongest hyperscaler capex stories of 2026.** [[META]] capex now $125-145B for 2026 (raised May 27); [[GOOGL]] capex $190B with $460B cloud backlog (per [[2026-05-12-hyperscaler-capex-q1-revisions]]). **Which means** Celestica is positioned at the two-most-active customers in 2026 capex.
- **The wiki gap that DELL exposed.** Per [[2026-05-28-DELL-Q1-FY27-earnings]], DELL's $51.3B backlog pulls upstream networking; ANET is the design winner, but Celestica builds the actual hardware for some of those 1.6T programs. The CLS revenue line is *bookable revenue* before the hyperscaler even deploys.
- **Diversification through ATS** (aerospace + defense + industrial + healthcare) — provides margin diversification + 2 distinct demand cycles (commercial AI + defense AI). Reduces single-cycle exposure.
- **The "AI Infrastructure Winner No One Wanted This Quarter" framing** (per Seeking Alpha headline) is the bull-side framing: Celestica is the cheapest way to access the 1.6T hyperscaler networking ramp without ANET / AVGO / NVDA-Spectrum-X valuations.
- **2025 revenue +28%, earnings +94%, raising 2026 guide by $2B = +12% guide raise** — the operational momentum is the strongest in CM history.

## Bear case / risks

- **Contract manufacturer historical pattern is low margins.** Even at 8% adj op margin (Q1 2026 multi-year high), the structural ceiling on EMS / ODM business is well below product-company margins. **Which means** if hyperscaler 1.6T programs commoditize, margin compression is inevitable.
- **Customer concentration risk.** Google + Meta = significant majority of revenue. If either hyperscaler shifts share to Foxconn (which has the OpenAI Stargate relationship now) or Wiwynn (Meta has historically split with Wiwynn), revenue impact is material.
- **ATH closing $422.21 April 27** = stock has rallied massively. **Recent +28% in 2025 + +53% YoY Q1 2026 = much of the AI-CM trade is reflected in spot price**. New buyers are entering momentum, not value.
- **Hyperscaler capex moderation risk.** Per [[2026-05-16-damodaran-profg-markets]] / Damodaran's near-term-top warning, if hyperscaler capex moderates from the +77% YoY 2026 pace, CLS revenue growth slows directly — and the multiple compresses.
- **Foxconn + Wiwynn + Quanta competition.** Taiwan ODMs operate at structurally lower cost (Asia labor + supply chain integration). Celestica's North American base is more expensive; the strategic advantage is the AI/ML platform partnership, not cost.
- **1.6T = current-generation lock-in.** 3.2T networking is on the AVGO + ANET roadmap. Each generational transition reopens contract negotiations. CLS has to win each generation.
- **NVDA Spectrum-X share gain risk.** Per [[NVDA]] Q1 FY27: Networking $14.8B (+199% YoY). If hyperscalers shift from Ethernet (Arista / Celestica) to NVDA-direct Spectrum-X (NVDA NVL switching), Celestica's design-win flow slows.

## Why this fits the wiki framework

Celestica is **the hyperscaler-ODM networking layer** of the AI infrastructure stack. Specifically distinct from:
- [[JBL]] (Jabil) — diversified across AI servers + humanoids + medical + automotive
- [[FLEX]] (Flex) — diversified contract manufacturing
- [[SMCI]] (Super Micro) — server OEM, not ODM
- Foxconn / Quanta / Wiwynn — private or Taiwan-listed, not US-accessible

The "DELL-upstream-second-derivative" framing applies cleanly: DELL ships AI servers → those servers need Ethernet networking → hyperscalers buy Arista → Arista designs are built by Celestica. **Which means** CLS captures rent at one of the layers DELL itself doesn't own.
