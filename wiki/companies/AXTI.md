# AXT Inc (AXTI)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium (NOT high — China-manufacturing binary + 600% prior run cap risk-reward) · **Time horizon:** 2-5yr photonics ramp

## One-line thesis

**The pure-play public InP substrate name** — AXT supplies ~36% of the world's indium phosphide wafers (the substrate for the *laser source* in every silicon photonics system, since silicon can't emit light efficiently). Q1 2026 was a step-change: **InP revenue $13.6M (>50% of total for the first time), $100M+ InP backlog, 29.6% gross margin recovery from -6.4%, and capacity doubling 2026 + doubling again 2027** to a $65-70M quarterly InP run-rate by end-2027/early-2028. The bottleneck thesis is real. **The catch is that AXT manufactures entirely in China through Beijing Tongmei** — and China imposed indium export controls on Feb 4 2025, with Tongmei needing (and receiving in June 2025) permits to resume shipping. **The stock is up 600%+ YTD 2026 to ATH; the easy-money entry point is gone.** Treat as a 2-3% satellite position with a binary tail, not a core holding.

> **What this means:** If you believe AI photonics is the next major buildout (TSMC COUPE in production 2026, AVGO Tomahawk 6, NVDA Spectrum-X/Quantum-X CPO, 800G + 1.6T transceivers scaling), then every photonic system needs an InP-substrate laser source. AXT is one of only three credible suppliers globally (Sumitomo Electric ~42%, AXT ~36%, JX Nippon ~13%). It's a structurally good bet *operationally* but a politically fraught bet because everything is fabbed in China.

## Role in the AI / photonics stack

- **InP (Indium Phosphide) substrate** — the wafer on which laser diodes and photodetectors are fabricated; required for CW (continuous-wave) lasers, DFB lasers, EMLs (electro-absorption modulated lasers), and the laser arrays inside every co-packaged-optics (CPO) engine
- **GaAs (Gallium Arsenide) substrate** — secondary product line; used for VCSELs in short-wavelength optical applications (consumer LiDAR, optical mice, lower-end transceivers)
- **Ge (Germanium) substrate** — niche product; used in space solar cells + high-frequency electronics
- **Raw materials integration** — controls upstream supply through subsidiary Jinmei (indium and gallium refining)
- Adjacent to [[SOITEC]] (the other substrate-layer monopolist, but for *passive* photonic-SOI not active InP), [[TSM]] (COUPE customer), [[AVGO]] (Tomahawk 6 CPO downstream customer), [[NVDA]] (Spectrum-X CPO downstream), [[COHR]] / [[LITE]] (transceiver customers), [[MRVL]] (Photonic Fabric downstream)

## Bull case

- **Real bottleneck thesis with real demand.** Goldman projects 800G optical module shipments >34M units in 2026 and 1.6T >25M units in 2026 — each one needs an InP laser. TSMC COUPE enters production 2026 with 2x power efficiency vs pluggables; every COUPE engine has InP laser arrays. AVGO Tomahawk 6 ships 200G/lane CPO. NVDA Spectrum-X/Quantum-X CPO is in production. **All converging on InP demand.**
- **Q1 2026 is a genuine inflection.** Revenue +39% YoY, +17% QoQ; InP crossed 50% of revenue for first time; gross margin recovered from negative to ~30%; near non-GAAP breakeven. Step-change, not noise.
- **$100M+ InP backlog** provides demand visibility into late-2026 and early-2027 — a level of visibility very few sub-$1B specialty semiconductors have.
- **Capacity expansion is funded and underway.** Path from ~$35M/quarter end-2026 to $65-70M/quarter by late-2027 ($260-280M annualized) implies ~2x more revenue ahead even at current ASP. At $700M cap on $260M run-rate = 2.7x forward sales — not crazy expensive if execution holds.
- **Only 3 credible global InP suppliers.** Sumitomo Electric (~42%, but conglomerate-diluted), AXT (~36%, pure-play), JX Nippon (~13%, not separately tradeable). AXT is the **only public pure-play** — the only way to own this layer cleanly in the public equity market.
- **Vertically integrated raw materials.** Subsidiary Jinmei refines indium and gallium upstream — partial protection against raw material price spikes + structural cost advantage.
- **Acquisition optionality.** At $700M, AXT is a digestible target for Sumitomo (consolidate the market), [[INTC]] (vertical integration for silicon photonics), or a Chinese state-aligned acquirer (sovereign-stake on critical materials).
- **Hyperscaler photonics roadmap is locked in.** [[GOOGL]] TPU v7 + [[META]] MTIA + [[MSFT]] Maia + [[AMZN]] Trainium2/3 all require optical interconnect ramping; each generation increases InP intensity per accelerator.

## Bear case / risks

- **The stock has already run 600%+ YTD 2026 to all-time-high.** The "ignored micro-cap" entry point is gone. From ATH, expansion of the multiple is constrained — further upside requires actual operational execution + sustained demand growth, not narrative discovery. **Which means** risk-reward is meaningfully worse than 6 months ago.
- **China manufacturing concentration is the binary risk.** AXT's Beijing Tongmei subsidiary makes 100% of AXT's wafer output in China. **China imposed export controls on indium (and tungsten, tellurium, bismuth, molybdenum) on Feb 4, 2025.** Tongmei needed export permits to resume shipments — these were granted June 2025 but Q4 2025 revenue was *constrained* by permit delays. **If China tightens the export regime, AXT's revenue is capped by permit cadence regardless of demand.** This is materially the same risk class as [[ASML]] China exposure (via [[MATCH-Act]]) but AXT is much smaller and has fewer countervailing customer relationships.
- **Past restatement / SEC concerns historical baggage.** AXT has a history of accounting restatements + China-subsidiary disclosure complexity. While the company has cleaned up significantly post-2020, the underlying complexity of operating a US-listed Chinese-manufacturing company persists. Trust deficit with sell-side coverage.
- **Capacity is being added across the supply chain.** Sumitomo expanding +40% by 2026; JX +20%; new Chinese entrants. **The competitive response means the bottleneck thesis has 18-24 months of intensity, not a multi-year secular spread.**
- **Heterogeneous integration reduces InP material per device.** TSMC SoIC, Intel hybrid integration, AVGO photonic engines bond thin InP films onto silicon — reducing per-laser material consumption by 5-10x. **Volume can scale faster than material consumption.**
- **GaAs substitution at the edges.** Some applications (consumer LiDAR, short-wavelength VCSELs) can use GaAs instead of InP. Bounds the absolute InP demand growth ceiling.
- **Customer concentration risk in transceiver vendors.** AXT's InP customers are concentrated in [[COHR]], [[LITE]], [[FN]], and a handful of Chinese transceiver makers. If COHR/LITE pivot to in-house InP epi (via the NVDA-funded expansion) or if CPO displaces pluggable transceivers faster than expected, AXT customer relationships could shift.
- **No US ADR liquidity premium** — micro-cap dynamics, $700M cap below most institutional investability thresholds; volatility is retail-driven.
- **Geopolitical tail: MATCH Act / new China-export controls.** Even partial restrictions on Chinese-manufactured InP wafers entering Western markets would be catastrophic for AXT's reported revenue (since Tongmei IS where 100% of shipping originates).

> **What this means (bear synthesis):** AXT is two stocks compressed into one ticker. There's the *operational* business — real customers, real demand, real capacity expansion, real fundamentals improvement — which is bull. And there's the *geopolitical* overlay — China-only manufacturing, indium export controls, MATCH Act tail — which is binary. The conviction is medium because you can't separate them; you have to underwrite both at once. If China-US trade dynamics deteriorate, AXT's stock can drop 40-60% irrespective of operational performance. If they hold steady or improve, AXT runs another 100-200% as 2027-2028 capacity ramps. The risk-reward at $700M cap post-6x run is materially worse than it was at $100M cap.

## Investment framing for our 8-stock long-term thesis

- **AXTI fits as a satellite InP-bottleneck position, NOT a core holding** — China-manufacturing binary risk and post-6x-run valuation argue against larger sizing
- Position sizing: **2-3% maximum**, treating the China geopolitical exposure as a binary tail
- Pairs with [[SOITEC]] (passive-substrate side of the photonics stack) — together you own both substrate-monopolists. Soitec is the cleaner geopolitical bet but Paris-listed only; AXT is the US-listed pure-play but with China-manufacturing tail.
- Compared to downstream beneficiaries ([[AVGO]], [[COHR]], [[LITE]], [[NVDA]]) — AXT has more leverage per dollar of photonics demand, but more risk per dollar of geopolitical headline
- **If China-US trade regime stabilizes (or improves)** — AXT compounds attractively on capacity expansion through 2027-2028
- **If MATCH Act passes or China tightens export controls** — AXT is the most exposed name in the photonics layer; avoid or hedge with paired puts
- **The "smart" version of this trade** is owning [[AVGO]] + [[COHR]] + [[LITE]] for downstream photonics demand and *separately* sizing AXT small to capture upstream substrate-layer upside; this gives you the photonics ramp exposure without going all-in on a single Chinese-manufacturing dependency
