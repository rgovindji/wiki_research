# Arm Holdings (ARM)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term · **Listing:** NASDAQ ADR

## One-line thesis
The IP / instruction-set layer underneath the entire mobile world AND now the data center — every NVIDIA "Vera" CPU in the [[NVDA|Vera Rubin]] platform is built on Arm v9.2-A. Royalty stream scales with AI server volumes from late 2026.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **CPU architecture license** — NVIDIA's Vera CPU = 88-core Arm v9.2-A "Olympus" custom design
- Every Vera Rubin Superchip ships an Arm-based CPU paired with the Rubin GPU
- Available 2H 2026 (per CES 2026 announcement)
- Same dynamic with Grace CPU on prior generation (GH200, GB200, GB300)

## Bull case
- **Royalty model is high-margin and capital-light** — every Arm-based chip pays a royalty regardless of who designs/fabs it
- **Data center penetration accelerating** — AWS Graviton, Microsoft Cobalt, Google Axion, NVIDIA Vera/Grace = all hyperscalers + NVDA on Arm
- **AI inference at the edge** — every smartphone, IoT device, automotive SoC ships Arm; AI features pull more compute (and royalties) per device
- **v9 architecture mix-shift** — higher royalty per chip vs. v8

## Bear case / risks
- **Customer concentration / SoftBank ownership** — float is small; SoftBank still holds ~90%
- **Royalty disputes** — public spat with Qualcomm (Nuvia license) could re-pattern licensing terms
- **RISC-V** — long-term threat; growing in embedded; data center adoption nascent but rising
- **Valuation** — has historically traded at high multiples relative to its mid-teens revenue growth

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Vera Rubin volume ramps** in 2H 2026 — direct read-through to royalty growth
2. **Royalty rate trajectory** — v9 mix shift narrative
3. **Cloud / data center revenue disclosure** — what's the AI-attached share?
4. **SoftBank monetization** — any secondary offerings affecting float
