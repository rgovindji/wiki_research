# Keysight Technologies (KEYS)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
Test and measurement (T&M) leader — *not* a wafer fab equipment vendor like [[KLAC]] or [[LRCX]], but adjacent. Design verification tools for high-speed digital, RF, optical, and AI silicon — every new chip generation pulls Keysight content for design, characterization, and validation.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Design verification & validation** for AI silicon (GPUs, custom silicon, networking ASICs)
- High-speed digital + optical test for CPO and 800V power architectures
- Indirect / adjacent to NVDA's stack — used by NVDA, NVDA's customers, and the broader semis ecosystem

## Bull case
- **AI silicon complexity** drives test budgets per design — chips are denser, packaging more 3D, validation harder
- **Optical / CPO ramp** drives high-speed optical test demand
- **Recurring software / service revenue** mix grows with the installed base
- **Diversified across communications, automotive, defense, semis** — less cyclical than pure-play WFE names

## Bear case / risks
- **Lower direct AI exposure** than KLAC/LRCX/ASML — adjacent rather than core
- **Capex / orders sensitive** to customer R&D budgets
- **Currency exposure** — global revenue mix
- **Multiple vs. growth** — historically priced like a quality compounder, less like a WFE name

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Communications / Aerospace Defense / EISG segment trends**
2. **Software & subscription revenue** mix
3. **R&D customer commentary** — any signs of design-cycle pull-back?
4. **800V DC architecture-related demand** read-through
