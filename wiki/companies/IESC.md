# IES Holdings (IESC)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** 2026-2030 data center electrification cycle

## One-line thesis

**The best pure-play electrical contractor for data center wiring + low-voltage infrastructure.** Q2 FY26: revenue **$974M (+17% YoY)**, EPS **$5.44 (+56% YoY)**, **backlog $3.9B (+62% since FY25 end)** — the Goldman Sachs "wires every new data center" thesis confirmed by the numbers. Communications segment (data center + distribution) **+35% YoY revenue**, driven by data center build-out + AI campus electrification. Gulf Island acquired Jan 2026 for $143M to expand industrial electrification capability. **The catch**: stock has run substantially; Seeking Alpha consensus is "great quarter, but chasing now is unadvisable." Valuation tight at current levels.

> **What this means:** Hyperscaler campuses need miles of electrical conduit, low-voltage cabling, fiber distribution, and power systems integration BEFORE any servers go in. IES is the contractor doing that work across [[STRL]]-prepped sites. Better quality story than most electrical contractors because their data center + distribution segment is now leading growth, not lagging.

## Role in the AI / data center infrastructure stack

- **Communications segment**: data center + distribution center electrical contracting — fiber, low-voltage cabling, conduit, power distribution
- **Infrastructure Solutions**: industrial + utility electrical
- **Commercial & Industrial**: general electrical contracting
- **Residential** (legacy + smaller): homebuilder electrical
- Adjacent to [[FIX]] (mechanical contracting), [[EME]] (electrical + mechanical), [[PWR]] (specialty electrical/transmission); IESC is more pure-play DC-electrical than peers
- **Sequencing**: IES wires the building after [[STRL]] preps the site; before final fit-out

## Bull case

- **+62% backlog growth in two quarters** is exceptional for a construction-services business. Speed of bookings = customer urgency + IES winning share.
- **Communications segment +35% YoY** with the data center + distribution mix is the most relevant growth line. The data center demand thesis is showing up in the right segment.
- **+56% EPS growth on +17% revenue** = operating leverage. IES is converting growth into margin expansion, not just top-line.
- **Gulf Island acquisition** expands capability into industrial electrification (semiconductor fabs, manufacturing) — geographic + adjacency expansion.
- **Multi-segment exposure** = some downside support if one vertical (residential) softens.
- **Backlog quality**: data center work is higher-margin + lower-risk than residential. The mix shift toward Communications is structurally positive.
- **Pure-play DC electrical exposure** is rare in public markets; most electrical contractors are diversified across multiple verticals.
- **Insulated from materials price volatility** vs. heavy civil (Sterling) because electrical work is more labor-intensive + smaller materials share.

## Bear case / risks

- **Stock has run substantially YTD.** Seeking Alpha published "great quarter but chasing the stock now is unadvisable" — sell-side starting to call valuation. Future returns require continued operational beats + sustained AI capex.
- **Customer concentration in data center contractors.** Tier-1 hyperscaler campuses pull most growth; if hyperscaler 2027 capex slows, growth flattens.
- **Labor constraints in electrician supply.** Same wage-inflation + supply pressure as other construction names; could compress margins faster than revenue grows.
- **Gulf Island integration risk.** $143M acquisition is digestible but needs operational integration.
- **Residential segment cyclicality.** Some exposure to homebuilder cycle which is less correlated with AI capex.
- **Multiple sub-contractor structure.** IES Holdings runs multiple operating units which creates complexity for analysis + occasional segment surprises.
- **AI capex digest risk.** Same systemic risk as STRL, NVT, MOD, etc.
- **Permitting + utility connect delays** could push project timelines + delay revenue recognition.

> **What this means (bear synthesis):** IES Holdings has the right business in the right segment at the right time — but the stock price already reflects that. Returns from here depend on execution sustaining + AI capex curve not blinking. Higher-conviction names in the same theme might be [[STRL]] (pre-IES in the sequence, with even higher growth) or [[NVT]] (better pure-play, even more leverage). IESC is a high-quality 2-3% position, not a 5-8% conviction bet.
