# Microsoft (MSFT)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
The enterprise AI distribution leader — Azure scale + tight OpenAI partnership + Copilot embedded across Microsoft 365 = the highest-quality way to monetize enterprise AI demand at scale.

## Bull case
- **Azure + OpenAI** = enterprise AI distribution at scale; M365 Copilot is a per-seat AI product with the world's stickiest enterprise install base
- **Cloud + software cross-sell** — every M365 customer is an Azure prospect and vice versa
- **AI capex leader** but capex is fully cash-funded by the existing software business
- **Enterprise lock-in** — multi-year EAs, identity (Entra/AD), Office productivity make competitive displacement extremely hard
- **GitHub + dev tools** — early adopter ecosystem for AI coding assistants

## Bear case / risks
- **OpenAI partnership concentration risk.** Heavy dependence on a single AI lab partner. Strategic reorganization at OpenAI could change the economics.
- **Capex burden.** AI capex weighs on FCF in 2026; valuation depends on monetization keeping pace.
- **Multiple compression** alongside Mag 7 broadly (see [[valuation-environment]], [[market-concentration]]).
- **Pricing power on Copilot.** Per-seat AI pricing is being tested in the market — if attach rates disappoint, the AI revenue narrative resets.
- **Antitrust scrutiny** on OpenAI partnership, Activision integration.

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Azure growth rate** — vs. AWS / GCP, especially the AI-attached portion
2. **Copilot attach rate** — what % of M365 seats convert at the higher AI price point
3. **Capex trajectory** — when does it inflect/normalize as a share of revenue?
4. **OpenAI dynamics** — any change in commercial terms or product overlap
5. **Maia silicon traction** — does internal silicon reduce NVDA dependence?
