# Fabrinet (FN)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
Specialized contract manufacturer for optical transceivers and modules — pulls revenue from [[NVDA]], Cisco, AWS, [[LITE]], and others. Throughput is currently constrained by upstream EML (electro-absorption modulated laser) supply, not by Fabrinet's own capacity.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Manufactures transceivers and modules** for NVDA and downstream networking customers
- **Adjacent to** [[COHR]] / [[LITE]] but at a lower-margin contract-manufacturing tier
- Volume directly tied to AI fabric build-out

## Bull case
- **Pure-play exposure** to AI optical build-out without the design-house valuation premium
- **Customer breadth** — NVDA, Cisco, AWS, Lumentum, multiple optical OEMs
- **Quality reputation** in precision optics manufacturing — high switching costs for customers
- **Operating leverage** if EML constraints ease

## Bear case / risks
- **Throughput bottleneck is upstream** — EML supply (mainly [[LITE]]/[[COHR]]) caps near-term growth
- **Contract manufacturing margins** are thinner than design houses
- **Customer concentration** — relatively few large customers
- **Optics trade volatility** — same May 2026 cool-off that hit COHR/LITE
- **Smaller cap** — higher volatility

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **EML supply trends** at COHR / LITE — easing eases Fabrinet's bottleneck
2. **Operating margin direction** as mix shifts higher-spec
3. **Customer concentration disclosures**
4. **Cisco / hyperscaler demand commentary**

## Recent developments
- **2026-05 (recent Q)** — Set **company records on both revenue and EPS**. Management explicitly stated on the call that **data center optical module demand continues to outstrip supply**. Stock initially declined post-print (sector "sell the news") but participated in the May 11 optics-trade rebound.
