# Lockheed Martin (LMT)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis

Largest US defense prime — F-35 program backbone + Sentinel ICBM modernization + Aegis missile defense + space systems = multi-decade revenue lock with Golden Dome upside. Q1 2026 revenue $18.0B (+9.1% YoY), cash-flow heavy with ~3% dividend yield. **Mature compounder, not a hypergrowth story.**

> **What this means:** Lockheed is the steady defense dividend payer that benefits from every modernization cycle — the F-35 is still rolling out, the ICBM force is being replaced, and the Golden Dome missile defense program is a new mega-revenue stream. Not exciting, but a high-quality hold whose value goes up with every geopolitical event.

## Role in aerospace-defense

- **F-35 program prime** — 3,500+ aircraft commitment globally; multi-decade sustainment revenue
- **Sentinel ICBM** (with [[NOC]]) — replacing Minuteman III
- **Aegis missile defense** — naval + ground-based variants
- **Space Systems** — military satellites, space-based missile warning, GPS III
- **Golden Dome layered missile defense** — co-prime alongside NOC, RTX

## Bull case

- **F-35 sustainment is structural.** Long-life cycle revenue from existing fleet (~1,000 delivered; 3,500+ planned). Multi-decade lock-in.
- **Golden Dome layered missile defense** — new mega-program; LMT is a co-prime with multi-billion contract exposure
- **Q1 2026 rev $18.0B (+9.1% YoY)** validates the modernization wave
- **Sentinel ICBM** modernization adds another multi-decade program
- **Aegis missile defense** demand surging on global threats (Iran, Taiwan, Russia)
- **Dividend yield ~3%** with consistent buybacks — total-return story
- **Defense budget tailwind.** $40.2B Space Force FY26 budget + broader DoD spending = funding floor

## Bear case / risks

- **Mature growth profile.** 9% revenue growth is solid but not multiple-expansion territory. LMT is a cash-flow story, not a P/E expansion story.
- **F-35 cost pressure.** Pentagon has periodically pushed for price concessions on F-35 sustainment contracts. Margin sensitivity.
- **Political risk on defense pay + buybacks.** Trump's Jan 2026 executive order on defense contractor pay + buybacks is a marginal headwind.
- **Backlog conversion + execution risk** on multi-year programs (Sentinel cost overruns historically a concern).
- **China + Iran tail risk works both ways** — defense spending tailwind is also geopolitical risk that compresses equity multiples broadly.

## Key questions / what to watch

1. **Q2 2026 print** — does $18B+ run-rate hold?
2. **F-35 sustainment contract renegotiations** — Pentagon pricing pressure
3. **Golden Dome contract structure** — LMT share vs NOC, RTX, Anduril, RKLB
4. **Sentinel program milestones** — cost overrun risk
5. **Buyback pace** — given Trump executive order
