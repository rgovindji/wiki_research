# Marvell Technology (MRVL)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium-high · **Time horizon:** 2026-2028 hyperscaler custom-XPU + 1.6T/3.2T optical interconnect cycle

> **Why this page was opened 2026-06-01:** the wiki had MRVL on the "not yet covered" list (per [[watchlist]] "next research targets"), with the **Celestial AI Photonic Fabric acquisition** (announced Dec 2025, closed Feb 2026 at $3.25B) already referenced inside [[SOITEC]] and [[2026-05-17-photonic-memory-stack]]. Two consecutive material prints — **Q4 FY26** in March 2026 and now **Q1 FY27 on May 27, 2026** — combined with the **+130% YTD rally** and stock at **ATH $218.26 on May 27** justify a tier-1 promotion. MRVL is the **custom-silicon companion to [[AVGO]]** and the **optical-DSP companion to [[COHR]] / [[LITE]] / [[FN]] / [[GLW]]** — two distinct AI-infrastructure-stack roles in a single ticker. The page is opened **medium-high not high** because (a) [[AVGO]] dominates the custom-silicon scoreboard at 6 hyperscalers vs MRVL's 3, (b) the +130% YTD means new entry is into momentum, not dislocation, and (c) NVIDIA's $2B investment + NVLink Fusion partnership (Mar 2026) is a double-edged sword — validating but also containing.

## One-line thesis

**The second-leg custom-AI-silicon play to [[AVGO]] + the cleanest pure-play public optical-DSP franchise.** Q1 FY27: revenue **$2.418B (+28% YoY)** record; **Data Center $1.833B = 76% of revenue (+27% YoY)**; Q2 guide **$2.7B (+35% YoY)** with non-GAAP EPS guide $0.93. **FY27 outlook raised by $5B to $11.5B (+40% YoY)** — one of the largest single-quarter guide raises in semis. **Interconnect growth raised from 50% → 70%+** driven by 1.6T PAM4 ramps + DCI modules + scale-up optics. **Three custom-silicon anchors**: AWS Trainium (visibility through Trainium3 in 2026+), Microsoft Maia (silicon IP + back-end design services), Google Axion CPU + adjacent. **Strategic moves**: closed **Celestial AI $3.25B** Feb 2026 (Photonic Fabric for scale-up optical interconnect) + announced **Polariton Technologies April 2026** (plasmonics-based modulation for 3.2T+ optical) + **NVIDIA $2B strategic investment + NVLink Fusion partnership** announced March 2026. **Stock**: ATH **$218.26 (May 27)**, market cap **~$179B**, **+130%+ YTD**, ~70x trailing P/E.

> **What this means:** MRVL has built — through two acquisitions in 90 days plus the NVIDIA partnership — the only public-market vertically-integrated stack covering custom silicon for hyperscalers (the AVGO-adjacent franchise) AND the optical interconnect layer end-to-end (PAM4 DSP + coherent DSP + Photonic Fabric scale-up + plasmonics-based 3.2T roadmap). The Q1 FY27 print confirmed both legs are accelerating simultaneously — Data Center +27% YoY and Interconnect raised to +70%+ YoY. The risk is that AVGO continues to dominate the custom-silicon scoreboard and that the NVDA NVLink Fusion partnership signals "MRVL is the friendly competitor NVDA is willing to coexist with" — which is bullish for revenue but caps the multiple expansion ceiling vs. the "non-NVDA hedge" framing AVGO has.

## Role in the AI infrastructure stack

MRVL plays at **two distinct layers** that no other public name owns simultaneously:

**1. Custom AI silicon for hyperscalers (the AVGO-adjacent franchise)**
- **AWS Trainium** — visibility extends through **Trainium3 in 2026+**. Marvell does the silicon back-end (physical design, IP, fab/packaging interface) on AWS-architected accelerators
- **Microsoft Maia** — silicon IP + back-end design services for the Maia accelerator family
- **Google Axion CPU + adjacent** custom programs — the third anchor
- **Compares to [[AVGO]]**: AVGO ships 6 hyperscaler XPU programs (Google TPU, Meta MTIA, OpenAI Project Titan, ByteDance, Anthropic-via-TPU, +1) at much larger run rates; MRVL ships 3 at smaller scale but is growing faster on a smaller base
- **Compares to [[ALAB]]**: Astera is a smaller pure-play at the PCIe retimer + Scorpio AI fabric layer; MRVL is broader (DSPs + custom silicon + CXL controllers + Photonic Fabric)

**2. Optical interconnect (the Inphi-heritage franchise)**
- **PAM4 DSPs at 100/200G per lane** — the workhorse silicon inside every 400G + 800G optical transceiver (Inphi acquisition 2021)
- **1.6T PAM4 + scale-up optics** — ramping now per Q1 FY27 disclosure; the cycle [[ANET]] / [[CLS]] are also riding
- **Coherent DSPs for ZR / ZR+ / DCI** — long-haul optical interconnect for hyperscaler campus links
- **Polariton Technologies (April 2026)** — plasmonics-based modulation enabling **3.2T and beyond** optical performance; positions MRVL ahead of the next generation
- **Companion to [[COHR]] / [[LITE]] / [[FN]] / [[GLW]]**: those are the optical-module/component layer (lasers, EMLs, transceiver assemblies, fiber); MRVL is the DSP silicon inside the module
- **Companion to [[SOITEC]]**: Soitec is the photonic-SOI substrate beneath every silicon photonics foundry process; MRVL's Celestial chiplet substrates flow through that supply chain

**3. Photonic Fabric (Celestial AI acquisition — the new optionality leg)**
- **Celestial AI acquired Feb 2026 for $3.25B cash + 27.2M shares; up to $5.5B with revenue earnouts**
- Photonic Fabric is an **optical interposer** that bridges compute (GPU/XPU) to memory (HBM) at the package level using light instead of copper
- Per [[2026-05-17-photonic-memory-stack]]: "the closest thing to 'photonic memory' in public markets — MRVL post-Celestial is the only public exposure to this layer"
- **Compares to [[ALAB]]**: ALAB plays at electrical CXL retimers; MRVL Photonic Fabric is the optical-bridge alternative for the same memory-bandwidth bottleneck
- **Wiki framing**: per [[bottleneck-roadmap]], optical scale-up is the 2027-2028 bottleneck after the current memory bottleneck resolves — MRVL is the public-market exposure

**4. Network ASICs (Innovium heritage)**
- Teralynx switching silicon — competes with AVGO Tomahawk + NVDA Spectrum-X at the data-center Ethernet switching layer
- Smaller share than AVGO but the third credible name behind AVGO + NVDA

**Upstream**: [[TSM]] (foundry), [[SOITEC]] (photonics-SOI substrate), [[AJINY]] / [[IBDXY]] / [[SSEHY]] (ABF substrates), [[SNPS]] / [[CDNS]] (EDA)
**Downstream**: AWS + Microsoft + Google (custom silicon); [[COHR]] / [[LITE]] / [[FN]] (optical module integrators using MRVL DSPs); hyperscalers direct (Photonic Fabric)
**Competitors**: [[AVGO]] (dominant custom silicon), [[NVDA]] (NVLink + Spectrum-X + integrated optics roadmap), [[ALAB]] (CXL/PCIe retimers), Cisco (networking ASICs)

## Bull case

- **Q1 FY27 is the single biggest guide raise in MRVL history.** FY27 revenue outlook lifted **$5B to $11.5B (+40% YoY)** — that is a 77% upward revision of the prior framework in a single quarter. **Which means** sell-side models from three months ago are stale across the board; the analyst-catchup trade is structurally active.
- **Three named hyperscaler custom-silicon anchors with multi-year visibility.** AWS Trainium (through Trainium3 in 2026+) + Microsoft Maia (silicon IP + back-end) + Google Axion CPU + adjacent. **Which means** customer concentration is real but bounded — losing any one is painful but not existential, the same diversification benefit AVGO has at its 6-hyperscaler scale.
- **Interconnect growth raised 50% → 70%+** driven by 1.6T PAM4 + DCI modules + scale-up optics. **Which means** the optical-DSP franchise is in a structural up-cycle exactly when [[COHR]] / [[LITE]] / [[FN]] are also raising guidance — independent corroboration across the layer.
- **NVIDIA $2B investment + NVLink Fusion partnership (March 2026).** Per the same NVDA $2B pattern that funded [[COHR]] / [[LITE]] / [[GLW]] / [[SNPS]] / [[NOK]] / [[CRWV]] / [[NBIS]], MRVL is now in the inner ring of NVDA equity-backed AI-infrastructure suppliers. **Which means** MRVL gets explicit NVDA architectural roadmap access, which is the structural moat that's hardest for competitors to replicate.
- **Celestial AI Photonic Fabric — the under-priced optionality leg.** Per [[2026-05-17-photonic-memory-stack]]: "The single most under-priced position on this list is MRVL post-Celestial acquisition. Most sell-side coverage treats MRVL as 'custom silicon + DPU + storage' and either ignores or applies modest optionality value to Photonic Fabric. If photonic interposers become the standard memory-bridge architecture for AI clusters by 2028 — which is what AVGO, NVDA, and TSMC's roadmaps all imply — Marvell's acquisition begins to look like a bargain." **Which means** there's a real call-option embedded in the current multiple that's not yet in consensus models.
- **Polariton Technologies (April 2026) extends the optical roadmap to 3.2T+.** Plasmonics-based modulation is a generation-ahead device technology. **Which means** MRVL has bought the technology stack to remain at the front of the 1.6T → 3.2T transition that's the next bottleneck after the current 800G ramp.
- **Operating leverage flipping on.** Q1 FY27 operating cash flow **$638.8M record**; non-GAAP operating margin 35%; non-GAAP gross margin 58.9%. **Which means** the AI mix shift (now 76% Data Center) is structurally lifting margins above the legacy MRVL framework — the company is being rerated for what it is, not what it was.
- **Companion-not-competitor framing with AVGO sharpens the duopoly thesis.** Per the wiki's [[AVGO]] page: "the single best NVDA hedge." MRVL is the second-leg of that hedge — a portfolio long [[AVGO]] + MRVL captures the non-NVDA custom-silicon trade more completely than AVGO alone. **Which means** the structural setup is duopoly, not winner-take-all.
- **DELL-upstream-second-derivative read-through.** Per [[2026-05-28-DELL-Q1-FY27-earnings]] + [[2026-05-29-dell-upstream-second-derivative]]: the $51.3B DELL backlog pulls networking ([[ANET]] / [[CLS]]) → optical ([[COHR]] / [[LITE]]) → DSP silicon (MRVL). MRVL captures rent at a layer DELL doesn't own.

## Bear case / risks

- **+130% YTD = the easy money is behind.** ATH $218.26 May 27, ~70x trailing P/E, ~$179B market cap. **Which means** the marginal buyer is entering momentum, not dislocation — the same setup that produced [[ANET]] -14% post-Q1-earnings on a raised guide. Earnings beats from here have to be larger to move the stock.
- **[[AVGO]] dominates the custom-silicon scoreboard.** AVGO has 6 hyperscaler XPU programs (including OpenAI Project Titan, 10 GW by 2029) at much larger run rates than MRVL's 3 anchors. **Which means** if hyperscalers consolidate custom-silicon spend with a single vendor for design-team-bandwidth reasons, AVGO is structurally more likely to be that vendor. MRVL's growth on smaller base is real but the long-term TAM-share question favors AVGO.
- **NVDA NVLink Fusion partnership is a double-edged sword.** Yes, the $2B investment validates MRVL — but the partnership terms structurally position MRVL as the **complement** to NVDA NVLink, not the alternative. **Which means** MRVL's revenue is partially gated by NVDA's willingness to share architectural surface area; if NVDA's roadmap shifts to absorb more of the interconnect stack (Spectrum-X expansion + NVLink Fusion extensions), MRVL's optical DSP TAM compresses.
- **Q2 guide of $2.7B is a high bar to clear.** +35% YoY at the midpoint means MRVL needs to print at least $2.7B to avoid a "garden variety beat" reaction. **Which means** Q2 earnings (around late August) is the single biggest tactical event — any miss or modest beat after a +130% YTD run is asymmetric downside.
- **Customer concentration is structurally high.** AWS + Microsoft + Google = significant majority of Data Center custom-silicon revenue. If AWS shifts Trainium volume to TSMC-direct or AVGO partnership, the revenue impact is sharp. **Which means** the quarterly named-customer commentary is the single most important disclosure.
- **Celestial AI integration risk.** $3.25B in cash + 27.2M shares is a real balance-sheet event. Photonic Fabric is a pre-revenue technology that needs 2-3 years to commercialize. **Which means** the bull-case "under-priced optionality" framing is also a "high integration-execution risk" framing — both can be true.
- **Custom-silicon TAM is bounded by hyperscaler in-housing.** Per the [[AVGO]] page bear: "AI companies can't make their own silicon — but they ARE making it (with Broadcom). The thesis depends on hyperscalers continuing to outsource design rather than going fully in-house long-term." Same risk applies to MRVL. **Which means** the secular trajectory of hyperscaler design-team build-out is the existential bear case 2027-2030.
- **Communications & Other segment (24% of revenue) is structurally lower-growth.** Carrier + enterprise networking + consumer + automotive/industrial all collapsed in 2024-2025; +29% YoY recovery is real but won't sustain that pace. **Which means** the long-term mix shift to Data Center is bullish for margins but exposes MRVL more nakedly to AI-capex cycle risk.
- **Multi-cycle correlation factor.** Long MRVL + [[AVGO]] + [[NVDA]] + [[MU]] + [[ANET]] + [[CLS]] is a single AI-capex-factor bet. Per [[hedging-risk]] tier-1 framework, portfolio sizing should explicitly account for the correlation, not just the standalone thesis.

5. **Polariton 3.2T+ roadmap milestones** — first-product-tape-out / first-customer-sampling disclosures.
6. **NVDA NVLink Fusion ecosystem progression** — does MRVL remain the lead partner or do other names (AMD, ALAB) get added?
7. **AVGO + MRVL duopoly vs fragmentation** — does the custom-silicon market stay rational (two big players sharing scoreboard) or fragment (each hyperscaler picks unique partners)?
8. **Operating cash flow trajectory** — Q1 FY27 record $638.8M; sustaining $500M+ quarterly is the validation that the AI mix shift has structurally lifted FCF.

## Position sizing considerations

This is a **medium-high conviction bull with valuation discipline required**. The +130% YTD + ~70x trailing P/E + ATH-$218.26 mean **entry today is into momentum, not dislocation**. **Which means**:

- **Sizing should be smaller than [[AVGO]]** (the dominant custom-silicon name with deeper scoreboard) and smaller than [[NVDA]] / [[TSM]] / [[MU]] (the higher-conviction structural names).
- **Pairing MRVL + AVGO captures the non-NVDA custom-silicon hedge more completely than either alone** — that's the "duopoly" framing.
- **Q2 FY27 print (late August) is the single biggest tactical event** — pre-print adds should be sized small.
- **Hedging-risk framework**: MRVL + AVGO + ANET + CLS + JBL + NVDA = all hyperscaler-capex factor exposure. Per [[hedging-risk]] tier-1 indicators, portfolio overweighting this stack should size the correlation, not the standalone thesis.
