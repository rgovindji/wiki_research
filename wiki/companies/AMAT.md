# Applied Materials (AMAT)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
The largest US wafer fab equipment vendor — Applied provides solutions for **15 of 19 extra processing steps required for HBM stacking** and captures **>50% of equipment value in HBM**. CEO guides equipment business growth **>20% in 2026** with **12-24 months of forward visibility** from customers. Per [[bottleneck-roadmap]], the 2027 sweet spot is the new fab build-out — AMAT is the canonical play.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Deposition (CVD/PVD/ALD), etch, ion implant, CMP** — the broadest WFE portfolio in the industry
- **HBM stacking dominance** — 15 of 19 incremental process steps; >50% equipment value capture
- **Logic + DRAM + NAND** — diversified across all three semi end markets
- **AGS service business** — high-margin recurring annuity from installed base
- Indirect — sells to fabs ([[TSM]], Samsung, Intel, [[MU]], SK Hynix), not to NVDA

## Bull case
- **HBM is structurally Applied-rich.** 15 of 19 incremental HBM steps are AMAT's; >50% equipment value capture. As HBM stacks move from 12-die → 16-die → 20-die, **content-per-wafer compounds**.
- **The 2027 fab buildout sweet spot** (per [[bottleneck-roadmap]]). Memory makers + foundries didn't build new fabs through 2023 trough; the supply response coming online in 2027-2028 will pull massive equipment orders.
- **WFE forecast: $126B in 2026, growing into 2027** (industry); AMAT growth >20% means it's gaining share or matching market cap pricing
- **12-24 months forward visibility** is unusual for equipment cyclicality — signals demand sustained even if AI capex moderates near-term
- **Diversified across logic + DRAM + NAND** vs. ASML's pure-EUV exposure or KLAC's pure-process-control
- **Highest gross margin of major equipment names** (per Seeking Alpha "margin machine" framing)
- **Service revenue (AGS)** is a recurring annuity from installed base
- **Per Dylan Patel** ([[2026-05-09-dwarkesh-dylan-semianalysis]]): "Applied Materials, Lam Research, and all these other companies expanding capacity a lot as well" — broader equipment cycle, not just ASML
- **$143B AI-driven chip gear spending explosion** thesis has become consensus

## Bear case / risks
- **Cyclical** — equipment orders historically boom and bust; 12-24 months visibility doesn't extend to 2028 cycle peak
- **China exposure / export controls** — significant historical revenue from Chinese memory + logic build-out
- **Customer concentration** — [[TSM|TSMC]] + Samsung + SK Hynix + Micron + Intel = ~80% of revenue
- **Competition from peers** — Lam Research ([[LRCX]]) overlaps in deposition/etch; [[KLAC|KLA]] ([[KLAC]]) competes in process control adjacencies
- **Stock has run hard** — +170% in 2025; some of the AI-fab-buildout thesis already priced
- **EUV is not AMAT** — when ASML is the binding constraint (2028-2030), AMAT benefits less than ASML directly
- **3D DRAM transition (late decade)** — uncertain how it reshapes AMAT's deposition/etch dominance

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Calendar 2026 actual growth** vs. >20% guide
2. **HBM equipment revenue mix** — direct beneficiary of HBM4 / 16-Hi / 20-Hi transitions
3. **AGS service revenue** — annuity growth
4. **China revenue mix** under tightening export controls
5. **Operating margin trajectory** — does the share gain flow through?
6. **2027 fab buildout** — first orders converting to revenue
7. **Process node mix** — leading-edge vs. ICAPS (mature nodes)
