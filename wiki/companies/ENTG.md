# Entegris (ENTG)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
The most overlooked **fab consumables specialist** in public markets — Entegris makes the **FOUPs (Front-Opening Unified Pods)** that protect wafers from contamination, the **ultra-pure materials** that go into chip manufacturing, and the **gas/liquid purification systems** that every advanced fab depends on. As [[AMAT]]/[[LRCX]]/[[KLAC]] sell the equipment and [[TSM]] builds the fabs, Entegris quietly sells the **consumables that get used up every wafer**. Pure-play AI-content-per-wafer growth at a fraction of the multiple of WFE peers.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Wafer carriers / FOUPs** — protect 300mm wafers between process steps; every advanced AI chip wafer rides in an Entegris carrier
- **Ultra-pure specialty materials** — photoresists, CMP slurries, etch chemistries, advanced deposition precursors
- **Gas & liquid filtration** — particle-free delivery of process gases and chemicals to the wafer
- **Microcontamination control** — the binding quality constraint as nodes shrink and packaging stacks grow
- Indirect — sells to fabs ([[TSM]], Samsung, Intel, [[MU]], SK Hynix), not to [[NVDA]] — but **content per wafer grows as chip complexity grows**

## Bull case
- **Content per wafer is rising** as advanced AI chips use more EUV layers, advanced packaging, and HBM stacking
- **Highly recurring revenue** — consumables get used up every wafer, unlike capital equipment which is lumpy
- **Pricing power** — quality is the binding constraint; defects kill yield; customers can't switch easily
- **Aligned with [[bottleneck-roadmap]] 2027 fab buildout** — new fabs being built now will pull massive Entegris content for years
- **Colorado Springs Manufacturing Center** opening for most advanced products = US manufacturing footprint advantage
- **Multi-decade customer relationships** with TSMC / Samsung / Intel / Micron
- Smaller market cap and lower analyst coverage than WFE peers = less crowded trade

## Bear case / risks
- **Cyclical at the wafer level** — when wafer starts dip, Entegris revenue dips with them
- **Lower direct AI exposure framing** than headline names — has to be sold to investors as "AI plumbing"
- **Customer concentration** — TSMC + Samsung + Intel + memory makers ~80% of revenue
- **China exposure** — semi materials trade subject to export controls
- **CMC Materials integration ongoing** — large acquisition still settling
- **Lower margin profile** than ASML / AMAT — mid-30s gross margin
- **Indirect AI exposure** — needs the fab build-out cycle to play out

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Revenue growth** — when does it inflect higher with fab capex
2. **Content per wafer disclosure** — direction matters
3. **Advanced Materials Handling segment** — leading-edge tied
4. **Microcontamination Control segment** — fab build-out tied
5. **Specialty Chemicals & Engineered Materials** — CMC integration progress
6. **Gross margin trajectory** — has been inflecting
