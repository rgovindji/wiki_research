# GE Vernova (GEV)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
One of three Western combined-cycle gas turbine OEMs (with Mitsubishi and Siemens Energy), with **lead times stretching beyond 2030** for blade-specific orders. Gas turbines are the workhorse for AI data center "behind-the-meter" generation, and order books at GEV/Mitsubishi/Siemens collectively support **hundreds of GW** of AI infrastructure demand.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Combined-cycle gas turbines** for AI data center power generation
- One of three Western OEMs at scale (others: Mitsubishi, Siemens Energy)
- Critical for **behind-the-meter** AI factory build-outs (Elon's Memphis Colossus precedent)
- Combined industry capacity from these three OEMs: **~60 GW/yr** of gas turbines (per SemiAnalysis)
- Per [[2026-05-09-dwarkesh-dylan-semianalysis]]: "lead times go out beyond 2030" on specific blades

## Bull case
- **Multi-year backlog** with high visibility — orders go beyond 2030
- **Pricing power** in a 3-vendor oligopoly with capacity-constrained delivery
- **Behind-the-meter ~50% of new AI capacity by EOD** — direct demand pull
- **Power demand growing across grid + data center** — durable thesis even if AI cycle moderates
- **Peaker plant + grid-scale battery support roles** add second leg of growth

## Bear case / risks
- **Per Dylan: power is NOT going to be the binding constraint** for AI by EOD because there are 16+ alternative power vendors (aeroderivatives, reciprocating engines, ship engines, Bloom Energy, solar+battery)
- This means GEV's *pricing power* is bounded by alternatives even if its order book stays full
- Cyclical industrial business at heart — has historically had boom/bust cycles
- Premium multiple already reflects much of the AI capex thesis
- Combined-cycle is **not great** for behind-the-meter (per Dylan) — favors aeroderivatives + reciprocating engines for that use case

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Combined-cycle order book** trajectory
2. **Aeroderivative** product mix as behind-the-meter share grows
3. **Service revenue** — installed-base annuity
4. **Lead-time direction** — extending or compressing?
5. **Mix of grid vs. behind-the-meter orders**
