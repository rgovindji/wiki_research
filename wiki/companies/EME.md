# EMCOR Group (EME)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** high · **Time horizon:** long-term

## One-line thesis
The **largest US electrical + mechanical contractor** by revenue — Q1 2026 RPOs (remaining performance obligations) hit a record **$15.62B**, FY26 EPS guide raised to **$28.25-$29.75**, and management explicitly cited **AI infrastructure as the primary driver of "unprecedented activity"** in the Network and Communications sector. Broader and more diversified than [[FIX]]; less pure-AI but more durable through cycles.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **Electrical + mechanical construction** for data centers — both layers in-house
- **US Electrical Construction** (12.1% operating margin) — switchgear, busway, conduit, conduit
- **US Mechanical Construction** (10.9% operating margin) — cooling, plumbing, HVAC, controls
- Building Services + Industrial Services provide non-data-center ballast
- Customers include hyperscalers + neoclouds + sovereign-AI buyers

## Bull case
- **Largest US MEP contractor** — scale advantages on multi-site hyperscaler programs
- **Record $15.62B backlog** with multi-year visibility
- **Operating margin expanding** (+50 bps YoY to 8.7%) — pricing power on AI scarcity
- **Raised guidance twice this year** — execution + macro both cooperating
- **Diversification across segments** — Electrical + Mechanical + Building Services + Industrial Services + UK provides non-data-center ballast (~50%)
- **Sovereign AI fit** — domestic US contractor of choice for sovereign / government AI campuses

## Bear case / risks
- **Already a large, well-followed name** — less under-the-radar than [[FIX]]
- **Lower margin profile** than FIX (8.7% blended vs FIX 26.9% mechanical gross) — less direct AI leverage in margin
- **Cyclical core** — if AI capex pauses, bookings rate softens
- **Acquisition integration** — EME grows via acquisition; integration risk
- **Skilled labor scarcity** — wage pressure compresses margin if pricing lags
- **UK segment** — non-AI exposure can be volatile

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **RPOs growth rate** — sequential direction
2. **Network & Communications segment growth** — the AI-direct line
3. **Operating margin trajectory** — does 8.7% continue to expand?
4. **Acquisition discipline** — quality of tuck-ins
5. **Mix of data center vs. other commercial / institutional work**
6. **UK segment** — drag or contributor
