## Bull case
- **De-rated power, near 52-wk low (−29%), ~17x fwd** — sold off largely *independently* of the AI trade, so you're not paying the AI premium that's embedded in [[VRT]]/[[GEV]]/[[BE]]. The cleanest value entry to the power theme.
- **Real data-center PPAs.** Long-dated (10-20yr) contracted DC power at $70-90/MWh — durable, visible cash flow tied to the buildout, not a story.
- **Power is the #1 binding constraint** (Satya: "chips I can't plug in"; Katti: ~100 GW US hyperscaler demand = double-digit % of the grid — see [[ai-capex-cycle]]). NRG owns generation + the ERCOT retail book at the epicenter (Texas) of US data-center growth.
- **Mispricing:** the crowd models NRG as a Texas utility, not an AI-power name — the re-rating optionality is if DC PPAs become the headline story.
- **Capital return** — generates real FCF; buybacks/dividend support the floor.

## Bear case / risks
- **Merchant-power cyclicality.** A chunk of generation is exposed to wholesale power prices — earnings are more volatile than a regulated utility; a power-price normalization compresses the thesis.
- **Rate-sensitive.** Like all power/utility names, a higher-for-longer rate regime weighs on the multiple and the dividend-proxy bid.
- **Execution on DC PPAs** — converting the pipeline into signed, energized, paid contracts is the swing factor; slippage delays the re-rate.
- **Texas/ERCOT concentration** — weather/grid-event tail risk (cf. Winter Storm Uri) and regulatory exposure concentrated in one market.
- **Retail-power + Vivint** mix means part of the business is a competitive, lower-multiple consumer franchise, not pure AI-power.
- **🆕 Contrarian flag (2026-06-20):** Aschenbrenner's Situational Awareness LP power long is **Bloom-centric, not NRG** (per the primary 13F, [[2026-06-20-aschenbrenner-situational-awareness]]) — i.e., the AGI-fund expression of "power = the constraint" didn't pick the merchant IPP. Not a thesis-breaker, but worth noting the smart-money power bet went elsewhere.

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **DC PPA cadence** — new signings, MW contracted, counterparty quality. The re-rate catalyst.
2. **Wholesale power prices / ERCOT spreads** — the cyclical swing factor.
3. **Rate path** — utility/IPP multiples are rate-sensitive.
4. **Capital allocation** — buyback pace vs the de-rated valuation.
