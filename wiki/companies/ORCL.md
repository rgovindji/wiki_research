# Oracle (ORCL)

**Stance:** bull · **Conviction:** medium · **Time horizon:** medium-term tactical

## One-line thesis
Legacy database giant repositioned as a **major OpenAI infrastructure partner** — Oracle Cloud Infrastructure (OCI) has captured significant OpenAI compute commitments alongside [[CRWV]] and [[NBIS]]. Stock and credit got hit in late 2025 on "OpenAI can't pay for these deals" worries; post-OpenAI's $110B raise, the market has re-rated.

## Role in the NVDA stack
- **OCI** — OpenAI compute partner (one of "tons with CoreWeave and Oracle" per Dylan)
- Tier-2 cloud relative to AWS/Azure/GCP, but AI-specific commitments give outsized growth optionality
- Database + apps business provides cash-flow ballast independent of AI

## Bull case
- **Major OpenAI capacity deals** with multi-year revenue commitment
- **Database business** is a high-margin annuity that funds the cloud build-out
- **Re-rated post H2 2025 stress** — credit markets got over the "OpenAI can't pay" concern
- **AI-attributable revenue acceleration** quarter-over-quarter
- **Smaller cloud than hyperscalers** = bigger relative growth on incremental AI commits

## Bear case / risks
- **Capex burden** for AI cloud build-out compresses near-term FCF
- **OpenAI concentration** — a single customer drives a lot of incremental revenue
- **AWS/Azure/GCP scale advantage** — Oracle is structurally Tier-2 in cloud
- **Credit-market stress** if AI capex narrative weakens — Oracle was specifically called out by Dylan as one of the names that "tanked" in H2 2025 on the funding panic
- **Database business growth** is mid-single-digits — leverage to AI is on top of a slow base

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Cloud (OCI) revenue growth** — direction and AI mix
2. **OpenAI revenue disclosure** — concentration data points
3. **Operating margin** — through the capex peak
4. **Credit-market read** on Oracle bonds during AI sentiment swings
5. **Database business growth** — does the legacy business hold?
