# Galaxy Digital (GLXY)

**Stance:** neutral · **Conviction:** low-medium · **Time horizon:** 12mo+ (Helios AI-datacenter ramp) + crypto-cycle beta

> **Why a page now (2026-06-20):** Created during the Cluster E fantasy-draft evaluation. GLXY earns a page because it is **quietly an AI-datacenter landlord, not just a crypto shop**. Its **Helios campus in West Texas** is leased to **[[CRWV|CoreWeave]]** under a 15-year, $1B+/yr AI-compute agreement, with **>1.6 GW of ERCOT-approved power**. That is a direct, on-mission tie to the [[ai-capex-cycle]] / [[datacenter-construction]] / power-bottleneck thesis.

## One-line thesis
GLXY is a **crypto financial-services business stapled to a fast-emerging AI-datacenter landlord**: the Helios campus converts cheap West-Texas power + a former Bitcoin-mining site into long-duration AI-compute lease revenue from CoreWeave. **Which means** the equity is a hybrid — part crypto-cycle beta, part [[datacenter-construction]] power play — and the AI-datacenter optionality is the reason a tech book would look at it at all.

## Bull case
- **AI-datacenter landlord optionality**: Helios delivered its **first data-center hall to CoreWeave** with a long-term lease expected to pay out $1B+/yr over ~15 years — a contracted, long-duration AI-compute revenue stream layered on top of the crypto business. Directly tied to [[datacenter-construction]] and the power bottleneck.
- **Power is the scarce asset**: **>1.6 GW of ERCOT-approved capacity** (doubled via an additional 830 MW approval, Jan 2026) in West Texas — power interconnect is *the* gating constraint for AI buildout ([[ai-capex-cycle]]). Galaxy acquired the Helios site (ex-Bitcoin mining) for ~$65M in 2022; the embedded power value is now far higher.
- **Financed**: closed a **$1.4B project-financing facility** (Aug 2025) to fund the Helios retrofit/expansion — de-risks the build capital.
- **Crypto-cycle leverage**: trading, asset management, and staking give upside beta to a strong digital-asset cycle.
- **Q1 2026 loss narrowed** while the AI push grew — trajectory improving.

## Bear case / risks
- **Still unprofitable**: net loss TTM, ~239x forward P/E — the market is pricing the Helios option, not current earnings. **Which means** any slip in the CoreWeave ramp or AI-capex sentiment hits hard.
- **Single-tenant concentration**: the AI-datacenter thesis leans heavily on **one counterparty, CoreWeave** — itself a leveraged AI-neocloud ([[CRWV]], [[ai-infrastructure-debt]]). Counterparty/credit risk is real if the AI-capex cycle cools.
- **Crypto beta cuts both ways**: a digital-asset downturn would pressure the financial-services side and sentiment.
- **Execution risk on the build**: GW-scale power delivery, interconnect, and construction timelines slip routinely ([[datacenter-construction]]).
- **Hybrid identity = valuation confusion**: part crypto, part datacenter REIT-like landlord — neither pure-play; harder to underwrite than a clean neocloud or a clean exchange.
- **AI-infra debt overhang**: the broader neocloud financing chain ([[ai-infrastructure-debt]]) is a systemic risk if AI demand disappoints.

## Key questions / what to watch
- **Helios delivery cadence** — how fast do the remaining halls / the full 1.6 GW come online, and on budget?
- **CoreWeave health** — counterparty creditworthiness is the linchpin; watch [[CRWV]] balance sheet and contract coverage.
- **Additional tenants** — does Galaxy diversify Helios beyond CoreWeave (hyperscaler offtake)?
- **Path to GAAP profit** — when does the datacenter revenue flip the company to sustained profitability?
- **Crypto cycle** — digital-asset prices still drive a large share of the P&L.
