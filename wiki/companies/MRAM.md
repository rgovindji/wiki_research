# Everspin Technologies (MRAM)

**Stance:** neutral (speculative) · **Conviction:** low · **Time horizon:** event-driven (design-win ramp / defense) · **Verdict:** a real specialty-memory + domestic-defense moat, but **richly valued for the growth** (~11x sales / ~2,238x earnings on ~16% growth) and already ran ~5x off its lows. An optionality/momentum bet, not an obvious value buy.

## One-line thesis
The leading maker of **MRAM** (magnetoresistive, non-volatile "persistent" memory) — a genuine niche moat in defense/aerospace and industrial, with small data-center optionality (IBM FlashCore persistent memory) — but it's a ~$57M-revenue micro-cap growing ~16% at a rich multiple, so you're paying up for a narrative + optionality, not cheapness.

## What they do
Designs/makes **MRAM** — non-volatile memory that retains data without power, with fast write endurance and radiation tolerance. Not a DRAM/NAND/HBM competitor at scale; it serves niches that need persistence + reliability + long lifecycles: **aerospace/defense, industrial automation, energy management**, and **data-center persistent memory/cache** (IBM FlashCore Module FCM4/FCM5 + RAID reference designs with hyperscalers).

## Bull case
- **Domestic-defense moat.** A new **$40M contract** to supply MRAM process tech to the US Defense Industrial Base (vs ~$57M annual revenue = material). DoD/aerospace value MRAM's reliability + long lifecycles + US manufacturing — a sticky, high-barrier niche.
- **Design-win ramp.** 238 design wins in 2025 (up from 178), expected to ramp to production 2026-27 → forward revenue visibility.
- **Data-center optionality.** IBM FlashCore persistent-memory + RAID engagements with hyperscalers — a real (if small) AI-adjacent leg.
- **Margins improving** (52.7% GM) and now (barely) profitable, with the $40M defense deal + ramp as near-term catalysts.

## Bear case / risks
- **🔴 Rich for the growth.** ~11x sales and ~2,238x earnings for ~16% revenue growth on a $57M base. The valuation already prices in the optionality; little margin of safety.
- **🔴 Micro-niche.** MRAM is a tiny corner of the memory market; DRAM/NAND/HBM are orders of magnitude larger. The "AI memory" association is mostly narrative — MRAM is not where the AI-memory dollars (HBM) flow.
- **🟠 Barely profitable / tiny.** $0.28M net income, $57M revenue, 23M shares — any miss or design-win slip swings the stock hard; thin liquidity.
- **🟠 Already ran ~5x off lows** ($5.76 → $27), now −47% off its high — a volatile momentum name, not a stable compounder.
- **🟠 Concentration / lumpiness.** Defense + design-win revenue is lumpy and customer-concentrated; the $40M deal is great but episodic.

## Key questions / what to watch
1. **Design-win-to-revenue conversion** — do the 238 wins actually ramp in 2026-27, and how much revenue?
2. **Defense pipeline beyond the $40M** — is it a one-off or a repeatable DoD franchise?
3. **Data-center traction** — does IBM FCM / hyperscaler persistent-memory move from "engagement" to real revenue?
4. **Does growth accelerate above ~16%** to justify the 11x sales — or does the multiple compress?
