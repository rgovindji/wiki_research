US markets are dark today. Juneteenth falls on a Friday this year, so the NYSE and Nasdaq are both closed and reopen Monday at the normal 9:30 ET bell. There's no cash session, no futures close that moves our levels, and no US data print. So this is a short one.

A scheduling wrinkle worth flagging, because it changes *when* our open calls get graded: the quarter's biggest options-and-futures expiration — triple-witching — got pulled forward to yesterday, Thursday, precisely because today is a holiday. The event we'd been pointing at all week already came and went. Yesterday's pin to 7,500, the calm half-percent range, the clean rejection at 7,510 — that *was* the witching tape. Everything we logged "to the June 19 close" now resolves at Monday's close instead, because there is no June 19 close. The 7,510 line; the bet that a drained-cushion market would still throw one wild 1.5%-plus day before it re-stacked — all of it carries to Monday. We'll grade it then, honestly, same as always. (For the record, that wide-range call is in trouble: yesterday's millpond session used up one of its two chances, and the holiday just deleted the other one inside its window.)

The one thing actually happening today is happening four thousand miles away. The US and Iran sign their deal in Geneva. Oil has spent two weeks pre-paying for it — crude is down near $75 and tankers are already moving back through the Strait of Hormuz — so the signature itself is the least surprising part of the story. A clean signing is a Monday non-event. A last-minute slip is the only version that re-arms the war premium, and we'd see it in the crude tape before we ever saw it in stocks. That's the lesson that earned its keep this month: when a war headline and the oil price disagree, trust the oil price.

So here's the entire game plan for a day with no game: nothing. The desk is flat — not by read, but because there's no session to trade. When the bell rings Monday the map is unchanged from last night. Above a *close* over 7,510, the broken-floor story is over and the record near 7,600 comes back into view; the dealers who'd been selling that ceiling flip to chasing. Below it, the bounce was just a bounce, and the next shelf sits around 7,430. Two real catalysts wait on the far side of the long weekend: Micron's earnings Wednesday — the memory super-cycle finally meeting its own income statement — and Friday's PCE inflation print, the Fed's preferred gauge and the first read since a genuinely hawkish dot plot. Both can move the book. Neither can move it today.

Enjoy the day off. We'll see you Monday.
