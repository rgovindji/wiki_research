## The setup

Yesterday the most crowded trade on earth fell apart for a day. Overnight, the companies *inside* that trade fought back — with their own balance sheets.

The Korean market that crashed 10% and tripped a circuit breaker on Tuesday rallied 3.3% overnight on two corporate moves, not a change in the macro weather: Samsung floated a share buyback worth roughly 90 trillion won — about **$59 billion**, one of the largest a chipmaker has ever proposed — and SK Hynix is accelerating a **$26 billion** US listing of its shares. A company buying back tens of billions of its own stock the morning after a 12% crash is sending one message: *we think this is a sale, not a warning.* Samsung jumped as much as 9.8% and retook the title of Korea's most valuable company.

That tone carried west. As of early this morning, US futures are modestly green — the Nasdaq tracker up about half a percent — which would put the S&P back near 7,400, clawing back a slice of yesterday's drop but still short of the line that matters. Micron is up about 4% pre-market, Intel and Qualcomm each recovering more than 1% of Tuesday's beating. The macro backdrop quietly improved too: oil cracked again to around **$70** (disinflationary), the 10-year eased toward 4.43%, and the VIX settled back under 19.

But none of it is settled until tonight. **Micron reports after the close**, and its guidance — not the rebound, not the buyback headline — decides whether yesterday was a crowded trade clearing its position or the first crack in the demand story. The bounce is real. The verdict is pending.

## The buyback is the tell, but Micron is the verdict

What the buyback says about the people closest to the data is the real signal. Memory pricing runs on signed, multi-quarter contracts; the executives floating $59 billion of repurchases have seen their own order books, and they're voting with cash — a louder tell than any analyst note, and a direct rebuttal to the "AI speed-bump" fear Dan Niles lit yesterday.

But corporate confidence isn't confirmed demand, and that's the gap Micron closes tonight. The quarter is essentially pre-sold — consensus near $34–35 billion in revenue and roughly $20 in earnings at ~80% margins, with four straight beats behind it. The number isn't the event. The **2027 guide** is: reaffirm or raise the multi-year HBM trajectory against Samsung's and SK Hynix's roadmaps, and yesterday's 13% air-pocket reads as the valuation reset we've called it. A cautious word on pricing, and the rout was the market seeing something real first.

**Our take:** the overnight buyback raises the odds the guide confirms tightness — you don't repurchase $59 billion of stock into a demand cliff you see coming. We own Micron and hold the franchise at high conviction. But we've logged the tactical reality separately too: a stock that ran ~200% this year into all-time highs can sell the news even on a good print, because the bar sits at the highs. Strong franchise, stretched entry.
