## The number

Core CPI for May: **0.2% on the month** — below the 0.3% consensus, with the annual rate at 2.9%, right on expectations. The headline was every bit as ugly as advertised — 0.5% monthly, 4.2% annually, the hottest reading since 2022 — and it doesn't matter, because all of the ugliness is war-spiked energy, and the Fed knew that before breakfast.

Yesterday we drew one line under this report: the market can absorb a scary headline *if core prints 0.3% or below*. Core didn't just clear that bar — it came in under it. The "rate hike next week" conversation, which was the single scariest item on June's calendar, just lost its ammunition. We'll grade the full call at tonight's close like we promised, but the condition we named broke our way, cleanly.

## The complication

The tape has a second problem this morning that has nothing to do with inflation. Iran answered last night's U.S. strikes with strikes of its own overnight, and minutes before the CPI hit, S&P futures were down about 1% with oil at $89.92. So the morning opens as a tug-of-war: the best inflation news in months pulling one way, a hot war pulling the other.

Keep using the tell that's worked all week: **crude, not headlines.** Oil up $1.76 and still under $90 is a market pricing skirmishes, not a closed Strait of Hormuz. Under $95, the de-escalation trade survives and soft core should win the day. If crude runs through the mid-90s, war beats macro and today gets rough regardless of what the Fed does.

## The game plan, updated

With the macro branch resolved soft, our logged call from Monday night is now armed and on the clock: a tame core means the S&P should reclaim **the 7,460 line by Friday's close** — that's the level where the market-making machinery stops amplifying every move and starts absorbing them again. Close above it and the tape's whole character calms down into next week's giant options expiration. If the market *can't* get back above 7,460 by Friday with inflation fear off the table, we'll have learned the dip-buyers are weaker than we believed, and we'll say so in plain English.

Below, the map is unchanged: the first ledge of hedges at **7,355**, then the put-wall zone at **7,300–7,385** where downside cascades have stalled twice this week. A war-headline flush into that zone with core this soft would be, in our judgment, the dip the cash reserve has been waiting to meet — not all at once, but that's where our shopping list starts mattering.

## The desk stands down

This morning's logged trade — 7,300 puts — was double-keyed: it required a *hot* core print **and** a break of 7,355. Core printed soft, so by the card's own terms the trade does not exist today. No improvising the bullish version into a war-headline open; chasing a gap on a morning with two opposing forces is how lottery tickets become donations. The setup we're watching instead: if the index proves it can hold above 7,460 — not touch it, hold it — that's the regime change worth riding. The record stays 1-0, and "flat" is a position we're happy to defend.

Tonight's letter grades the CPI call, the desk's no-trade, and whether oil kept its promise. The next six hours are the most informative of the week.
