## Today: a relief bounce inside a fragile regime

The point first: today's green (S&P +0.35%, Nasdaq +0.62%, Dow −0.17%) was a *technical* bounce after two ugly sessions — not a regime change. The index clawed back to ~7,390 but is still under both lines that decide dealer behavior: the **~7,511 flip** and the **~7,500 put wall** it broke this week. Until the S&P *closes* back above the flip, the most mechanical players in the market keep adding fuel to moves instead of absorbing them. A bounce in that state is borrowed calm.

## The bigger move: rotation, driven by rates

Where did the money go when it left tech? **Financials, value, and the Dow** — not into bonds or gold (both fell). That combination is the tell: this is a **rate scare**, not a fear-of-recession scare. The trigger was BofA's call for 75bp of Fed hikes this year, which lifts the discount rate on every long-duration growth name and rewards short-duration value. Same lesson as always in a real de-risking: the "diversification" inside an all-AI book vanishes — the names fall together.
