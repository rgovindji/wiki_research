## Qualcomm Crashes Nvidia's Party

The overnight story that wasn't already priced: **Qualcomm** — the phone-chip company — used its investor day to declare itself an AI data-center player. A server CPU (Dragonfly C1000, 250-plus cores), an inference accelerator, its own memory tech to undercut high-bandwidth memory, and a software acquisition (Modular) to chip at Nvidia's CUDA moat. It signed **Meta** and Microsoft and put a number on it: **$15 billion** of data-center revenue by 2029. Stock +12-15% after hours.

**Our take:** another credible name pointed a second revenue engine at the AI buildout and the customers showed up to sign — demand for AI silicon is deep enough for a third merchant vendor, not just Nvidia and Broadcom. The catch, for later: most of the hardware doesn't ship until 2028, and a CUDA rival is a multi-year promise. The market's buying the declaration; we'd buy the silicon when it ships.
