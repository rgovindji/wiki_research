## The setup

The market is being offered a relief rally, and the question is who shows up to take it. Futures are up across the board — the S&P points to an open near 7,413, recovering most of Friday's wobble — on a weekend where the US and Iran traded fire near Hormuz and then agreed to stand down, plus two clean corporate stories (a Comcast breakup, a Rocket Lab acquisition) that have nothing to do with AI.

That's the catch. The names doing the lifting this morning aren't the names that got hit last week. The chips that drove the selloff stayed weak in Asia overnight. So the open is likely strong and the texture underneath it suspect: a tape that wants to bounce on everything *except* the thing it's actually worried about. Watch whether semis turn green by mid-morning — that's the difference between a real turn and a dead-cat lift carried by spinoff headlines.

## Iran Stands Down, Comcast Breaks Up

The overnight driver was geopolitics turning the right way. After the US and Iran exchanged fire near the Strait of Hormuz, both sides agreed to "stand down for now," with talks reported on track. Oil's reaction tells you how the market reads it: WTI ticked up only 0.85% to $69.82 and stayed under $70. Traffic through Hormuz is still far below normal, but there's no panic premium in crude — which, by our own rule that oil is the tell on war headlines, means equities are right to treat this as de-escalation, not escalation.

The bigger single-stock news is structural. Comcast is splitting into two public companies, spinning off NBCUniversal and Sky — the stock is up 25% premarket on the classic sum-of-the-parts logic that a conglomerate trades for less than its pieces. Separately, Rocket Lab is buying satellite-comms firm Iridium for $8 billion, sending Iridium up 22%. Neither moves our book, but both matter for tape psychology: on a day the AI complex is under a cloud, the market is happy to bid breakups and deals. Our take: that's a market looking for reasons to go up that don't require believing the AI-capex story — encouraging for breadth, not yet a vote of confidence in the names we own.
