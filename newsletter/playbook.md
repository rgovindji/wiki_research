# Playbook — what the letters have learned

This file is the newsletter's accumulated judgment. **Both prompts read it before writing; the close-of-market run updates it.** It grows from one source only: resolved predictions in `predictions.json` and observed process failures. No lesson enters without evidence; every lesson cites the resolution(s) that earned it.

## Rules for maintaining this file

- **Add** at most one lesson per day, only when a prediction resolution or clear process failure teaches something. Cite the prediction id or date.
- **Promote** a Hypothesis to Active only after it's been right twice (or right once in a high-stakes spot with a clean mechanism).
- **Retire** (don't delete) lessons that get falsified — move to Retired with the falsifying evidence. A playbook that only grows is a playbook nobody prunes.
- **Friday close runs** do the weekly distillation: merge duplicates, check Active lessons against the week's resolutions, keep Active under ~20 entries.
- Lessons are written as **operating instructions** ("when X, do Y"), not observations ("X happened").
- **Conviction discipline (anti-sycophancy, curator directive 2026-06-17).** Hold calls to the evidence, not the reader's mood. Pre-commit view + confidence + an explicit falsifier; when new info lands, classify it (factual correction / confirms / genuinely updates) and move conviction **proportional to evidence weight** — a detail nudges, a hard data contradiction flips. Distinguish "components moved but the verdict held" from an actual flip, and say which. Disagree visibly when the reader pushes; never reverse a call just to please. No validation theater. A real flip must be logged in the prediction ledger like any other resolution. See memory `conviction-discipline` + [[feedback-log|wiki/feedback-log.md]].

## Active lessons

*Friday check 2026-06-12: all six prior Active lessons verified against the week's resolutions — #1 reinforced (June 10-11 both traded the levels, not the catalysts), #2 reinforced twice (ORCL sold a record RPO quarter on the funding line; ADBE sold a beat-and-raise on a personnel line), #3 performed exactly as written (the regime call waited for a named-resistance close, which arrived June 12 above the flip), #4 untested this week (VELO watch continues — S-8 filed June 11, not the rug signature yet), #5-#6 are process rules now embedded in the ledger and the YOLO card template. No merges needed; 7 Active, well under the ~20 cap.*

1. **On multi-binary days, condition calls on levels, not on a single catalyst.** June 10 stacked CPI and an overnight US-Iran missile exchange; our CPI call ("soft core → flat-to-up close") and the YOLO card (trade exists only on hot core) both keyed everything to the inflation print — so the soft print left us positioned for a calm that the OTHER binary then destroyed (S&P −1.62% on war + supercore composition; the desk's no-trigger skipped a flush that actually happened). When two independent catalysts share a session, write the conditional on the tape level (e.g., "below 7,355, expect the cascade regardless of which headline causes it") and treat catalysts as context. Corollary: check the print's COMPOSITION before grading it benign — the 8:45 supercore flag beat the 8:31 headline read. *(Origin: 2026-06-09-cpi-core-asymmetry resolved wrong + 2026-06-10 YOLO no-trigger.)*
2. **Guidance beats earnings in a late-cycle tape.** AVGO beat Q2 revenue and EPS on June 3 but guided AI sales flat vs estimates → -14% next day, dragged the whole sector. When previewing a print, frame the guide as the event, not the beat. *(Origin: 2026-06-04 AVGO; wiki feedback-log. Reinforced 2026-06-10: ORCL beat everything incl. an $85B RPO add and fell 7% AH on the capex/financing line — in this tape the funding plan IS the guide.)*
3. **One green dip-buy session resolves nothing.** June 8's bounce recovered part of June 5's damage and felt like an all-clear; June 9 opened green and flushed 1.5% by lunch. Don't let a single up session flip the regime call — require a close above a named resistance level. *(Origin: 2026-06-08/09 tape.)*
4. **Small-cap "partnership pop + cash raise" is a rug signature.** Partnership announced with warrants → stock pops → company dumps equity into the pop within 48h. Any high-risk idea in a small cap must check for this pattern first. *(Origin: POET, 4 validations, wiki feedback-log 2026-05-15.)*
5. **Process: no falsifier, no call.** Every "our take" with a directional implication gets a ledger entry with an explicit falsifier, or gets rewritten until it has one. Vague conviction is the failure mode that makes scorecards impossible. *(Origin: ledger design, 2026-06-09.)*
6. **Every 0DTE card carries a time-stop, because walls are magnets, not promises.** The June 11 7,400-call card triggered correctly, rode the squeeze 155 points, was well in the money at 7,412 — and expired worthless when the move stalled 8 points short of the target wall, because the only exits were "wall" or "invalidation" and the market chose "neither." Theta resolves "neither" against the holder, always. Rule: any expiring structure gets a third exit — out at 3:30 ET (or a named profit-take) if neither target nor invalidation has hit. *(Origin: 2026-06-11-spx-7400c resolved loss.)*

8. **In a positive-gamma regime, set the YOLO target AT the dominant wall, not beyond it — the wall is where the move dies.** Both desk losses (June 11, June 15) were the same trade: direction right, trigger right, squeeze materialized, up nicely intraday — then stalled AT the call wall and faded, short of a target placed beyond it (June 11 target 7,420-7,430 vs wall ~7,395; June 15 target 7,590-7,600 vs wall 7,555, which the close pinned to exactly). When dealers are long gamma they SELL into the call wall, so a rally into it is most likely to die there. Rule: in a positive-gamma tape, the YOLO target is the dominant wall itself (take profit AT it), and any strike should sit at or below that wall — never structure a card whose payoff requires closing ABOVE the wall the regime says caps the move. The time-stop (lesson 6) caps the bleed; this caps the fantasy. *(Origin: 2026-06-11-spx-7400c + 2026-06-15-spx-7575c, both resolved loss, identical signature.)*

10. **Positive-gamma containment is proportional to net GEX — when the cushion has drained, retire the pin/box framework rather than betting on it.** On June 17 two calls broke on the identical error: fomc-day-box (close stays inside the 7,510-7,600 walls) and pinned-into-opex (sessions starting above the flip print sub-1.5% ranges) both assumed dealers-long-gamma would dampen the FOMC reaction — the night AFTER net GEX had already collapsed ~80% (from +$80-101B Monday to +$13-21B). The hawkish dot plot then sliced through the 7,510 put wall to close 7,420.10 and printed a 1.75% range. The diagnosis had been correct and explicit (the June 16 letter said the thin cushion meant 'the presser can move the tape more than the recent grinds imply') — we just didn't let it govern the calls. Rule: a put wall / call wall / pin is only as strong as the gamma behind it; once net GEX collapses toward neutral, treat the tape as a NEUTRAL-gamma tape (walls are reference points, not magnets) and do not log containment predictions keyed to them. A genuine catalyst (a hawkish Fed, a guide-down) flushes through a thinned wall the same as through no wall. This is the upside-mirror of lesson 8 (which caps the YOLO fantasy on rallies); lesson 10 caps the containment fantasy on events. *(Origin: 2026-06-16-fomc-day-box + 2026-06-12-pinned-into-opex, both resolved wrong on the same FOMC session, identical structural error.)*

7. **When a call keys to a computed level, state at logging time whether the number or the recomputed level governs the grade.** The June 9 gamma-reclaim call quoted "~7,440" as shorthand for the dealer flip; by deadline day the recomputed flip was 7,424 and SPX closed 7,431.46 — above the mechanism's level, below the quoted number, and the morning brief had re-affirmed the stale number ("no partial credit for 7,439") hours after our own compute had moved the line. The ambiguity was self-inflicted twice. Rule: every level-keyed prediction names its governing standard in the claim text ("graded against the flip as recomputed nightly" or "graded against this fixed number"), and no letter re-affirms a level without checking it against the freshest compute first. *(Origin: 2026-06-09-pm-gamma-reclaim resolved partial.)*

9. **When a war/geopolitics headline and crude disagree, trust crude.** Equity reaction to Middle East headlines follows the oil tape, not the headline text. Tested four sessions June 9-15 including two adversarial ones — June 11 Iran DECLARED Hormuz closed and crude FELL 4% while stocks rallied 1.75%; June 12 Tehran disputed the deal text and crude fell 3% while stocks rose — and resolved RIGHT on June 15 (deal 'complete,' WTI -4.8% to $80.75, S&P +1.65%). Rule: on a war headline, check WTI before trading the equity move; if oil doesn't confirm the fear (or the relief), fade the equity reaction. Bound: this held in a market that WANTED the de-escalation — the lesson is 'when oil and the headline disagree, trust oil,' not 'oil predicts everything'; in a tape fearful for other reasons crude may not dominate sentiment as cleanly. *(Origin: 2026-06-09-oil-is-the-tell resolved right after 4 consecutive confirmations; promoted from Hypotheses 2026-06-15.)*

11. **A thin-gamma WIDE-range read is only valid while gamma stays thin — when net GEX re-stacks positive, the dampening returns and ranges compress, so retire the wide-range call the moment the cushion rebuilds.** On June 17 we logged thin-gamma-wide-range (≥1 of the next sessions prints a 1.5%+ range) off a drained net GEX (~+$22B). But within ONE session the cushion re-stacked positive (June 18 pinned the 7,500 call wall in a 0.57% range; June 22 ran 0.93%), the dampening came back, and the call graded WRONG. This is the exact mirror of lesson 10: lesson 10 says don't trust CONTAINMENT after gamma drains; lesson 11 says don't trust WIDE RANGES after gamma re-stacks. The unifying rule: do not assume the gamma STATE persists — re-read net GEX each night and let the FRESH state govern the call, not the state that was true when the regime first changed. The tell we ignored: we wrote in the June 18 letter that 'the shock absorbers re-stacked faster than we expected' and left the wide-range call live anyway. When you observe the cushion rebuilding, retire the open wide-range prediction the same way you'd retire a containment prediction when it drains. *(Origin: 2026-06-17-thin-gamma-wide-range resolved wrong; pairs with lesson 10.)*

13. **Single-name short-horizon PRICE-REACTION calls are the ledger's soft spot — demand a mechanism that DOMINATES the name's tape over the horizon, or downgrade to a coin flip.** On June 25 the two WRONG resolutions were both this format: mu-sells-the-news ("MU closes lower the day after the print") and intel-apple-pop-fades ("INTC closes below $126 within five days"). Both keyed a specific name's specific level/close to a *setup* read ("ran up into the event → pop fades", "termless tweet → sentiment fades") — but a single name's price over a few sessions is dominated by sector/macro forces and by the event's own capacity to surprise, not by the setup you spotted: MU's $50B guide + take-or-pay SCAs re-rated the franchise straight through the run-up; INTC held on the standing AI-foundry narrative through a memory-selloff intraday dip to $125.41. Contrast with the same night's RIGHTs, all LEVEL/MECHANISM reads on the index or a basket (no-reclaim-7450, floor-becomes-ceiling, supply-leads-on-mu). Rule: before logging a single-name price-reaction call, ask "does my identified mechanism OVERPOWER everything else that moves this name over the horizon?" — if it's a contracted catalyst, a mechanical index event, or a structural cost pass-through, fine; if it's "it ran up" or "the pop will fade", that's a weak base in a multi-force tape, so either reframe it around the dominant force or log it 'low' and don't let it carry weight in the letter. The edge stays where it's always been: levels and mechanisms on the index/baskets, not price-reaction bets on individual tickers. *(Origin: 2026-06-22-mu-sells-the-news + 2026-06-18-intel-apple-pop-fades, both resolved wrong 2026-06-25, identical format; contrast the three level/mechanism RIGHTs the same night.)*

12. **Set a fade-trigger at the resistance the tape is ACTUALLY respecting (the recent rejection high / broken shelf), not at the round flip number above it.** On June 24 the YOLO fade card keyed entry to a poke into the 7,440-7,450 flip zone; the day's high was 7,428.06 — the tape got sold ~15 pts BELOW the trigger, at the same 7,424-7,428 broken-shelf that had rejected it June 23. No poke into 7,440+, so the desk stood aside — and then watched the fade pay (SPX faded to 7,358; a 7,400 put would have closed ~42 ITM). This is the 2nd no-trigger that missed a winner (June 10 the first), and the cause was trigger PLACEMENT: in a negative-gamma tape below the flip, the operative intraday resistance is the most recent rejection high, not the round flip number — the flip is where dealer BEHAVIOR changes, but price routinely gets sold well below it. Rule: when carding a fade in a sub-flip tape, anchor the trigger to the level the tape just rejected from (yesterday's high / the broken shelf), and treat the flip as the invalidation, not the entry. Pairs with lesson 6 (time-stop) and lesson 8 (target AT the wall): together they say structure the card around where price ACTUALLY turns, not around the round numbers. *(Origin: 2026-06-24-spx-7400p resolved no-trigger, fade paid from the sidelines.)*
