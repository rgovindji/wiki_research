Does the earlier thesis still hold? Mostly yes — with two refinements. The time-sequenced spine — memory (2026) → packaging/substrates (2027) → EUV & wafer (2028-2030) — is intact and confirming (June's Korea export data validated the memory leg in real time). Two things changed: (1) Power was promoted to a co-primary near-term bottleneck — the original "power is not the bottleneck" call is now outdated for the deployment layer (Satya: "I have chips I can't plug in"; Katti: ~100 GW US demand = double-digit % of the grid). The 2028-2030 EUV math (the hard ceiling)

ASML EUV output: ~70 tools/yr now → ~80 next → ~100 by 2030
Cumulative tools by end-of-decade: ~700
1 GW of AI capacity ≈ 3.5 EUV tools' worth of fab time
→ 700 ÷ 3.5 = ~200 GW/yr theoretical AI capacity if 100% allocated to AI
Leverage: $50B of DC capex per GW vs only ~$1.2B of EUV tooling per GW

So the entire AI buildout funnels through ~$1.2B/GW of ASML tools — the thinnest, least-substitutable link in the chain. That's why EUV is the 2028-2030 ceiling, and why it's the most durable franchise on this page.

⚠ The caveat that decides how you play it (Gavin Baker)

"The bottleneck trade is nearing its end… the next game is what has enduring franchise value on the other side." Bottleneck rents are cyclical and self-liquidating — when supply catches up, the pure rent-capturers (commodity DRAM, pluggable optics, OSAT) de-rate, while the durable franchises keep pricing power. The split: TSM / ASML / NVDA / MU-HBM = durable; commodity memory, pluggable transceivers, low-end packaging = rent. Tilt to the franchises as the cycle matures.

✓ Why the demand side keeps the roadmap live

Inference is now the majority of compute, heading to 80%+ (Katti) — and RL post-training + synthetic data are themselves inference workloads. So the demand bid under every bottleneck above survives even if pretraining scaling plateaus. GPU capacity is sold out; "excess capacity is gone in 6 weeks." The buildout is demand-real, not just announced.

What is NOT the bottleneck

  • Raw GPU supply — tight, but NVDA/TSMC are scaling it; the constraint is upstream (EUV) and downstream (power/packaging), not the GPU itself.
  • Training data — labs "ran out of real data," but synthetic data + RL scale around it (it consumes compute rather than gating it). Data is the driver of capability, not the ceiling on capacity.
  • Algorithms / model architecture — diffuse fast (open models lag ~4-6 months); not a physical ceiling.
  • Capital — ~$1T+/yr is flowing; funding mechanism (equity vs debt) is a risk, not a hard limit (yet).

How to play it (by durability)

  • Durable franchises (own through the cycle): ASML (the ceiling), TSM (wafer discipline), NVDA (platform), MU-HBM (bandwidth franchise).
  • Power — the elevated near-term play: de-rated entries preferred — NRG, CEG over the at-highs names; VRT for power+cooling (watch the 800VDC disintermediation flag).
  • Cyclical rent (trade, don't marry): commodity DRAM/NAND, pluggable optics, OSAT packaging — harvest the shortage, exit before the supply wave (~2028-29 fabs).
  • The tell to watch: TSMC capex guidance vs NVDA order intent (gap widening = discipline held; closing = bubble setup begins). The earliest cycle-peak signal there is.
