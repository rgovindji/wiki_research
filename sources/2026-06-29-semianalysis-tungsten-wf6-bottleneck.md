# SemiAnalysis — Tungsten / WF₆ as an emerging semiconductor critical-materials bottleneck

*Bias: MODERATE. SemiAnalysis is high-quality and the wiki weights it, but it sells research and is structurally inclined to surface "the next bottleneck." A 6-tweet thread, not a full report — treat the figures as indicative.*

## TL;DR
- **Tungsten** is critical in fab CVD — it fills the deep, high-aspect-ratio vertical **vias** that link multi-layered chips (demand rises with 3D NAND stacking + more metal layers + advanced logic).
- **Supply is increasingly constrained and overwhelmingly China-dominated.** High-purity tungsten metal powder is the raw input for **WF₆ (tungsten hexafluoride)**, the CVD gas; China controls the raw supply chain.
- **Japan's WF₆ makers (SK Materials, Shin-Etsu, others) are squeezed** — skyrocketing prices + falling tungsten raw-material imports make domestic WF₆ manufacture "all but impossible." **Korean WF₆ import pricing +151% YTD.**
- **Takeaway:** as AI/semi complexity rises, the binding constraints may sit not just in chips/equipment but in the **critical materials at the base of the supply chain.**

## Key facts / claims (confidence)
- Tungsten/WF₆ is a real, single-source-ish critical input (high — well-established that W CVD fills vias and China dominates tungsten).
- Korean WF₆ imports +151% YTD; Japan import volumes falling (their figures — indicative, thread-sourced).
- China-dominance of tungsten ties it to the broader **China critical-minerals export-control weaponization** theme (gallium, germanium, antimony, graphite, rare earths — tungsten swept into the same fight). See [[us-china-relations]].

## The honest investability read (the Baker tension)
- This is the textbook **"esoteric bottleneck"** — *real* but *hard to monetize*: WF₆ is a tiny fraction of a chip's cost, and the suppliers are buried inside diversified conglomerates (Shin-Etsu = huge chemical co where WF₆ is a rounding error; SK Materials = part of SK; Foosung/Kanto Denka = small/illiquid). A niche-gas price spike doesn't move those equities.
- **Lands in direct tension with Gavin Baker (All-In #278, [[2026-06-29-allin-278-china-coding-micron]]),** who derided the "bottleneck bros" hunting esoteric Japanese suppliers and said the only bottleneck that matters is **DRAM**. Both can be true: **DRAM = where the *capital/pricing power* accrues (the biggest binding constraint); WF₆/helium/etc = *tail-risk/fragility* inputs** (small cost, but a China supply-shock could gate fab throughput). Pricing power lives at the biggest constraint; tail risk lives at the thin single-source inputs.
- **Most useful as a risk flag, not a position:** (1) **China supply-security / weaponization** (ex-China tungsten supply gains strategic value — e.g., Western/Korea tungsten miners like Almonty-type names, *speculative, verify*); (2) **cost-push** — another AI-hardware input inflating, reinforcing the memory-cost/device-price inflation theme; (3) diversified materials names ([[ENTG]], LIN, [[APD]]) are too diluted to be a clean play.
