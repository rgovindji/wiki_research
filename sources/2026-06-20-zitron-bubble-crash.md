# Ed Zitron — AI Bubble Crash (The Majority Report, Jun 16 2026)

## TL;DR
- Bear/perma-bear thesis: AI economics "suck." Claims **OpenAI spent ~$34B to make ~$13B revenue in 2025, lost ~$21B**, and is committed to burn **>$852B by 2030** — a degenerate-gambler loop where costs scale faster than revenue.
- **Contagion map:** OpenAI is "load-bearing" — Zitron claims it underpins ~$250B of Microsoft, ~$300B of Oracle, ~$138B of Amazon, ~$22B of CoreWeave future revenue. If OpenAI fails, "the semiconductor rally dies" (NVDA, Marvell, SanDisk, TSM all hit).
- **The systemic tail he most fears: private credit.** Pensions/insurance annuities fund private credit → private credit funds AI data centers (Blackstone, BlackRock, Carlyle, Apollo named) → no capital reserve requirements. If data-center loans go bad, the hit lands on retirement funds.

## Key facts as Zitron states them (and confidence)
- OpenAI FY2025: ~$34B spent, ~$13B revenue (up from $3.7B), ~$21B loss, ~$22B cash at year-end; $5.73B sales & marketing, $19.18B R&D. (LOW-MED confidence — sourced to a "financial audit" Zitron says he obtained; figures unverified, advocacy outlet, he himself flags uncertainty on the R&D/headcount mismatch.)
- OpenAI plan to burn >$852B by 2030. (LOW — his projection.)
- Revenue-dependency claims: MSFT ~$250B, ORCL ~$300B (Ellison stock leveraged on it), AMZN ~$138B, CRWV ~$22B of future revenue tied to OpenAI. (LOW — magnitudes uncited; treat as directional contagion thesis, not facts.)
- ChatGPT growth "stalled" ~a month ago; both OpenAI and Anthropic reportedly discussing **price cuts**. (MED if true — would be a genuine demand/pricing tell. Track for confirmation.)
- SpaceX IPO: Russell 1000 waived profitability requirement + agreed to add within 5 days of listing; S&P 500 / Nasdaq did NOT waive. SpaceX = X + Grok (loss-making AI) + Starlink (profitable) + rockets; now renting compute to Anthropic. (MED — index-rule claim is checkable; flagged as retail/pension risk.)
- Hallucinations are "mathematically certain" (says OpenAI itself stated this); models must be continuously retrained or they drift → R&D never goes to zero. (MED — directionally true on retraining cost; "mathematically certain" is rhetorical.)

## Key claims (and confidence)
- AI exists because tech has **no other hyper-growth idea** ("not quantum, not robotics"); trillions in capex is desperation, not demand. (LOW — thesis, not evidence; ignores Laffont/Dario demand framing.)
- "Pretty much all economic activity is buying/building data centers + renting compute to ~two companies"; **no provable ROI / productivity gains** from AI. (LOW-MED — contestable; this is THE crux falsifier vs. the bull camp.)
- If OpenAI dies, **every AI-related stock crashes** because OpenAI is "symbolic" of the data-center-forever narrative. (LOW — reflexivity argument.)
- Private credit (funded by pensions/insurance, no reserve requirements) is the real systemic transmission channel; already holed by 2018-22 software LBOs (Medallia, Pluralsight cited). (MED on mechanism, LOW on magnitude — he admits he "cannot confirm" the size.)

6. **ORCL contagion** — Ellison/Oracle revenue concentration on OpenAI; watch Oracle RPO/backlog quality.
