# Intel Should Raise Capital (SemiAnalysis, Jun 11 2026)

## TL;DR
- SemiAnalysis argues Intel should issue ~$25B of fresh equity *now* (≈4-5% dilution), while the stock sits near its richest valuation since the dot-com era and the US-government floor de-risks the offering — the foundry buildout needs capital far beyond what operations can fund.
- The full multi-phase foundry/Terafab project is framed at up to **$119B**; Lip-Bu Tan has already pulled in ~$20B from the US government, SoftBank, Altera, and Nvidia strategic stakes.
- Order book is filling (Nvidia DGX Rubin NVL8 dual Xeon 6 hosts, Google multiyear Xeon + custom IPU deal, SambaNova inference, plus prior SpaceX/Tesla mentions) — but execution risk on $119B of capex is the whole bear case.

## Key facts (with confidence)
- **High confidence (stated by SemiAnalysis):**
  - Proposed raise ~$25B via ~4-5% dilution; "equity remains Intel's cheapest scalable source of capital."
  - ~$20B already raised: US government stake (433M shares @ $20.47 = 9.9%; 149M more in escrow Q1), SoftBank entry $23.00, Nvidia $23.28.
  - Apollo Fab 34 buyback $14.2B (~$7.7B cash + $6.5B bridge), closed Apr 8 2026.
  - Total multi-phase project cost framed at **up to $119B**; Terafab target 100k WSPM scaling toward 1M WSPM (described as "going to be hard").
  - Customer momentum: Nvidia DGX Rubin NVL8 lists dual Intel Xeon 6 host CPUs; Google multiyear Xeon + custom IPU; SambaNova inference.
- **Medium confidence (figures cited but pre-paywall / approximate):**
  - Current debt ~$45.0B (~$51.5B incl. Apollo bridge); market cap ~$498B at time of writing.
  - Comparable cited: Cerebras raised $5.55B.
- **No analyst price targets in the article.** (House rule: none invented.)

## Key claims (and how confident)
- "It's time to undo a decade of financial engineering and issue equity now." — clear editorial thesis (high confidence it's their view; it is an *opinion/recommendation*, not a forecast).
- "Intel can raise cheaply with the U.S. government holding the floor." — the government 9.9% stake acts as a valuation backstop, lowering equity cost of capital. Plausible but assumes the floor holds through a dilutive print.
- Bear framing they themselves note: share price is "not discounting real execution risk"; historical capex deferment when CPU demand disappointed; 1M WSPM Terafab target hard.
