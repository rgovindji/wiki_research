# How China's Unitree Will Dominate Global Robotics

## TL;DR
- Unitree is running the "DJI/BYD playbook" in humanoids/quadrupeds: vertically integrate components, undercut Western pricing massively, and iterate weekly off China's supply chain.
- G1 humanoid: ~$8,976 BOM, $27,300 price (down from $50K+), ~67% gross margin, tripling revenue YoY, ~$300M planned AI R&D. Quadruped costs down 94–96% over 6 years.
- **Unitree is PRIVATE** (IPO filing with Shanghai Stock Exchange noted). Investing read-through is to public competitors (Tesla Optimus) and Western component incumbents (HarmonicDrive) facing China cost pressure.

## Key facts (with confidence)
- **HIGH** — Authors: Reyk Knuhtsen, Niko Ciminelli, Jacob Rintamaki + others. Published Jun 8 2026.
- **HIGH** — Unitree is private; an IPO filing with the Shanghai Stock Exchange is referenced.
- **MEDIUM (SemiAnalysis estimates)** — G1 BOM ~$8,976; price $27,300 (from $50K+ over 12–18 mo); ~67% gross margin.
- **MEDIUM** — Revenue tripling YoY; ~$300M planned AI R&D.
- **MEDIUM** — In-houses motors, gearboxes, LiDAR, depth cameras; actuators/motors ~30–40% cheaper than Western parts.
- **MEDIUM** — Quadruped unit cost down 94–96% over 6 years; SemiAnalysis estimates ~250 units in productive labor settings (2025) and Unitree "may ship its 10,000th humanoid in coming weeks."

## Public companies / read-through (key for investing)
- **PRIVATE (no direct equity):** Unitree itself; Boston Dynamics (owned by Hyundai); Agility Robotics (Digit); ANYbotics.
- **[[TSLA]] (public, competitor/potential loser):** Optimus framed as a work-in-progress that "sources from Chinese supply chain" — Unitree's cost curve is a margin/competitive threat to Western humanoid ambitions.
- **HarmonicDrive (TSE 6324, public, potential LOSER):** gearbox/strain-wave incumbent flagged as facing pressure from Chinese alternatives.
- **LeaderDrive (Chinese, public on China exchanges, potential WINNER):** cheaper gearbox alternative — China-supply-chain beneficiary.
- No US-listed pure-play beneficiary identified; the cleanest US-equity angle is the *bear/competitive-pressure* read on [[TSLA]] Optimus economics.
