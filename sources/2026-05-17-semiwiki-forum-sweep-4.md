TSMC reported strong momentum in its April 2026 revenue update, reinforcing the company's dominant position in the global semiconductor industry amid accelerating artificial intelligence (AI) infrastructure demand... - **[[TSM]]** thesis remains intact; bottleneck-roadmap N2 sold-out claim confirmed

## Thread B: Google passes TSMC relative capex in 2026
**URL**: https://semiwiki.com/forum/threads/google-passes-tsmc-relative-capex-in-2026.24498/
**Started**: by user-nl. 13 posts (user-nl), 10 (hist78), 4 (siliconbruh999, mkwventures, meo9725).

### Key facts
- **GOOGL capex split (verbatim Google CFO disclosure)**:
  - **~60% to machines/servers** (short-duration assets)
  - **~40% to long-duration assets** (data centers, networking equipment)
  - Buildings depreciate over **40+ years**
  - "Other components may be less than that"
- **TSMC management 2024-2029 revenue CAGR target: 25%**
- TSMC gross margins ~60-65%; net profit/gross margin ratio ~0.75 → "TSMC is an enormous oil producer for AI infrastructure"

### Forum-member observations on memory rationing (verbatim from siliconbruh999, repeated 5x in thread)
> "Side note: We are not at equilibrium so we don't know what the impact of constraints are. Qualcomm is not growing because there is not enough memory. Small companies cannot get memory. PC OEMs are having memory issues."

### Structural framing (mkwventures)
> "One powerful result is that the CapEx and R&D spending by Google, Microsoft, Amazon, and Oracle complements TSMC's CapEx and R&D spending, and vice versa. Because of the pure-play foundry business model, each company operates in a focused field, and they end up reinforcing each other's growth."

### Implications
- **[[GOOGL]]**: 60/40 split disclosure is useful for FCF modeling — depreciation accelerates only on the 60% machine component; 40% buildings are 40-year capex with minimal income statement drag
- **[[TSM]]**: management's own 25% CAGR target through 2029 is a hard disclosure — significantly above wiki's previously-cited 10-15% long-term sector growth
- **[[MU]] / memory thesis**: forum's "Qualcomm not growing because of memory" + "PC OEMs having memory issues" is direct corroboration of Dylan Patel's [[2026-05-16-dylan-patel-invest-like-best]] "DRAM 2-3x" thesis — rationing has **already cascaded to QCOM and PC OEMs**
- **Pure-play foundry complementarity** — hyperscaler capex + TSMC capex reinforce each other; not zero-sum

## Thread C: Apple reportedly controls more than half of TSMC's 2nm production capacity
**URL**: https://semiwiki.com/forum/threads/apple-reportedly-controls-more-than-half-of-tsmcs-2nm-production-capacity-depriving-rivals-of-cutting-edge-technology.23659/
**Started**: earlier 2026. Heavy Nenni participation (11 posts).

### Key facts
- **Apple = TSMC's #1 customer**: **NT$624.3 billion 2024 revenue (+14.2% YoY)** = **22% of TSMC's total 2024 revenue**
- **Apple booked >50% of TSMC's 2026 N2 capacity**
- **Baoshan plant fully booked by Apple** for N2; **Kaohsiung plant** serves non-Apple customers
- **TSMC capacity expansion plan**: Baoshan → 4 phases; Kaohsiung → 5+ phases; Southern Taiwan Science Park can also upgrade
- **N2P mass production: H2 2026** (extension for smartphones + HPC)
- **WMCM (Wafer-Level Multi-Chip Module)**: new packaging tech for Apple — MacBook Pro M6, Vision Pro 2026, entry-iPhone

### Tim Millet (Apple VP Platform Architecture) on strategic moat
> "The key lies in competitors being unable to directly access the latest technologies"
> *Apple's strategic tactic*: buying out leading-edge capacity to deny competitors.

### Daniel Nenni's counter (debunking the headline narrative — repeated 3 times in thread)
> "As we all know TSMC builds fabs well in advance based on wafer agreements of all customers so I can assure you there will be no starving of 2nm competitors. I have seen this parroted by other shameless outlets that should know better. Even Yahoo got duped!"

### Reconciling the apparent contradiction with our [[AAPL]] bear thesis
The wiki currently says: TSMC structurally deprioritizing Apple (per Dylan).
This thread says: Apple controls 50%+ of N2 and is TSMC's largest customer at 22% of revenue.

**Reconciliation**:
- **Today**: Apple dominates N2 because Apple was first to commit volume to the node
- **Tomorrow (A16, A14)**: TSMC's priority is shifting to AI customers (per [[2026-05-09-dwarkesh-dylan-semianalysis]] "first customer for A16 is AI, not Apple")
- The bear thesis is about **future** priority for the smaller, denser nodes Apple needs to keep iPhone competitive — not current N2 share
- Apple's response (Intel diversification, own modem, multi-source AP) is consistent with seeing this shift coming
- Apple's 22% of TSMC revenue **today** = bull data point for TSMC; Apple's declining share **tomorrow** = bear data point for AAPL
- These are NOT contradictory once you separate time horizons

### Implications
- **[[TSM]]**: 22% Apple concentration is a real risk data point — single-customer dependence; needs to be on the page bear case
- **[[AAPL]]**: 50%+ of N2 means Apple's product roadmap through 2027 is fully supported; the bear case is about A16/A14 trajectory (2028+)
- **[[bottleneck-roadmap]]**: N2P mass production H2 2026 timing confirms (was implicit)
- **Apple's WMCM packaging** is new — read-through to [[AMKR]] / [[ASX]] / [[CAMT]] (Apple's packaging volume is now in advanced multi-chip territory)
