# Anthropic Closes $30B Series at $900B+ Valuation — Surpasses OpenAI

Anthropic is closing a fresh **>$30B funding round at a >$900B post-money valuation** as soon as this week (May 26-30, 2026). The round is co-led by **Sequoia Capital, Dragoneer Investment Group, Altimeter Capital, and Greenoaks Capital Partners** — each investing roughly $2B.

This is **the first time in history Anthropic has overtaken OpenAI on private market cap**. OpenAI's most recent post-money valuation was $852B in the March 2026 Series G ($122B raise, per [[2026-05-17-multi-agent-research-batch]] Agent A IPO-pipeline notes).

In just **3 months**, Anthropic's valuation has gone from $380B (Series G in February 2026) to >$900B — **a 2.4x rerating in 90 days**.

## TL;DR

- **$30B+ raise at $900B+ post-money valuation**, closing this week (May 26-30, 2026).
- **Co-leads: Sequoia, Dragoneer, Altimeter, Greenoaks** — each writing ~$2B checks. This is institutional buy-side conviction, not strategic-investor buy-side.
- **Anthropic now > OpenAI** ($900B vs. $852B post-Series-G).
- **2.4x rerating in 90 days** (from $380B Feb 2026 to $900B+ May 2026).
- **Corroborates Gavin Baker's "Anthropic added $11B ARR in March alone"** claim from [[2026-05-21-gavin-baker-invest-like-best]] — the absolute ARR growth rate is the underlying reason for the rerating.
- **Implications for AI capex cycle**: a $900B+ private valuation requires sustained 100%+ ARR growth through 2027 to be defensible. **Which means** the implied AI infrastructure demand (compute, memory, networking) implicit in this valuation is structurally enormous and pulls ramp-acceleration through the supply chain.

## What this signals

### 1. Capex demand pull intensifies

A $900B+ valuation embeds enormous forward growth assumptions. Even with Gavin Baker's "5x more capital efficient than OpenAI" framing (from [[2026-05-21-gavin-baker-invest-like-best]]), sustaining valuation requires:

- **Compute commitments scale further** — Anthropic already has 5 GW Google/Broadcom TPU + 5 GW Amazon Tranium = ~10 GW committed. **Which means** another tranche of GW-scale compute commitments is implied at the new valuation; demand pull on hyperscalers, memory, and power infrastructure continues.
- **Hyperscaler revenue concentration deepens.** Damodaran ([[2026-05-16-damodaran-profg-markets]]) flagged hyperscaler revenue concentration in OpenAI+Anthropic: MSFT 50%, ORCL 50%+, GOOGL 43%, AMZN 51%. **Which means** the $900B Anthropic valuation makes those concentrations even more load-bearing for hyperscaler equity stories.

### 2. AI bubble debate: bull leg strengthens, bear leg strengthens

This is a **both/and** event for the [[ai-bubble-debate]]:

- **Bull**: Real ARR ($30B+) backing a real (not vapor) $900B valuation. Anthropic is the only AI startup with disclosed enterprise revenue at this scale. Multi-vendor capital efficiency moat (per Gavin Baker, Anthropic is multi-platform: AWS, Azure, GCP, OCI, Vertex — fungibility moat).
- **Bear**: $900B+ private valuation embeds heroic forward growth assumptions. If the LLM token economics shift (open-source distillation, Mythos 3/4 dynamics per Gavin Baker), Anthropic could face revenue pressure faster than the valuation can absorb. The Anthropic > OpenAI moment also raises the question: is there room for both at $1T+ in 2027, or is this a winner-take-most race?
