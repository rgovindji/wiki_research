# UBS: "generational" semi boom — $2.38T industry by 2027, memory leads

## TL;DR
- UBS (analyst **Nicolas Gaudois**) forecasts global semiconductor **sell-in of $1.62T in 2026 (+118% y/y), then +46% to $2.38T in 2027** — driven by agentic AI broadening demand across memory, logic, and CPU.
- **Memory leads: $961B in 2026 (+318% y/y) → $1.638T in 2027.** Agentic AI "boosting not only HBM but also DDR5/LPDDR5 and NAND flash."
- CPU: $75B 2026 → $96B 2027 despite a sluggish PC market.
- UBS: other cycle indicators "remain in the green" — foundry utilization, assembly-equipment revenues, memory operating profits "all pointing upward into late 2027"; AI upside "could continue to push out the inflexion point in the cycle."

## Key facts
- The +118%/+318% growth rates look extreme; treat the *absolute* 2027 levels ($2.38T total, $1.638T memory) as the anchored claim and the y/y deltas as base-effect-sensitive. (UBS itself flags decel from elevated levels.)
- Corroborating SemiWiki memory feature (2026-06-11): AI servers carry **several TB of high-bandwidth memory vs hundreds of GB for traditional cloud servers** — memory content/server is the structural driver; HBM demand > supply "well into future production cycles" per Micron mgmt; SK hynix leads HBM3E share, Samsung scaling, Micron designed into next-gen accelerators.

## Key claims (and confidence)
- **Directionally high-confidence, magnitude medium.** A $2.38T 2027 industry is a sell-side outlier on the high side; useful as the *bull ceiling* anchor for the memory super-cycle, not a base case.
- The "cycle green into late 2027" call is the investable core: it pushes the cycle-peak debate out, supporting continued memory/equipment exposure.
