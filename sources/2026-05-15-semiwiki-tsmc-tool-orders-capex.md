# TSMC's Record Tool Orders Hint at Another CapEx Shockwave (Daniel Nenni / SemiWiki, May 15 2026)

## TL;DR
- **Q1 2026 board capital appropriations: $31.3B** — headline figure looks lower than Q4 2025's $45.0B, but the composition is the real story
- **$21.0B of the $31.3B is Advanced Node equipment** — the **highest single-quarter advanced-node authorization since tracking began in Q4 2019**
- **Trailing twelve-month Advanced Node equipment approvals = ~$55B** — nearly the entirety of TSMC's full 2026 CapEx guide of ~$56B. Math forces either (a) sharp spending slowdown next quarters or (b) upward guidance revision
- Nenni's call: **probability of a 2Q26 earnings CapEx raise is "increasing"** — direct independent corroboration of Dylan Patel's "approaching $100B by 2028" trajectory from [[2026-05-16-dylan-patel-invest-like-best]]

## Key facts
- **Q1 2026 board appropriations: $31.3B total**
  - Advanced Node equipment: **$21.0B** (record since Q4 2019)
  - Infrastructure: **$10.3B** (normalized down from record $21.4B prior quarter)
  - **Specialty Devices: $0** (no new approvals — see "Note" below)
  - **Advanced Packaging: $0** (no new approvals — see "Note" below)
- **TTM Advanced Node equipment approvals: ~$55B**
- **2026 total CapEx guide: ~$56B** (TSMC's current public number)
- Q4 2025 total appropriations: $45.0B for comparison
- Equipment categories named by Nenni: **lithography, process control, deposition, etch, metrology systems**
- Author: Daniel Nenni (SemiWiki founder, semi industry veteran)
