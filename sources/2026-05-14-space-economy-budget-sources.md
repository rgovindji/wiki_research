# Space Economy Budget Foundations — Consolidated Source Summary

Three foundational documents the wiki had flagged as Phase 2 PDFs. Consolidated into one summary because they're complementary:

1. **Aerospace Corporation — FY 2026 Defense Space Budget: Emergence of Golden Dome** (August 2025) — most authoritative budget breakdown
2. **CSIS — Where Is the Space Force Budget for Commercial Services?** (April 2026, Clayton Swope) — commercial-services-gap analysis
3. **Morgan Stanley — Capital Flows as Space Opens for Business** (2020) — foundational ecosystem framing

## TL;DR

- **FY26 Space Force budget reconciles to $40.2B total** (Aerospace Corp): $26.3B appropriations request (a 9% CUT from FY25 enacted $28.9B) + $13.8B reconciliation = +40% YoY total
- **FY27 Space Force request: $71B (+77% over FY26)** per CSIS — the budget trajectory steepens dramatically
- **Golden Dome = $24.4B in reconciliation funding** = 16% of $153.4B national security reconciliation total. **First space-based missile interceptor program since Reagan SDI ended.**
- **Moving-Target-Indicator satellites: $500M (FY25) → $8.8B (FY26) = 17.6x growth.** Replaces E-3 Sentry AWACS + retired E-8C JSTARS aircraft.
- **Commercial services starvation pattern:** Of $71B FY27 request, only **$1.4B clearly earmarked for commercial — and $1.3B of that is launch (SpaceX)**. Non-launch commercial services (TacSRT, SDA, Earth obs, weather) get the "Pentagon ignores it → Congress adds it" treatment year after year.
- **Commercial satcom: $1.2B annually** — but funded by *other* service branches (Air Force, Army, Navy) NOT Space Force. Space Force runs a working-capital fund for it. New FY26 line for $190M of proliferated LEO satcom (Space Force providing for rest of military).

## Aerospace Corp — Golden Dome detailed breakdown

The $24.4B Golden Dome reconciliation funding splits into 4 categories:

| Category | $ | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Tracking threats from space (pre/post launch)** | **$9.2B** | Military space-based sensors + moving-target-indicator satellites (LEO missile warning) |
| **Space-based missile interceptors** | **$5.6B** | First public program since Reagan SDI. RKLB selected for Space Based Interceptor with Raytheon. |
| **Launch and test range infrastructure** | $910M | National security launch program support |
| **Terrestrial elements (non-space-based)** | $8.7B | Hypersonic + ICBM defense + ground radars |

**Trump claim:** Project cost ~$175B, fielded in ~3 years. **CBO range:** $161B-$542B over 20 years.

**Historical context:** Reagan SDI averaged 1.3% of DOD total appropriations FY86-90. Entire Space Force in FY25 = 3.4% of DOD total. Golden Dome alone would meaningfully add to that.

## Moving-Target-Indicator (MTI) satellites — underappreciated mega-program

- **$500M FY25 → $8.8B FY26 = 17.6x growth.** One of the biggest Golden-Dome-related adds.
- Two capabilities funded:
  - **Ground-moving-target-indicator satellites** — replaces E-8C JSTARS aircraft (retired 2023); tracks adversary surface targets
  - **Air-moving-target-indicator satellites** — replaces E-3 Sentry AWACS + cancelled E-7 Wedgetail; tracks airborne/maritime threats
- E-7 Wedgetail program cancelled in FY26 due to "significant delays, cost increases, and survivability concerns in a contested environment"
- House Appropriations and HASC propose **reinstating E-7** — committees not yet willing to rely entirely on spacecraft

**Investment translation:** Whoever builds these MTI satellites captures a major new revenue stream. Strongest beneficiaries based on radar + bomber heritage: [[NOC]] (E-8 JSTARS legacy + B-21 + radar/EW heritage); [[LMT]] (general space systems prime); RTX (radar IP).

## CSIS — Commercial services starvation pattern

The Pentagon "asks for little for commercial → Congress adds money each year" pattern is well-documented:

| Program | FY24 requested | FY25 requested | FY26 requested | FY27 requested |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| TacSRT (Tactical Surveillance, Reconnaissance, Tracking) | $0 | $0 | $36.6M | $23.7M (LESS than FY26) |
| TacSRT + non-launch commercial line | $0 → Congress added $40M+$10M | $0 → Congress added | $36.6M → $131.4M added by Congress, $80M for TacSRT | $23.7M (without congressional adds yet) |

**The Space Force has NEVER requested a robust budget for non-launch commercial space.** Pattern is structural, not cyclical.

**Names exposed to this gap (negatively):**
- **BKSY (BlackSky)** — Earth observation; TacSRT-class buys are the addressable market
- **PL (Planet Labs)** — same exposure
- **[[ASTS]]** — partially exposed; the $190M new proliferated LEO satcom line is mildly bullish but the broader pattern is that DoD-funded commercial buys are starved
- IRDM, SATS — commercial satcom but $1.2B is funded by service branches, not Space Force

**Names structurally favored (commercial gap is a moat):**
- **SpaceX (private)** — already captures the $1.3B launch line + COMSATCOM
- **[[RKLB]]** — small/medium launch with Department of War Golden Dome win + HASTE hypersonic

**The non-obvious insight:** The Pentagon's commercial gap isn't because primes lobby for it — it's because of "deep-seated reticence to use commercial services for critical space functions, which can be mistaken for either inherently governmental functions or activities closely related to inherently governmental functions." Per CSIS, "non-launch commercial space services are merely extras that are not integrated into the broader force structure."

## Morgan Stanley — Ecosystem framing (2020 baseline)

Older qualitative piece but useful framing for the wiki's [[space-economy]] theme:
- Self-sustaining ecosystem requires patient capital (some funds have **50-year exit horizons**)
- Permanent and long-term capital funds central to the space ecosystem
- "Right now, you see a lot of attention on the launch business" — launch is foundational
- Strategic partnerships are how legacy corporations engage ("Do I watch, invest, or buy?")
- **Government (NASA, NOAA, DoD) still biggest clients** — confirmed by today's CSIS analysis
- Generational shift: Millennials/Gen Z view space careers as viable (vs decades of only NASA path)

This piece is from 2020 — predates the SpaceX IPO confidential filing, Starlink Direct-to-Cell launch, AST SpaceMobile public listing, and the full Golden Dome cycle. **Use as foundational framing, not for current quantitative data.**

## Investment implications by ticker

### [[RKLB]] — Sharpest bull case data point

The Aerospace Corp PDF confirms RKLB's **Space Based Interceptor program selection ties into a $5.6B Golden Dome program category**, not just a single contract. This is a multi-year, multi-billion line item with RKLB-Raytheon partnership. The wiki's existing bull/HIGH stance is reinforced.

### [[NOC]] — Material new data point (MTI satellites)

NOC's bull case sharpens significantly:
- **B-21 Raider** (existing wiki data)
- **Sentinel ICBM** (existing wiki data)
- **NEW: Moving-Target-Indicator satellites** — $500M → $8.8B (17.6x growth) replacing JSTARS/AWACS missions; NOC's radar/EW heritage + E-8 legacy positions them as a primary beneficiary
- Effectively: NOC is now positioned to capture material share of 3 mega-programs simultaneously

### [[LMT]] — Reinforces bull case

LMT touches:
- F-35 (existing wiki)
- Sentinel ICBM co-prime
- Aegis missile defense
- **Golden Dome co-prime** — confirmed via DoD designations
- Space-Based Interceptor adjacency (general space systems)

### [[ASTS]] — Mixed signal

- **Slight positive:** $190M new proliferated LEO satcom line (Space Force buying for rest of military)
- **Negative context:** Broader commercial satcom funding ($1.2B annually) comes from other service branches, not Space Force budget. ASTS positioning depends on which budget line a contract sits in.
- **Watch:** TacSRT-class (Earth obs / SDA) funding for FY27 is actually DOWN from FY26 ($23.7M vs $36.6M). Commercial services not getting the boost in this budget cycle.

### Names NOT in current wiki but flagged

- **BKSY (BlackSky)** — Earth observation; exposed to TacSRT starvation
- **PL (Planet Labs)** — same; possibly more diversified
- **MAXR-equivalents** — defense Earth observation

### Cross-cycle [[MP]] reinforcement

Golden Dome is built on military hardware. F-35 contains ~920 lbs of rare-earth NdFeB magnets per aircraft. Sentinel ICBM + B-21 Raider + Aegis radars + interceptor electronics all use rare earth magnets. Aerospace Corp PDF doesn't quantify magnet demand directly, but the $24.4B Golden Dome funding is a TAM expander for downstream rare-earth processing — reinforces [[MP]]'s bull/HIGH stance.
