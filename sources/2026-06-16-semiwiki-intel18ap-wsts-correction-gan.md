# SemiWiki daily sweep — Intel 18A-P risk production, WSTS "correction inevitable," China GaN ban

## TL;DR
- **Intel 18A-P entered risk production on schedule** (VLSI Symposium 2026): +9% perf at iso-power / −18% power at iso-perf vs 18A, full design-rule compatibility with 18A; 18A itself now ramping in two US fabs with defect density declining ahead of plan. Execution datapoint for the foundry-binary bull leg.
- **Future Horizons (Malcolm Penn) flags a coming correction**: April WSTS semis +106.3% y/y but **ASP-driven, not unit-driven** — Memory +359%, Logic +48.6%, while IC-ex-memory only +38.4% and Analog/Micro/Opto/Discretes still subdued. The contrarian "this growth is untenable" datapoint, well-sourced, for the bear file.
- **China's Supreme Court bans Infineon from selling GaN power chips in China** (upholds Suzhou ruling, ~$1.48M damages); market-leader Innoscience wins its home court while the US ITC runs the inverse case. GaN power-semi competitive/IP datapoint.

## Key facts
- **Intel 18A-P** (first performance enhancement in the 18A family): dual-contact "Power Boost" transistor (front + backside PowerVia contacts) for higher drive current; new 5th Vt option between ULVT and LVT; 20–40% better thermal resistance; 10–30% lower via resistance; 33% tighter skew corners. Iso-perf/iso-power figures measured on a fully-routed **Arm core** test vehicle. 18A = Intel's first RibbonFET GAA + PowerVia backside-power node, "already powering multiple client products, data center to follow." Lip-Bu Tan's TSMC-style "say it, do it, show it" cadence cited as the cultural change.
- **WSTS April 2026**: total semis +106.3% y/y (+22.3% m/m vs −8.5% prior month); 32nd consecutive month of positive y/y growth (2nd-longest on record). Penn's thesis: a single end-market (AI datacenter) driving Memory/Logic/GPU on ASP, not broad unit demand or GDP — historically the setup that precedes a correction. He's explicitly "out on a limb and alone" vs consensus calling for years more growth.
- **GaN**: Infineon barred from selling/importing GaN power chips in China (upholds May 27 Suzhou judgment, 10M yuan ≈ $1.48M). Innoscience (China) is the volume leader; parallel cases run in the US (ITC affirmed an Innoscience infringement of an Infineon patent, but cleared Innoscience's redesigned parts) and Munich. Multi-region patent war over the GaN power layer.

## Key claims (and confidence)
- 18A-P on schedule + 18A defect density ahead of plan → **medium-high** (Intel self-reported at a technical symposium; directionally corroborates the June 8 Google-TPU-on-Intel-Foundry win, but vendor-sourced).
- WSTS "ASP-driven, correction inevitable" → **the data is hard WSTS; the conclusion is one analyst's contrarian read.** Useful precisely because it's the strongest-sourced bear datapoint on the memory/logic super-cycle, not because it's consensus.
- GaN ruling → **high** (court rulings, multiple regions).
