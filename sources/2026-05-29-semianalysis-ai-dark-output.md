# AI Dark Output: The Visible Cost of Invisible Output

**Authors:** Malcolm Spittler and Dylan Patel. Published 2026-05-29.

## TL;DR
- "Dark Output" = real AI-generated economic value that national accounts (GDP, prices, labor stats) cannot currently see, because services lack quantity units and AI work leaves no receipt beyond token spend. The costs (GPUs, power, tokens) are fully visible; the output is not.
- This is the modern Solow Paradox: "AI output will be real before it is measurable." Risk: if statistics can't capture the output, the AI boom could read in the macro data as an AI bust — distorting the bubble/ROI debate.
- Framing matters for investors: the "AI has no ROI" bear case (Zitron et al.) partly rests on un-measured returns. Dark Output is the steelman that the returns exist but are statistically invisible — though by construction it's a thesis you can't yet prove with the data.

## Key facts (with confidence)
- **Two categories:** Substitution Dark Output (AI doing work humans did — ~$1.5T in task EXPOSURE, not confirmed output) and New Dark Output (work that didn't exist until AI made it cheap — likely larger long-term). (Medium — conceptual; the $1.5T is exposed-labor, explicitly NOT realized value.)
- **Why services break the metric:** national accounts measure receipts/surveyed prices, not quantities. Price collapse reads as output DECLINE; displaced junior staff raises AVERAGE wages → recorded as inflation + productivity decline, the inverse of reality. (Medium-high — sound accounting logic.)
- **Illustrative price collapse:** legal doc drafting ~$400 (1990s) → $150 (2010, LegalZoom) → ~$0.50 (2026, frontier API). The final 99%+ drop vanishes from stats. (Medium — illustrative, not a measured series.)
- **Four measurement errors:** boundary shift (purchased services move in-house via AI), price collapse, sector misrouting (value created in healthcare shows up as software-vendor revenue), new-work invisibility. (Medium.)
- **Observable "fingerprints":** AI-exposed sectors show falling employment + rising average wages (junior-displacement signature); 37% of tokens in computers/math (Anthropic Economic Index, Mar 2026) yet software-investment GDP contribution unchanged → implies NEW work, not substitution. (Medium — suggestive correlations, not causal proof.)
- **Evidence ladder (deployment, not benchmarks):** Tier 1-2 benchmarks (weak) → Tier 3 public claims → Tier 4 production use → Tier 5 legal defense of AI work → Tier 6 insurance underwriting AI risk (strongest). Current evidence supports augmentation, not replacement; NO Tier 5-6 documented yet. (Medium — useful framework; the augmentation-not-replacement read is a notable bear-leaning admission.)
- **Historical precedent:** feminist/care economics (Waring 1988; Reid 1934) — unpaid household work = 78% of market economy (Australia), 63.1% of GDP (UK), ~$11T/yr globally — shows national accounts have systematically omitted huge real output before. (Medium — analogy.)
- **Policy hook:** incoming Fed Chair Kevin Warsh (Dec 2025): "If you're looking at the data... you're backward looking." (High — attributed quote.)
