# Dylan Patel (SemiAnalysis) — Invest Like the Best

## TL;DR

Highest-density semiconductor / AI-economics source ingested to date. Recorded immediately after Anthropic's **Opus 4.7 launch** (~May 16, 2026). Key claims:

- **Anthropic gross margins now floor at 72%** (was 30-something% at start of 2026 per leaked funding docs) — massive margin expansion as token pricing can't drop fast enough to match capability gains.
- **"DRAM will double or triple from here still"** — Dylan emphatically rejects the consensus that memory is "overplayed."
- **"GPU useful life is clearly not 5 years. It's maybe 7 or 8."** — Hopper clusters resigning for 3-4 more years; A100s for 2+ more. **Directly refutes the Burry-style depreciation bear case** the wiki tracks.
- **TSMC 2028 capex potentially $100B** — Dylan's projection vs current 2026 guide of $57.4B.
- **Anthropic now MORE compute-constrained than OpenAI** — major strategic-positioning shift since Dario "used to gloat" about Anthropic's conservative compute approach.
- **SemiAnalysis internal Claude Code spend: $7M/yr vs $25M salary = 28% of payroll on AI** — productivity reset data point.
- **Few-shot robotics breakthrough in 6-18 months** — VLA models are dead-end (per Dylan + LeCun); sample-efficient pre-training is the unlock.
- **Dylan prediction**: "Large-scale protests against Anthropic and OpenAI in 3 months."

## Connect-the-dots / investment implications

### 1. Memory thesis sharpens decisively for [[MU]]
"DRAM double or triple from current" is the highest-conviction quantitative call Dylan has made on the wiki record. **Reinforces [[MU]] bull case** but the wiki's existing concerns (declining ASIC mix, Samsung HBM4 catching up) still apply. Adds urgency to the [[bottleneck-roadmap]] memory-as-binding-constraint framing.

### 2. GPU depreciation bear case structurally weakens
The Burry-style "GPUs depreciate in 3-5 years → hyperscaler asset write-downs coming" bear thesis the wiki tracks is **directly refuted** by Dylan's resigning-contract data. **Bullish for [[CRWV]], [[NBIS]], and the hyperscaler-cloud-economics thesis**. The wiki's [[2026-04-01-semianalysis-gpu-rental-index]] H100-worth-more-today framing extended further.

### 3. Anthropic margin expansion is the lab-economics narrative arc of 2026
**30-something% → 72% gross margin in 5 months** is the cleanest single data point the wiki has on lab profitability. Strongly bullish for the lab-economics-work bull case in [[ai-bubble-debate]]. Also indirectly bullish for compute layer ([[NVDA]], [[CRWV]], [[NBIS]]) because Anthropic margin expansion = more capex they can fund.

### 4. Compute-constrained Anthropic = positive for OpenAI competitive position
Dylan: "OpenAI will hit Opus 4.6 tier next... maybe Anthropic gets 70%+ gross margins, but if OpenAI hits it, they charge 50% gross margins. They still get all the incremental demand." Important for evaluating the lab competitive landscape.

### 5. TSMC 2028 $100B capex if realized
At $100B, that's 2x current 2026 spend. Direct read-through to [[ASML]] (EUV), [[AMAT]], [[LRCX]], [[KLAC]] equipment vendors. Reinforces [[bottleneck-roadmap]] EUV-as-2028-bottleneck framing.

### 6. Concentration of model access tier
Anthropic restricting Mythos/Opus 4.7 to high-ARR enterprise customers + "Ken Griffin signs $10B token reservation" scenario. **Bear for retail / individual access; bull for top-tier enterprise advantage.** Consistent with Dylan's "permanent underclass" framing.

### 7. Robotics breakthrough timing tightens
**6-18 months for few-shot learning robotic models** is more aggressive than Levine (5yr) and Fan (2-3yr Turing test). Adds urgency to [[robotics]] component thesis (MP, OUST, ALGM).

### 8. Supply-chain breadth of sold-out status validates [[copper-thesis]]
Dylan's spontaneous list of sold-out inputs includes copper foil for PCBs — independent validation of the [[copper-thesis]] page. Also names: glass fibers, lasers, MKSI components, CPUs, FPGAs.

### 9. Phantom GDP framing (from SemiAnalysis economist)
The "AI productivity doesn't show up in GDP because it deflates output prices faster than it grows volume" framing connects to Damodaran's "AI is net expense for non-chip Mag 7" data ([[2026-05-16-damodaran-profg-markets]]). Both can be true simultaneously.

### 10. Anti-AI protest prediction (low conviction)
Dylan predicting large-scale anti-AI protests in 3 months. Sam Altman's house Molotov'd twice in two weeks per Dylan. **Worth tracking as a sentiment indicator**, not as a wiki action item — sentiment shifts on AI could affect equity multiples for the lab-adjacent stack.
