# Baker calls the end of the bottleneck trade — and the tape confirms it the same day

## TL;DR
- **Gavin Baker (Atreides):** *"The bottleneck trade is nearing its end. That was the game for the last year. The next game is what has enduring franchise value on the other side of these bottlenecks, whenever that is."*
- **June 16 tape is a live illustration:** on a BoJ-hike + Fed-eve risk-off day, the supply-side/bottleneck book got routed while demand-side platforms and value held — exactly the rotation Baker describes.
- This is framework-level evidence and a source the wiki already weights heavily (TerraFab thesis, "TSMC capacity discipline = bubble-prevention").

## Key facts — the rotation (2026-06-16 closes, consecutive-session % )
- **Supply-side / bottleneck names crushed:** [[MRVL]] −9.8%, [[ALAB]] −7.1%, [[MU]] −6.2% ($1,020.76), [[AVGO]] −4.4%, [[VRT]] −4.0%, [[TSM]] −3.5%, [[NVDA]] −2.4%; AMD −5.9%, INTC −7%.
- **Demand-side / franchise / value held:** [[GOOGL]] +1.1%; **Dow +0.64% to a record** (~52,000); S&P roughly flat; [[CRWV]] +9.7% (idiosyncratic, see below).
- **Macro trigger:** BoJ raised its policy rate to **1.0% (from 0.75%), first time since 1995**, 7-1 vote, hawkish forward guidance (more hikes coming) — carry-unwind pressure that historically hits the most-crowded momentum first. Fed-eve (first FOMC under Chair **Warsh**, decision June 17).

## Key claims (and confidence)
- **Baker's two claims, graded:**
  1. *Timing* ("nearing its end") — directionally right; it's the cyclical-vs-secular distinction. Bottleneck rents self-liquidate (scarcity→capex→supply→rent compresses). The Street pricing cyclical rents as secular franchises (MU as "infrastructure not commodity", AAOI ~14x fwd sales) is the late-cycle tell. **Medium-high confidence on direction.**
  2. *The next game* ("enduring franchise value") — right question, hard answer. Split the bottleneck basket: **durable franchise that's also bottlenecked** ([[TSM]], [[ASML]], MU's HBM specifically) vs **pure cyclical rent-capturer** ([[AAOI]], AXT, commodity DRAM, pluggable modules). NVDA is the open question (CUDA franchise vs GPU-scarcity-bottleneck).
- **The hedge that matters:** "whenever that is." The wiki's own demand-underestimation evidence (HBM sold out to 2027-28, GPU rental booked out, Dylan Patel "everyone's building X-1") argues against being early — the price peak front-runs the fundamental resolution by quarters. **Framework to tilt, not a switch to flip.** Watch the second derivative: memory contract prices decelerating, CoWoS lead times shrinking, first hyperscaler capex guide-down.

## Contradicting evidence / tensions (surface, don't resolve)
- **Directly challenges the newsletter ledger prediction `2026-06-01-supply-over-demand`** (supply-side AI outperforms demand-side platforms through Sept 1). June 16 is the strongest single-day evidence *against* it logged so far; GOOGL-green-while-semis-bleed is the rotation in miniature. NOT resolved — one risk-off day before a Fed isn't a trend — but the close run should weigh it.
- Counter: BofA's "AI Big 10" (adds AVGO/AMD/MU to Mag 7) and Micron-may-supply-more-HBM4-for-Vera-Rubin-than-expected both landed the same week — the bottleneck names still have live structural bull catalysts. Baker's call is about *relative* forward returns, not absolute collapse.
