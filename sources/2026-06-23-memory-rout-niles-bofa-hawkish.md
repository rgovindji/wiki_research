# Global Memory Rout — Niles "speed-bump" + BofA's 75bp-of-hikes Call Crack the Crowded Trade

## TL;DR
- The most crowded trade on earth (memory) unwound at once: **KOSPI −9.99% with a circuit-breaker halt** (4th this year), Samsung & SK Hynix each −12%+, ASML −5% in Europe, **MU −13.2% to $1,051.77 the day before its own print**, SanDisk −13.6%.
- US tape: **SPX −1.44% to 7,365.47** (low 7,347.60, high 7,424.17 — made its low early, rallied 76 pts off it), **Nasdaq −2.21% to 25,587.04**, **Dow −0.09% to 51,665.49** (near-flat Dow = a chip event, not broad risk-off). VIX +12.8% to 19.49; gold *fell* (de-risking, not panic).
- Trigger was positioning + sentiment, not a fundamental datapoint: **Dan Niles** said he cut chip holdings and warned of a near-term AI "speed bump"; a cautious Broadcom AI-chip note; and **BofA went to the Street's most hawkish Fed call — 75bp of HIKES in 2026** (Sep/Oct/Dec to 4.25–4.5%, "inflation has gotten unambiguously worse").

## Key claims (and how confident)
- **A valuation reset, not a demand change (high confidence on mechanism).** Nothing overnight touched a signed memory contract; a crowded trade gaps because there's no marginal buyer when everyone is the same way. The disambiguator is MU's June 24 guide.
- **The intra-tech split is the supply-over-demand rotation in reverse (high).** Supply side led down (−7 to −13%) while demand-side platforms held up (GOOGL −1%); a supply-side-specific de-risking, not a broad AI-bubble pop.
- **The new macro leg is real (medium-high).** BofA's hike call is a genuine higher-for-longer shock that compounds the AI-ROI doubt — two independent reasons to sell a record-high trade.
