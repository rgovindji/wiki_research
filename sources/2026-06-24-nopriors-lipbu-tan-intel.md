# Re-engineering the Semiconductor Supply Chain — Intel CEO Lip-Bu Tan (No Priors, ~June 18 2026)

## TL;DR
- **Primary-source confirmation** of the INTC recap + foundry-double-down thesis from the CEO himself: US-government shareholder + Jensen/NVDA $5B (now *"$25B or more"* — i.e. the NVDA stake has ~5x'd) + SoftBank rebuilt a *"horrible"* balance sheet. Stock has returned *"six time"* in his 14 months; his stated goal is **10x over 5-10 years** (vs ~76-85x he made at Cadence).
- **CPU/agentic-demand inflection, quantified (loosely):** the training CPU:GPU attach ratio was historically *"one to eight,"* now *"one to four, maybe one to [fewer]"* — CPUs are *"highly in demand"* for agentic AI + inference + RL orchestration. Corroborates the +27% server-ASP leg and Sachin Katti's identical claim.
- **Foundry timeline is explicitly long-dated: 2030-2032.** *"By 2030, 2032, 31, 32 I think it's starting to surface."* Admits foundry is *"very distant from TSMC."* The binary still converts on yield-at-volume, which he does not quantify.

## Key facts / claims (CEO, bias-flagged)
- **Recap:** US gov became a *"big shareholder"* (he pitched Trump on the TSMC/Japan/Singapore precedent of government backstops); Jensen put in *$5B → now "$25B or more"*; SoftBank (he was ex-board) *"lend a hand."*
- **CPU demand:** agentic AI + inference CPU *"highly in demand"*; attach ratio 1:8 → 1:4(or better); AI-model devs tell him CPU is *better* for *"orchestrating all the agents"* in RL. → demand-side corroboration of the server-ASP bull leg.
- **Foundry double-down (decided to NOT exit):** 18A → **14A (=1.4nm) in production planning**, then *"10 and 7"* (1.0 / 0.7nm) on the roadmap. Advanced packaging **EMIB ("e-IP")** next-gen vs TSMC CoWoS; *"starting to run out of steam,"* so investing in new materials: **glass substrate (invested in 3DGS), GaN / SiC / indium phosphide, artificial diamond (Diamond Foundry)**. New **India + New Mexico advanced-packaging program** announced.
- **Terafab / Elon Musk:** *"He decided he want to build his own fab"*; Intel collaborates *weekly*, enabling Musk *"faster and quicker to production"* using Intel tech/process. Calls Musk *"the best entrepreneur of this century"*; both believe semiconductor infrastructure *"is not catching up with AI growth."*
- **Bottlenecks named by Tan:** (1) **power** (some countries simply lack it); (2) **helium** — *"a lot of people didn't realize the helium impact can be quite significant"* (NEW flagged input bottleneck); (3) **memory shortage** — *"everybody scramble for memory,"* pricing going up and *"we have to pass the cost to the customer"* (corroborates memory supercycle); (4) CPU/GPU capacity (*"couple of years"* to build a fab).
- **Compute location:** bullish on **edge / client / physical-AI** compute, not only centralized DC. *"Game is not over yet"* on PC client + edge + agentic + physical AI. Believes some applications are *"better served by client or edge compute than... only by the data [center]."*
- **Political color:** Trump *"asked me to resign"* over a conflict-of-interest one morning *"with no exceptions"*; Tan got a meeting, explained himself (Malaysia-born, Singapore-raised, MIT, lifelong US resident), and was reinstated.
- **Market-cap snapshot he cited (recording date):** NVDA $5.3T, Broadcom & TSMC ~$2T each, AMD ~$800B, Intel ~$600B.
