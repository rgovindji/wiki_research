# Micron Fiscal Q3 2026 Earnings — Prepared Remarks (reported June 24, 2026 AMC)

## TL;DR
- **A record blowout that beat the high end across the board** — rev $41.5B (+74% QoQ, +346% YoY), GM **84.9%**, non-GAAP EPS **$25.11**; FQ4 guide is **$50B ±$1B rev / ~86% GM / ~$31 EPS**, miles above Street (~$43B). Stock **+9–10% AH**.
- **The structural headline: 16 Strategic Customer Agreements (SCAs)** — multi-year (CY2026–2030) **take-or-pay** contracts covering ~20% of DRAM volume + ~⅓ of NAND volume, expected to be ~half-or-more of company revenue when complete. Big ones carry a **price floor** through the term that locks in *"a very robust gross margin… well above our peak quarterly margins in any past cycle."* This is the most direct answer yet to the cycle-peak-P/E bear.
- **The cycle-maturity tells are in the same print:** FQ4 GM reflects *"a meaningful moderation in the rate of price increases"* (second derivative rolling); FY27 capex guided *above* FQ4 levels with construction pull-in (funding the 2028 supply wave); 85% GM is itself an apex signal.

## Key facts

### The print (fiscal Q3, non-GAAP)
- **Revenue $41.5B**, +74% QoQ, +346% YoY — 5th straight record; the $17.6B sequential add is the largest in company history (prior record $10.2B).
- **DRAM $31.3B** (76% of rev, +343% YoY, +67% QoQ): bits +low-single-digit %, **prices +low-60s %**.
- **NAND $9.9B** (24% of rev, +361% YoY, +99% QoQ): bits +mid-single-digit %, **prices +mid-80s %**.
- **Gross margin 84.9%** (+10pp QoQ), record. **Op income $33.7B, op margin 81.2%** (+54pp YoY). **EPS $25.11** (+106% QoQ). Tax rate 14.9%.
- **OCF $25.4B; capex $7.1B; FCF $18.3B (record).** Inventory $8.6B / 120 days (DRAM below 120, "very tight").
- **Net cash $24.4B** (cash/investments $30.2B; reduced debt $4.4B). Upgraded to **BBB+** by all three agencies.
- Business units: **CMBU $13.8B (83% GM), CDBU $11.5B (87% GM), MCBU $11.5B (87% GM), AEBU $4.6B (79% GM)** — all records.
- **Data center revenue >$25B** (>$100B annualized run rate); **DC SSD >$5B** (>2x QoQ).
- **Declared $0.15/sh dividend.**

### Guidance (fiscal Q4)
- **Revenue $50.0B ±$1B** (record; Street ~$43B). **GM ~86.0%.** Opex ~$1.65B. Share count ~1.15B. **EPS ~$31.00 ±$1.00.**
- FQ4 GM outlook *"reflects a meaningful moderation in the rate of price increases."*
- **Capex:** FQ4 ~$10B → FY26 ~$27B total; **FY27 quarterly capex above FQ4 levels**, >half the YoY increase from **construction** (pulling in cleanroom for long-term demand).
- Opex +~$1B in FY27 (R&D); FY27 tax ~15%. After Dec 9, 2026 (2nd anniversary of CHIPS definitive agreements), intend to raise capital return toward **100% of excess cash** over time.

### Strategic Customer Agreements (the structural change)
- **16 SCAs** across data center, consumer, auto. 4 very large + 3 medium + smaller auto customers. ≈**20% of DRAM volume + ⅓ of NAND volume** over the period; **~half or more of company revenue** under SCAs when complete.
- **Take-or-pay**, binding volume commitments; **5-year term (CY2026–2030)**, auto 3-year.
- Largest agreements: **ceiling price at current CQ2 market price + a floor price held through the term.** Fixed/ceiling-near-CQ2 ≈ 40% of revenue when all SCAs execute. *"For our SCAs with price bands, the floor price enables a very robust gross margin for Micron, well above our peak quarterly margins in any past cycle."*
- **14 of 16 carry ~$100B cumulative revenue at the *minimum* price** over the term.
- **$22B of cash deposits + financial commitments** ($18B as cash deposits), hitting the balance sheet mostly in FQ4 (financing cash flow, does **not** affect FCF; returned to customers in the back half of the term).
- **RPO** >$5B at FQ3-end; **~$100B** including SCAs executed after FQ3 (minimum committed volumes/pricing — conservative; revenue expected to well exceed RPO).

### Technology / product
- **HBM4 12-high ramping 2x as fast as HBM3E; already >$1B HBM4 revenue**, expects mature yields faster than HBM3E.
- 1γ DRAM + G9 NAND ramping to become highest-volume nodes in company history; next-gen nodes to volume in H2 CY2027.
- Multi-year **EUV supply agreement with ASML** for 1δ (1-delta) and beyond. ID1 first wafers mid-CY2027; ID2 late CY2028; NY cluster broke ground Jan 2026. Singapore HBM advanced packaging contributes from H1 CY2027.
- Blended DRAM cost-per-bit projected to **rise** from here (higher-value mix: LP5→LP6, DDR5→DDR6, new HBM gens) — SCAs provide for price premiums on new products.

### Market outlook
- Supply-demand for **both DRAM and NAND tight beyond CY2027**; industry supply improves only *gradually* in 2028, with **no line of sight** to when supply catches demand.
- CY2026 industry bit shipments (DRAM + NAND) expected to **more than double vs two years ago**. DRAM industry bits CY2026 +low-to-mid-20s % (slightly above prior); NAND ~+20%. Micron DRAM supply ~in line with industry; Micron NAND somewhat below.
- Server units CY2026 raised to **high-teens %** (from low-double-digits), with a *modest reduction in avg server DRAM content* as customers maximize unit ships amid tight allocation.

## Key claims (and how confident)
- **The SCAs materially de-risk the downcycle for ~half the book — high confidence the contracts exist as described, lower confidence on enforceability through a real bust.** Take-or-pay is binding, but counterparties in distress renegotiate; the floor "above past-peak margins" is struck at the most extreme pricing environment in 25 years, so "above past peak" and "well below today's 85%" can both be true.
- **The cycle is still maturing.** "Meaningful moderation in the rate of price increases" + capex/construction pull-in for FY27 = the second derivative is rolling and Micron is funding the 2028 supply response. The blowout and the late-cycle tells are in the same document.
- **Demand caution from the rout did NOT show up in the guide** — resolves [[overview|mu-guide-holds-demand]] RIGHT: the FQ4 guide was raised hugely, not cut, and the multi-year shortage was reaffirmed/extended. The June 23 −13% was a positioning reset, not a demand break.
