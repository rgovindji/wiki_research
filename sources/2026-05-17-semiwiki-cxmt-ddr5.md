# Chinese Memory Module Makers Ramp Up Production as CXMT DDR5 Breakthrough Hits Market

Thread started May 15, 2026 by Fred Chen (SemiWiki contributor). Small thread (5 posts) but high-signal: detailed market data on CXMT (ChangXin Memory Technologies), China's only domestic DRAM maker at mass production.

## TL;DR
- **CXMT shipping DDR5 in commercial volumes** through Chinese module vendors (Powev/Sinker, Comay, Gloway, KingBank)
- DDR5 launched **November 2025** at **speeds up to 8,000 Mbps** and die densities **16Gb / 24Gb**
- **One generation behind** Samsung/SK Hynix/Micron (32Gb dies are current frontier)
- **CXMT planning Shanghai STAR IPO**: 29.5B yuan (~$4.34B) raise; prospectus accepted Dec 30, 2025
- **CXMT 9M 2025 revenue: +97.8% YoY to 32.08B yuan (~$4.5B)**; net loss narrowing
- **Big 3 still dominate**: Samsung 32.6%, SK Hynix 33.2%, Micron 25.7% = **91.5% global DRAM share** in Q4 2025 (TrendForce)
- China Merchants Securities: memory sector in "AI-driven structural supercycle"; tightness through 2027

## Key facts

### CXMT product roadmap
- DDR5 official debut: November 2025
- Speeds: up to **8,000 Mbps**
- Densities: **16Gb and 24Gb** — vs Samsung/Hynix/Micron's 32Gb frontier
- LPDDR (low-power for mobile) already in supply chains of Xiaomi, OPPO, Vivo, Transsion, Lenovo
- Hefei-based, founded 2016

### CXMT financials (per Shanghai STAR prospectus)
- Revenue 9M 2025: **32.08B yuan** (+97.8% YoY)
- Net loss: 5.98B yuan (narrowed from 6.84B prior year)
- Target raise: **29.5B yuan** (~$4.34B) at Shanghai STAR Market
- Application accepted by SSE on Dec 30, 2025

### Module ecosystem shipping CXMT-based product
- **Powev (Sinker brand)**: 64GB DDR5-5600 RDIMM in **mass production**, passed enterprise testing
- **Comay**: DDR5 modules for industrial / enterprise
- **Gloway, KingBank**: consumer DDR5 since **late 2024**
- **Sugon**: launched FlashNexus 9000 storage May 13, claims **100% domestic** core components

### Global DRAM market structure (Q4 2025 per TrendForce)
- Samsung Electronics: 32.6%
- SK Hynix: 33.2%
- Micron Technology: 25.7%
- **Big 3 combined: 91.5%**
- Others (including CXMT): 8.5%

## Connect the dots: this is complement, not counter, to the [[MU]] HBM thesis

**Initial framing** (when this thread surfaced): CXMT DDR5 = supply expansion = potential counter-thesis to Dylan Patel's "DRAM 2-3x" thesis from earlier today ([[2026-05-16-dylan-patel-invest-like-best]]).

**On closer reading**: CXMT is *complementary* to the thesis, not a substitute.
- Big 3 are vacating commodity DRAM to make HBM (Dylan's "wafer conversion" claim)
- This creates a commodity DRAM supply gap
- CXMT is the supply response that plugs the gap
- Net effect: DRAM market **bifurcates** — leading-edge / HBM stays expensive (Big 3 dominate); commodity DRAM eroded to Chinese supply
- **Which means** the structural pricing power lives at HBM/leading-edge, where [[MU]] is positioned

**Implications for [[MU]]:**
- HBM4 ramp thesis intact — CXMT can't reach HBM without EUV
- Commodity DRAM share lost to China structurally — but commodity DRAM is the lowest-margin segment anyway
- The "DRAM 2-3x pricing" claim probably applies specifically to **HBM and leading-edge DDR5/LPDDR5X** that remain Big 3 territory
- CXMT's 24Gb is competing with Big 3 16Gb (one gen back) at lower price points — pulls commodity supply back into balance but doesn't touch the AI premium
