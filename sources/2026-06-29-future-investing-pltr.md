# Future Investing — "Why I Changed My Mind On Palantir Stock"

*Bias: HIGH / retail. A YouTube investor who bought the ~$107 dip and is talking his book ("screaming buy"). Captured because the **numbers check out against the Q1 2026 8-K** even if the framing ignores valuation risk. Triggered a refresh of the existing [[PLTR]] page.*

## TL;DR
- Creator was a long-time PLTR skeptic ("overvalued software name") from $20→$200; **changed his mind after a ~40% drawdown** ($207→~$106), bought ~$107.50, adds under $130.
- The case is **fundamentals + drawdown**: revenue growth *accelerated* to ~85%, margins exploded, Rule-of-40 at 145%, Maven became a program of record. Verified vs primary data.
- **What he underweights: valuation.** Even post-drawdown, PLTR is ~62x sales / ~71-80x forward earnings — the binding risk the page now leads with.

## Key facts (verified vs Q1 2026 8-K / market data)
- Q1 2026 revenue **$1.633B, +84.7% YoY** (total, not just a segment — confirmed); **US commercial +133%**; FY26 guide **+71%**.
- **NDR 150%** (and PLTR *excludes* first-year customers, so understated); **Rule of 40 = 145%** (vs ~64% two years ago).
- Net margin ~5%→~53%; net income ~$27M→~$876M; FCF ~$86M→~$891M/qtr (~10x in 3 yrs); **~$8B cash, zero debt**.
- Expenses falling as % rev (R&D 18.6%→9%, SBC 21.4%→11%, SG&A 59.4%→30%).
- RPO growing ~135% YoY (faster than revenue); >$10M deals the fastest-growing cohort.
- **Maven = Pentagon program of record** (no re-bidding; locked-in staple at ~73% gov contribution margin).
- Valuation: **~62x sales, forward P/E ~71-80x** (GuruFocus/stockanalysis); creator frames "cheapest PLTR has been" (true only *relative to its own 200x+ peak*). Consensus PT cited ~$200.

## Key claims (and how confident)
- **Fundamentals are genuinely elite (HIGH — verified).** I assumed retail cherry-picking; the 85%/150%/145% figures are real per the 8-K. Full credit.
- **"Screaming buy under $130" (LOW — ignores valuation).** 62x sales prices ~a decade of flawless execution; the drawdown made it *less insane vs its own mania*, not cheap. Multiple compression is the dominant return driver.
- **Implicit "AI strengthens PLTR's moat, doesn't commoditize it" (MEDIUM-HIGH).** Correct — PLTR is the deployment/ontology layer (winner side of [[ai-software-disruption]]); AI raises its ceiling.
