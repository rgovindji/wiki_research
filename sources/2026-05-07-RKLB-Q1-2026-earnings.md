# Rocket Lab (RKLB) Q1 2026 Earnings — Source Summary

## TL;DR

- **Revenue $200.3M (+63.5% YoY)** — record; beat Space Systems + Launch segment estimates
- **Backlog $2.2B (+20% QoQ, ~2x YoY)** — sold more launches in Q1 2026 than full year 2025
- **GAAP gross margin 38.2% — record**
- **Cash $1.21B** (up from $829M EOY 2025; raised >$2B liquidity access)
- **Q2 guide $225-240M** (continued sequential growth)
- **Neutron debut on track later 2026** — first medium-lift rocket competing with SpaceX Falcon 9
- **Department of War Space Based Interceptor program win (with Raytheon)** — Golden Dome missile defense; material defense diversification
- Closed Mynaric acquisition; agreed Motiv Space Systems acquisition

## Why this matters strategically

RKLB is the only US-listed pure-play small-/medium-lift launch company with both commercial and defense customers at scale. Three structural drivers:

1. **Neutron entry into medium-lift** — currently SpaceX Falcon 9 is the only credible medium-lift in the West. Even partial market share at Neutron's debut creates a step-change in TAM access for RKLB.
2. **Space Systems growing faster than Launch** — Mynaric (laser inter-satellite links) + Motiv (mech systems) + Gauss propulsion deepens the vertically-integrated story. This is the "pickaxe vs gold miner" framing — RKLB sells parts AND launches.
3. **Defense pivot accelerating** — Space Based Interceptor (Golden Dome) + $190M HASTE hypersonic deal + Department of War partnerships convert RKLB from "space startup" to "Tier 2 defense contractor with launch capability."

## Key claims (and confidence)

- **Claim:** Neutron debuts later 2026. → **Medium confidence.** Hardware integration progressing; Archimedes engine qualification ongoing. Historical base rate for small-launch companies missing maiden-flight dates is high.
- **Claim:** Defense diversification is durable. → **High confidence.** Department of War Golden Dome program is a multi-year build; RKLB-Raytheon partnership is a real lock.
- **Claim:** Cash position $1.21B + $2B liquidity = no near-term dilution risk. → **High confidence.** Strong relative to capex pace.
- **Claim:** $2.2B backlog converts to revenue. → **Medium-high confidence.** Backlog quality varies by customer (defense + commercial); some legacy Electron backlog already proven to convert.
