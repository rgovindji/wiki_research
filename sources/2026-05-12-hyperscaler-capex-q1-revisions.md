# 2026-05-12 — Big 4 hyperscaler 2026 capex consolidated at $725B (+77% YoY)

A synthesis of Q1 2026 earnings prints from Microsoft, Google, Amazon, and Meta, plus follow-on analyst coverage. The headline: combined 2026 capex is now $725B, materially above the $670B figure that anchored our prior [[ai-capex-cycle]] page. **Up from ~$410B in 2025 — +77% YoY.**

## The numbers (Q1 2026 earnings calls)

| Company | 2026 Capex Guide | vs. Prior / Consensus | Drivers cited |
|---|---|---|---|
| **[[MSFT]]** | **$190B** | vs. $152B analyst consensus (+25%) | $25B attributed by CFO Hood to **rising memory chip and component costs** |
| **[[GOOGL]]** | **$190B** | Raised by $5B; **cloud contract backlog now $460B** (vs $240B EOY 2025 — doubled in 1 quarter) | Cloud demand outpacing supply |
| **[[AMZN]]** | $200B | Held flat (already published Feb 2026) | AWS infra + memory |
| **[[META]]** | **$125-145B** | Raised from $115-135B | "Higher component pricing and additional data center costs" |
| **Total** | **~$725B** | **+77% YoY from $410B in 2025** | Memory inflation + capacity expansion |

Some analysts now putting the **total hyperscaler capex (including non-Big 4) at ~$830B for 2026** (Evertiq).

## Key signals to extract

### 1. Memory cost inflation is now an explicit capex driver
Microsoft's CFO **directly attributed $25B of its $190B capex to memory chip and component cost inflation**. **Which means:** the SemiAnalysis "memory parabolic" data point (LPDDR5 +4x YoY, DDR5 +5x YoY in Q1 2026 — see [[2026-04-01-semianalysis-gpu-rental-index]]) is now flowing through to hyperscaler P&L statements as a line item, not just a supply-chain anecdote. This is direct validation of the [[MU]] / [[SNDK]] / [[WDC]] AI memory supercycle thesis with hyperscaler buyers explicitly absorbing the price.

### 2. Google cloud backlog doubled in a single quarter
GOOGL's cloud contract backlog went from **$240B EOY 2025 → $460B Q1 2026**. **Which means:** demand is being signed in advance at a velocity that materially exceeds the rate at which capacity can be deployed — i.e., GOOGL is sold out for years on contracts that haven't yet flowed through revenue. This directly underwrites the [[GOOGL]] bull case (already wiki bull/high) and is also bullish for [[TSM]] (TPU manufacturer) and [[NVDA]] (GCP also runs NVDA chips).

### 3. AMZN is the outlier — flat capex while others raise
While MSFT, GOOGL, META all raised 2026 capex in Q1, AMZN held at $200B (its February number). **Which means:** either AMZN is supply-constrained on AI hardware (the more bullish read for the supply chain) or it's leaning on internal Trainium silicon over NVDA buys (the more bearish read for NVDA's AMZN revenue line, neutral for AMZN). The Stratechery "Amazon's Durability" piece (see [[2026-05-12-stratechery-amazon-durability]]) sharpens this — AWS's Nitro/EFA networking left it structurally weaker on AI than competitors using Nvidia/Broadcom networking. The flat-capex framing could mask a slower AI ramp than peers.

### 4. The bear thesis is being directly contradicted
Tom's Hardware quoted an analyst calling the bear thesis "garbage." **Which means:** Q1 2026 prints emphatically broke the "capex peaking / hyperscalers will moderate" narrative that bears have repeated since mid-2024. The capex curve has actually re-accelerated. Combined with Anthropic ARR $9B → $30B+ (see [[2026-04-01-semianalysis-gpu-rental-index]]), the case that demand could underwrite the spend is stronger now than at any point in the cycle.

## Investment implications

- **[[ai-capex-cycle]]:** Update the headline number from ~$670B to **$725B** (Big 4) or **~$830B** (all hyperscalers). The "incremental US data center capacity ~20 GW critical IT" framing holds; the dollar figure backing it is bigger.
- **[[NVDA]]:** Bull case reinforced. $725B hyperscaler capex with explicit memory-cost inflation = $1T+ multi-year revenue pull.
- **[[GOOGL]]:** Cloud backlog doubling in one quarter is the cleanest data point of the cycle. Bull case strengthened.
- **[[META]]:** Capex raise on "component pricing" — same memory squeeze flowing through. Bull case stable.
- **[[AMZN]]:** Watch the AWS positioning carefully. If Trainium underdelivers and AMZN can't expand capex without breaking margin guides, the AWS AI revenue lag could persist. See [[2026-05-12-stratechery-amazon-durability]] for the sharper read.
- **[[MU]] / memory complex:** Hyperscaler memory cost inflation is being absorbed by MSFT directly into capex. Validates the supercycle thesis at the buyer level.

## Caveats and contradictions

- Some sources cite **$700B** (Fortune) vs **$725B** (Tom's Hardware) vs **$830B** (Evertiq). The $725B Big-4 figure is the most-cited; $700B and $830B differ on whether secondary hyperscalers (Oracle, ByteDance, Alibaba) are included.
- Microsoft's $190B is calendar year 2026; some company fiscal years end differently — apples-to-apples comparisons require checking the basis on each company's 10-Q.
