# June 11 session: Trump cancels strikes / deal "in final throes" — de-escalation rally rips through a hot PPI; software de-rates while silicon squeezes

## TL;DR

- **De-escalation rally**: Trump canceled the night's planned strikes on Iran via Truth Social, said a settlement is "largely done" with a signing possible this weekend in Europe, and that Hormuz "officially opens" on signing. S&P +1.75% to 7,394.30, Nasdaq +2.54% to 25,809.66, Dow +930 to 50,848.75, Russell +3.02%. **WTI crashed >4% to settle $85.94 — lowest since April** — through Iran's standing "Hormuz closed" declaration. VIX fell 22.22 → 19.44.
- **May PPI printed HOT and was ignored**: headline +1.1% m/m vs +0.6% consensus, +6.5% y/y (highest since Nov 2022); ~80% of the acceleration was goods, of that ~80% energy (wholesale gasoline +23.4%); core +0.4% m/m. The 10Y yield *fell* to ~4.46% — the bond market treated it as last month's war premium being refunded in real time by the crude collapse.
- **Software de-rated while AI hardware squeezed**: ORCL −8.5% to $184.10 (follow-through on the $40B raise; intraday low $175.28; Guggenheim said buy "aggressively"), CRM near 52-week lows, ADBE −6.25% to $218.80 *into* its own print, then ~−5% AH despite a beat-and-raise (Q2 rev $6.62B +13%, EPS $5.96 beat, **AI-first ARR tripled to >$500M**, FY26 guide raised) because **CFO Dan Durn quit — for Marvell**. Meanwhile **MU +11.7% to $995.87** (Wolfe PT $535→$1,250: DRAM +200% / NAND +216% price forecasts through 2026, FY27 rev forecast ~$226.5B / EPS ~$135), **MRVL +11.1% to $280.71**, **ALAB +11.1% to $367.47**, VRT +6.0%, DELL +5.9%.

## Key facts

- SPX session: open 7,287.67 (hot-PPI fade), low 7,257.33 (briefly *below* the 7,265 put wall, no closing break), high 7,412.68 (stalled ~8 pts under the 7,420-7,450 flip zone), close 7,394.30. High-to-low range 2.14% — the amplified-tape regime working in both directions in one session.
- Sector split: tech, industrials, materials led; energy, staples, real estate fell. Brent $89.09. Gold *rebounded* ~+2% to ~$4,234 (front-month futures) the day after breaking its 200-DMA — consistent with the June 10 break being liquidation flow, not trend.
- CRWV priced its notes: $1.25B USD at **9.625%** + €2B at **8.50%**, both due July 2032, at par; closing June 18; ratings Ba3/B+/BB−. Stock +0.14% on a +2.5% Nasdaq day.
- NVDA +2.2% to $204.87; **S&P Global upgraded NVDA's credit rating to AA**, citing AI demand and revenue durability.
- ORCL FY27 capex framing wrinkle: some coverage cites **$95B gross** FY27 capex vs the **~$70B net** figure from the call (net of customer GPU prepayments / BYO-hardware). Both can be true; the gap *is* the customer-funded portion. Worth keeping both numbers.
- MU fiscal Q3 print reportedly ~June 24 (multiple coverage references; confirm date).

## Key claims (and how confident)

- **The market is trading the deal as done** (high confidence on the *positioning*, not the outcome): oil −4% through a declared-closed Hormuz is the tape calling Iran's bluff. **Which means:** the whole rally inherits headline risk — if the weekend signing slips, the war premium gets re-added at short notice; if it signs, the oil/CPI-headline drag on the Fed equation lifts within days.
- **The PPI was stale on arrival** (medium-high): it measured May's war-premium gasoline; June crude is unwinding it live. The Fed-relevant residue is core +0.4% m/m — firm, second hot services-adjacent reading in three days. **Which means:** June 17 stays a hold-with-hawkish-tilt setup, not a hike — but the supercore/core-PPI pair keeps a December hike priced.
- **The software de-rate is a multiple problem, not (yet) a revenue problem** (medium): ADBE tripled AI ARR and raised guidance and still got sold; ORCL booked $85B RPO and fell 8.5%. The market is repricing *who captures AI economics*, paying the toll-takers (memory, networking, power) and de-rating the toll-payers. The Durn move (Adobe CFO → Marvell) is the same rotation expressed as a career decision. **Which means:** the supply-over-demand barbell keeps working until either silicon supply catches up or software shows AI revenue at scale — ADBE's $500M AI-first ARR is real but ~2% of revenue, too small to flip the narrative.
