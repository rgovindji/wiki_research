# June 5, 2026 — Worst S&P day of the year: hot jobs report + AVGO AI-guide scare + META dilution fear

## TL;DR
- **S&P 500 −2.2%, Nasdaq −3.7% (worst day since April 2025), Dow −510 (−1%).** The 9-week S&P winning streak — longest since 2023 — is snapping. Worst S&P day of 2026.
- **Two triggers stacked:** (1) a hot May jobs report (172K vs ~80K consensus) killed Fed-cut hopes and spiked yields/dollar → risk-off; (2) a 3-day semiconductor cascade after [[AVGO]]'s Q2 AI-revenue guide came in *below the elevated whisper* despite a strong print.
- **[[META]] −7%** on a separate, company-specific catalyst: an FT report it's weighing a multi-billion equity raise to fund AI capex (following [[GOOGL]]'s $85B raise). Dilution fear on top of the Q1 capex shock. META now ~21% off its all-time high.

## Key facts
- **Indexes (June 5):** S&P 500 −2.2% (into the red for the week); Nasdaq Composite −3.7%, biggest drop since April 2025, third straight down day; Dow −510 / −1%.
- **May jobs report (released June 5):** Nonfarm payrolls **+172,000** vs Dow Jones consensus ~80K and Reuters ~85K — a huge beat. Unemployment **4.3%** (unchanged). Avg hourly earnings +0.3% m/m, **+3.4% y/y**. Leisure/hospitality led (+70K). No inflationary signal, which "quiets hike chatter" but firmly kills near-term cut odds.
- **Rates/FX reaction:** 10Y UST **+5bp to 4.534%** (highest since May 21); 2Y **+7bp to 4.115%** (highest since May 20). Dollar index ~**99.9**, highest since April 7. Classic "good news is bad news" risk-off.
- **VIX:** firmed to **just under 17** — elevated but "few signs of panic." This is a repricing, not a crash (yet).
- **Semis (the 3-day cascade):**
  - June 3 (Wed) — [[AVGO]] reported Q2 FY26 AMC. Strong headline (rev $22.2B +48%; AI semi rev $10.8B +143%) but **Q3 AI-chip guide ~$16B vs ~$17.2B Street whisper** = "soft AI guidance."
  - June 4 (Thu) — **AVGO −12.6%** (steepest single-day since Jan 2025); Nasdaq dropped the most in over a year as chips tumbled.
  - June 5 (Fri) — **AVGO another −6.27%**; AMD −2.2%; NVDA ~−1% premarket and lower on the day. Chip weakness + rates did the damage.
- **[[META]] −7%:** FT reports Meta is *considering* selling "tens of billions" in new stock to fund AI infra, after [[GOOGL]]'s successful $85B raise. **Has not hired banks; may not issue.** Stock ~21% below all-time high; compounds the Q1 capex-outlook shock ($125–145B 2026 capex).

## Key claims (and how confident)
- **The selloff is rates/sentiment-driven, not a break in AI demand** — HIGH confidence. AVGO's actual numbers and bookings ($30B+ AI backlog, FY26 AI $56B reaffirmed, FY27 >$100B reiterated) were strong; the stock fell on *expectations vs. an elevated whisper*, plus a macro rate shock. The demand thesis did not get a new negative data point — the *valuation/positioning* got one.
- **"Great print sold off" is itself a late-cycle tell** — MEDIUM-HIGH. When a +143% AI-revenue quarter with +84% next-Q total guidance drops the stock 12.6%, the marginal buyer is exhausted relative to expectations. This is the [[overview]] "exhausted-buyer" pattern the wiki has been flagging (NVDA drift post-print; DELL +33% blow-off; 9-week streak).
- **META is the most exposed Mag 7 name to the "who's still self-funding cleanly?" question** — MEDIUM-HIGH. Consistent with the June 1 [[GOOGL]]-raise read-through already in the wiki: META carries hyperscaler-tier capex without the cloud-revenue offset or Berkshire-validated equity access. An equity raise would *confirm* the capex trajectory the market fears.
