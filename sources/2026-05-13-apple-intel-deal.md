# Apple-Intel Foundry Deal — Confirmed May 8

## TL;DR
- Apple-Intel chip-manufacturing deal **confirmed** by WSJ May 8 (previously flagged as "preliminary" in our [[AAPL]] update of 2026-05-11)
- Intel +15% on the news; +33% early May; doubled in April
- Primary node: **Intel 18A-P** (enhanced version of 18A); Intel 14A targeted for 2028
- Apple **also exploring Samsung** Texas plant in parallel
- "TSMC has more limited capacity for chips made for consumer devices, and Apple has less leverage" — validates wiki's [[AAPL]] bear thesis directionally

## Implications
- **[[AAPL]] stance:** UNCHANGED bear. The Apple-Intel deal partially counters the TSMC-deprioritization argument BUT:
  - Intel 18A-P is not bleeding-edge by 2027-2028 standards
  - Memory cost + smartphone volume collapse bear case remains intact
  - Leading-edge SoCs (A-series, M-series) likely still TSMC N2/A16
  - This is a diversification + cost play, not a competitive advantage
- **[[TSM]]:** Confirms the framing — Apple becoming just another customer at the leading edge
- **Intel:** Worth reconsidering wiki coverage. Was "not currently coverage-worthy" per index. Apple deal + share rotation narrative changes the picture. **Open question — flag for next research session.**
