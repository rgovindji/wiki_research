# Alphabet drops ~5% as two marquee AI researchers leave for rivals

## TL;DR
- Alphabet fell ~5% (intraday-quoted 4.99%-6.5% across outlets; closed $349.68) — its worst day in about a year — after losing two of its most prominent AI researchers to direct competitors.
- **Noam Shazeer** — DeepMind VP of engineering and co-lead of the Gemini models, who returned to Google in Aug 2024 via a ~$2.7B reverse-acquihire of Character.AI — left to join **OpenAI**. Nobel laureate DeepMind scientist **John Jumper** (AlphaFold) departed for **Anthropic**.
- The selloff dragged the mega-cap platform complex (AMZN -4.8%, MSFT -3%, META -2.3%) and the Nasdaq (-1.32%) while the Dow rose — a clean duration/rotation tape, AI-hardware up, platforms down.

## Key facts
- GOOGL closed $349.68 (June 22), the first close BELOW Berkshire's $350 anchor since the June 1 raise (Berkshire bought at $348.20 Class C / $351.81 Class A).
- The story is talent-retention, not fundamentals: nothing in the day touched the $460B GCP backlog, search, or the TPU/Apollo OCS stack.
- Framing shift flagged by outlets: the market is moving from valuing Google on financial strength to valuing it on its ability to retain technological leadership in AI.

## Key claims (and how confident)
- **Sentiment shock vs. regime change is the open question (high confidence it's the question; low confidence on the answer).** Two departures don't change unit economics, but if they signal a broader DeepMind brain-drain into OpenAI/Anthropic, the moat narrative weakens. Watch for a third high-profile exit.
- **Bears on the supply-over-demand thesis (medium).** A platform-leadership crack on a company-specific narrative is consistent with the wiki's logged 2026-06-01-supply-over-demand call (supply-side hardware outperforms demand-side platforms).
