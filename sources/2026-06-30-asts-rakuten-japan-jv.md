# AST SpaceMobile × Rakuten — Japan sovereign LEO JV (~$1B government support)

## TL;DR
- **Rakuten + AST SpaceMobile to form a 50/50 JV** (within 2026, Rakuten operationally leading) to build Japan's own low-Earth-orbit, direct-to-smartphone satellite network.
- **Japan government support up to ¥150B (~$1B)**, with ~**$926M in subsidies** for equipment, ground facilities and control systems — framed as an **economic-security** move to cut reliance on **Starlink**.
- **ASTS +21% to $86.77** (2026-06-29; Polygon-verified close, day range $75.98–$86.95) — best day in nearly two years. Service phases from **end-2026**, nationwide **FY2027**.

## Key facts
- Structure: **50/50 JV**, Rakuten assumes operational leadership; formation targeted within 2026.
- Funding: up to **¥150B / ~$1B** Japanese government support; **~$926M subsidy** figure widely cited.
- Timeline: phased launch starting **end of 2026**, full nationwide Japan coverage targeted **FY2027**.
- Strategic frame: Japan treats a **domestically-controlled LEO D2C network** as economic security vs. foreign networks (Starlink). Rakuten CEO called it "critical" for Japan security.
- Price: ASTS **$86.77** close 2026-06-29 (+~21%), second straight up session; still well below the 180-day high $133.86 (2026-05-28).
