# Culper Research Short Thesis on NVDA — Export-Control Diversion to China

## TL;DR

- **Culper Research has disclosed a short position in NVDA** (May 2026) alleging >20% of FY2026 compute revenue is sustained via illegal GPU diversion networks through Southeast Asian intermediaries
- **DoJ indictment on March 19, 2026 is the verified anchor:** Supermicro cofounder + board member **Wally Liaw** charged with conspiring to illegally export ≥$2.5B in NVDA AI tech to China. SMCI stock fell **-33% on the news**. Liaw subsequently left the SMCI board.
- **NYT (Paul Mozur) published parallel reporting:** *"A Mystery C.E.O. and Billions in Sales: Is China Buying Banned Nvidia Chips?"* — establishing this is mainstream-press-validated, not just short-seller speculation
- **Culper's central allegations** add color around Megaspeed (Singapore data-center co), Alice Huang (Megaspeed exec), YTL AI Cloud (Malaysia, NVDA partner), Aivres Systems (NVDA Elite OEM), Speedmatrix, Novagate Cloud, and OBON Corp as alleged diversion vehicles
- **Net read:** Material risk vector, NOT stance-changing for NVDA (bull/high holds). Bear case sharpens meaningfully. May 20 NVDA Q1 FY27 earnings call now has a new analyst-question dimension.

## Verified facts (court records + mainstream reporting)

### DoJ indictment — March 19, 2026

Three individuals indicted for conspiring to illegally export ≥$2.5B in Nvidia AI tech to China:

| Defendant | Role | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Yih-Shyan "Wally" Liaw** | SMCI **cofounder + sitting board member** | Arrested; placed on administrative leave; subsequently left board |
| **Ruei-Tsang "Steven" Chang** | SMCI Taiwan General Manager | Fugitive |
| **Ting-Wei "Willy" Sun** | Third-party fixer | Arrested |

**The DoJ-described scheme (per the indictment):**

1. Liaw + Chang direct executives at an unnamed Southeast Asian company to place purchase orders with Supermicro as though for that company's operations
2. Servers assembled in US → shipped to Supermicro Taiwan
3. Delivered to SE Asian intermediary at a different location
4. Handoff to a logistics company that strips identifying packaging
5. Repackaged in unmarked boxes → shipped to China

**Stock reaction:** SMCI -33% the day the indictment was unsealed.

### YTL AI Cloud Johor data center — verified operational

- Completed late 2025; fully operational as of early 2026
- NVDA Grace Blackwell (GB200) GPUs
- 1,640 acres at YTL Green Data Center Park in Kulai, Johor
- **605 MW capacity** powered by 500 MW solar + 600 MW grid backup
- **NVDA invested ~$2.3B with YTL Power**
- Government + commercial customers (including unnamed hyperscalers)

### Culper's underlying trade-record claims (cited verbatim from coverage)

- **Speedmatrix imported $4.6B in product Dec 2024-Jan 2026**, with $4.0B from Aivres Systems (NVDA Elite OEM compute partner)
- **June 2024 Apex/Alibaba pledge** (confirmed via Malaysian filings): Speedmatrix pledged its entire business as collateral to Apex Enterprise Solutions (Singapore entity, Alibaba parent)
- Giga Computing (NVDA Elite OEM) shipped >$500M in H200 & Blackwell servers to Speedmatrix and Novagate Cloud
- Novagate's Malaysian subsidiary was incorporated 2 days after April 2025 export restrictions announced

### NYT reporting

Paul Mozur published *"A Mystery C.E.O. and Billions in Sales: Is China Buying Banned Nvidia Chips?"* establishing the Megaspeed/Alice Huang/Speedmatrix story at NYT-tier mainstream coverage.

**These claims are coverage-worthy but should be treated as Culper's interpretive layer, not established fact.** They become structurally important only if subsequent DoJ indictments or congressional testimony names additional defendants or NVDA personnel.

## NVDA response

NVDA has stated it is "100% out of China" (per coverage). **Culper argues this phrasing is misleading** — "covers equity, not financing." NVDA denies that accused parties have China ties.
