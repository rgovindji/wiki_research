# Google's rumored 3rd TPU design partner = Marvell (MRVL)

## TL;DR
- **The two known TPU design partners are Broadcom ([[AVGO]]) and MediaTek. The rumored third is Marvell ([[MRVL]]).** Google is reportedly **in talks (not yet signed)** with Marvell to build **two new chips: an inference-optimized TPU + a "memory processing unit" (MPU)** — which would make it the third design house in the TPU program.
- **Strategy = a multi-supplier "automotive" architecture:** Broadcom, MediaTek, and (potentially) Marvell each handle *different segments* and compete on them, so no single vendor gets leverage to dictate terms. Reflects Google's shift toward **inference as the dominant compute cost**.
- **Bull datapoint for [[MRVL]]; incremental share-dilution risk for [[AVGO]]** (which already lost the TPU v9 SerDes socket to MediaTek) — though Broadcom just **locked a through-2031 TPU agreement** and remains the anchor/training partner.

## Key facts
- **Broadcom = anchor design partner** — multi-generation (incl. Anthropic's Ironwood TPU v7), **training chip "TPU 8t"**, and a newly-locked **through-2031** agreement.
- **MediaTek = inference/cost variant** — won the **v9 SerDes socket** (336G after Google's 448G target hit signal-integrity/thermal walls), designs the cost-optimized **"TPU 8i" (codename "Zebrafish")**, v7e/v8e orders (TrendForce: 7× CoWoS increase requested).
- **Marvell = the rumored third** — in talks for an **inference TPU + a memory processing unit (MPU)**; would be the third *design* partner. **Not signed** as of the reports.
- **A "fourth" is sometimes cited — Intel Foundry — but as a secondary *fabrication* option from ~2028, not a design partner.** So the 3rd *design* partner is Marvell.
- Frame: Google is building "the AI industry's most diversified custom chip supply chain," roadmap from Ironwood (shipping in the millions) to **TPU v8 at TSMC 2nm late 2027**.

## Key claims (and how confident)
- Marvell as the rumored 3rd TPU design partner (inference TPU + MPU) — **medium** (multiple outlets; talks *not yet a signed contract*).
- Broadcom = training anchor / through-2031 lock; MediaTek = inference/cost + v9 SerDes — **high** (consistent, corroborates the wiki's existing [[2026-06-24-semiwiki-daily]] v9-loss note).
- Intel Foundry = *fab* option (2028), not design — **medium-high**.
