# SK Hynix throttles HBM4, pivots to commodity DRAM (commodity-DRAM margins > HBM)

## TL;DR
- **The buried lede: commodity DRAM operating margins have SURPASSED HBM** (>15pp gap; Daishin floats a *theoretical 90%* within the year). SK Hynix — the HBM *leader* — is delaying HBM3E→HBM4 line conversions to chase the higher-margin commodity DRAM market.
- **This inverts a core [[MU]] bear point** ("70% is commodity = the drag"): the commodity leg is now the *profit engine* — near-term bullish for Micron, whose biggest segment is commodity DRAM.
- **But it's the loudest cycle-peak signal yet:** a *commodity* at theoretical 90% op-margin is the definition of a shortage-rent top; Morgan Stanley reframes the driver as the **memory-wide pricing cycle, not HBM-share defense** — i.e. a classic cyclical up-cycle, which peaks and crashes.

## Key facts / claims (with confidence)
- SK Hynix **delaying some HBM3E→HBM4 line conversions** to expand commodity DRAM output. *Industry-source reported; single-ish source on specifics.*
- **Commodity DRAM op-margin now >HBM by >15pp**; Daishin: commodity DRAM op-margin could reach a **theoretical ~90%** in 2026. *Analyst estimate.*
- SK Hynix **HBM revenue share >40%**; HBM market share **57% Q4'24** → Counterpoint sees it falling toward **50-60%** if Samsung mass-produces HBM4 H2'26.
- **NVIDIA Rubin production forecasts "trending downward"** + HBM4 still in NVDA qualification → SK Hynix sees no urgency to rush HBM4. *Industry-source; a notable demand-side flag — verify.*
- Goldman: SK Hynix only needs **>50% HBM3/3E share through 2026** to hold its position.
- Morgan Stanley: **DRAM ASP +62% in 2026**; raised SK Hynix EPS estimates **+56-63%**; value driver = **memory-wide pricing cycle**, not HBM-share defense.
- SK Hynix Q1: **DRAM ASP +mid-60s% **; focus on high-density server modules + mobile; **signed a 3-yr DDR5 supply deal with Microsoft** (commodity-DRAM earnings visibility / forward-contracted pricing).

## Why it matters (both sides — surface, don't resolve)
- **BULL (near-term):** commodity DRAM margin surge benefits [[MU]]'s largest segment (~70% of revenue) — the part the wiki treated as the cyclical drag is now the margin engine. DRAM ASP +62% and a 90%-theoretical-margin backdrop is a huge profit tailwind through ~2026.
- **BEAR (cycle-peak):** 90% theoretical commodity op-margin = the top, by definition; MS's "it's a pricing cycle" reframe puts the cyclical end-date back in view (classic memory up-cycles crash 40-70%). Reinforces the [[ai-bubble-debate]] "ASP-driven, not unit-driven" thread and the Korea-export DRAM-plateau (+3% MoM) read.
- **HBM competitive/demand flags:** (1) Rubin forecasts trending *down* — a demand caution for NVDA + the HBM4 complex; (2) SK Hynix ceding HBM4 tempo opens share for **Samsung** (confirms the standing MU bear) *and* a mild HBM4-socket *opportunity* for **MU**.
- **Smart-money tell:** the HBM leader voluntarily reallocating to commodity says the marginal profit is in commodity, and "HBM is everything" is incomplete.

## Bias / reliability
Korean industry-press (translated); the HBM4-throttle specifics are industry-source-based (treat as reported, not confirmed); the margin/ASP/share figures are analyst estimates (Daishin/Goldman/MS/Counterpoint). The Rubin-forecast-down line is the single most important claim to independently verify.
