# Stop Saying Half of 2026 US Datacenter Capacity Is Canceled

**Authors:** Reyk Knuhtsen, Maya Barkin, Jeremie Eliahou Ontiveros, + 2 others. Published 2026-06-18.

## TL;DR
- The viral "~50% of 2026 US datacenter capacity is delayed/canceled" claim is a misread of one speculative pipeline layer, not the real build-out. SemiAnalysis's own YE2026 NA hyperscaler self-build forecast moved only ~1% over 6 months; colocation <5%.
- The claim traces to a Bloomberg piece (Apr 1, 2026) using Sightline Climate data (~12 GW expected 2026, ~5 GW under construction), then amplified into clickbait by TechRadar / Tom's Hardware / The Register. SemiAnalysis says the top two hyperscalers ALONE exceed Sightline's 5 GW under-construction figure.
- What is genuinely slipping is early-stage, speculative MW that never had site control / financing / interconnection — capacity SemiAnalysis already modeled as structurally oversupplied and bound for 2028+, not 2026. This is a direct rebuttal to the "AI bubble / DCs being canceled" bear narrative.

## Key facts (with confidence)
- **YE2026 NA hyperscaler self-build forecast moved ~1% in 6 months; NA colocation <5%.** (High confidence — SemiAnalysis's own tracked model; this is their core data claim.)
- **Origin of the meme:** Bloomberg, "America's AI Build-Out Hinges on Chinese Electrical Parts" (Apr 1, 2026), citing Sightline Climate: ~12 GW expected 2026 US capacity, ~5 GW under construction. (High — sourced to named outlet/date.)
- **Three real delay buckets:** (1) aggressive announcements from new/inexperienced players (Data City 5 GW Laredo, APR Energy 400 MW TX, Cloudburst 1.2 GW San Marcos); (2) advanced projects with over-optimistic construction timelines (Nebius/DataOne NJ slipped 4mo→10-11mo; Core Scientific Denton 250 MW missed YE2025 on transformer explosion + delays); (3) permitting/NIMBY (Oracle/STACK New Mexico "Project Jupiter" pushed to 2029 from 1H2027). (High — specific named projects.)
- **Equipment lead times remain the real constraint:** GE Vernova / Hitachi Energy / Mitsubishi Electric main lines booked 3-4 yrs; some SKUs (Reinhausen tap-changer bushings) 3-5 yrs. Hitachi Energy South Boston plant ~2028. (High — consistent with prior SemiAnalysis bottleneck work.)
- **Grid demand is phantom-inflated, not collapsing:** ERCOT large-load queue >410 GW (87% datacenters) vs ~85 GW Texas peak — SemiAnalysis flags "311 GW of Phantom Datacenter demand." US large-load queue >1 Terawatt (flagged Dec 2025). (Medium-high — SemiAnalysis estimate; the phantom-demand framing cuts BOTH ways: queues overstate demand AND cancellations overstate cuts.)
- **CoreWeave 2026 guide:** 1.7 GW active power, ~$35B RPO signed by Q1 2026 — SemiAnalysis says it matched this "exactly." (Medium — self-reported accuracy.)
- **Margin color:** pure-play DC electricals (Vertiv, Schneider) >20% margins; behind-the-meter prepayments 10-15% standard. (Medium.)
- **Methodology moat:** custom satellite tasking on a Vision Model trained on "hundreds of thousands" of images, 550+ equipment suppliers across 75 subcategories / 6,000+ facilities, on-the-ground site visits. They explicitly contrast this with "Claude Coded" models that treat press releases as ground truth. (Medium — self-described; not independently verifiable but consistent with their published product.)
