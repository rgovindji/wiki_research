# Aswath Damodaran — Prof G Markets Quarterly Live Stream

## TL;DR
- **"AI correction will be worse than dotcom because the macro spillover is real."** Damodaran's headline: dotcom was Silicon Valley + websites + hopes. AI is data centers + power + water + employment "across the country" — so correction propagates marketwide, with a longer macro hangover. Strengthens [[ai-bubble-debate]] bear leg.
- **Hyperscaler revenue concentration in OpenAI + Anthropic spending commitments**: MSFT ~50%, ORCL >50%, GOOGL 43%, AMZN 51% of revenue backlog. The cleanest single-source confirmation of the circularity concern.
- **"AI is still net expense, not income, for most Mag 7."** Damodaran: even just depreciation on AI capex puts downward pressure on earnings. Real AI earnings concentrate at chip makers, not broadly.
- **Chip sector market-cap top warning**: 10 years ago = 3% of S&P; today = **17%**. Up 60% in 2 months. [[MU]] +160% YTD. Damodaran: "the bulk of the run is done."
- **Banks distributing data center debt (JPM, MS, SMBC)** — Damodaran independently corroborates the [[ai-infrastructure-debt]] thesis. Concerned about regulators needing to track bank overexposure.
- **SpaceX intrinsic value $1.2T** per Damodaran's recent paper. Anthropic at $1T = "asking for a lot" but best LLM trajectory. OpenAI corporate governance = "nightmare."

## Connect-the-dots / investment implication

1. **The "macro spillover" framing is the single most important addition.** The wiki has captured the AI bubble debate from many angles — capex magnitude (Dwarkesh × Dylan), valuation environment (CAPE 39.8), circularity (Anthropic CFO, infrastructure debt), and skeptic voices (Hashimoto, Kedrosky, LeCun). What Damodaran adds is the **transmission mechanism for what a correction looks like**: not a 2000-style tech-only drawdown but a real-economy spillover via construction labor, power utilities, water, regional capex. **Which means** portfolio hedging logic should think about cyclicals + industrials + REITs exposure to the AI buildout, not just direct tech exposure — and the wiki's [[hedging-risk]] page may need a Damodaran-driven framing update separately.

2. **The hyperscaler-revenue-concentration percentages tighten the circularity case quantitatively.** Previously the wiki had qualitative framings (Anthropic CFO disclosed $100B+ compute commitment; CRWV backlog $99.4B includes Anthropic Claude). Damodaran citing **50/50/43/51% of revenue backlog from just OpenAI + Anthropic** for MSFT/ORCL/GOOGL/AMZN puts a number on it. **Which means:** if either OpenAI or Anthropic delivers a downgrade in 2026-27, the revenue revision flows to four hyperscalers simultaneously. This is the cleanest single quantitative argument for "correlated risk across Mag 7 from lab-side disappointment."

3. **The chip-sector top warning should be recorded but not over-weighted.** Damodaran is a long-horizon valuation expert calling a near-term tactical top — that's not his expertise. He himself acknowledges he was lucky on Intel ("I never anticipated the kind of buildup that happened"). But the 17% S&P share + 60% in 2 months data points are real. **Which means:** for [[MU]] and the broader chip basket (NVDA, AMAT, KLAC, LRCX, ASML), this is a *position-sizing prompt*, not a stance-change prompt. If MU pulls back 20-30% in coming weeks, the long-term thesis remains intact and Damodaran's framing was right. If it grinds higher into Q3 2026, Damodaran's framing was wrong.

4. **Damodaran's SpaceX $1.2T is meaningfully higher than current private-market $1.75-2T marks.** The wiki notes SpaceX confidentially filed for IPO April 2026 at ~$1.75-2T. Damodaran's DCF says $1.2T is the *intrinsic* value. **Which means:** if SpaceX IPOs at $1.75T+, Damodaran would view it as ~30-45% overvalued at issue. This is a real anchor for any retail decision on the SpaceX IPO when it lists. Worth recording in [[space-economy]] as the highest-credibility independent valuation marker.

5. **The "AI is still net expense" framing should be visible in bull-case framings.** The wiki's [[ai-capex-cycle]] bull case currently leans on capex magnitude + contracted demand + monetization at labs. Damodaran's reframing — that even just depreciation on AI capex is dragging earnings for non-chip Mag 7 — is a useful counter that helps the wiki avoid over-celebrating the bull thesis. **Which means** any future weekly synthesis should mention "AI is currently an earnings drag for MSFT/GOOGL/META/AMZN excluding their chip business" alongside the "but the chip sector is up 60%" framing. Both are true.
