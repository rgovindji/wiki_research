# June 10 session: soft core, hot supercore, hotter war — S&P −1.62% closes below the 7,300 shelf; AI mega-IPO supply emerges as a named rotation driver

## TL;DR

- **The CPI binary resolved soft on its stated terms and the market sold off anyway**: core 0.2% m/m (below 0.3% consensus) / 2.9% y/y; headline 0.5% / 4.25% (worst since April 2023, energy-driven). But **supercore (core services ex-housing) printed +0.55% m/m — worst since March 2024, 6-month annualized 4.3%, accelerating since last fall** — and the war escalated: S&P −1.62% to 7,266.99, Nasdaq −1.98% to 25,169.50, Dow −953 (−1.87%) to 49,918.78, closing near the lows.
- **Iran:** overnight IRGC missile/drone strikes on US bases in Jordan/Bahrain/Kuwait; Trump: US will be "attacking them very hard," threatening power plants and bridges; Tehran "reconsidering" talks; Qatari mediators flew to Tehran. **WTI +2.07% to $90.03** — up, but still nowhere near the $95 escalation line. Korea took the brunt overnight: KOSPI −4.5%, Samsung −6%, SK Hynix −7.5%, **VKOSPI >90, a record above its 2008 peak**.
- **A new named bear vector: AI mega-IPO supply.** Coverage attributed part of the tech rotation to the OpenAI (S-1 filed, ~Sept window, ~$730–850B) + Anthropic (S-1 June 1, ~Oct) + SpaceX (June) pipeline — **~$3.6T of combined targeted valuation competing for the same institutional pools**; BNP Paribas explicitly warned investors may liquidate chip positions (NVDA named) to fund IPO allocations.

## Key facts

- CPI May: headline +0.5% m/m / 4.25% y/y; core +0.2% m/m / 2.9% y/y (y/y ticked UP from 2.8%); supercore +0.55% m/m (+6.8% annualized), y/y 3.67% from 3.38%
- CPI components: gasoline +7.0% m/m (+40.5% y/y); **electricity +0.63% m/m / +5.9% y/y — driven by AI data-center demand, +43% since 2021**; airfares +2.7% m/m (+26.7% y/y — war jet fuel); motor vehicle insurance −1.7% m/m; OER +0.30% m/m / +3.3% y/y; core goods −0.1% m/m
- Rates/commodities: 10Y eased to ~4.52% (from 4.55% intraday) — the bond market bought the soft core even as equities sold the war; **gold fell to ~$4,165–4,200, lowest since March, below its 200-DMA for the first time since Oct 2023** (25% below the Jan ATH $5,589)
- VIX closed ~22.2 (+11.8%; June 9 close 19.87)
- Sector tape: industrials −3%+, tech and materials −2%+; NVDA −3%, AVGO −4%, AMD −4%
- TSMC May revenue NT$417B, all-time monthly record, +30% y/y; Taiwan weighing export controls restricting advanced AI chip sales to ALL Chinese customers (Bloomberg)
- SoftBank −8% on report that talks for a $6B margin loan tied to OpenAI stalled; Japan May wholesale inflation 6.3% (3-year high)
- AI financing-scrutiny cluster: CRWV founder sales tallied at $2.3B since lockup (pre-arranged, disclosed; Chanos amplifying), SMCI −10% on a $7B financing package, ORCL $40B raise after the close (see [[2026-06-10-ORCL-Q4-FY26-earnings]])

## Key claims (and how confident)

- **The supercore acceleration predates the war** (high confidence — Wolf Street trend data: accelerating since fall 2025). Which means: the "scary headline is just war energy, look through it" framing — which the soft core m/m superficially validated — is weaker than it looked at 8:31 AM; the Fed's hawks have a domestically-generated services story for June 17 that Iran doesn't explain. Airfares (+2.7% m/m on jet fuel) contributed, and insurance actually fell — so it's *mixed*, not cleanly ugly — but a series accelerating for eight months isn't a passthrough artifact.
- **AI electricity demand is now feeding the CPI** (medium-high confidence — Wolf Street attribution, consistent with utility data). Which means: a genuinely reflexive loop has closed — the AI buildout pushes up electricity prices, electricity pushes up inflation, inflation pushes up the discount rate that AI equities are priced on. The buildout is, at the margin, raising the cost of capital for its own financing.
- **Mega-IPO supply as a chip-selloff driver** (medium confidence — named by BNP and session coverage, but rotation attribution is always fuzzy). Which means: a new, *datable* supply overhang sits on Q3-Q4 2026 — OpenAI ~Sept, Anthropic ~Oct, SpaceX June — that is bearish for existing AI positions on pure flow-of-funds grounds even if every fundamental thesis holds. This belongs in [[market-concentration]] and [[ai-bubble-debate]] as a mechanism distinct from valuation or demand.
- **Korea's record fear print is positioning, not demand** (medium-high confidence). Which means: the memory thesis ([[MU]]) gets its stress from crowdedness and war proximity, not from any demand datapoint — NVDA signed a multi-year HBM pact with SK Hynix days earlier. Watch whether MU weakness persists once the war tape settles; if it does, that's information positioning can't explain.
