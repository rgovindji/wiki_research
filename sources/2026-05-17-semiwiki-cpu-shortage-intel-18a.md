# AI-Driven CPU Shortage + Lip-Bu Tan / Intel Strategic Reset (SemiWiki forum, May 2026)

Two separate SemiWiki forum threads, combined into one source summary because they reinforce a single thesis: an **AI-driven CPU supply shortage** is creating a structural ASP cycle benefiting Intel + AMD, with Intel's execution risk and AMD's outperformance as the key tension.

## Thread 1: "Industry faces 'acute' CPU shortage with hope that Intel 18A yields improve"
Started by Fred Chen (SemiWiki contributor) Apr 29, 2026. **48 replies across 3 pages**. Heavy participation: OneEng (12 posts), MKW Ventures (10), siliconbruh999, hist78, Fred Chen, Barnsley.

## Thread 2: "Intel's stock tripled under Lip-Bu Tan. He still has not told most employees the plan"
Started by Daniel Nenni May 12, 2026. **18 replies**. Key contributors: blueone (8 — Intel insider sources), Xebec (6), Barnsley (6), Daniel Nenni (5).

## TL;DR
- **Global AI-driven CPU shortage**: CPUs effectively unavailable at any price; Intel + AMD both production-constrained
- **Intel + AMD raised processor prices 10-15%** to reflect rising costs (Apr 2026)
- **Intel Q1 2026 10-Q (verbatim from forum)**: DCAI revenue +$926M YoY, **27% ASP increase**, **5% unit volume DECLINE** — "Market demand exceeded our available product supply due to internal supply constraints ... we expect these supply constraints to persist throughout the remainder of 2026"
- **AMD Q1 2026: +38% YoY revenue** vs Intel +7% YoY — AMD significantly outperforming despite both being constrained
- **Intel CapEx forensics (hist78)**: Intel spent **$125.5B 2020-2025** building capacity; now claims shortage. $3.1B Intel 7 write-off is only 2.5% of total — can't explain the gap. *"Intel spent the money on Intel 3/4/18A and buildings. Those did not ramp like expected."* (OneEng)
- **Intel insider quote (BlueOne, longtime SemiWiki member):** *"I've heard this over and over again from employees I know. They, and I, can't imagine what LBT is waiting for. The thinking I hear most often is that there really isn't a grand strategy. There are only tactics. Which is why they think the grand plan hasn't been communicated. It's actually weird to hear a similar story about it from multiple people."*
- **Daniel Nenni's view (contra):** *"Never bet against Lip-Bu, absolutely. GO INTEL!"*

## Key facts

### CPU shortage
- AI datacenter demand driving Xeon scarcity → squeezing all other Intel/AMD CPUs
- Source: Digitimes Apr 22, via Mark Campbell article reposted to forum
- **Intel 18A is INTEL-ONLY** — capacity expansion won't divert to AI accelerators/GPUs/DRAM
- Intel rumored to release "another Raptor Lake Refresh" (LGA-1700 13th/14th gen revival) to combat shortage using legacy nodes
- Notebook supply chain says product availability — not price — is the primary constraint

### Intel financials (Q1 2026)
- Revenue $13.6B vs $12.3B consensus
- Data Center + AI revenue **+22% YoY** (DCAI segment)
- DC CPU **server ASPs +27% YoY** but **server unit volume -5% YoY**
- Stock +24% single day in April (best since 1987); **+114% in April alone** (best month in 55 years)
- US government invested **$8.9B for 9.9% stake** at $20.47/share (Aug 2025) — now worth ~$36B
- Other DCAI product revenue $935M, +$230M YoY, driven by higher networking customer demand

### Intel strategic moves (under Lip-Bu Tan)
- **Terafab Texas**: $55B initial / **$119B total** project; Tesla/xAI anchor customers
- Tesla using Intel **14A** process; xAI's **AI5** chip among first products targeted
- Apple processor diversification rumor (see [[2026-05-17-semiwiki-kuo-apple-intel]])
- Tan's hiring approach is **VC-style** — relationships over detailed business plans; backs trusted people; "opens doors rather than scrutinising strategies"

### Intel competitive position (vs TSMC)
- Per New Street Research: **Intel cost per chip ~3x TSMC**
- Yield gap: **Intel ~65% vs TSMC 80%+** — accounts for **40%+ of the cost differential**
- Only 8% of cost difference attributable to higher US labor costs
- 18A yields hitting **industry-standard levels only in 2027** (not 2026)
- Naga Chandrasekaran (Intel Foundry head, ex-Micron): *"Intel products alone, even in a wildly successful scenario, cannot fund the capital and filling the fabs and the scale that's needed to be successful enough in a silicon business today"*
- Kevork Kechichian (Intel server head, ex-Qualcomm/Arm) on internal culture: *"When teams fell behind on deadlines by a couple of weeks, the recovery plan was to adjust the schedule to go another two weeks"*

### AMD positioning (vs Intel)
- Q1 2026 revenue +38% YoY (far above Intel's +7%)
- Both supply-constrained, but AMD's unit volumes appear to be growing where Intel's are declining
- Mercury Research data on ARM/custom CPU market awaited
