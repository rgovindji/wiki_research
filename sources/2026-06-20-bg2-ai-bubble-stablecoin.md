# BG2 — AI money-bubble, circular revenue & stablecoin (Gurley's last episode)

> **Bias flag:** Gerstner is long NVDA/OpenAI/META and consistently steel-mans the bull case ("I'm not concerned as a shareholder in NVDA"). Gurley is the more skeptical voice on circularity/capex but is *not* calling a top — he explicitly agrees multiples are too low for classic bubble-watch. The most useful, least-conflicted content here is Gurley's *taxonomy of circular-revenue red flags* (he's the bear) and the specific deals he names. Stablecoin section is enthusiastic boosterism from two crypto-friendly VCs — flag as promotional. **Note: this is Gurley's final episode as co-host of BG2.**

## TL;DR
- **OpenAI–Broadcom in-house inference accelerator announced ("this morning"), adding >$1T incremental capex** on top of the ~$3T/60GW already-mapped buildout. Gurley's frame: circular/vendor-financing deals are now a competitive *norm* ("we're fairly pregnant with it"), which raises over-provisioning risk AND delays the signal that demand is slowing.
- **Gurley's circular-revenue spectrum (the durable analytical tool):** sham (no real demand, money round-trips) → middle ("would this much have been bought *but for* the investment?") → benign (real demand + incidental investment relationship). He puts the **NVDA equity deals near the benign end** (NVDA = ~$450B FCF generator, not levered) but flags **the CoreWeave / NVIDIA backstop** (NVDA buys CRWV's unsold capacity) as genuinely unusual — it *hides* the demand signal a pure-play would otherwise give.
- **Capex discipline metric:** Mag5 capex ≈ **66% of operating cash flow in 2025 (the peak)**, consensus has it declining to ~45-50% as OCF keeps growing 15-20%/yr. Total Mag-group capex $156B (2023) → $379B (2025). The "watch this ratio" signal.
- **Glut math (Gerstner):** NVDA sell-side ~$200B revenue 2025 → ~$350-400B by 2029-30 ≈ ~4-5 GW of compute now → ~9 GW then. OpenAI ~$150B capex in 2030 would need ~$150B revenue to justify — "more than plausible" for OpenAI, "very difficult for anybody other than the hyperscalers."
- **Stablecoin = third major supercycle** (after AI + reindustrialization): Coinbase/Circle paying 4% "rewards" (interest in all but name post-GENIUS Act) on stablecoin balances + instant settlement. Prediction: hyperscalers (Amazon, Meta) re-enter the stablecoin business because money is a network-effects game and they own the merchants.

## Key facts / claims (with confidence)
- **OpenAI–Broadcom custom inference chip, >$1T incremental capex** (Gerstner). Both note these are *frameworks, not contracts* — AMD/AVGO must deliver workable chips or the GW don't get built. (Medium — directional; framework not binding.)
- **NVDA FCF ~$450B; investing a small fraction over 3 yrs into OpenAI/xAI etc.** — Gerstner argues most investees (OpenAI oversubscribed, Elon can self-fund) didn't *need* the capital, so revenue would have happened anyway. (Bull framing from an NVDA holder; medium.)
- **CoreWeave / NVIDIA backstop** — NVDA promised to buy CRWV service availability it can't sell elsewhere; disclosed in a CRWV filing. Gurley: "very unusual... we don't know what's going on with real demand for CoreWeave." (High that the disclosure exists; this is a real [[CRWV]] earnings-quality flag — analysts will press on Q calls.)
- **Mag5 capex/OCF ~66% (2025 peak) → ~45-50% (consensus out-years); total capex $156B (2023) → $379B (2025).** (Medium-high — Gerstner's charts; verify exact figures before publishing.)
- **NVDA revenue ~$200B (2025) → ~$350-400B (2029-30) sell-side; ~4-5 GW → ~9 GW.** Jensen (prior pod) said "extremely low" glut chance "until we fully convert all general-purpose computing to accelerated computing." (Bull consensus from interested parties; surface as such.)
- **OpenAI ~$150B capex / ~$150B revenue needed by 2030** (Gerstner's add-up, "may be off by a bit"). (Low-medium — host's own estimate.)
- **Circularity precedent risk:** Gurley invokes Enron/WorldCom (sham end), Cisco vendor-loans (real blowup precedent — loans to startups that couldn't repay); notes equity is *more* risk-seeking than loans but *easier* on the buyer (no repayment). Names a **Meta deal backstopping debt on a facility it doesn't own = classic off-balance-sheet financing.** (High as analytical framing; the Meta detail is Gurley's characterization — verify.)
- **AI regulation:** Colorado AI Act (12 protected classes incl. limited English / reproductive health; liability back to frontier-model level) now law; CA SB 243 (signed by Newsom) mandates chatbot-companion safety protocols + private right of action for emotional harm. Both hosts want federal preemption / state moratorium; Gerstner floats labs *blocking citizens of non-compliant states*. (High that laws passed; opinion on response.) **Dated catalyst: Colorado AI Act full effect ~Feb 2026.**
- **Stablecoin:** GENIUS Act bank lobbying forced "rewards not interest" wording; Coinbase markets Circle's 4% as effectively interest. Visa/MC ~50k TPS vs Solana/ETH <4k TPS (per Vinny Lingham) = real throughput gap. Brazil PIX cited as the model the US should envy. (Medium — TPS figures secondhand.)
- **Invest America / "Trump accounts":** ~65M kids eligible; every kid <2 auto-gets $1,000; must be funded by July 4, 2026; launch site ~early December. (Color; Gerstner's own legislation — heavy talk-his-book.)
