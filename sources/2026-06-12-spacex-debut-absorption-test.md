# SpaceX debut: the $75B absorption test passes on day one; proxies round-trip; SPX closes above the flip

## TL;DR

- **Largest IPO in history traded and the market absorbed it**: SpaceX (SPCX) opened at $150 (+11% over the $135 fixed price), spiked to $176.52, and closed $160.95 (+19.2%) — a >$2T market cap on day one, after raising ~$75B selling 555.6M new shares. The S&P **rose** 0.5% to 7,431.46 the same session. First hard evidence against the June 10 "$3.6T IPO pipeline drains the chip bid" bear mechanism.
- **SPX closed above the dealer-hedging flip** (7,424 per June 11 dual-feed compute) for the first time since June 4 — 7,431.46, +0.5%; week +0.65%. VIX 17.68 at the close (intraday snippets showed ~19 early). The amplified-tape regime that defined June 5–11 is, for now, over.
- **Iran deal in the endgame but unsigned**: Trump denied the Iranian-leaked draft text ("NOTHING to do with the terms agreed"); a senior admin official put 80% odds on signing "in the coming days" (Hormuz reopens, naval blockade lifts, nuclear program dismantled); Araghchi said an MOU has "never been closer," possibly timed to the G7. WTI fell 3%+ to below $85 — an eight-week low.

## Key facts

- Indexes: S&P 500 +0.5% to 7,431.46; Nasdaq +0.31% to 25,888.84; Dow +353.51 (+0.7%) to 51,202.26. Week: S&P +0.65% (7,383.58 June 5 close → 7,431.46) — first up week since the 9-week streak snapped (June 1-5 was −2.6%).
- SPCX: open $150, high $176.52, close $160.95 (+19.2% vs $135 offer). ~$75B raised (555.6M new shares), largest IPO ever; valuation >$2T at the close; Musk becomes the world's first trillionaire (CNN). xAI consolidated inside since the Feb 2026 merger — also the largest AI listing ever.
- Proxy round-trips on the debut: EchoStar (SATS) traded $135.45 high / $115.52 low (−10% midday from Thursday's $128.13) and closed $133.40 (+4.1%) — dumped at the SPCX open, bought back by the close. Rocket Lab (RKLB) closed −10.8% at $102.39 (high $118.38, no recovery) **despite** the June 22 Nasdaq-100 inclusion announcement — joining the MRVL/FLEX June 22 index-date cluster.
- ADBE −6.8% to $204.02 (intraday low $196.90, multi-year lows) on CFO-exit downgrades — third consecutive down session through a beat-and-raise print; now ~13.5% below the $233.38 pre-print level.
- Univ. of Michigan June prelim sentiment 48.9 vs 46.0 consensus and May's record-low 44.8 — first rise in three months, on falling gas prices; level still historically depressed (−19% y/y).
- Rates/commodities: 10Y ~4.47%; WTI "below $85," −3%+, eight-week low (exact settle unconfirmed at write time); deal-signing watch into the weekend.
- MRVL: B.Riley raised its PT citing the NVDA NVLink-Fusion partnership (minor; momentum-chasing note).

## Key claims (and how confident)

- **The IPO-supply bear mechanism just took a direct hit (high confidence on the fact, medium on the read).** BNP's June 10 warning framed the $3.6T OpenAI/Anthropic/SpaceX pipeline as a structural drain on the chip bid. Day one of the largest ship in that pipeline: $75B absorbed, stock +19%, S&P up, semis fine. **Which means:** public-market appetite for AI mega-listings is, at minimum, deeper than the June 10 selloff implied — the pipeline is a *pacing* risk, not a wall. Contradicting evidence note added to [[ai-bubble-debate]] rather than overwriting the bear mechanism; one day proves absorption of one deal, and OpenAI/Anthropic listings would each be larger asks in a worse tape.
- **Proxy de-rating is mechanical, not thesis-driven (medium).** SATS/RKLB got sold at the SPCX open because the proxy trade's reason-to-exist expired — then SATS closed green. **Which means:** watch the June 22 index-date cluster (MRVL → S&P 500, RKLB + FLEX adjacent) for the same pattern: the anticipation trade dies on the access date, not on the fundamentals.
- **GOOGL's ~5% SpaceX stake is now marked by the market (high).** At a >$2T close, the stake the wiki carried at "~$100B at a $2T IPO" is now a liquid, public ~$100B+ asset inside Alphabet. **Which means:** the sum-of-parts case for the cheapest Mag-7 name got a hard, tickered data point — and a future monetization lever nobody models.
