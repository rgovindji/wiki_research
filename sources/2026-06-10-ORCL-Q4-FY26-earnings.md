# Oracle Q4 FY2026: record everything, and the stock fell anyway — the funding question beat the demand answer

## TL;DR

- **Beat on both lines, record RPO**: rev $19.2B +21% (beat), non-GAAP EPS $2.11 (vs $1.96), **RPO $553B → $638B (+$85B QoQ)** — almost all of the Q3+Q4 RPO growth from large-scale AI contracts. IaaS +93% to $5.8B.
- **The market sold the funding, not the demand**: FY26 capex printed **$55.7B vs ~$50B guided**; FY27 net capex outlay guided to **~$70B**; and Oracle announced a **~$40B debt+equity raise for FY27** (incl. $20B ATM). Stock fell as much as ~7% after hours despite the beats.
- **One genuinely new structural disclosure**: **$75B of cumulative customer prepayments** for GPUs, plus bring-your-own-hardware contracts — customers are co-funding Oracle's buildout, materially softening the pure-debt-contagion read.

## Key facts

- Q4: rev $19.2B +21%; cloud $9.9B +47% (low end of 46–50% guide); IaaS $5.8B +93%; SaaS $4.1B +10%; non-GAAP EPS $2.11 +24%
- FY26: rev $67.4B +17%; cloud $34.0B +39%; OCF $32B +54%; capex $55.7B (guide ~$50B)
- RPO $638B; ~12% recognized within 12 months, ~34% within 13–36 months, both "expected to accelerate"
- FY27: revenue target ~$90B (~+33%); non-GAAP EPS raised to $8.05; net capex cash outlay ~$70B ex $20–25B customer prepayments
- ~$40B FY27 debt+equity raise (one outlet said $50B; call transcript supports $40B incl. the $20B ATM); no new debt in calendar 2026; existing debt ~$153B
- AI infra gross margins declining during the DC ramp; BYO-hardware contracts at-or-better margins
- Call run by co-CEOs Sicilia + Magouyrk and CFO Maxson; no OpenAI-specific update on the call

## Key claims (and how confident)

- **"Almost all incremental RPO from large-scale AI contracts"** (high confidence — company statement). Which means: the $638B is even more concentrated in a handful of AI counterparties (OpenAI's $300B Stargate commitment is the known anchor) — the conversion question and the counterparty-credit question are now the same question.
- **Customer prepayments $75B cumulative** (high confidence — company statement). Which means: the wiki's [[ai-infrastructure-debt]] framing of ORCL as "the debt-funded counter-example to GOOGL's equity raise" needs a real amendment — a meaningful slice of the buildout is customer-funded, which is *neither* debt nor dilution. The contagion thesis doesn't die, but the cleanest version of it (Oracle borrows, OpenAI maybe can't pay) now has a documented offset.
- **Capex overshoot + $40B raise = the bear exhibit** (market's read, not ours alone). Which means: Oracle just did, via debt+equity mix, what GOOGL did via equity and what META was punished for merely considering — the "who funds the buildout, and how" question is now THE differentiator across the AI complex, exactly as the June 1 GOOGL read-through framed it.
