# Philippe Laffont (Coatue) — SpaceX IPO / AI Bubble / Anthropic Valuation (~Jun 2026)

## TL;DR
- Laffont puts **80% "not a bubble" / 20% "bubble."** Key difference vs. 1999: AI capex is funded by hyperscalers generating ~$1T EBITDA/yr with ~no net debt, not retail/capital markets; market P/E ~20-21x vs. ~50-60x in 1999.
- The 20% bear case he names: **earnings are front-loaded and "melt"** — i.e. current AI/SaaS earnings are not sustainable, so cheap-looking multiples are a trap. (His one falsifiable bubble condition.)
- On Anthropic: declines a buy/sell call, but says it "absolutely" could be worth $5-7T in 10 years; treats lab equity as **a basket of options on "intelligence as the fifth utility."**

## Key facts (and confidence)
- Coatue ~$100B AUM, started at $50M, ~50/50 public/private split. Has held up to 80% cash at times; "five good ideas in 25 years." (High — self-reported bio.)
- ~20 companies now ≥$1T (vs. old Mag7); adds TSM, SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron as the semiconductor $1T cohort; NVDA in the $2-4T tier. (Med — directional, undated.)
- Owns Anthropic and OpenAI in private book; also flags Revolut as a potential future $1T "digital bank." (High he said it; positions not quantified.)
- Bought Intel "maybe at 80," now ~130 after US equity stake; framed govt stake as a good decision. (Med — round numbers, his recollection. Relevant to [[ORCL]]? no — to Intel/govt-stake theme.)

## Key claims (and confidence)
- **Not a bubble (80%):** funding structure (hyperscaler cash flow, low debt) + reasonable index multiple make this structurally unlike 1999. (Med — this is the core bull macro frame; he is an AI long.)
- **Bubble risk (20%):** front-loaded/unsustainable AI earnings that later melt. (This is the falsifiable bear condition to track.)
- AI is a "10+ year trend, ~2 years in" — expect ups and downs, not a 1-year blowup. (Low-Med — directional conviction.)
- AI models are "digital 3D printers" — can replicate any digital good (e.g. clone Excel in hours) → **structurally bad for software**, argues for overweighting semis vs. software relative to index weights. (Med — notable: an AI bull who is bearish SaaS, aligns partly with Dario's "big losers" + Zitron's SaaS-rot.)
- Telecom infrastructure migrates to space (Starlink-type) → bad for land-based wireless/cable/charter. (Low — thematic speculation.)
- On SpaceX IPO (listing "Friday"): can't value off current metrics; SpaceX "maybe worth 1-3T," IPO "probably at 2." Explicitly **refuses to say buy it.** (Med — hedged, no recommendation.)
- Negative Bitcoin tactically (sees SpaceX/other IPOs as a new speculation outlet drawing flows away from BTC) — then self-deprecates that he's usually wrong near bottoms. (Low.)

## Quotes worth keeping
> "80% that we're not [in a bubble] and 20% that we are."
> "The people funding the AI CapEx... generate about $1 trillion of EBITDA a year... and have no net debt. Very different than 1999, when it was retail and the financial markets that were financing."
> "The 20% where we could be in a bubble is what if stocks are cheap because the earnings have been front-loaded and at some point the earnings melt."
> "These AI models, they're like digital 3D printers... that could be very bad for software."
> "Could Anthropic be worth 5 or 7 trillion in ten years? Absolutely. There's a chance."
> [On SpaceX at IPO] "Is it worth three? Yeah, maybe. And is the IPO going to be at two? Probably." (explicitly not a buy call)

## Bias / talking-their-book flags
- **Laffont = AI long with private positions in Anthropic + OpenAI** → every "could be worth $5-7T" line marks his own book. The $5-7T figures are his hypotheticals, NOT analyst price targets — do not log as targets.
- Anti-index/"anti-Darth-Vader" framing is partly Coatue marketing (active vs. passive).
- Notably he is bearish SaaS despite being an AI bull — that's a non-trivial, less-conflicted tell (cuts against his own broad-tech longs).
