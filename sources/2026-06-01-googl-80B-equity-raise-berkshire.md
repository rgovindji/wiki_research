# Alphabet Announces $80B Equity Raise + Berkshire Hathaway $10B Private Placement

On the morning of 2026-06-01, Alphabet announced **the largest equity capital raise in tech history at $80 billion**, with Berkshire Hathaway anchoring via a $10B private placement at negotiated prices below the prior-day close. Berkshire's prior position — built quietly since Q3 2025 — is also disclosed for the first time.

This is a **tier-1 watershed event** for the wiki framework. It reframes:
- The "internally funded capex" bull pillar of [[ai-bubble-debate]] (broken at GOOGL specifically)
- The [[ai-capex-cycle]] curve (2026-27 capex now demonstrably steeper than the $185-190B guide implied)
- The [[ai-infrastructure-debt]] systemic-risk thesis (GOOGL chose dilution over debt — the *opposite* of the Oracle/SRT/private-credit pattern)
- The [[GOOGL]] bull case (Berkshire stamp at a specific price; institutional validation of the cloud + TPU thesis)
- The [[market-concentration]] math (Mag 7 capital allocation paradigm just split — GOOGL is now alone among the seven in raising equity at scale)

## TL;DR

- **$80B total raise** = largest tech equity offering in history. Mechanism mix: $30B concurrent underwritten + $40B ATM (begins Q3 2026) + $10B Berkshire private placement.
- **🔥 Berkshire Hathaway $10B private placement** at **$351.81 (Class A)** and **$348.20 (Class C)** — ~10% below prior day's $390 close. Position is *on top of* what Berkshire has built since Q3 2025 (newly disclosed).
- **Proceeds:** general corporate purposes, primarily **AI compute infrastructure**. ~$30B of the ATM is earmarked for **2026 employee equity vesting tax obligations** (an administrative change in how RSU vesting is funded — see "underpriced detail" below).
- **Stock reaction (June 1):** GOOGL −2.51% to $380.34 (from $390.13). The drop is dilution-driven, not Berkshire-driven; the Berkshire stamp limited the downside.
- **Dilution:** ~228M new shares against ~12.2B outstanding = **~1.9% gross dilution** at the headline (some offsets from the RSU-tax mechanism netting). Berkshire-anchored pricing floor ~$348-$352.

### 1. "Internally funded capex" bull pillar broken at GOOGL

The wiki's [[ai-bubble-debate]] bull case includes (verbatim):

> *"Capex is internally funded — hyperscalers are spending out of operating cash flow, not debt — this is not a 1999 telco / dot-com leverage bubble"*

**That is no longer true at GOOGL.** Alphabet's 2026 capex guide of $185-190B + this $80B raise = the AI buildout has exceeded what even Google's monopoly-search cash flow can fund. **Which means** the wiki's bull-case framing needs sharpening: the cycle is *no longer* "every hyperscaler self-funds" — it's "MSFT and AAPL self-fund; GOOGL self-funds + dilutes; META self-funds + cuts costs; AMZN self-funds + slows other businesses; ORCL leverages." The supplier-rent story remains intact, but the *funding-source* story is now layered.

The wiki's [[ai-infrastructure-debt]] thesis warns about $120B+ of AI DC construction debt being shifted from banks to private credit / pension funds / insurance via SRT — the contagion mechanism converting industry slowdowns into financial crises.

The [[ai-infrastructure-debt]] systemic-risk vector concentrates further at ORCL + tier-2 names that *can't* tap equity markets at this scale.

## The underpriced detail: $30B RSU mechanism

Buried in the use-of-proceeds disclosure: ~$30B of the ATM is for **2026 employee equity vesting tax obligations** — explicitly an "administrative change to how it meets tax obligations associated with vesting of employee equity awards."

**The standard mechanism** for funding RSU vesting taxes is "net-share settlement": company withholds a portion of vested shares, sells them in the open market, uses proceeds to pay employee taxes. **Net dilution is fixed at vesting.**

**Alphabet's new mechanism** issues *new* shares via ATM, raises cash, and pays the tax obligations from cash — letting employees keep the full share grant. The effect: **slightly higher dilution than the prior mechanism, but the company gets to keep treasury cash that would otherwise have gone to taxes.**

Why now? Three reads:
1. **Treasury preservation for AI capex** — every dollar of cash kept is a dollar that doesn't need to be raised
2. **Employee retention** — full share grants (no withholding) is a soft comp increase
3. **Optics** — the change separates "AI capex funding" from "RSU dilution" in the company's narrative

**Which means**: the headline "$80B AI raise" overstates the *new AI capex* dimension by ~$30B. The actual new AI capex tranche is closer to **$50B** ($30B underwritten + $10B Berkshire + ~$10B of the ATM net of RSU mechanic).
