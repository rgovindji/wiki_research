# Inside Anthropic, the $965B Juggernaut — Dario & Daniela Amodei (The Circuit / Bloomberg, ~Jun 17 2026)

## TL;DR
- Anthropic is now the highest-revenue, highest-valuation frontier lab (~$965B implied), **profitable for the first time**, on the back of enterprise + coding (Claude Code, Claude Cowork), not consumer. Growth "faster than the exponential" — Q1 2026 annualized ~80x/yr.
- A new internal model, **Mythos** (Project Glasswing), found thousands of cyber-vulnerabilities; Anthropic withheld it ("super weapon"), saying it cost them "enormously commercially" — bull tell on capability slope, but unverifiable.
- Anthropic was **banned from the Pentagon** after refusing to drop red lines (mass surveillance, autonomous weapons); collision with DoD/Trump admin is a live political/regulatory risk for the whole lab cohort.

## Key facts (and confidence)
- Anthropic profitable for the first time; revenue "skyrocketed"; described as the company with most revenue + most valuation in the space. (Med — narrator/Dario framing, no audited figures given. ~$965B is the episode title's implied valuation, not a stated raise.)
- API volume up ~17x YoY "on the cloud"; 8 frontier models shipped in last 12 months; Q1 2026 ~80x annualized growth. (Med — self-reported, undated specifics.)
- Claude Cowork launch coincided with ~"$285B in market value vanished overnight" in software ("SaaSpocalypse"), software stocks down 9 days in a row. (Med — narrator claim; magnitude uncited. Directly relevant to [[ai-software-disruption]].)
- Internally Claude writes "almost all" code; Boris Cherny (Claude Code/Cowork creator) says 100% of his code for 6+ months. (High that they say it; productivity claim self-serving.)
- Dario reiterates: AI could eliminate ~half of entry-level white-collar jobs in 1-5yr; "still same order of concern." (High he said it — consistent with prior statements.)
- $200M Pentagon contract (2025) w/ OpenAI, xAI, Google; Anthropic later banned after refusing full unguarded use. Claude reportedly used via Palantir Maven Smart System in Iran war targeting. (Med — Bloomberg reporting cited in-show.)
- Dario: ~10-25% chance of "civilization collapse" from AI broadly (not Anthropic-specific). (High he said it.)
- Wants **required pre-release testing/auditing** of models; opposes both nationalization and pure laissez-faire. (High.)

## Key claims (and confidence)
- "The pie gets bigger" — software industry grows even as some incumbents shrink or die; losers are those who don't identify their moats. (Dario's central rebuttal to SaaS-doom; Low-Med — talking his book, this is the bull case for [[ai-capex-cycle]] demand durability.)
- "Smooth exponential" — capability/revenue compounding is predictable, not a fad. (Self-serving but consistent w/ scaling-law thesis.)
- Mythos withholding "hurt us enormously commercially" — i.e., capability is real and ahead. (Low verifiability; classic capability-signaling.)

## Bias / talking-their-book flags
- **Dario = lab CEO, maximally conflicted.** Capability claims (Mythos as "super weapon," 80x growth, "faster than the exponential") are unaudited and serve fundraising/valuation. The "doom that benefits Anthropic" critique is raised in-show (Jensen Huang: "conflating tasks with jobs... scaring people"); Dario rebuts but the structural conflict stands.
- Safety-as-moat narrative also doubles as marketing.
- No price targets / valuation raises stated verbatim; $965B is title framing — treat as directional, not a cited round.
