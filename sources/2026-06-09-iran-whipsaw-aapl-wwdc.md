# June 9 session: Iran whipsaw bought back, then US strikes after the close; AAPL day-2 WWDC selloff on AI-monetization silence

## TL;DR

- **Session:** S&P 500 −0.26% to 7,386.65 (intraday low −1.46% at ~7,297, defended), Nasdaq −0.97% to 25,678.82, Dow **+0.17% to 50,872.11**. The midday lurch came when news circulated that an Iranian Shahed drone had downed a US Army Apache helicopter off Oman on Monday (both pilots rescued by sea drone, unhurt) and Trump said the US "must respond." Buyers walked it back over the final two hours; tech and energy were the only red sectors. WTI **fell 2.3% to $89.22** and held under $90 through the headline — the de-escalation tell.
- **After the close (~5:00 PM EDT): CENTCOM launched retaliatory strikes on Iran**, calling them "self-defense strikes" and "a proportional response" to the Apache downing. Trump simultaneously kept saying a deal is "two or three days" away with Hormuz reopening "immediately upon signing." The ceasefire is nominally intact but visibly fraying. Overnight oil/futures reaction not yet observable at write time.
- **AAPL −3%+ (second straight down day post-WWDC).** Stock hit an all-time intraday high of $317 on June 8 as the Siri AI reveal landed, then reversed and kept falling June 9. The market's complaint is not the product — it's the **absence of a ship date (Siri AI launched in beta, no timeline) and the absence of any monetization plan** at a ~36x multiple. Gene Munster: the stock is falling because "Apple gave no timeline on Siri."

## Key facts

- Indexes: SPX 7,386.65 (−0.26%); Nasdaq Composite 25,678.82 (−0.97%); Dow 50,872.11 (+0.17%). SMH −1% after Monday's +6% rebound.
- SPX technicals (as of June 8): 50-DMA ~7,174.5, 200-DMA ~6,863.3 — index comfortably above both despite the pullback from the ~7,575-7,600 record zone.
- Oil: WTI $89.22 (−2.3%), Brent $92.55 (−1.8%) — *down* on a war-headline day.
- US strikes began 5:00 PM EDT June 9 per CENTCOM ("proportional response... in response to yesterday's downing of a US Army Apache helicopter").
- AAPL: ATH $317 intraday June 8 → two-day slide; −3%+ June 9. New Siri AI is LLM-based, partly powered by Google Gemini, beta-only, no user ship date, no pricing/revenue framework given.
- Analyst split on AAPL: Wedbush (Ives) Outperform / $400 PT, "AI could add $75-100/share"; Maxim raised PT to $350; KeyBanc (Nispel): "no clear signs of monetization... reliance on Google Gemini... slightly better standalone Siri, still worse than other LLMs"; UBS (Vogt): won't drive iPhone demand.
- MRVL −7.6% to $266.88, giving back most of Monday's +9.6% S&P-inclusion pop. No new company news; inclusion-pop profit-taking into a de-risking tape.
- Of the day's 22 earnings reports, none were wiki tickers (SJM beat/warned; SailPoint missed). ORCL Q4 prints June 10 AMC; May CPI June 10 8:30 ET.

## Key claims (and how confident)

- **The market is trading the Iran deal, not the strikes** (high confidence as description of June 9 behavior): equities absorbed a literal war headline to a −0.26% close and oil *fell*. The after-close US strikes are the first real test of whether that read survives contact with actual kinetic escalation — overnight crude is the arbiter. **Which means:** if WTI is still under ~$95 tomorrow morning, the de-escalation trade is intact and CPI remains the binary; if crude gaps toward $95+, the equity resilience thesis was conditioned on a deal that is now slipping.
- **AAPL's WWDC silence is a bear-thesis data point, not a new thesis** (medium-high): the wiki's bear stance rests on TSMC deprioritization + smartphone-volume/BOM compression; WWDC adds a third leg — the market is now explicitly refusing to pay for un-monetized AI at a premium multiple. **Which means:** the burden of proof on the "AI sells more iPhones" bull leg has moved from "show a demo" to "show a date and a dollar figure," and Apple declined to do either at its own showcase event.
- **MRVL's round-trip validates the "inclusion is lagging confirmation" framing** (medium): the June 8 ingest explicitly warned the inclusion bid was not a new bull leg. One session later the pop fully unwound. The live test of whether the mechanical bid *defends* a price floor is the open `2026-06-09-mrvl-inclusion-bid` prediction ($250 line, resolves June 22).
