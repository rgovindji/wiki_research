# SemiWiki scan 2026-06-18 — Apple-Intel + Google-TPU packaging war + Samsung 1d DRAM + EORB photonics

## TL;DR
- **Trump announces Apple will build chips with Intel in the US** — disclosed via Truth Social, not by either company; light on specifics (no volumes, timeline, dollar figure). INTC ripped ~+14.5% to $133.99; a year after Washington took a ~10% stake. SemiWiki's own framing is skeptical: "a rally built on relationships rather than the manufacturing execution Intel has yet to demonstrate at scale" (Intel cost/chip ~3x TSMC, yields trail). Apple silent; Intel "won't comment."
- **Google 9th-gen TPU = an Intel EMIB-T vs TSMC CoWoS packaging war worth billions.** Intel CFO Zinsner's April "demand in billions/yr, not hundreds of millions" advanced-packaging comment is implied to be almost entirely the Google TPU program (MediaTek-designed). Bernstein: ~$1B revenue per 1M AI chips packaged on EMIB-T → "several million units." TSMC counter-attacking with a 14-reticle CoWoS-L platform (up from 9.5) to win the business back; the decision "was never purely technical."
- **Intel Foundry stands up advanced packaging as a focused business under Seok-Hee Lee** (ex-CEO of SK hynix *and* SK On) reporting to Lip-Bu Tan — to ramp EMIB-T and HBI to high volume. Naga Chandrasekaran keeps front-end (18A/14A ramp).
- **Samsung accelerating 1d DRAM** (next node after 1c; = Micron 1-delta) to mass production end-2027, targeting HBM5 — first shift to **vertically stacked capacitors** + **dual-wafer bonding** (array and periphery on separate wafers).
- **Electro-Optical Realization Corridor (EORB)** — photonics forum framing: the winning CPO/optical-I/O architecture isn't the best optical *device*, it's the one that proves the full electro-optical *realization path* (launch → conversion → driver → package/substrate → alignment → thermal drift → fiber attach → test → yield → reliability) can be manufactured and trusted at scale.
- Lower tail: RISC-V/AI (SHD Group: ~36B RISC-V SoC units / >$300B semi revenue by 2031; NVIDIA's portfolio-wide RISC-V deployment a "validation event"); PDF Solutions "Data Feed Forward" (DFF) chiplet-era test intelligence. Defacto IP-XACT webinar = vendor PR, skipped.

## Key facts
- INTC closed $133.99, ~+14.5% on the day (from $117.05). Broad search also attributes part of the move to "Alphabet tapped Intel for chip manufacturing" — i.e., the Google-TPU-on-Intel-Foundry story is being re-priced alongside the Apple headline.
- Apple's logic for Intel is supply, not patriotism — a second source vs TSMC, whose leading-edge lines are fought over by Nvidia/AMD. Intel 18A is "the first American node theoretically capable." Apple has internal doubts on non-TSMC yield/performance/timing.
- Google 2025 TPU shipments est. ~2-3M units; Zinsner's "billions" implies the 9th-gen program is several million units = MediaTek's 2nd TPU design win is both successful and larger than expected.
- Seok-Hee Lee will lead all advanced packaging, system integration, back-end tech and back-end manufacturing — Intel naming packaging as a defining capability (EMIB-T, HBI).
- Samsung 1d DRAM: dedicated equipment into lines before Q2 2027; mass production possibly end-2027; the "watershed" node that governs Samsung's HBM5E competitive position.

## Key claims (and how confident)
- Apple-Intel deal is REAL but EARLY/THIN — high confidence it exists (WSJ May preliminary-deal report + months of talks per Semafor), low confidence on scale/timeline (no terms; both firms silent; President announced it). Treat as sentiment/optionality, not modeled revenue.
- Google TPU packaging at Intel is the single biggest external Intel Foundry program for 2-3 years (high confidence per Zinsner + industry consensus); TSMC is actively trying to claw it back (medium confidence; CoWoS-L 14-reticle is real, the win-back is reported).
- Samsung 1d DRAM 2027 timing (medium; South Korean media/industry sources).
