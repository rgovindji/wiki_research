# Meta goes closed-frontier — Muse Spark (MSL) + Llama 5; a frontier *follower*, not leader

## TL;DR
- **Meta Superintelligence Labs (MSL)**, under new Chief AI Officer **Alexandr Wang** (ex-Scale, via the ~$14B deal), shipped a **dual-track on 2026-04-08: open-weights Llama 5 + closed/proprietary "Muse Spark"** — Meta's first closed model.
- **Reality check on "crush Opus":** Muse Spark currently ranks **4th on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index (score 52)** — *behind* Gemini 3.1 Pro, GPT-5.4, and the Claude Opus tier. Meta is a frontier **follower**, not leader. Next-gen **"Mango" / "Avocado"** models are rumored for later-2026.
- **Investing reframe:** even a leading Meta model would not directly move [[META]] — Meta doesn't sell frontier-model API access as a revenue line. It's an ad-targeting / consumer-AI / capex-justification lever. The stock answers to capex discipline, the dilution overhang, and ad-ROI.

## Key facts
- MSL assembled summer 2025; Wang (29) recruited as Chief AI Officer to lead it.
- 2026-04-08 dual-track: **Llama 5 (open) + Muse Spark (closed)** — first time Meta split open/closed.
- Efficiency claim: small models ~as capable as mid-size Llama 4 for **~10× less compute**; "competitive in multimodal perception, reasoning, health, agentic tasks."
- Benchmark: **Muse Spark = 4th, score 52** on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index.
- Rumored next-gen: **Mango / Avocado** (later-2026).
- Price (Polygon-verified, close 2026-06-29): **META $562.60**, below 20/50/200-EMA ($577/$598/$634), **~26% off** the 180-day high $759.15 (2025-10-29). 2026 capex guide **$125–145B**.
