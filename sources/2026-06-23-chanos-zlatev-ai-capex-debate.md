# Jim Chanos vs Val Zlatev — Where's the value in AI, long and short (MacroMinds panel)

## TL;DR
- **A real bull-vs-bear debate, not a recap.** Jim Chanos (legendary short — Enron/Wirecard/China RE) vs **Val Zlatev** (CIO, Analog Century Capital — multi-billion long/short semis & hardware fund, **net long AI**). Recorded ~early June, released ~June 20.
- **They agree on the core trade:** own *where the chips are produced* (NVDA/AVGO/TSM — oligopolistic, "much cheaper"), short/avoid *where the chips reside* (neoclouds/Bitcoin-miner-DCs = equipment-leasing finance cos at single-digit ROIC). Chanos is **NOT short any semiconductor** — his shorts are data-center landlords.
- **They split on memory & duration:** Zlatev's biggest long is **memory** (supply-constrained, shallow 2-4yr peak); Chanos won't touch DRAM ("40 years, never made a dollar shorting it — it's cyclical, can't time it"). Both warn: don't price long-duration equities off spot-shortage prices.

## Key facts & claims (and confidence)

### Chanos — the bear (accounting + cycle, NOT short semis)
- **Accounting mismatch (his central macro point):** picks-and-shovels (NVDA, GEV/GE Vernova, VRT) book revenue & profit *immediately*; hyperscalers *capitalize* the very same dollars → the high-tech profit boom is structurally inflated. *Med-high confidence — this is a real accounting fact; the magnitude is the debate.*
- **'98–'00 parallel:** S&P operating profits +30% over two years into mid-2000, then **−40% mid-2000→2001** (as deep as the GFC) when order books collapsed but depreciation/costs continued.
- **Construction-in-progress lag:** 12–18 months where money is spent (chips, labor, interest all capitalized) but the DC isn't online or depreciating yet → earnings flattered today, depreciation hits later.
- **He uses a 10-YEAR GPU life in his models** — explicitly rebuts the bear caricature ("bears say 2–3yr"). Even at 10yr life + heroic profitability, neoclouds/miner-DCs pencil to **4–8% pre-tax ROIC** in the out years → "de minimis." → long the chipmakers, not the "financial middleman."
- **Neoclouds = finance/equipment-leasing cos, not tech.** "CoreWeave guys are the old Magnetar guys; Blackstone just got into this business." Tell: hyperscalers *can* buy chips themselves but choose to rent → gold-rush capacity grab. *Zlatev partially disputes — see below.*
- **SpaceX IPO (S-1 out, listing "next week"):** numbers don't work on the existing business. **Starlink** ~$4B operating profit on ~$25–30B invested capital (good, not insanely good; growth slowed, cut price). **Launch business still loses money** after billions (surprised him in the S-1) AND subsidizes Starlink (so Starlink may be less profitable than reported). **xAI = cash sinkhole** (cut a *short-term* deal renting space to Anthropic). ~$2T valuation requires believing Mars/moon/space-DCs — "like Tesla, can't justify on the cars." Won't short it (shorting Musk hasn't worked).
- **Data centers in space:** power is only **5–7% of DC revenue** → "free sun" is the wrong reason. Real costs = radiation (hard to radiate in a vacuum), redundancy/insurance (can't dispatch a tech — needs a launch). **Starship hasn't reached Earth orbit in 12 flights, blew up 6–7 of 12.**
- **The nightmare for picks-and-shovels** (what "keeps me awake about my longs"): a *new, non-transformer* architecture that breaks scaling laws → multiplies compute-for-nothing. **DeepSeek (Jan-2025, sold AI off 30–60% over 3 weeks) was NOT that** — just cost reduction with known algorithms. Skeptical China has broken anything ("People's Republic of Madoff").
- **Relative value, not blanket froth:** Intel/AMD (competitive CPU) re-rated on *revenue* despite no earnings; vs NVDA/AVGO/TSM (oligopolistic) "much cheaper." Networking ~50–60x fwd = most extreme. **NOT '99** (Cisco was 160x then). '99 myth-buster: fiber cos + CLECs spent only ~$100B total over 5yrs; the real spenders were *enterprises* + Y2K. MCI's "traffic doubles every quarter" was fiction (Odlyzko: doubled per *year*); order books cut 20,000 routers → 4,000 overnight.
- **Final word:** "Don't put magical valuations on mundane businesses. Capital flooding in reduces returns; the mundane models get exposed **within 18–24 months.**"

### Zlatev — the (net-long) operator
- **AI impact is already in the financials:** across 500+ hard-tech names, headcount flat/declining while operating profit surged. CEOs excited. *Bias: talks his long book.*
- **GPU rental prices inflected:** down 20–30% YoY into December (normal — new architectures are more token-efficient, so old GPUs *should* depreciate). **Since January, UP 40–50%+** on tightness as token usage explodes. Changes neocloud near-term economics (doesn't know if it lasts). *Corroborates Baseten B200 $263→$510/hr renewal in [[inference-economics]].*
- **Track demand directly via OpenRouter token counts** — don't wait for a CFO "to tell you something he made up in the back room."
- **Neoclouds aren't all dumb shells:** CoreWeave/Nebius have real software/optimization layers. **Nebius only ~50–60% contracted to hyperscalers; the other ~40–50% is inference at spot** → captures the spot price increases. But value ultimately migrates to the chip tech (NVDA/AVGO), not landlords. *("Nimbix" in transcript = Nebius mis-transcription.)*
- **Bottlenecks have a clock:** power/labor are real for ~18 months but likely **gone in 3 years**. Equities are long-duration → don't price off spot.

### Zlatev's memory thesis (his biggest long) — lands day before MU's print
- 3 producers (MU + Samsung + SK Hynix). Stocks up so much that **forward P/E went DOWN** (pricing power rose faster than the stock) → trading **5–7x 2026 EPS**, which only makes sense if you believe a sharp ASP rollover is **6–9 months out**.
- He argues that rollover is **unlikely soon**: peak amplitude is the highest in 25 years and **shallow — could last 2–4 years.** Supply can't catch up because **(1) equipment makers (ASML/AMAT) can't grow shipments >30–35%/yr** (supply-chain complexity ceiling = the bit-growth ceiling = "the brakes on the whole AI boom"); **(2)** not enough clean-room space — memory makers were planning for a *downturn* through 2024/early-2025 (prices were weak then), and the 60–70yr-old memory execs are too cautious to believe Silicon Valley's demand forecasts.
- **DRAM/flash prices already +4–5x, "100% data-center-driven"** (reasoning models store more tokens → context windows → agents over the last 5 months "suck a ton" of storage). "Will go more — +30% q/q easily."
- **Downstream SHORT idea (the memory-supercycle bear's real bite):** memory was ~20% of a PC/phone bill-of-materials, now ~50%. OEMs on 5–6% margins must pass it through → smartphone/PC prices up (visible at Best Buy) → elastic demand → **PC/smartphone units down ~mid-teens this year** (very rare). Short the *component makers* into PC/phone that lack pricing power.
- **Semi-equipment (ASML/LAM/AMAT):** sound semi-monopolies but growth capped ~30% and ~35x fwd → expensive *relative* to NVDA (~15x 2027) / AVGO (~12x 2028 after this-morning's drop). A relative call, not "bad companies."

### Contradiction surfaced (do not resolve)
- **TSMC CEO pushed back "last night"** on the "cautious old guys keeping the brakes on" thesis: "we're gearing up / building fabs **as fast as we can**." Zlatev's rebuttal: that *is* the constraint — equipment can't exceed ~30%/yr regardless of will. → The supply-discipline-vs-physical-ceiling distinction matters for how durable the [[MU]] cycle is.

### Host (Jack Farley) closing — flagged as host's own book
- Bullish semis on AI capex; likes broad semi exposure; MRVL & Teradyne call options worked; **most excited name = NVDA.** Won't short semis even if it's a bubble. **Bear on META** ("AI strategy makes no sense"). US market is "increasingly a concentrated bet on whether AI works."

## Bias flags
- **Zlatev is net-long AI and runs a semis fund** — every long-side claim (memory durability, demand-real) talks his book. **Chanos is a professional short** and is talking his neocloud/SpaceX short book. Both are interested parties; the *agreements* (chip-tech > landlords; not-'99-froth; don't price spot) carry more weight than either's directional call.
- Memory ASP/multiple figures are Zlatev's estimates; valuation multiples (NVDA 15x'27, AVGO 12x'28, memory 5–7x) are his characterizations, not audited.
- Single key claim to verify: PC/smartphone units "down mid-teens" YoY.
