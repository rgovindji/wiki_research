## 1. Iran deal — Trump-Oman Hormuz fee tension

**Update vs. [[2026-05-19-iran-mou-near]]:**

Trump told reporters May 23 that the deal to end the war and reopen the Strait of Hormuz had been *"largely been negotiated."* Iran's semi-official Fars news agency indicated there were still wide disagreements, but momentum is positive.

**New wrinkle: Iran and Oman jointly regulating the Strait + charging a so-called "environmental fee" (transit toll) on ships.** Trump's response:

> *"Oman will behave just like everybody else or we'll have to blow 'em up... No, the strait's going to be open to everybody."*

Iran's Foreign Ministry: *"The Strait had nothing to do with the US... Tehran was engaging with Oman to decide what happens there."*

**Oil price reaction:**
- WTI briefly under $90/bbl May 24-26 — first time in ~3 weeks
- Brent ~$98/bbl (down 4.62% from prior Friday close)
- May 27 saw further -5%+ moves on Rubio comments giving Iran "every chance to succeed"

**Market reaction:** S&P 500 +0.02% to **7,520.36 record close**; Nasdaq +0.07% to **26,674.73**; Dow +0.36% to **50,644.28 record close**. The ceasefire-extension report drove the tape. **Chip stocks pulled back** intraday.

**Which means** for the wiki:
- [[2026-05-19-iran-mou-near]] Scenario A path is **partially confirmed but with new transit-fee complication**. Probability mix shifts toward de-escalation; complication adds optionality risk that needs to be priced.
- [[hedging-risk]] oil-vol hedges (XLE / USO) should be lighter at these levels.
- [[overview]] May 27 daily card needs the record-close data.
- Energy-sensitive positions (commodity producers page) — re-evaluate as Brent compresses.

## 2. Samsung HBM4 qualified by NVDA + AMD; supply begins June 2026

Per multiple sources (TradingKey + SBS Biz + DCD):

- **Samsung passed final quality tests for HBM4 from Nvidia and AMD** in early May 2026.
- **Full-scale supply expected to begin June 2026.**
- **Samsung raised its foundry price for 4nm HBM4 logic dies by 40-50%** — signaling technological progress and pricing power.
- **SK Hynix HBM share at NVDA reportedly dropped from ~70% to ~62%** — Samsung is taking material share back.
- **MU separately reported overtaking Samsung in HBM total market share** in 2026 (separate metric from NVDA-specific share).
- **MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN proposing to fund SK Hynix capacity expansion** in exchange for guaranteed HBM allocation in coming years — the **forward-funded capacity** signal already covered in [[2026-05-26-micron-1T-ubs-1625-pt]].

**Which means** for the wiki:
- [[MU]] bear case bullet "Samsung HBM4 closing the gap" — **validated this week**. The qualification + 40-50% logic die price hike + June 2026 supply ramp is the empirical confirmation of the May 17 wiki flag.
- [[NVDA]] supply triangulation thesis strengthened: NVDA now has 3 working HBM suppliers (SK Hynix 62% / MU ~18% / Samsung newly qualified) for Rubin generation. **Which means** NVDA has more pricing leverage with memory makers, not less.
- [[bottleneck-roadmap]] memory row — note that the supply response has begun (Samsung qualification = supply expansion), but demand is also expanding (hyperscaler forward-funded capacity).
