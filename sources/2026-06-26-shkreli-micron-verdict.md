# Martin Shkreli — "Verdict on Micron" (livestream)

*Bias: VERY HIGH / low-rigor. Shkreli is a convicted securities fraudster turned streamer/entertainer; this is a stream-of-consciousness live teardown (voice-clone gags, trading banter, "I barely know this space," pulling figures from ChatGPT live, promoting his own "Godell terminal"). He explicitly says he's **"long just playing with momentum"** and flip-flops intraday (builds, then "sold out all my Micron"). Treat as a sharp-but-unreliable contrarian's instinct, NOT research. Captured because a few of the analytical nuggets are genuinely useful and land on a core wiki name ([[MU]]).*

## TL;DR
- **Verdict: net BEARISH** — "I think this trade may come to an end. Now I'm bearish." The crux is **durability of earnings**: 13× earnings if they make $100B "this year" is meaningless if "are they going to make $100B *ever again*?" (the cycle-peak-P/E paradox, stated externally).
- **The SCA bear (sharpest point):** when new supply hits, "**maybe you can cancel those [take-or-pay] agreements**... you might just see a massive supply that cripples pricing — you won't see that with Nvidia because Nvidia controls supply." Directly sharpens the enforceability risk on [[mu-core-book-reconsideration]].
- **HBM is only ~15% of Micron's revenue** (his ChatGPT lookup, unverified) — so the revenue spike is mostly **commodity DRAM/NAND price increases, not the HBM franchise**. "That changes the story quite a lot." (Note: *lower* than the wiki's ~30% figure — verify.)
- **Relative-value inversion:** total memory/HBM revenue (~$124B last qtr across Micron+Hynix+Samsung) now **exceeds Nvidia's (~$81B)** — a year ago NVDA was bigger. "Why would the commodity in the box be worth more than the box?" → his bet is the memory revenue spike mean-reverts toward NVDA, not vice-versa.
- **Live tape (June 26):** "**chips down, software's up**" — names the exact rotation (MU −6%, SNDK −8%, NVDA −2%, AMD −5% vs ServiceNow +5%, Veeva, Workday, HubSpot, Snowflake green); cites "SemiAnalysis saying GPU pricing softening" (vs AWS *raising* GPU prices — conflicting). Corroborates the [[ai-software-disruption]] rotation note.

## Key facts / claims (and how confident)
- **Durability/certainty is the whole question (medium-useful, low-rigor).** Frames value correctly as the discounted sum of cash flows × *certainty* (the Jane Street "what's your certainty?" anecdote); his certainty on memory's earnings durability is low → bearish. This is the cycle-peak-P/E bear restated by an outside sharp instinct — corroborates the wiki's standing #1 MU bear.
- **SCA-cancelability (the genuinely additive bear, medium).** "What happens after supply hits the market — maybe you can cancel those agreements." The wiki has flagged take-or-pay *enforceability through a real bust* as untested; Shkreli independently lands on the same risk and adds the *cancellation* angle. **Surfaced, not resolved.**
- **Commodity vs secular (he waffles, lands bearish).** "Is it a commodity at 85% margins? ... but it's a standard product with specs multiple suppliers can make — that makes it a commodity," and "it's all coming from price increases." Memory lacks NVDA's *supply control* → more cyclical/commoditizable. The durable-franchise-vs-cyclical-rent fork, voted "cyclical rent."
- **HBM ~15% of MU rev (LOW confidence — ChatGPT-sourced, contradicts wiki's ~30%).** If true, even more of the spike is commodity-price-driven. Flag to verify against the FQ3 segment disclosure.
- **The memory>NVDA inversion (interesting but partly double-counts).** He himself surfaces the flaw: NVDA's reported revenue *includes* the HBM cost (HBM is a COGS component inside NVDA's rack revenue), so "total memory rev vs NVDA rev" double-counts the HBM that sits in both. Chat rebuttal (Gordian): "the fact that hyperscalers pay these prices tells you something." So the framing is suggestive, not clean.
- **Souring on NVDA too:** since HBM is a *cost* in NVDA's revenue, rising HBM prices pressure Rubin margins — "I'm starting to get a little bit [bearish] on Nvidia myself."
