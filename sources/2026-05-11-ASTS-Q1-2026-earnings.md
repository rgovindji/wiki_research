# AST SpaceMobile (ASTS) Q1 2026 Earnings — Source Summary

## TL;DR

- **Revenue $14.7M** — small but on plan; from U.S. government milestones + gateway hardware
- **Net loss $(191.0M)**; EPS $(0.66) — still in build-phase economics
- **Cash $3.5B (well-capitalized)**; long-term debt $2.97B after raising >$1B in Q1
- **2026 revenue guide $150-200M** — material vs Q1 actual $14.7M; back-half loaded
- **45 BlueBird satellites target in orbit by EOY 2026**; BlueBird 8/9/10 on Falcon 9 mid-June
- **Peak data speed achievement: 98.9 Mbps direct to unmodified smartphone** — proves the core technical claim
- **~60 mobile network operator partners globally; 3+ billion subscribers covered**
- **FCC Supplemental Coverage from Space authorization approved** — regulatory unblock for US direct-to-device service
- **Block 2 satellites expected to nearly double peak data speeds**

## Why this matters strategically

ASTS is making the single biggest binary bet in the space sector: direct-to-cell satellite-broadband-to-unmodified-phones. If it works at commercial scale, it disrupts terrestrial cellular networks globally — a multi-hundred-billion-dollar TAM. If it doesn't, the cash burn / dilution math is brutal.

The Q1 print is **technically validating** (98.9 Mbps to unmodified smartphone is a real achievement) but **commercially still pre-inflection** — $14.7M revenue against $191M net loss. The 2026 guide $150-200M is the next binary check.

## Key claims (and confidence)

- **Claim:** 45 BlueBirds in orbit by EOY 2026. → **Medium confidence.** Phased arrays completed through BB28; BB11-33 in advanced production. Falcon 9 launches scheduled. Execution risk: typical for satellite manufacturing.
- **Claim:** $150-200M revenue 2026 achievable. → **Medium-low confidence.** Requires Q4 ramp from Q1 $14.7M. Government contracts + gateway hardware can scale but the math is back-loaded.
- **Claim:** Direct-to-unmodified-smartphone is structurally differentiated. → **High confidence on technology.** The 98.9 Mbps record is real. Commercial differentiation requires regulatory + MNO build-out which is also progressing.
- **Claim:** $3.5B cash + $2.97B debt is enough to reach cash-flow break-even. → **Medium-low confidence.** Likely needs further capital before commercial revenue scales meaningfully.
