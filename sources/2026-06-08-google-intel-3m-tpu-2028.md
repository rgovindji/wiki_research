# Google taps Intel for 3M+ TPUs in 2028; Nvidia evaluating Intel foundry

## TL;DR
- **Google placed an order with Intel Foundry to manufacture >3 million TPUs in 2028** (The Information, reported Jun 8 2026, citing sources with direct knowledge). First large external AI-accelerator win for Intel Foundry.
- **Nvidia is separately evaluating** whether Intel's tech can make a processor combining four GPUs into a single unit — **no order placed yet.**
- INTC shares **+9% on the report**, extending a **~169% YTD gain**; the catalyst is TSMC's AI capacity crunch pushing chip designers to a credible second source, plus political tailwinds for US-based manufacturing.

## Key facts
- The 3M+ TPU figure is a 2028 production order — multi-year lead time, consistent with foundry qualification cycles.
- Mechanism corroborated by a separate SemiWiki/Synopsys piece (2026-06-11): **Intel's EMIB** (Embedded Multi-die Interconnect Bridge) advanced packaging is mature enough for heterogeneous AI-accelerator integration — silicon bridges embedded in the package substrate, 2.5D-class performance without a full interposer, co-designed via Synopsys EDA. Intel reportedly won Google's TPU **packaging** via EMIB-T (vs TSMC CoWoS). This is the technical basis for the foundry+packaging win.
- Supporting context: Tesla is Intel's first 14A customer (Terafab/Austin); WSJ reported a preliminary Intel–Apple chip deal; Intel has taken investments from the Trump administration, Nvidia, and SoftBank under CEO Lip-Bu Tan.
- Analyst color: Jacob Bourne (eMarketer) — "evidence AI's biggest players are racing to diversify a supply chain still heavily concentrated in TSMC." Gil Luria (DA Davidson) — supporting Intel supports US manufacturing, which matters for the administration relationship.

## Key claims (and confidence)
- **Order is real but unverified by Reuters** (single-sourced to The Information; Intel/Alphabet/Nvidia declined/no comment). Medium confidence on the specifics, high confidence on the directional theme (designers diversifying off TSMC).
- **This is the first genuine external-AI-accelerator validation of the Intel 18A/14A foundry thesis** the wiki has tracked as a binary. Moves the foundry-binary bull leg, not the product-side leg.

## Contradicting / tension notes
- Cuts against the wiki's TSMC-monopoly framing — but at 3M units in 2028 it's a *diversification* order, not a share-shift; TSMC's scale and CoWoS roadmap remain dominant. The bull read for TSM ([[TSM]] capacity sold out) and the bull read for INTC (second-source emerging) are *both* true.
- Tension with [[UNIMICRON]]'s "industry pivoting Yangmei from EMIB → CoWoS" framing: if Intel EMIB-T wins marquee AI packaging, EMIB is not dead — it's a parallel track, not a legacy one.
