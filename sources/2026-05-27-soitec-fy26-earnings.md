# Soitec FY26 Full-Year Results — Source Summary

Soitec released FY26 (fiscal year ended March 31, 2026) results on the morning of 2026-05-27. This was the **binary catalyst flagged in the [[SOITEC]] page since the May 17 page-creation** — the print that would clarify whether the +447% YTD photonics-revaluation was justified or front-run.

The stock closed pre-print May 26 at **€154.20** (after sliding 4.7% intraday on positioning trims). Following the print, **Soitec opened May 28 at €186.05 — a +20.7% gap up — on what financial press called "FY26 cash flow beat and AI photonics growth."**

This validates the wiki's pre-print bull/low-medium framing. The photonics-SOI thesis is now first-print-confirmed.

## TL;DR

- **FY26 revenue €592M, −34% reported YoY (−30% constant currency/scope)** — driven by mobile/auto inventory correction, as expected.
- **Q4 FY26 revenue €202M (−38% YoY but +25% QoQ)** — Q4 sequential growth **beat the ~20% guide**, signaling the bottom is in for mobile/RF.
- **🔥 Photonics-SOI revenue exceeded $100M in FY26 — achieved earlier than originally anticipated** (CEO Laurent Rémont's first-print disclosure).
- **EBITDA €151M / 25.4% margin** — operationally resilient through the downcycle.
- **Free Cash Flow €63M positive vs. −€23M FY25** — major working-capital improvement; balance sheet strengthens further (net debt €56M vs. €94M prior).
- **FY27 Q1 guide: revenue +15% YoY at constant currency and scope** — sequential acceleration confirmed.
- **FY27 capex €100M vs. €135M FY26** — capacity buildout phase ending; operating leverage flips on.
- **Net debt/EBITDA 0.4x** with €120M undrawn credit lines + 3.7yr avg debt maturity — balance sheet pristine.

## Photonics-SOI: the data point that mattered most

The pre-earnings wiki framing set up a single binary question: *does Photonics-SOI absolute revenue confirm the bull thesis?*

**The answer: yes, and earlier than anticipated.**

> CEO Laurent Rémont (first earnings print since taking the seat April 1, 2026): *"Photonics-SOI revenue exceeded $100 million in fiscal 2026"* — explicitly framed as earlier than original expectations, attributed to *"strong traction in AI markets"* driving high-speed optical transceivers and co-packaged optics qualifications.

**Which means** for the [[SOITEC]] page bull case:
- The "Photonics-SOI ~17% of revenue, growing 20-30%+" framing in the pre-print snapshot is now **confirmed and front-loaded** — at €592M FY26 revenue and >$100M (~€92M) photonics-SOI, the segment is actually ~15-17% of revenue *with growth running ahead of plan*.
- Edge & Cloud AI segment +8% YoY in FY26 and +6% YoY in Q4 — through a horrific mobile/auto downturn, the AI-photonics leg of the business grew. **Which means** the bear case "race against time" framing is moderating: photonics is already big enough to matter, and the broader mobile cyclical bottom is forming.
