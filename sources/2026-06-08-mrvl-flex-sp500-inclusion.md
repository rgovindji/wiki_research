# Marvell + Flex join the S&P 500 (effective June 22); semis lead a partial rebound off Friday's rout

## TL;DR
- **S&P Dow Jones Indices announced (after Friday's close, June 5) that [[MRVL]] and [[FLEX]] will join the S&P 500 effective June 22**, replacing Pool Corp (POOL) and The Campbell's Company (CPB). Part of a quarterly reshuffle touching 20+ companies.
- **Both additions are wiki tickers** — MRVL (bull / medium-high) and FLEX (bull / medium). MRVL is the third semiconductor name added to the index in the recent run; the inclusion forces mechanical index-fund buying into a stock already +130%+ YTD.
- **Monday June 8 was a partial rebound** off Friday's worst session of 2026: S&P **+0.30% to 7,405.73**, Nasdaq **+0.86% to 25,929.66**, Dow **−0.16% to 50,786.01**. Semis led: **[[MU]] +9.9%** (after −13.3% Friday), **MRVL +9.6%**, [[NVDA]] and [[AVGO]] higher; VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) **+~5%**.

## Key facts
- **Effective date:** June 22, 2026 (next rebalance). Announced June 5 after the close.
- **Additions / deletions:** MRVL replaces Pool Corp; FLEX replaces Campbell's. (Both leavers fell on the news; both joiners jumped ~10% Monday.)
- **MRVL market cap ~$179B**, +130%+ YTD, ~70x trailing — already an S&P-500-scale company; the index was a laggard to the fundamentals, not vice versa.
- **Jensen Huang** (Computex / partnership context) floated MRVL as a potential **"next trillion-dollar company"** — sentiment tailwind layered onto the mechanical inclusion bid.
- **Rebound breadth:** MU was the single largest S&P gainer Monday; Marvell's S&P add news + Micron's HBM-sold-out narrative drove the chip-led recovery. Friday had wiped ~$1T from semis; Monday clawed part of it back.

## Key claims (and how confident)
- **S&P 500 inclusion is a real, datable demand event for MRVL/FLEX — high confidence.** Index funds tracking the S&P 500 must buy on/around June 22; for a ~$179B name this is a multi-billion-dollar forced bid. This is mechanical, not a thesis change.
- **The inclusion is a *lagging* confirmation, not new information about the business — high confidence.** MRVL qualified because the stock tripled on the Q1 FY27 print and guide raise already captured in [[MRVL]]. The index is catching up to the fundamentals; it does not add a new bull leg beyond the one-time flow.
- **Monday's rebound is price action, not a fundamentals signal — high confidence.** No new demand print landed; the bounce is dip-buying + the inclusion headline + Micron's sold-out-HBM narrative. The June 5 "burden of proof shifted toward bears" read (per [[overview]] / [[ai-bubble-debate]]) is not resolved by one green day.
- **The week's binaries still dominate — high confidence.** **May CPI (June 10, 8:30am ET)** + **[[ORCL]] Q4 earnings (June 10 AMC)** + **Adobe Q2 (June 11)** are this week's real tests; the inclusion flow and Monday bounce are noise relative to those.
