The next sector to watch: AI-Security

My forward call: cybersecurity / AI-security is the AI trade's next leg that's still under-owned relative to its catalyst stack. Reasons:

  • A budget shift, quantified: Gartner sees AI-tied spending going from 8% of cyber budgets (2023) → 40% (2027); ~2/3 of orgs are raising cyber investment in 2026, 1-in-4 by >25%.
  • A brand-new attack surface: agentic AI is "the most consequential unsecured asset in the enterprise," and models can now autonomously find & exploit vulnerabilities (the concern behind the Claude-Mythos export controls).
  • A regulatory tailwind: the June-2, 2026 EO ("Promoting Advanced AI Innovation and Security") strengthens federal + private cyber defenses and stands up a Treasury cyber clearinghouse — a spending forcing-function.
  • Platform consolidation favors the scaled players (PANW's +44%-in-a-month re-rate is the tape confirming it).
How to play it (with the entry caveat): the leaders CRWD (+11%) and PANW (+18%) have already run — the sector is rising but the megacaps aren't at pullback entries. Watch for dips, or the second tier (ZS, S/SentinelOne, FTNT) and the AI-native-edge angle (NET, already on the watchlist). This is a forward hypothesis, not yet a high-conviction wiki thesis — but the catalyst stack is the strongest of any adjacent sector right now.

Position discipline (the late-cycle overlay)

Even the "purchasable" picks should be accumulated in tranches with hedges on, not chased. The tape flipped bull, but CAPE ~39.8 (highest since 2000), Mag-7 ~35% of the S&P (dot-com-peak concentration), and a higher-for-longer Fed mean sentiment alone can compress multiples. The wiki's posture stands: hold structural compounders at reduced size · buy quality on pullbacks (this is one) · keep the hedge stack · don't chase the froth.
