OUST + BB — why they both rocketed

Physical-AI / autonomy momentum + two real catalysts · 1 July 2026

Both at 52-wk highs Extended — chase risk high Prices: Polygon (06-30 close)

The one-liner. Both hit 52-week highs on 2026-06-30 riding a physical-AI / autonomy momentum wave (a retail run that lifted the whole autonomy group) — but each also had a real, separate catalyst: OUST = a stack of commercial lidar deals; BB = a clean QNX earnings beat. The difference that matters: BB's move is earnings-backed (a genuine turnaround); OUST's is momentum + deal-flow on a stock that's now parabolic and structurally out-scaled by China's Hesai. Don't chase either at the high.

OUST — Ouster

What it does

Makes digital lidar sensors — the Rev8 / OS family (incl. native-color lidar) for automotive, robotics, industrial machines, and smart infrastructure (traffic). Post-Velodyne-merger, it's repositioning as a "Physical AI" sensor supplier — the eyes for autonomous machines. Western lidar pure-play; a real business (Q1'26 rev +49% YoY), but small.

Why it rocketed (a stack of deals)

  • Benchmark manufacturing expansion → capacity for 100,000+ Rev8 sensors/year (robotics/auto/industrial/infra).
  • BlueCity highway deployment around MetLife Stadium (NJ DOT) — a permanent lidar traffic "digital twin" ahead of the 2026 FIFA World Cup.
  • AIM Intelligent Machines partnership — sensors into autonomous heavy machinery (mining/construction/defense).
  • FieldAI collaboration — Rev8 native-color lidar into general-purpose industrial robots.
  • BABA compliance — Rev8 eligible for federally-funded US infrastructure.

But it doesn't fix the structural bear: China's Hesai is ~25× larger and already profitable (4M unit capacity vs OUST's ~50–70K); the wiki's condition for an upgrade was a *humanoid-OEM design win* or *US tariffs on Hesai* — the FieldAI/AIM deals are traction, not that. BB — BlackBerry

What it does (not phones anymore)

Two businesses: QNX — an automotive real-time OS in 250M+ vehicles (digital displays, ADAS, autonomous functions), with a ~$1B royalty backlog — and Secure Communications / cybersecurity (government-grade secure comms, FedRAMP, device management). The phone business is long gone; this is an automotive-software + secure-comms company.

Why it rocketed (a real earnings beat)

  • Fiscal Q1 2027 beat: revenue $152.9M (+26% YoY), adjusted EBITDA $36.3M (+144%), GAAP net income $8.5M — the stock popped ~20%.
  • QNX is the engine — automotive-software demand; ~$1B royalty backlog; management guides QNX rev ~+15%.
  • Secure Communications growth + FedRAMP wins + buybacks — the turnaround shifts from cost-cutting to growth.

The shared thread

Both got an extra lift from a physical-AI / autonomy / edge momentum wave — the same day, a Stocktwits piece grouped SLS, BB and OUST hitting 52-week highs together, and Aeva jumped ~11% alongside OUST. This is a retail-driven momentum theme (AI leaving the datacenter for robots, vehicles and infrastructure) lifting the autonomy/edge basket. It amplifies moves in both directions — which is exactly why both names are extended and chase-risky here, even though the underlying catalysts (OUST's deal flow, BB's earnings) are real.

Bottom line. Real catalysts, momentum-amplified, both at 52-wk highs. BB is the higher-quality move (earnings-backed turnaround, legit QNX franchise). OUST is the higher-beta one (genuine traction, but parabolic and structurally out-scaled by Hesai — the wiki keeps it bull/low). Neither is an entry at the high; let them pull back to the marked zones and reassess.
