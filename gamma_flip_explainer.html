What you're looking at

Big trading desks sell options to the rest of the market, then hedge what they've sold by buying and selling index futures all day. Which way they hedge depends on where the index sits relative to the "gamma flip."

🛡️ Above the flip — they lean AGAINST you

Hedging is negative feedback: sell rallies, buy dips. It quietly smooths everything out. Dips are shallow and bought instantly. This is the calm market we've had for weeks.

🔥 Below the flip — they lean WITH you

The math inverts to positive feedback: sell into weakness, buy into strength — adding fuel to whatever direction price is already going. No shock absorbers.

Today the S&P closed below that flip for the first time in this stretch. That's why the brief was nervous about a number, not a headline: crossing 7,450 changes whether the most mechanical participants are absorbing the moves or accelerating them. Until the index closes back above it, every catalyst — Micron, a Fed speaker, a stray headline — lands on a market with no cushion. That's why we want a close back over the line before trusting a bounce.
