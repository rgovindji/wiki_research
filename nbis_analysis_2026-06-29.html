What actually happened (the Q1 inflection)

Revenue +684% YoY to $399M; profitable on Adj EBITDA ($129.5M, 45% margin); operating cash flow swung to $2.26B; cash $9.3B. Cost of revenue 49%→26%, R&D 72%→17% — a one-quarter jump from build- to harvest-phase. Backed by NVDA's $2B stake (8.3%), a $17.4B Microsoft deal and a $27B Meta deal (~$44B combined contracted).

The two cases

▲ Bull

  • Thesis validated, not hopeful — the strongest quarter any public neocloud has printed; profitability inflection is real.
  • Least commodity-like neocloud — real software/optimization + goodput moat answers the 'dumb shell' short.
  • Owned power — ~4GW for 2026, "sold out again," 4+ customers per cluster. Power, not GPUs, is the bottleneck.
  • Spot-inference optionality — only ~50–60% contracted; the rest captures the rent spike.
  • Deepest NVDA tie + supply priority on next-gen Vera Rubin.

▼ Bear / risks

  • Demanding price — consensus PT (~$242) sits below spot; the stock has outrun the Street.
  • "Profit" is optics — mostly a non-cash ClickHouse mark; core ops still lose ~$100M/qtr.
  • Extreme concentration — MSFT + Meta are the story.
  • Dilution risk — $20–25B capex on ~$3.2B revenue, ~40% equity-funded via serial converts.
  • Fast GPU depreciation + legacy Russia/post-Yandex perception + macro multiple-compression overlay.

What flips it — falsifiers & tells

  • Q2 print (master switch): does Adj EBITDA profitability hold?
  • ARR march to $7–9B — any stall breaks the forward-multiple math.
  • A third hyperscaler customer — best de-risk on concentration.
  • Convert / equity issuance in a weak tape — the dilution trigger.
  • Spot GPU rents rolling over — the ~40–50% spot leg cuts both ways.
  • Technical invalidation: a daily close below $234.

My read. Hold bull / medium — strongest operational case in the group, real owned-power + goodput moat, but forward returns are now a price question gated on Q2 durability and orderly financing. Don't chase $261. Accumulate in Zone B ($232–240), back up the truck in Zone C ($200–215), and treat a close below $234 as the stop.
