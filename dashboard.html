Six Leading Theses

The current investment view. Stances are research stances, not recommendations.

01AI infrastructure picks-and-shovels remain the highest-conviction long-term holds

NVDA ($1T confirmed demand through 2027), TSM (every advanced AI chip flows through their fabs), ASML (EUV monopoly). These three remain the cleanest long-term semi holds despite stretched valuations.

Owning the top 3 alone gives you a third of the S&P 500's AI-infra exposure. Diversify within the supply chain (memory, equipment, fab consumables, power) rather than concentrate further — the marginal $ in NVDA at $5.4T is buying you the same cycle exposure as the marginal $ in MU at $850B, with less upside and more drawdown risk.
02Hyperscalers are the buyers AND the monetizers — pick your spot carefully

MSFT / AMZN / GOOGL / META together account for the largest share of 2026 AI capex but also the customer base. GOOGL looks cheapest (fwd P/E ~29.7) and has its own silicon (TPU, co-designed with AVGO).

Of the four hyperscalers, GOOGL is the cleanest single-name play on both sides of the AI cycle — they buy the chips AND monetize the tokens at the lowest forward multiple. If you can only own one Mag 7 for AI exposure, this is the trade.
03Be cautious on consumer tech at premium multiples

TSLA at 178x 2026 EPS. AAPL flipped to bear May 2026 — TSMC deprioritization at A16; memory crunch projected to halve smartphone units (1.4B → 550M by 2027).

Short-term these names can rally on news (Apple-Intel announcement bumped both up). But the structural setup says trim premium-consumer-tech and rotate the capital into AI-infra picks-and-shovels where the bull thesis still has multi-year runway.
04The non-NVIDIA hedge is real and concrete

AVGO designs custom AI accelerators for six hyperscalers and just disclosed OpenAI Project Titan = 10 GW by 2029. AI revenue +106% YoY to $8.4B last quarter; $100B AI sales target by 2027. AMD is the credible #2 GPU with OpenAI 6 GW + Meta 6 GW + Oracle 50K MI450 deals; Helios rack Q3 2026 on TSMC 2nm.

If NVDA gross margins compress (Blackwell ramp, competitive threats), AVGO is the disproportionate gainer — it's the only public name with a serious custom-silicon revenue ramp. Pair-trade NVDA long with AVGO long instead of NVDA-only concentration.
05Overlooked downstream: data center construction

~$76B combined contracted backlog across FIX ($12.46B backlog +81% YoY, 45% data center mix), EME ($15.62B RPOs, FY26 EPS guide $28-$30), and PWR ($48.5B record backlog). Classified as industrials, not technology.

These names aren't priced as AI plays — they're priced as industrials. When the AI narrative finally maps to them (will happen as construction backlogs become visible quarterly and ETF rebalances follow), there's a re-rating catalyst that the obvious AI names don't have.
06The "invisible" fab layer

TER (chip test — Q1 +87% YoY, 70% AI exposure), ENTG (FOUPs, ultra-pure materials), APD (industrial gases for fabs — Samsung Pyeongtaek is APD's largest semi investment ever). Three different industry classifications, same demand driver.

TER/ENTG/APD give you AI-fab exposure at non-AI multiples. APD especially — a dividend aristocrat trading at industrial-gas multiples while shipping the largest semiconductor investment in company history. Cheap optionality on the 2027 fab buildout.

Open Questions

Unresolved variables that will determine which direction the multi-year thesis breaks.

Does ASML finally take pricing power in 2028-2030, or remain the generous monopolist? Historically the latter — supply-constrained, not commercially constrained.

If YES: ASML gross margin expands from ~50% toward 60%+, market cap re-rates 30-50% on multiple alone (separate from volume). The single highest-leverage call on the multi-year semi thesis. If NO: the bottleneck rents stay diffused across the customer base; ASML grows with volume but doesn't get the multiple boost.

Does SNDK's HBF (High-Bandwidth Flash) ship and scale? Vertically stacked NAND with 8-16x HBM capacity at lower cost. First samples 2H 2026.

If YES: HBF creates a new memory category that partially substitutes for HBM in cost-sensitive inference workloads — bearish for MU's HBM moat, bullish for SNDK on a 2-3 year horizon. If NO: SNDK stays a high-beta NAND play and MU's HBM thesis stays intact.

When does the first hyperscaler cut AI capex guidance? Most important single leading indicator of cycle peak.

First capex cut from MSFT / AMZN / GOOGL / META is the moment to derisk infra exposure aggressively. The full supply chain (NVDA → AMAT → MU → FIX) re-rates simultaneously on that signal. Set alerts on hyperscaler quarterly capex guides; this is the canary.

How to size NVDA + GOOGL + MSFT + META given correlated AI exposure? Owning all four is one bet, not four.

The marginal AI-thesis dollar should go into the supply chain (memory, equipment, fab consumables, power, construction), not more Mag 7.
