The model rumor — what's real vs. what breaks

▲ What's real

  • Meta Superintelligence Labs under Alexandr Wang is real and shipping.
  • Dual-track on 08-Apr-2026: open-weights Llama 5 + closed Muse Spark — Meta's first proprietary model.
  • Efficiency claim: small models ~as capable as mid-size Llama 4 for ~10× less compute.
  • Mango / Avocado next-gen models rumored for later-2026 — the "secret model" you heard about.

▼ What breaks the "crush Opus" claim

  • Muse Spark ranks 4th on the Artificial Analysis Index (score 52) — behind the Opus tier, not crushing it. Meta is a frontier follower today.
  • Research-talent erosion: Yann LeCun left to start AME Labs, confirming Meta "fell behind after Llama 4," FAIR "isolated," good people gone.
  • A short-seller (Chanos/Zlatev) is flatly bear on META — "AI strategy makes no sense."
  • "Beats Opus but not Fable" is the optimistic read of an unproven next-gen model.
The investor reframe — the part that matters: even if Mango/Avocado did crush Opus 4.8, it wouldn't directly move META. Meta doesn't sell frontier-model API access as a revenue line (unlike OpenAI / Anthropic / GCP). A leading model helps three indirect ways: better ad targeting (the real, here-and-now ROI), a consumer-AI product across 3B users, and justifying the $125–145B capex. Which means: the binding variables for the stock are capex discipline, the equity-raise / dilution overhang, and ad-ROI — not benchmark rank.

What flips it — falsifiers & the catalyst

  • Q2 print (the catalyst): capex trajectory + any equity-raise signal + ad-ROI color. This, not a leaked benchmark, is the mover.
  • An equity raise → confirms the self-funding pillar is broken (the binary overhang fires) → bearish.
  • Ad revenue acceleration attributable to AI targeting → the capex starts paying → bullish.
  • Mango/Avocado actually leading the index AND a monetization mechanism → genuine upside surprise vs a skeptically-positioned stock.
  • Technical: reclaim the 20-EMA ($577) = de-rate maybe over; close below $540 = base broken.

My read. Don't anchor a META thesis on a secret frontier model — it's a secondary lever, not the driver. The asymmetric bull case is "the $125–145B capex shows up as accelerating ad revenue + a monetizing Meta-AI product, and they keep self-funding without dilution." A model that leapfrogs would help that (lifts the research-talent bear, validates the spend) but the stock answers to capex/dilution/ad-ROI. Bull / medium, leaning cautious; let the chart base and the Q2 print do the talking, not the rumor.
