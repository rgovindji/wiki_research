Catalyst-driven asymmetry, not cheap-and-boring. A barbell of under-followed small/mid-caps with hard, recent catalysts — plus two overlooked-quality anchors — for the money rotating off the crowded AI mega-cap trade.

Inception 2026-06-16 · $100,000 notional · 8 names / 80% invested / 20% cash · entry = 6/16 close · model portfolio, not advice

What changed (v2). v1 was a de-rated large-cap value screen (ADP/ICE/KKR/Molina…). The verdict was fair: too boring, low upside, and "down = broken." It confused out-of-favor with undiscovered. v2 swaps the screen for the real brief: every name has a hard, recent catalyst and genuine upside — and "down" is only allowed when it's sector-beta on an intact catalyst (and sized as a bet).

The thesis is unchanged — war over, oil ~$80, breadth widening, and Baker's call that the bottleneck trade is ending. What changed is the kind of name: instead of cheap mega-caps, this owns the catalyst-rich edges the crowd hasn't fully paid for — the fuel, the power, the grid labor, the minerals, the inference layer, the AI-infra conversion.

The filter (v2)

1. A hard, recent catalyst. A DOE award, a hyperscaler contract, a government stake — not "it's cheap."
2. Real upside. 2-3x potential on the asymmetric sleeve; anchors with genuine growth, not low multiples.
3. Not a falling knife. Prefer names working/inflecting; catalyst names that pulled back on sector beta are flagged and sized down.
4. Under-followed. Off the consensus AI-mega-cap and value-screen radar.
