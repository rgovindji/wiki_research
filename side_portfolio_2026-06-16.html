The Rotation Side Book

Quality the crowd left behind. A complement to the AI-heavy After Hours book — built for the money that rotates out of the bottleneck trade now that the war is over, into franchises that haven't run and aren't yet in the masses' eyes.

Inception 2026-06-16 · $100,000 notional · 10 names / 80% invested / 20% cash · entry = 6/16 close · model portfolio, not advice

The AI trade isn't dead — but three forces argue for a parallel book of overlooked names: the Iran war is over (oil cracked to ~$80, risk-on breadth, the rate-cut conversation reopening); Gavin Baker's call that the bottleneck trade is ending and the next game is "enduring franchise value on the other side"; and a rotation already underway in 2026 — leadership broadening out of mega-cap tech into financials, industrials, materials and (lately) healthcare. This book owns the franchises that money rotates toward, not the picks-and-shovels it already bid to the sky.

The filter — what earns a seat

1. Hasn't run. Well below its 52-week high, de-rated on a narrative — not deteriorating fundamentals.
2. Quality / franchise. Durable moat, real cash flow. No pre-profit story stocks — that's a different risk book.
3. A rotation catalyst. Benefits from breadth, lower rates/oil, the IPO wave, M&A, or money leaving crowded AI.
4. Not in the masses' eyes. An under-followed angle or an out-of-favor sector — not the obvious trade.

What I refused to buy (the discipline)

Your "hasn't run" rule did real work. The obvious post-war and electrification trades flunked it — so they're out, on purpose:

  • Grid / electrical equipment (ETN −6%, HUBB −11%, NVT, AGX) — superb theme, all near 52-week highs. Already run.
  • Nuclear / uranium (URA +120% past year) — structural AI-power story, but crowded and extended.
  • Copper at the high (FCX) — took the cheaper, lower-cost long-life name (SCCO) instead.
  • The run AI bottleneck names (NVDA / MU / AVGO / AXTI) — that's the After Hours book's job; this is the complement.
How it fits with After Hours
After Hours owns the AI build-out (supply-side chips, memory, neocloud). This book owns what the build-out's success rotates money toward — de-rated franchises, the listing-wave plumbing, contrarian defensives, a real-asset deficit. If Baker is right and the bottleneck peaks, this is the hedge that also makes money; if AI keeps running, these are quality names bought cheap with limited downside. Deliberately low correlation.
The honest risks
Rotation head-fake is the #1 risk — money has tried to leave mega-cap tech before and been yanked back by the next AI catalyst (June 15 tech +3%, June 16 tech −1.2% — it whipsaws on every headline). That's why cash is 20% and this is a 6-18mo tilt, not all-in. Sticky rates (BoJ just hiked to 1%, Warsh Fed unknown) blunt the rate-levered names (KKR, MOH, ICE-mortgage). Value traps — "cheap" can stay cheap unless the catalyst (M&A, cuts, IPO wave) actually shows. New book, zero track record: conviction labels are hypotheses to be scored.
