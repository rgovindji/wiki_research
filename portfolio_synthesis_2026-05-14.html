Suggested entry mechanics

Risk-managed deployment
Tranching strategy
Deploy the 80% structural bucket in thirds over 4-6 weeks: 1/3 immediately on any >3% pullback in mega-cap names; 1/3 after June 10 CPI clears; 1/3 reserved for any catalyst-driven re-rating. Avoid lump-sum at ATH.
YOLO sizing
Each YOLO position is 1-4% of total portfolio. NVDA calls = 3.5% MAX; ASTS = 3% MAX. Total YOLO must not exceed 20%. Any single -100% loss in YOLO bucket = -3-4% portfolio drawdown, survivable.
Exit triggers (structural)
Trim cycle-sensitive names (MU, SNDK) when SemiAnalysis flags memory ASPs peaking. Trim mega-cap AI on first hyperscaler capex cut. Hold construction labor (FIX/EME/PWR) through cycle — they're contracted backlog.
NVDA options play execution
Buy $240C May 22 expiry on any pullback to $215 or lower before May 20 close. Pay no more than $10-12 per contract. Set hard stop: exit at 50% loss or after May 21 IV crush even if break-even. Don't roll into next earnings.
ASTS squeeze positioning
Enter equity (not options — short interest data implies hard borrow). Add on any pullback to $35 before mid-June Falcon 9 launch. Hold through launch; trim 50% if pop >30% post-launch, hold rest for AT&T/Verizon commercial contract.
Rebalance cadence
Review the 80/20 split quarterly on Q2/Q3 print cycles. Conviction changes (e.g., another stance flip like ON neutral→bull) trigger ad hoc rebalances. Don't rebalance on price action alone — wait for thesis change.

NVDA earnings is binary. ±12.9% expected move. IV elevated. Single-day move could compress full month of allocation gains. Position-size NVDA core (not just calls) accordingly.
04
The 20% YOLO bucket can lose 50%+ in aggregate in a true risk-off scenario. NVDA calls expire worthless; ASTS squeeze unwinds; speculative small-caps crash. -10% portfolio drag from a single bad week is the realistic downside.
05
Don't average down on YOLO. If NVDA calls drop -50% pre-earnings, exit. If ASTS drops -25% pre-launch, exit. YOLO is binary by design — averaging down converts a thesis trade into a value trade with no margin of safety.
